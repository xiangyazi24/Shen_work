INTERPRETATION
--------------
(i)  NO first-order right factor over Q(n).
     rsolve_hyper (Petkovšek's algorithm) returned 0 for the original operator,
     meaning the recurrence has no hypergeometric term solution over Q(n).
     Equivalently, the operator cannot be written as L = R1 * R0 with R0 of
     order 1 and coefficients in Q(n).

(ii) NO first-order left factor over Q(n).
     rsolve_hyper returned 0 for the formal adjoint operator as well.
     Since a first-order left factor of L exists iff the adjoint L* has a
     first-order right factor, this confirms no left factor either.

(iii) Q(sqrt(2))(n) DOES NOT change either answer.
     The unique degree-6 irreducible (over Q) factor of the trailing coefficient
     ell0 and the unique degree-6 irreducible factor of the leading coefficient
     ell3 both remain irreducible over Q(sqrt(2)).  The Petkovšek dispersion
     structure (which governs the possible degree of the certificate polynomial)
     is therefore identical over Q(sqrt(2)) to that over Q, so no new
     hypergeometric solutions can arise.
