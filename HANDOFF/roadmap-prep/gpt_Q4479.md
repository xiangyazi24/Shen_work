## Important distinction: orbit compactness vs time-translate compactness

The proposed theorem

```javascript
orbit_tail_precompact : ∃ T*, IsCompact (closure {u t | t ≥ T*})
```

is useful but may be weaker than the repo field TimeTranslateCompactnessRaw. For omega-limit and stability arguments, the usual required compactness is:

```plain text
For every sequence t_n → ∞, the time-shifted trajectories
  (s,x) ↦ u(t_n+s,x)
are precompact locally uniformly on compact s-windows.
```

So do not stop at single-slice compactness unless the chosen K.locallyConverges is literally a slice-convergence relation. If K.locallyConverges is trajectory-local convergence, build the parabolic space-time version directly.

## Relation to Paper 3 step-3 spectral engine

This compactness route is mostly independent of the step-3 linear spectral engine. It uses:

- Paper 2 global existence and boundedness / positive-time smoothing;

- logistic/max-principle eventual bounds;

- elliptic resolver bounds;

- Arzelà-Ascoli or a concrete sequential compactness lemma.

The spectral engine is used later for local stability / decay around equilibria. Compactness is what lets the global dynamics enter an omega-limit set or a local stable basin; it is not itself a modal stability calculation.
