# 2. Consistency of (A) and (D): order 21 versus reduction order 7

Let

\[
K=L_\pi,
\qquad \pi=a-1,
\qquad v_\pi(3)=3,
\]

and use the good integral model with unit discriminant at `π`.  Its reduction is an elliptic curve over `F_3` with

\[
\#\widetilde E(\mathbf F_3)=7.
\]

Since a group of prime order is cyclic,

\[
\widetilde E(\mathbf F_3)\cong \mathbf Z/7\mathbf Z
\]

and its exponent is `7`.

Good reduction gives a reduction homomorphism

\[
\operatorname{red}:E_0(K)\longrightarrow \widetilde E(\mathbf F_3)
\]

whose kernel is the formal group `E_1(K)`.

Assume the independently checked global torsion statement

\[
E_0(L)_{\rm tors}\cong\mathbf Z/21\mathbf Z.
\]

Write its unique primary subgroups as

\[
T_3\cong\mathbf Z/3\mathbf Z,
\qquad
T_7\cong\mathbf Z/7\mathbf Z.
\]

## 2.1 The order-7 subgroup

For good reduction in residue characteristic `3`, reduction is injective on torsion of order prime to `3`.  Therefore

\[
T_7\hookrightarrow\widetilde E(\mathbf F_3).
\]

Both groups have order `7`, so this injection is an isomorphism:

\[
\operatorname{red}(T_7)=\widetilde E(\mathbf F_3).
\]

## 2.2 The order-3 subgroup

For every `P in T_3`, the order of `red(P)` divides both `3` and `7`.  Hence

\[
\operatorname{red}(P)=O.
\]

Thus

\[
T_3\subseteq E_1(K).
\]

On torsion, the reduction sequence is exactly

\[
0\longrightarrow\mathbf Z/3\mathbf Z
\longrightarrow\mathbf Z/21\mathbf Z
\longrightarrow\mathbf Z/7\mathbf Z
\longrightarrow0.
\]

This completely resolves the apparent contradiction.

A formal group over a ramified `3`-adic field can contain nonzero `3`-torsion.  Here the explicit rational point of order `3` is such a point.  The standard prime-to-residue-characteristic torsion-injectivity theorem never claims otherwise.

## 2.3 Important consequence for the separatedness lemma

Because the formal kernel contains a nonzero point `T` with `[3]T=O`, the following unqualified statement is false if the chosen valuation assigns a finite value to zero:

```text
for every nonzero Q in E1(K),
  v(z([3]Q)) >= v(z(Q)) + 1.
```

The correct form is one of:

```text
Q != O and [3]Q != O
  -> v(z([3]Q)) >= v(z(Q)) + 1,
```

or a statement using an extended valuation with `v(0)=infinity`.

The shared-filtration proof only applies the growth lemma along a chain whose final point is assumed nonzero.  Every intermediate point is then nonzero, so the corrected lemma is sufficient.

# 4. Consistency of (C) and (E): the Selmer line is torsion

Use the convention

```text
phi    : E  -> E',
phihat : E' -> E,
phihat o phi = [3] on E,
phi o phihat = [3] on E'.
```

Then

```text
Sel^phi
```

controls the quotient

\[
E'(L)/\phi E(L),
\]

while

```text
Sel^phihat
```

controls

\[
E(L)/\widehat\phi E'(L).
\]

The relevant exact sequence is

\[
0\to
\frac{E'(L)[\widehat\phi]}{\phi(E(L)[3])}
\to
\frac{E'(L)}{\phi E(L)}
\to
\frac{E(L)}{3E(L)}
\to
\frac{E(L)}{\widehat\phi E'(L)}
\to0.
\tag{1}
\]

Here

\[
E'(L)[\widehat\phi]=0,
\qquad
E(L)[3]=E[\phi](L)=\langle T\rangle.
\]

The first term of (1) is therefore zero.

## 4.1 The surviving cubeclass

In Tate coordinates,

\[
T=(0,0),
\qquad
-T=(0,-2).
\]

For the dual descent map,

\[
\delta_{\widehat\phi}(T)=[1/2]=[4],
\qquad
\delta_{\widehat\phi}(-T)=[-2]=[2].
\]

Since `-1=(-1)^3`, the signs do not change cubeclasses.  Hence

\[
\langle[2]\rangle=\{1,[2],[4]\}
\]

is precisely the image of

\[
H=\langle T\rangle\cong\mathbf Z/3\mathbf Z
\]

in the quotient `E(L)/phihat E'(L)`.

The order-`7` torsion contributes no class: multiplication by `3` is an automorphism of `Z/7`, so

\[
T_7\subseteq3E(L).
\]

This is why a cyclic torsion group of order `21` contributes only one `F_3`-dimension to `E(L)/3E(L)`.

## 4.2 Exact quotient consequences

From

\[
\operatorname{Sel}^{\phi}(E/L)=\{1\}
\]

one obtains

\[
E'(L)=\phi E(L).
\tag{2}
\]

From

\[
\operatorname{Sel}^{\widehat\phi}(E'/L)
=\langle[2]\rangle
\]

and the explicit nontrivial torsion class, one obtains

\[
E(L)/\widehat\phi E'(L)=\langle T\rangle.
\tag{3}
\]

For any `P in E(L)`, equation (3) gives

\[
P=iT+\widehat\phi(R)
\]

for some `i in {0,1,2}` and `R in E'(L)`.  By (2), write `R=phi(Q)`.  Then

\[
P=iT+\widehat\phi\phi(Q)=iT+3Q.
\]

Therefore the exact weak-descent endpoint is

\[
\boxed{E(L)=H+3E(L),\qquad H=\langle T\rangle,\quad [3]H=0.}
\]

This proves (E), not merely a dimension statement.

## 4.3 The familiar rank formula

If one invokes Mordell--Weil finite generation, then

\[
\dim_{\mathbf F_3}E(L)/3E(L)
=\operatorname{rank}E(L)+\dim_{\mathbf F_3}E(L)[3]
=\operatorname{rank}E(L)+1.
\]

Equation (1) gives

\[
\operatorname{rank}E(L)
=\dim E'(L)/\phi E(L)
 +\dim E(L)/\widehat\phi E'(L)-1
=0+1-1=0.
\]

The subtracted dimension is exactly the rational order-`3` subgroup `H`.

No vanishing or finiteness assumption on `Sha` is needed for the Selmer upper bounds.  However, in the requested MW-finite-generation-free build, this rank formula must not be used as the terminal argument.  The weak decomposition above and local separatedness replace it.

# 5. Verification of the annihilator chain (F)

## 5.1 Why `[21]E0(L)=0`

The two finite exponents are

```text
exp(H)                   divides 3,
exp(E~(F3))              divides 7.
```

Set

\[
e=\operatorname{lcm}(3,7)=21.
\]

For any `P in E(L)`, repeatedly apply weak descent:

\[
P=h_0+3P_1,
\quad
P_1=h_1+3P_2,
\quad\ldots
\]

with `h_i in H`.  Since `[21]h_i=0`, for every `n` one obtains

\[
[21]P=[3^n]([21]P_n).
\tag{4}
\]

Because `7 | 21`, every point `[21]P_n` reduces to the identity, so all points in (4) lie in the formal kernel.

If `[21]P` were nonzero, then every intermediate point in (4) would be nonzero.  Applying the strict formal valuation growth `n` times gives a lower bound tending to infinity for the fixed finite valuation of `[21]P`, a contradiction.  Hence

\[
\boxed{[21]E(L)=0.}
\]

The explicit order-`3` point in the formal kernel causes no problem because it is killed before the separatedness argument begins.

## 5.2 Why `[63]Ehat0(L)=0`

Let `R in E'(L)`.  Since `[21]E(L)=0`,

\[
[21]\widehat\phi(R)=0.
\]

Apply `phi` and use `phi o phihat=[3]`:

\[
0
=\phi([21]\widehat\phi(R))
=[21]\phi\widehat\phi(R)
=[63]R.
\]

Thus

\[
\boxed{[63]E'(L)=0.}
\]

The extra factor `3` comes exactly from the dual-isogeny composition.

This bound is deliberately conservative.  Because

\[
E'[\widehat\phi](L)=0,
\]

the map `phihat:E'(L)->E(L)` is injective.  Therefore

\[
\widehat\phi([21]R)=0
\]

already implies

\[
[21]R=0.
\]

So, once dual-kernel triviality is part of the formal build, one may replace `63` by `21`.  The ECNF value `E'(L)_tors≅Z/7` is an even stronger external cross-check, but is unnecessary.

## 5.3 Why `[126]Pic^0(C)(L)=0`

Let

\[
\Phi:\operatorname{Pic}^0(C)(L)\longrightarrow E(L)\times E'(L)
\]

and

\[
\Psi:E(L)\times E'(L)\longrightarrow\operatorname{Pic}^0(C)(L)
\]

satisfy

\[
\Psi\Phi=[2].
\]

The exponent `63` kills both factors of the target, because it is a multiple of `21` and kills `E'`.  Therefore, for every divisor class `D`,

\[
\begin{aligned}
[126]D
&=[63]([2]D)\\
&=[63]\Psi\Phi(D)\\
&=\Psi([63]\Phi(D))\\
&=0.
\end{aligned}
\]

Hence

\[
\boxed{[126]\operatorname{Pic}^0(C)(L)=0.}
\]

The factor `2` is exactly the push--pull degree in `Psi o Phi=[2]`.

If the improved bound `[21]E'(L)=0` is used, then `[21]` kills the whole product and the Picard exponent improves to

\[
\boxed{[42]\operatorname{Pic}^0(C)(L)=0.}
\]

Thus `126` is consistent but nonminimal.

## 6.2 Build-critical seams to enforce

### Seam 1: label normalization

Use a comment or theorem recording

```text
old Cremona 162b1 = LMFDB 162.c3
```

so that rational and ECNF records are not mistaken for different curves.

### Seam 2: quotient-model identification

The Velu quotient `E'` and the pinned `Ehat0` must be connected by an explicit Weierstrass variable change.  A database label is not a Lean equality.

### Seam 3: Selmer orientation

Keep the following directions fixed:

```text
Sel^phi       bounds E'(L) / phi E(L),
Sel^phihat    bounds E(L)  / phihat E'(L).
```

Reversing them breaks the derivation of weak descent.

### Seam 4: two different cohomology targets

Because `E[phi]` is the constant group `Z/3` while `E'[phihat]` is of multiplicative type `mu_3`, the two Selmer groups do not inhabit the same concrete cubeclass space over `L`.

```text
Sel^phihat  -> L(S,3),
Sel^phi     -> anti-invariant cubeclasses over L(sqrt(-3)).
```

### Seam 5: `[2]` notation

In

```text
Sel^phihat = <[2]>,
```

`[2]` denotes the cubeclass of the field element `2`; it is not the doubling endomorphism.  Distinct Lean names are advisable.

### Seam 6: good model at `pi`

The rational equation has discriminant `-648`, which is not a unit at `3`.  The point count `7` belongs to the explicitly transformed good `L`-model with unit discriminant (for example discriminant `-8`).  Reducing the rational model directly would invalidate (D).

### Seam 7: zero branch in formal growth

The known order-`3` point lies in the formal kernel.  Every valuation theorem must either use a valuation with top or assume the doubled/tripled output is nonzero.

### Seam 8: no direct Selmer-to-rank shortcut

For the axiom-free build, do not stop at

```text
dim Sel^phi + dim Sel^phihat = 1.
```

An infinitely `3`-divisible subgroup is invisible modulo `3`.  The proof must explicitly pass through

```text
E(L)=H+3E(L)
```

and then the local separatedness/annihilator argument.

### Seam 9: database torsion is a check, not an input

The ECNF assertions `Z/21` and `Z/7` confirm the result, but a self-contained Lean development must prove only the pieces it consumes.  For rank zero it is enough to prove the uniform annihilator; exact torsion classification is optional.

# 7. Final verdict

There is **no mathematical inconsistency** among (A)--(F).

The apparently problematic pair

\[
E_0(L)_{\rm tors}\cong\mathbf Z/21,
\qquad
\#\widetilde E(\mathbf F_3)=7
\]

is exactly what good reduction in residue characteristic `3` permits:

\[
\text{order-3 torsion in the formal kernel,}
\qquad
\text{order-7 torsion in the special fibre.}
\]

The dual Selmer dimension is entirely the order-`3` torsion contribution, so it does not signal positive rank.  The annihilator factors are correct:

```text
21 = lcm(3,7),
63 = 3 * 21 from phi o phihat = [3],
126 = 2 * 63 from Psi o Phi = [2].
```

The only changes required are proof-engineering clarifications, not arithmetic repairs.  With the stronger already claimed fact `E'[phihat](L)=0`, the final bound can be sharpened to

```text
[21]E0(L)=0,
[21]Ehat0(L)=0,
[42]Pic^0(C)(L)=0,
```

but the existing `21/63/126` chain is fully valid.
