## Verdict

Mathematically: **yes**.  For the truncated `ρ₊` limit after the negative-part step gives `u ≥ 0`, the mass balance gives

```text
m'(t) = ∫ u(t,x) (a - b u(t,x)^α) dx,
```

because the Neumann diffusion and divergence chemotaxis flux integrate to zero.  If `0 ≤ u ≤ M` and `α ≥ 1`, then

```text
u(a - b u^α) ≥ - b M^α u,
```

so with `C := b M^α` one gets

```text
m'(t) ≥ -C m(t),
```

hence

```text
m(t) ≥ m(s) * exp (-C * (t - s)).
```

Together with positive initial mass, this gives `m(s) > 0` for every positive restart time.  This is unconditional once `u ≥ 0`, boundedness, and the weak/mild mass identity are available.
