## 2.3 No CM shortcut

There is no weight-two CM newform of level exactly 25. For a CM newform attached to an imaginary quadratic field K, its level is divisible by the absolute fundamental discriminant |D_K|. No imaginary quadratic fundamental discriminant divides 25. Therefore the level-25 factors above are non-CM.

Consequently the slogan

```plain text
25 = 5^2, therefore J_1(25) splits over Q(zeta_5)
into elliptic curves
```

is not valid. The cyclotomic/diamond action explains the 4+8 isotypic decomposition, but it does not by itself produce elliptic quotients. In particular, there is no verified counterpart here of

```plain text
J_1(18)_L ~ E_0 × Ehat_0
```

with two rank-zero elliptic curves.

There may be additional inner-twist structure after base change, but it is not needed for the shortest proof and should not be treated as an elliptic splitting certificate without explicit correspondences and local-factor checks.

The argument is not merely “the reductions have the same cardinality.” It uses injectivity:

1. Fix a rational cusp O and define the Abel–Jacobi embedding

1. Since the curve has gonality at least 4, the degree-one Abel–Jacobi map is injective. For ordinary rational points this also follows from the standard fact that [P-O]=0 implies a degree-one principal divisor and hence P=O.

1. The group J_1(25)(Q) has order 227555, prime to 3. At good reduction, reduction is injective on prime-to-3 torsion. Hence

1. The Abel–Jacobi square commutes with reduction, so

1. The target has ten elements and the ten rational cusps already map to ten distinct elements. Therefore every rational point is one of those cusps.

# 6. The genuine Lean bottleneck

For a classical proof one writes

```plain text
rank J_1(25)(Q) = 0
therefore J_1(25)(Q) is finite
therefore reduction at 3 is injective.
```

That silently uses Mordell–Weil finite generation. In the project’s MW-FG-free discipline, the accepted input must instead be one of:

```javascript
/-- Strongest and cleanest. -/
noncomputable def J25_equiv :
    J25Q ≃+ ZMod 227555

/-- Sufficient weaker endpoint. -/
theorem J25_annihilated :
    ∀ D : J25Q, 227555 • D = 0
```

plus enough information to show the relevant reduction map is injective.

There are three possible ways to prove this endpoint:

### Route A — formalize the fixed modular-symbol/Kato certificate

This follows the published rank computation and the Hecke/cuspidal-torsion calculation. The finite modular-symbol matrices are executable, but the implication from nonvanishing L-values/winding quotient to algebraic rank zero invokes deep modular-abelian-variety results. This route is short mathematically but very large as foundational Lean work.

### Route B — a new explicit descent on the level-25 modular factors

Use the 4+8 diamond-isotypic decomposition and prove weak descent plus local separatedness on its higher-dimensional factors. This remains axiom-free in the N18 sense, but it is not an elliptic Route-C port and is likely much larger than N18.

### Route C — a direct fixed formal-immersion theorem at the ten mod-3 cusps

One can try to avoid enumerating the full Jacobian by constructing a suitable winding/Eisenstein quotient and proving formal immersion at each cusp. This still requires a certified finite rational quotient and enough Hecke/q-expansion infrastructure. It is a plausible modular-curve-specific alternative, not an elementary ring/native_decide proof.

Thus the local point-count part is tiny; the global annihilator is the real formalization wall.

# Bottom line

X_1(25) is neither genus zero nor a genus-two N18 clone. Its rational-point exhaustion is actually easier than N18 once the Jacobian is known to be finite: ten mod-3 points equal ten rational cusps. But obtaining the required Jacobian annihilator in a strictly MW-FG-free Lean development is a new high-dimensional modular-arithmetic block. The shortest published route is the rank-zero/cuspidal-torsion plus mod-3 local method, not a cyclotomic elliptic split.
