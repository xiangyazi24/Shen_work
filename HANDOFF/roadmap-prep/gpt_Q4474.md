# 6. Minimal regularity: the exact honest requirement

For the nonminimal entropy identity, the safe positive-time assumptions are:

- u is spatially C² on the closed interval and satisfies Neumann conditions;

- v is spatially C² on the closed interval and satisfies Neumann conditions;

- u is time differentiable, with u_t locally jointly continuous or at least dominated by one spatially integrable majorant on a time neighborhood;

- u has a positive lower bound on that compact positive-time slab.

The last two qualifications matter.

Bare statements “for each fixed x, t ↦ u(t,x) is C¹” and “for each fixed t, x ↦u(t,x) is C²” do not by themselves supply the uniform integrable dominator required by differentiation under the integral. The repository's intervalIntegral_hasDerivAt_time_of_local explicitly requires that dominator. Joint continuity of u_t on a compact slab is a sufficient producer.

Likewise, the entropy includes u^{-2m} and u^{-(2m-2)}. Pointwise positivity only on the open spatial interior is not enough to derive a bounded integrand near an endpoint. On [0,1], continuity plus strict positivity up to both Neumann boundary points gives a positive slab floor. This is a genuine interface requirement, although it is standard for the positive classical Paper 3 trajectories.

For the special signal energy, one additionally needs the differentiated elliptic relation, equivalently enough regularity to justify v_t and (v_t)_x. This can be obtained from elliptic-resolver linearity applied to u_t, but it should be a named lemma rather than silently inferred from pointwise C¹_t.
