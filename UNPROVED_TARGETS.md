# Non-real proof inventory

This file lists theorem-shaped objects that are not yet real Lean derivations of
the paper mathematics.  It is stricter than `0 sorry`: projection from an
assumption package is a valid Lean theorem, but the analytic proof still lives
in the package field.

Current mechanical scan:

- Numbered theorem-like paper targets in `Paper1/Statements.lean`,
  `Paper2/Statements.lean`, and `Paper3/Statements.lean`: 80.
- Targets/branches with same-name `_proved` theorem/lemma: 15.
- Still externalized without same-name `_proved`: many targets are supplied by
  `SemigroupEstimateData`, `StabilityNorms`, `CompactnessData`,
  `Paper3Constants`, or prior theorem assumptions.  Their theorem-shaped
  package projection wrappers have been removed from Lean sources; the
  remaining externalized work is visible as fields or open statement targets.
- Refuted targets: original Paper1 `Lemma_4_1`, `Lemma_4_2`,
  `Remark_4_2`, and `Remark_4_2_M_one` are not supplied; `not_Lemma_4_1`,
  `not_Lemma_4_2`, `not_Remark_4_2`, and `not_Remark_4_2_M_one` formally
  prove their negations.
- Direct holes/fake proof tokens in Lean: no `sorry`, `admit`, `axiom`,
  theorem-body `True`, or `PLACEHOLDER` currently found by grep.

Important: an inhabited statement target is not necessarily a real proof.  Many
inhabitants are projections from named assumption packages, not proofs of the
underlying analytic estimates.
