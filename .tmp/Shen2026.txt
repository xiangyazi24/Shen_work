                                                  This paper is devoted to the study of existence, uniqueness, stability, and monotonicity of
                                               traveling wave solutions to the following parabolic-elliptic chemotaxis system with logistic type
                                               source                  (
                                                                         ut = uxx − χ(um vx )x + u(1 − uα ),     x∈R

                                               biological species inﬂuenced by a chemical substance produced by the species itself. In this
                                               context, the function u denotes the population density of the biological species, and v denotes
                                               the concentration of the chemical agent. Traveling wave solutions of (CM) connecting the two
                                               constant solutions (1, 1) and (0, 0) are among important types of solutions, which characterize
                                               the front propagation phenomena in (CM). The existence of such traveling wave solutions to
                                               (CM) with m = α = γ = 1 has been studied in several papers. However, there is little study

1     Introduction
1.1   Overview
The current paper is concerned with the existence, stability, and monotonicity of traveling wave
solutions to the following parabolic-elliptic chemotaxis system with logistic type source,
                           (
                            ut = uxx − χ(um vx )x + u(1 − uα ),    x∈R
                                             γ
                                                                                            (1.1)
                            0 = vxx − v + u ,                      x ∈ R,

where m, α, γ ≥ 1 and χ ∈ R. System (1.1) can be used to describe the evolution of a biological
species inﬂuenced by a chemical substance which is produced by the species itself and diﬀuses very
fast. In this context, the function u denotes the population density of the biological species, and
v denotes the concentration of the chemical agent. The constant χ is referred to as chemotaxis
sensitivity coeﬃcient. The chemotaxis sensitivity coeﬃcient χ > 0 indicates that the signal is a
chemoattractant, in this case, (1.1) is called an attraction chemotaxis system, while χ < 0 indicates
that the signal is a chemorepellent, in this case, (1.1) is called a repulsion chemotaxis system.
    Considering chemotaxis models, it is interesting to study how the chemical signal aﬀects the
evolution of the biological species, say, whether the chemical signal induces ﬁnite time blow-up,
whether it creates certain pattern formation, etc. Numerous works have been carried out toward
these central problems for various chemotaxis models on both bounded and unbounded domains.
Chemotaxis models on unbounded domains can describe the inﬂuence of chemical signals from
more angles, say, from the angle of spatial spreading, which is strongly related to traveling wave
solutions connecting two diﬀerent states.
    It is clear that (1.1) has two constant states (1, 1) and (0, 0). Positive traveling wave solu-
tions of (1.1) connecting these two states with speed c are solutions (u(t, x), v(t, x)) of the form
(u(t, x), v(t, x)) = (φ(x − ct), ψ(x − ct)) for some one variable positive functions φ(·), ψ(·) satisfying
limx→−∞ (φ(x), ψ(x)) = (1, 1) and limx→∞ (φ(x), ψ(x)) = (0, 0). Such solutions describe how fast
the biological species spreads from higher density regions to lower density regions. Throughout this
paper, we only consider positive traveling wave solutions, which we may just call traveling wave
solutions.
    When χ = 0, the ﬁrst equation in (1.1) is decoupled from the second one and becomes

                                    ut = uxx + u(1 − uα ),        x ∈ R.                            (1.2)

Equation (1.2) is referred to as a Fisher-KPP type equation in literature due to the pioneering
work [14], [28] for the case α = 1. It is well known that (1.2) has positive traveling wave solutions
u(t, x) = φ(x − ct) connecting 1 and 0 (i.e., φ(−∞) = 1, φ(∞) = 0) of all speeds c ≥ c∗0 := 2 and has
no such traveling wave solutions of slower speed. The traveling wave solutions of (1.2) connecting
1 and 0 are also monotone and stable in proper weighted spaces. Moreover, for any nonnegative
solution u(t, x) of (1.2), if the support supp(u(0, ·)) of u(0, x) is nonempty and compact, then

                                   lim inf      u(t, x) > 0 ∀ 0 < c′′ < c∗0 ,                       (1.3)
                                |x|≤c′′ t,t→∞

and
                                     lim sup u(t, x) = 0        ∀ c′ > c∗0 .                        (1.4)
                                   |x|≥c′t,t→∞

Hence, in literature, the minimal wave speed c∗0 = 2 is also called the spreading speed for (1.2).
Since the pioneering works by [14] and [28], a huge amount of research has been carried out toward

the front propagation dynamics of reaction diﬀusion equations of the form,

                                     ut = ∆u + uf (t, x, u),    x ∈ RN ,                                 (1.5)

where f (t, x, u) < 0 for u ≫ 1, ∂u f (t, x, u) < 0 for u ≥ 0 (see [4, 7–9, 15, 16, 30, 31, 34, 36, 37, 46, 47,
51–53], etc.).
    Consider (1.1) with χ 6= 0. It is important to study whether (1.1) has traveling wave solutions
connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0), and if so, whether the traveling wave solutions are monotone and stable
in proper weighted spaces; whether (1.1) possesses a spreading speed; whether the chemotaxis
speeds up or slows down the wave speeds or the spatial spreading of the biological species, etc.
There are several studies on the existence of traveling wave solutions and spreading speeds of (1.1)
with m = α = γ = 1, see, for example, [6, 21, 22, 24, 25, 35, 38–43, 45].
    Suppose that m = α = γ = 1. Among those, the following have been proved/observed in
literature:

   • (Spatial spreading speeds). The spreading property (1.3) holds for nonnegative solutions
     (u(t, x), v(t, x)) of (1.1) with supp(u(0, ·)) being nonempty (see [22, Theorem 1.1], [45, The-
     orem 1.1]). This indicates that chemotaxis does not slow down the spatial spreading of
     the biological species. When 0 < χ ≤ 1, the spreading property (1.4) holds for nonnega-
     tive solutions (u(t, x), v(t, x)) of (1.1) with supp(u(0, ·)) being nonempty and compact (see
     [45, Theorem 1.1]). This indicates that when 0 < χ ≤ 1, chemotaxis neither slows down nor
     speeds up the spatial spreading of the biological species. In general, (1.4) holds with c∗0 being
     replaced by some c∗+ ≥ 2 (see [22, Theorem 1.1], [40, Theorem B], [43, Theorem C]).

   • (Lower bound of wave speeds or minimal wave speed). c∗0 = 2 is a lower bound of the wave
     speeds in the sense that if (u(t, x), v(t, x)) = (φ(x − ct), ψ(x − ct)) is a traveling wave solution
     of (1.1) connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) with speed c, then c ≥ 2 (it follows from [22, Theorem
     1.2], [24, Proposition 1.3], [25, Theorem 1.2], [43, Theorem C]). This indicates that no matter
     the chemotaxis sensitivity is positive or negative, it does not slow down the wave speeds.
     Moreover, if |χ| is relatively small, then c∗0 = 2 is the minimal wave speed of traveling wave
     solutions, or, there are traveling wave solutions of (1.1) connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) with
     speed c = 2 (it follows from [24, Theorem 1.4], [45, Theorem 1.4]). This indicates that the
     chemotaxis sensitivity does not speed up the spatial spreading of the biological species when
     |χ| is small.

   • (Negative chemotaxis sensitivity and speed-up). For any χ < 0, there is c∗χ ≥ 2 such that for
     any c > c∗χ , (1.1) has a traveling wave solution connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) with speed c (it
     follows from [43, Theorem A]). When χ ≪ −1, any speed c of a traveling wave solution of
     (1.1) connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) is very large and hence strong negative sensitivity speeds up
     the wave speeds (see [21, Theorem 2.1]).

   • (Positive chemotaxis sensitivity and oscillatory traveling waves). When 0 < χ < 1/2, for any
     c ≥ 2, (1.1) admits a traveling wave solution connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) with speed c (see
     [45, Theorem 1.4]). For general χ > 0, there exist a c ≥ 2 and a traveling wave solution
     (u(t, x), v(t, x)) = (φ(x − ct), ψ(x − ct)) with lim inf x→∞ φ(x) > 0 and limx→∞ φ(x) = 0 (it
     follows from [25, Theorem 1.2]). Note that this traveling wave solution may not converge to
     (1, 1) and may be oscillatory as x → −∞ (see the numerical simulations in [25]). Moreover,
     It is observed numerically that the minimal speed (1.1) appears to always be 2 for χ > 0 (see
     [25]).

    The above results from literature provide deep insights into the understanding of front propa-
gation phenomena in (1.1). Though there exist many studies on traveling wave solutions of (1.1),
there is still little study on numerous interesting problems about traveling wave solutions of (1.1),
for example, there is little study about the stability and monotonicity of traveling wave solutions
of (1.1) in literature, even when m = α = γ = 1; there is no study on the existence of traveling
wave solutions of (1.1) for general m, α, γ ≥ 1.
    The objective of the current paper is to study the existence, uniqueness, stability, and mono-
tonicity of traveling wave solutions (1.1) connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) for general m, α, γ ≥ 1, includ-
ing m = α = γ = 1 as a special case. To this end, we ﬁrst study the Cauchy problem for general
m, α, γ and the stability of the positive constant solution (1, 1) since they have not been studied
for general m, α, γ ≥ 1 and play important roles in the study of traveling waves. Before we state
our main results in next subsection, let us highlight some of the contributions of this paper in the
following:

   • (Global well-posedness of the Cauchy problem) This paper establishes explicit parameter re-
     gions for the global existence and boundedness of positive classical solutions (see Proposition
     1.1), which provides impacts of the parameters on the global existence and extends [23, The-
     orem 1.4] for the case m = α = γ = 1 to the general case m, α, γ ≥ 1.

   • (Stability of the positive constant solution) This paper establishes explicit parameter regions
     for the global stability of the positive constant solution (1, 1) with respect to strictly positive
     perturbations (see Proposition 1.2), which provides impacts of the parameters on the stability
     of the positive constant solution and extends [42, Theorem B] for the case m = α = γ = 1 to
     the general case m, α, γ ≥ 1.

   • (Existence of monotone traveling wave solutions with negative sensitivity) This paper ﬁnds an
     explicit lower bound c∗χ,m,γ for the wave speeds and shows that for any χ ≤ 0 and c > c∗χ,m,γ ,
     (1.1) with α ≤ m + γ − 1 has a monotone traveling wave solution connecting (1, 1) and
     (0, 0) with speed c and explicit decay rate (see Theorem 1.1(1)), which not only extends the
     existence result in [43, Theorem A] for the case m = α = γ = 1 to the general case m, α, γ ≥ 1,
     but also shows the monotonicity and explicit decay rate of traveling wave solutions for the ﬁrst
     time. Moreover, the lower bound c∗χ,m,γ is close to be optimal in certain sense (see Remark
     1.2(2)).

   • (Existence of traveling wave solutions with positivity sensitivity) This paper proves the exis-
     tence of traveling wave solutions of (1.1) connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) with explicit exponentially
                                                                   2γ+2
     decay rate for any speed c > 2 when 0 ≤ χ < min{ 12 , 2γ+m+1        } and α = m + γ − 1, which
     extends the existence results in [45, Theorem 1.2] for the case m = α = γ = 1 to the more
     general case α = m + γ − 1 (see Theorem 1.1(2)) and also recovers the existing results for the
     Fisher-KPP equation.

   • (Uniqueness and stability of traveling wave solutions) This paper ﬁnds a positive c∗∗  χ,m,α,γ ,
     which depends on χ, m, α, γ explicitly and goes to 2 as χ → 0, and proves that for any
     c > c∗χ,m,α,γ , traveling wave solutions of (1.1) connecting (1, 1) and (0, 0) with speed c (if
     exist) are stable in some weighted spaces (see Theorem 1.2), which is new even for the case
     that m = α = γ = 1. Uniqueness of traveling wave solutions of (1.1) connecting (1, 1) and
     (0, 0) with speed c > c∗∗χ,m,α,γ is also obtained in this paper.

   • (New techniques/ideas and extension of existing methods) This paper develops several tech-
     niques and extends some existing methods for the study of Cauchy problem and existence,
