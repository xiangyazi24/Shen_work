# Q3509: R2 frontier selection audit

Audit target: `main` at `595354e31017e3dcaa64a2e4daa5b5a7a090dad2`.

## Decision

The smallest non-circular analytic frontier to audit/attempt next is the **physical zero-window / spatial-Young data frontier**, specifically around

`H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareSpatialYoungDataBefore p u v T`

and its existing lowering lemmas to zero-window scalar majorants.

This is the best next target because it is local, does not require the H1 sup-bound conclusion, and already has nearby APIs that do the hard fixed-time Cauchy/Young reduction. In `IntervalChiNegH1PhysicalYoungSpatial.lean`, the three fixed-time estimates

* `H1PhysicalTaxisX_norm_le_half_lapL2sq_add_half_taxisPartSq_of_spatial`,
* `H1PhysicalUvxxX_norm_le_half_lapL2sq_add_half_uvxxPartSq_of_spatial`,
* `H1PhysicalReactX_norm_le_half_lapL2sq_add_half_reactPartSq_of_spatial`

are already proved. The file also has

* `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareZeroDataBefore_of_spatialYoungData`, and
* `H1PhysicalRHSYoungScalarZeroMajorantsBefore_of_spatialYoungData`,

so a source-side proof of the spatial-square data immediately lands a usable zero-window majorant for the strict/initial route.

The key point is that this frontier only asks for local zero-window space/time square-integrability and measurability. It should not use bounded-before, downstream RHS integrability, derivative-integrability producers, or the final strict/initial route. That makes it genuinely non-circular.

## Ranking of the candidate frontiers

1. **SpatialYoungData / lowered zero-window data — attempt first.**  This is the smallest analytic frontier with local APIs. It can be attacked through fixed-time spatial square estimates and local zero-window integrability. It does not require proving the exact H1 identity or the global sqrt bounds.

2. **Physical identity — audit after zero-window data.**  The wrappers exist, especially `H1PhysicalRHSIdentityBefore_of_parametric_and_substitution`, but the exact physical scalar identity still requires real analytic substitution into the exact three concrete scalars `H1PhysicalTaxisX`, `H1PhysicalUvxxX`, and `H1PhysicalReactX`. The Route-C theorem `H1EnergyIdentity_of_classicalSolution_and_uxxL1Cont` only produces existential RHS witnesses, not these exact scalars, so it is not enough by itself.

3. **Endpoint scalar regularity / boundedBefore endpoint data — not first.**  The bridge file has many endpoint upgraders, for example `H1IdentityRHSComponentsContinuousBefore_of_lapEndpoint`, `..._of_zeroSlabRepresentativeBefore`, and the strict-plus-zero variants. But these require actual zero-time lap/H1 continuity data. Strict-positive-time continuity cannot simply be promoted to closed zero-start windows.

4. **L2 seed frontier — useful but not the next H1 physical frontier.**  `IntervalDomainL2SeedFrontierProducer.lean` has a clean producer, but it still requires `IntervalDomainL2SeedZeroRightDerivative u`. The file explicitly notes that `IsPaper2ClassicalSolution` gives differentiability only on `(0,T)`, while endpoint continuity is not the same as a right derivative at zero.

5. **Sqrt bounds — largest/riskier; do not attempt first.**  `H1PhysicalRHSSqrtBoundsBefore` wraps `H1SqrtTermBoundsBefore`, whose constants `V₁ V₂ M L` must bound the concrete physical scalars by canonical H1 lap/grad square-root quantities. This is the most likely place to become circular, because natural sources for those constants are exactly the boundedness/sup estimates the route is trying to prove.

## Recommended next prover target

The next prover target should be a **source-side square-data theorem** in `IntervalChiNegH1PhysicalYoungSpatial.lean`: remove the explicit product-integrability fields from `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareSpatialYoungDataBefore` by deriving them from the square-integrability fields via the elementary estimate `|f*g| ≤ (f^2 + g^2)/2`, then use the already-proved fixed-time Young reducers.

That is small, analytic, local, and non-circular. A good exact statement to ask for is:

## What would be circular or invalid

### Identity frontier

Circular/invalid uses:

* Do not use `H1PhysicalRHSRouteBefore`, `H1SupBoundSqrtRHSIntegrableBefore`, `H1IdentityRHSIntegrableBefore_of_physicalRHSRoute`, or `IsPaper2BoundedBefore` to prove the identity. Those are downstream of the route.
* Do not use the existential identity from `H1EnergyIdentity_of_classicalSolution_and_uxxL1Cont` as if it were `H1PhysicalRHSIdentityBefore`. That theorem gives some `taxisX uvxx reactX`; it does not identify them with `H1PhysicalTaxisX`, `H1PhysicalUvxxX`, and `H1PhysicalReactX`.
* Do not skip the exact PDE/substitution step. The valid route must prove the `H1IdentityRHSValue` equality for the concrete physical scalar triple.

### Sqrt-bounds frontier

Circular/invalid uses:

* Do not obtain `V₁ V₂ M L` from `IsPaper2BoundedBefore` or from the H1 boundedness result being proved.
* Do not use `H1PhysicalRHSSqrtBoundsBefore` itself, or any package containing it, to establish the component inequalities.
* Do not multiply inequalities by `(-p.χ₀)` without the sign hypothesis `0 ≤ -p.χ₀`; this is an explicit field of `H1SqrtTermBoundsBefore`.
* Do not treat compactness bounds such as `resolverGradReal_bounded` as uniform-in-time quantitative bounds unless a non-circular uniform source bound is supplied.

### SpatialYoungData / zero-window frontier

Circular/invalid uses:

* Do not use `H1PhysicalRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore`, `H1IdentityRHSInitialWindowIntegrableBefore`, `H1IdentityRHSIntegrableBefore`, `H1EnergyDerivativeInitialWindowIntegrableBefore`, or the strict/initial physical route to prove the zero-window majorant. Those are downstream consumers.
* Do not use `IsPaper2BoundedBefore` or any H1 sup-bound theorem to get the local square data.
* Do not silently assume zero-time closed-slab continuity of `liftDeriv2 u`; that is endpoint data unless separately proved. For this frontier, local integrability on `(0,δ]` and a.e. spatial square-integrability are enough.
* Do not replace the concrete physical pieces by abstract `taxisX uvxx reactX`; the zero-window data must be for `H1PhysicalTaxisX`, `H1PhysicalUvxxX`, and `H1PhysicalReactX`.

### Endpoint-data frontier

Circular/invalid uses:

* Do not promote strict-positive-time continuity to `[0,b]` continuity without a real zero-endpoint input such as `H1LapComponentEndpointContinuousBefore`, `H1LapComponentZeroRightContinuous`, or `H1LiftDeriv2ZeroSlabRepresentativeBefore`.
* Do not derive endpoint H1 or lap continuity from bounded-before if the goal is to prove bounded-before.
* Do not conflate continuity of `H1energy` at zero with continuity/integrability of `lapL2sq` at zero; they are different endpoint facts in the current APIs.

### L2 seed frontier

Circular/invalid uses:

* Do not use `IntervalDomainL2SeedRegularityFrontier T u` to prove itself.
* Do not route through `intervalDomain_boundedBefore_of_H1supBound...`; those bounded-before wrappers already require the L2 seed frontier.
* Do not claim `IsPaper2ClassicalSolution` gives the derivative at `t = 0`. The producer explicitly needs `IntervalDomainL2SeedZeroRightDerivative u` because the solution differentiability is only on `(0,T)`.
* Endpoint power-energy continuity is not enough for `energyHasDerivWithin` at zero; the zero-right derivative remains separate.

## If the goal is an unconditional headline theorem

None of the audited candidates, by itself, appears to make the H1 physical bounded-before theorem unconditional. The spatial-Young/zero-window frontier is feasible and worthwhile, but it still leaves the exact physical identity, sqrt bounds, endpoint scalar regularity, and L2 seed/right-derivative data.

If the team does not want to add or prove the local square-data assumptions above, the next audit question should be:

**Which existing existence/regularity APIs can supply, non-circularly and on a zero window `(0,δ]`, the square-integrability of `liftDeriv2 u`, `H1PhysicalChemTaxisPart`, `H1PhysicalChemUvxxPart`, and `H1PhysicalLogisticReactionPart`, plus measurability of the assembled physical H1 RHS?**

That audit is narrower and safer than starting with the global sqrt bounds.
