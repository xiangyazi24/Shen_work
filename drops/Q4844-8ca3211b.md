# 1. The corrected Seifert lift and an explicit target pair

Put

\[
c=\cos\frac\pi{17},\qquad
d=\sqrt{c^2-\frac34}.
\tag{1.1}
\]

A concrete real uniformizing lift is

\[
\boxed{
X_0=\begin{pmatrix}0&-1\\1&0\end{pmatrix},\qquad
Y_0=\begin{pmatrix}
\frac12&d-c\\[1mm]
d+c&\frac12
\end{pmatrix}.}
\tag{1.2}
\]

Indeed,

\[
\det X_0=\det Y_0=1,
\qquad
\operatorname{tr}X_0=0,
\qquad
\operatorname{tr}Y_0=1,
\tag{1.3}
\]

and

\[
X_0^2=-I.
\tag{1.4}
\]

The characteristic polynomial of \(Y_0\) is

\[
t^2-t+1,
\]

so

\[
Y_0^3=-I.
\tag{1.5}
\]

Moreover,

\[
X_0Y_0=
\begin{pmatrix}
-(d+c)&-\frac12\\[1mm]
\frac12&d-c
\end{pmatrix},
\]

and hence

\[
\operatorname{tr}(X_0Y_0)=-2c.
\tag{1.6}
\]

Its eigenvalues are \(-e^{\pm i\pi/17}\), so

\[
(X_0Y_0)^{17}=I.
\tag{1.7}
\]

Define

\[
Z_0=-(X_0Y_0)^{-1}.
\tag{1.8}
\]

Then

\[
Z_0^{17}=-I,
\qquad
X_0Y_0Z_0=-I.
\tag{1.9}
\]

This is the exact target to which the beta word matrices must be compared. No explicit conjugating matrix is required: an irreducible pair in \(\mathrm{SL}_2\) is determined up to simultaneous conjugacy by

\[
\bigl(\operatorname{tr}X,\operatorname{tr}Y,\operatorname{tr}(XY)\bigr).
\]

The pair is non-elementary, since the Fricke identity gives

\[
\operatorname{tr}[X_0,Y_0]
=(\operatorname{tr}X_0)^2+(\operatorname{tr}Y_0)^2
 +(\operatorname{tr}X_0Y_0)^2
 -\operatorname{tr}X_0\operatorname{tr}Y_0\operatorname{tr}X_0Y_0-2
=4c^2-1>2.
\tag{1.10}
\]

# 2. The exact reduced Wirtinger representation at beta

The five-twist knot \(7_2\) can be represented in Ham--Lee's mirror convention by

\[
\boxed{
\pi_1(S^3\setminus7_2)
=\left\langle s,t\ \middle|\ s w t^{-1}w^{-1}=1\right\rangle,
\qquad
w=(ts^{-1}t^{-1}s)^{-3}.}
\tag{2.1}
\]

Both \(s\) and \(t\) are Wirtinger meridians. A nonabelian representation is put in the Riley normal form (0.7).

Ham and Lee use the complex-distance parameter

\[
V_{\rm dist}
=\frac{(M^2+1)(LM^2-1)}{(M^2-1)(LM^2+1)},
\tag{2.2}
\]

and

\[
\xi=\frac{(M-M^{-1})^2}{2}\left(V_{\rm dist}-1\right).
\tag{2.3}
\]

At beta, \(M=L=s\). Then

\[
V_{\rm dist}-1
=\frac{2s^2}{(s+1)^2(s^2-s+1)},
\]

and substitution into (2.3) gives

\[
\xi
=\frac{(s^2-1)^2}{(s+1)^2(s^2-s+1)}
=\frac{(1-s)^2}{s^2-s+1},
\]

which proves (0.8).

Using only the rounded value \(s=0.406813\) as an orientation check gives

\[
S_\beta\approx
\begin{pmatrix}
0.406813&1\\
0&2.458132
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
T_\beta\approx
\begin{pmatrix}
0.406813&0\\
0.463791&2.458132
\end{pmatrix}.
\tag{2.4}
\]

The proof uses the exact algebraic root, not these rounded numbers.

# 3. The peripheral matrices and the correct surgery relation

Choose \(\mu=s\), so \(\rho(\mu)=S\). The preferred longitude commutes with the meridian. In the chosen upper-triangular gauge, write

\[
\rho(\lambda)=
\begin{pmatrix}L&q\\0&L^{-1}\end{pmatrix}.
\]

The commutation identity \(S\rho(\lambda)=\rho(\lambda)S\) gives

\[
Mq+L^{-1}=L+qM^{-1},
\]

and therefore

\[
q=\frac{L-L^{-1}}{M-M^{-1}}.
\]

This proves (0.9).

At the beta point \(L=M=s_\beta\),

\[
q=1,
\]

so

\[
\rho(\lambda)=
\begin{pmatrix}s_\beta&1\\0&s_\beta^{-1}\end{pmatrix}
=\rho(\mu).
\tag{3.1}
\]

Thus the representation extends across the \(-1\) filling by killing

\[
\boxed{\mu\lambda^{-1}}
\tag{3.2}
\]

(or its inverse, depending on the orientation convention). It does not kill \(\mu\).

In particular,

\[
\rho(\mu)=S_\beta\ne\pm I.
\tag{3.3}
\]

A notation such as ``\((-1,0)\) filling'' would kill a meridian, not perform slope \(-1\) surgery. The correct primitive slope pair is \((-1,1)\) or \((1,-1)\), according to which ordered peripheral basis is used.

# 4. An independent longitude-word convention

For an independent word-level check, Mathews uses the twist-knot presentation

\[
\pi_1(S^3\setminus J(2,6))
=\langle a,b\mid a\omega^3=\omega^3b\rangle,
\qquad
\omega=ab^{-1}a^{-1}b,
\tag{4.1}
\]

where \(J(2,6)=7_2\) in that convention. Put

\[
\bar\omega=ba^{-1}b^{-1}a.
\]

A preferred longitude is

\[
\boxed{\lambda=\omega^3\bar\omega^3.}
\tag{4.2}
\]

Mathews' normal form is

\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}M&1\\0&M^{-1}\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
B=\begin{pmatrix}M&0\\Z&M^{-1}\end{pmatrix},
\tag{4.3}
\]

with

\[
Z=\frac{(M-M^{-1})(1-L)}{M+LM^{-1}}.
\tag{4.4}
\]

At \(M=L=s\),

\[
\boxed{Z=-\frac{(1-s)^2}{s}.}
\tag{4.5}
\]

Equations (4.1)--(4.5) give a second exact way to compute the meridian and longitude matrices. The Ham--Lee and Mathews lower-left parameters are not equal because the two papers use different bridge generators and conjugacy normalizations. One should not combine the word \(\omega^3\bar\omega^3\) from (4.2) with the matrix \(T\) from (0.7) without first writing the change of generators.

# 5. Why there is no canonical list of seven Wirtinger matrices

A seven-generator Wirtinger presentation depends on:

- the chosen planar diagram or PD code;
- the orientation;
- a starting arc;
- the numbering of the seven arcs;
- the sign convention at each crossing.

Therefore there is no diagram-independent list called

\[
\rho(g_1),\ldots,\rho(g_7).
\]

What is canonical enough for the calculation is the reduced two-meridian presentation (2.1). Given any explicit seven-arc presentation, eliminate five generators or reconstruct them crossing by crossing. If a crossing relation is

\[
g_{\rm out}=g_{\rm over}^{\varepsilon}
 g_{\rm in}g_{\rm over}^{-\varepsilon},
\qquad \varepsilon\in\{\pm1\},
\tag{5.1}
\]

then

\[
\rho(g_{\rm out})=ho(g_{\rm over})^{\varepsilon}
 \rho(g_{\rm in})\rho(g_{\rm over})^{-\varepsilon}.
\tag{5.2}
\]

Starting with the two bridge matrices \(S_\beta,T_\beta\), formula (5.2) produces every arc matrix exactly.

The four tetrahedral shapes also do not, by themselves, select a unique matrix basis. To obtain face-pairing matrices one must specify the ordered vertices, face permutations, a developed base tetrahedron, and paths from the base cell. All resulting matrices are simultaneously conjugate. For a trace certificate, the Riley matrices avoid this unnecessary marking ambiguity.

# 6. The exact missing word certificate

The remaining task is to find reduced words \(w_x,w_y\) in

\[
\{s^{\pm1},t^{\pm1}\}
\]

such that

\[
X=\rho(w_x),\qquad Y=\rho(w_y)
\]

obey

\[
\boxed{
X^2=-I,\qquad Y^3=-I,\qquad (XY)^{17}=I,}
\tag{6.1}
\]

and, at the selected real embedding,

\[
\boxed{
\operatorname{tr}X=0,\qquad
\operatorname{tr}Y=1,\qquad
\operatorname{tr}(XY)<0.}
\tag{6.2}
\]

The last trace is then checked against \(-2\cos(\pi/17)\). Set

\[
Z=-(XY)^{-1}.
\]

The full central-lift certificate is

\[
X^2=-I,\qquad Y^3=-I,\qquad Z^{17}=-I,
\qquad XYZ=-I.
\tag{6.3}
\]

Because \(17\) is prime, \((XY)^{17}=I\) and \(XY\ne I\) already show that the projective order is \(17\).

Once (6.1)--(6.3) are verified exactly, the trace triple agrees with (1.2). The Fricke--Vogt theorem then gives simultaneous conjugacy to the uniformizing pair, and (1.10) verifies non-elementarity. This completes the beta maximal-Fuchsian certificate.

## 6.1 Exact Sage search

The following code performs the calculation in the exact beta number field. It intentionally prints words only after exact matrix identities have passed.

```python
# SageMath
R.<u> = PolynomialRing(QQ)

p_beta = (
    u^16 - 7*u^15 + 22*u^14 - 48*u^13 + 87*u^12 - 133*u^11
    + 178*u^10 - 211*u^9 + 223*u^8 - 211*u^7 + 178*u^6
    - 133*u^5 + 87*u^4 - 48*u^3 + 22*u^2 - 7*u + 1
)

# If p_beta factors in the installed Sage version, select the factor possessing
# a real embedding near 0.4068 before constructing K.
assert p_beta.is_irreducible()
K.<s> = NumberField(p_beta)

xi = (1-s)^2 / (s^2-s+1)
S = matrix(K, [[s, 1], [0, s^-1]])
T = matrix(K, [[s, 0], [xi, s^-1]])
I2 = identity_matrix(K, 2)

assert S.det() == 1 and T.det() == 1

# Exact reduced Wirtinger relation.
C = T*S^-1*T^-1*S
W = C^-3
assert S*W*T^-1*W^-1 == I2

# Exact beta peripheral relation: lambda has the same matrix as mu.
Mu = S
Lam = matrix(K, [[s, 1], [0, s^-1]])
assert Mu*Lam^-1 == I2

alphabet = ('s', 'S', 't', 'T')
inv_letter = {'s':'S', 'S':'s', 't':'T', 'T':'t'}
gen = {'s':S, 'S':S^-1, 't':T, 'T':T^-1}


def word_matrix(word):
    A = I2
    for a in word:
        A *= gen[a]
    return A


def reduced_words(max_length):
    yield ''
    frontier = ['']
    for _ in range(max_length):
        new = []
        for w in frontier:
            for a in alphabet:
                if w and inv_letter[w[-1]] == a:
                    continue
                v = w + a
                yield v
                new.append(v)
        frontier = new


# Select the real embedding corresponding to s_beta.
RF = RealField(200)
real_embeddings = K.embeddings(RF)
phi = min(real_embeddings, key=lambda e: abs(e(s) - RF('0.4068')))


def real_trace(A):
    return phi(A.trace())


MAXLEN = 16
X_candidates = []
Y_candidates = []

for word in reduced_words(MAXLEN):
    if not word:
        continue
    A = word_matrix(word)
    # Cayley-Hamilton makes trace 0 equivalent to A^2=-I for det(A)=1,
    # and trace 1 equivalent to A^3=-I.
    if A.trace() == 0:
        assert A^2 == -I2
        X_candidates.append((word, A))
    if A.trace() == 1:
        assert A^3 == -I2
        Y_candidates.append((word, A))

print('X candidates:', len(X_candidates))
print('Y candidates:', len(Y_candidates))

for wx, X in X_candidates:
    for wy, Y in Y_candidates:
        Q = X*Y
        if Q == I2 or Q^17 != I2:
            continue
        if real_trace(Q) >= 0:
            continue

        Z = -Q^-1
        assert X^2 == -I2
        assert Y^3 == -I2
        assert Z^17 == -I2
        assert X*Y*Z == -I2

        print('CERTIFICATE FOUND')
        print('w_x =', wx)
        print('w_y =', wy)
        print('tr X =', X.trace())
        print('tr Y =', Y.trace())
        print('tr XY (selected embedding) =', real_trace(Q))
        print('X =')
        print(X)
        print('Y =')
        print(Y)
        raise SystemExit

print('No certificate through length', MAXLEN)
```

If no pair appears by length \(16\), that is not a no-go theorem. Use a meet-in-the-middle enumeration, or obtain the Tietze map directly from a Kirby-calculus presentation. The exact tests (6.1)--(6.3) remain the same.

## 6.2 A useful false lead to avoid

For the target triangle pair,

\[
\operatorname{tr}[X_0,Y_0]=4\cos^2\frac\pi{17}-1
=1+2\cos\frac{2\pi}{17}.
\tag{6.4}
\]

The beta meridian trace is

\[
\operatorname{tr}S_\beta=s_\beta+s_\beta^{-1}.
\tag{6.5}
\]

These numbers are numerically close, so the commutator is a natural word-search seed. It should **not** be declared equal without an exact reduction in \(\mathbf Q(s_\beta)\). The actual Tietze word must be certified by matrix equality, not selected by decimal trace matching.

# 7. What the completed proof should contain

A rigorous beta trace certificate is a short finite data package:

1. the polynomial \(p_\beta\) and the selected real embedding;
2. the two matrices \(S_\beta,T_\beta\) from (0.7)--(0.8);
3. an exact verification of the knot relation (2.6);

Items 1--4 are explicit above. Item 5 is the single missing marking computation. Once its output is recorded, items 6--8 are immediate exact matrix checks.
