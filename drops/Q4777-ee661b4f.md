## Scope and bottom line

This audit covers the chain beginning with the mean-value difference equation in equations (5.18)–(5.19), the definitions and estimates of `J₁`–`J₄` in (5.27)–(5.30), and the scalar collection in (5.31). It compares the printed PDF with the current `chatgpt-scratch` branch, especially:

The coefficient layer is not the next bottleneck. The next implementation should be an **exact, sign-checked, integrability-explicit weighted energy identity**, followed by four independent term estimates. The final quadratic inequality must be a derived collector theorem, not an input.

There are three substantive reasons not to formalize the printed calculation verbatim:

1. A direct expansion of the chemotactic flux gives different signs for the `a_{m+γ}` and `b₄` terms than the signs printed in (5.18)–(5.19).
2. The applications of Lemma 5.3 in `J₃` and `J₄` drop the factor `M^{2(γ-1)}` that is present in both the paper's resolver lemma and the proved Lean resolver theorem.
3. The scalar budget currently transcribed in `paper533DDoublePrime` is not an exact algebraic collector of (5.27)–(5.30): its `J₁` contribution lacks the required square of `B₁` unless an additional smallness lemma is supplied, and its `J₄` `b₄` contribution has the wrong power of `|χ|` under the displayed factorization.

Accordingly, the safe next target is:

```text
co-moving PDE + traveling-wave ODE
  -> exact unweighted perturbation equation
  -> exact exponential conjugation
  -> exact weighted elliptic difference equation
  -> whole-line weighted energy identity
  -> J1 bound
  -> J2 bound
  -> weighted resolver instantiation
  -> J3 bound
  -> J4 bound
  -> only then: scalar collector analogous to (5.31)
```

No theorem in the energy-identity or `J₁`–`J₄` layer should assume `paper531Quadratic < 0`, the final differential inequality, or any equivalent bundled conclusion.

## Notation discipline for the formalization

The paper overloads `U` and `V`: they denote wave profiles in one paragraph and exponentially weighted perturbations in the next. That is a serious source of sign mistakes. Use four visibly distinct levels:

```text
uᶜ(t,x), vᶜ(t,x)   Cauchy solution in the coordinate moving with speed c
U⋆(x), V⋆(x)       stationary traveling-wave profiles in that coordinate
w = uᶜ - U⋆         unweighted population perturbation
z = vᶜ - V⋆         unweighted signal perturbation
W = exp(ηx) w       weighted population perturbation
Z = exp(ηx) z       weighted signal perturbation
```

The energy should be the moving-coordinate half-energy

```latex
E(t)=\frac12\int_{\mathbb R} W(t,x)^2\,dx,
```

not the laboratory-coordinate weighted energy. `Theorem12CoordinateAudit.lean` already proves that the two differ by `exp (2 η c t)`, so the energy module should build on `coMovingPath` and never silently move the weight back to the laboratory coordinate.

## Sign firewall: derive (5.18) before using it

This is the highest-priority theorem. It should be proved from the original Cauchy PDE and the traveling-wave ODE, not stated as a hypothesis copied from the paper.

Let

```latex
w=u^c-U_\star,\qquad z=v^c-V_\star.
```

From the elliptic equations,

```latex
v^c_{xx}=v^c-(u^c)^\gamma,
\qquad
V_{\star,xx}=V_\star-U_\star^\gamma.
```

Expand the chemotactic flux exactly:

```latex
\begin{aligned}
\partial_x((u^c)^m v^c_x)-\partial_x(U_\star^m V_{\star,x})
={}&m(u^c)^{m-1}v^c_x\,w_x
  +mU_{\star,x}v^c_x\big((u^c)^{m-1}-U_\star^{m-1}\big)\\
 &+mU_\star^{m-1}U_{\star,x}\,z_x
  +v^c\big((u^c)^m-U_\star^m\big)\\
 &+U_\star^m z
  -\big((u^c)^{m+\gamma}-U_\star^{m+\gamma}\big).
\end{aligned}
```

Using `paper5A_mul_sub`, this is

```latex
b_1 w_x+\big(b_2+v^c a_m-a_{m+\gamma}\big)w+b_3 z_x+b_4z.
```

Therefore the mechanically derived unweighted perturbation equation is

```latex
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
w_t={}&w_{xx}+(c-\chi b_1)w_x\\
 &+\left[1-a_{1+\alpha}
   -\chi\big(v^c a_m+b_2-a_{m+\gamma}\big)\right]w\\
 &-\chi b_3z_x-\chi b_4z.
\end{aligned}}
```

The PDF's (5.18), as printed, instead has `+a_{m+γ}` inside the parenthesis multiplied by `-χ`, and has `+χ b₄ z`. Both signs conflict with the direct expansion above, assuming the paper's stated perturbation convention `z=v^c-V⋆`.

This is not a cosmetic issue. It changes the sign-sensitive part of `J₂` and the exact coefficient of `Z` in `J₄`.

### Exact weighted equation

With

```latex
W=e^{\eta x}w,\qquad Z=e^{\eta x}z,
```

use

```latex
w_x=e^{-\eta x}(W_x-\eta W),\quad
w_{xx}=e^{-\eta x}(W_{xx}-2\eta W_x+\eta^2W),\quad
z_x=e^{-\eta x}(Z_x-\eta Z).
```

The sign-checked conjugated equation is

```latex
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
W_t={}&W_{xx}+(c-\chi b_1-2\eta)W_x\\
 &+\left[\eta^2-c\eta+1-a_{1+\alpha}
   -\chi\big(v^c a_m+b_2-a_{m+\gamma}-\eta b_1\big)\right]W\\
 &-\chi b_3Z_x+\chi(\eta b_3-b_4)Z.
\end{aligned}}
```

The weighted elliptic difference equation is

```latex
\boxed{0=Z_{xx}-2\eta Z_x-(1-\eta^2)Z+a_\gamma W.}
```

The elliptic line agrees with the printed (5.19). The parabolic line does not: the printed coefficient has the opposite `a_{m+γ}` sign and the printed `Z` coefficient is `χ(b₄+ηb₃)` rather than `χ(ηb₃-b₄)`.

### Consequence for implementation

Create a dedicated theorem, conceptually named `paper518_coMoving_difference_identity`, and a second theorem, conceptually named `paper519_weighted_difference_identity`. The latter must depend on the former. Neither theorem should mention an energy inequality.

The exact flux expansion should also be a standalone lemma. It is the best regression test against future sign changes: a simplifier/ring proof of the flux identity is much safer than proving the whole PDE equation in one opaque block.

## Time-slice assumptions that are actually needed

`IsGlobalCauchySolutionFrom` is not sufficient by itself for this proof. It packages a global classical solution, initial trace, and positivity, but it does not automatically provide the whole-line weighted Sobolev, domination, boundary, or coefficient-boundedness facts required by the energy calculation.

The next energy file should expose an explicit time-slice regularity proposition or structure. It need not be the final theorem's public API; it can be built internally from stronger parabolic estimates later. It should provide, at a fixed `t>0`, at least the following facts.

### Differentiability and PDE facts

```text
- pointwise or a.e. weighted perturbation PDE;
- pointwise or a.e. weighted elliptic equation;
- spatial derivative identities for W and Z;
- a time derivative for W;
- enough regularity to identify deriv/iteratedDeriv with the supplied fields.
```

### Basic square integrability

```text
Integrable (W^2)
Integrable (W_x^2)
Integrable (Z^2)
Integrable (Z_x^2)
```

The last two should normally be obtained from `weighted_frozenElliptic_difference_l2_bound`, not assumed independently.

### Product integrability used by the identity

```text
Integrable (W * W_t)
Integrable (W * W_xx)            -- or a weak/cutoff substitute
Integrable (W_x * W)
Integrable (b1 * W_x * W)
Integrable (q * W^2)             -- q is the J2 multiplier
Integrable (b3 * Z_x * W)
Integrable ((η*b3-b4) * Z * W)
```

Most of these should be derived from `L² × L² -> L¹`, coefficient boundedness, and measurability. Do not leave them implicit: in Lean, a nonintegrable Bochner integral is defined as zero, so an equality of bare integrals can otherwise become vacuous.

### Time differentiation under the integral

For a local neighborhood of `t`, one needs an integrable envelope for `W(s,x) W_t(s,x)` and the pointwise time derivative of `W`. The existing theorem `wholeLine_timeLeibniz_of_dominated` already expresses precisely this kind of input.

### Boundary or cutoff facts

For the quick explicit-boundary route:

```text
W(x) * W_x(x) -> 0 as x -> +/- infinity
W(x)^2          -> 0 as x -> +/- infinity
```

For the more robust Sobolev route, replace these pointwise limits by cutoff lemmas proved from `W in H¹(R)`. The cutoff route is preferable long-term because a generic `H¹` representative need not come with convenient pointwise limits in the signature.

## `J₂` dependency map and sign-safe estimate

For the corrected PDE, the multiplier is

```latex
q_2=\eta^2-c\eta+1-a_{1+\alpha}
 -\chi(v^c a_m-a_{m+\gamma})-\chi b_2+\eta\chi b_1.
```

Dependencies:

```text

bounds for a_m and a_(m+γ)
0 <= v^c <= M^γ
B1 bound
B2 bound
η >= 0

```

A sign-agnostic estimate avoids the inconsistent `χ>0`/`χ<0` discussion in the PDF:

```latex
\begin{aligned}
-\chi(v^c a_m-a_{m+\gamma})
&\le |\chi|\big(v^c a_m+a_{m+\gamma}\big)\\
&\le |\chi|(2m+\gamma)M^{m+\gamma-1}.
\end{aligned}
```

Here

```latex
v^c a_m\le mM^{m+\gamma-1},\qquad
a_{m+\gamma}\le(m+\gamma)M^{m+\gamma-1}.
```

Together with

```latex
|b_1|\le B_1,\qquad
|b_2|\le |\chi|^{-2\sigma}B_2,
```

this gives the safe bound

```latex
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
J_2\le\Big[&\eta^2-c\eta+1
 +|\chi|(2m+\gamma)M^{m+\gamma-1}\\
 &+\eta|\chi|B_1+|\chi|^{1-2\sigma}B_2\Big]\int W^2.
\end{aligned}}
```

Thus the final numerical upper bound displayed in (5.28) can still be recovered, but not for the sign reason printed there. It should be proved by absolute domination of both `v a_m` and `a_{m+γ}` for both signs of `χ`.
