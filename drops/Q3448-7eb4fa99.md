## What this would and would not prove

This task would prove only wiring:

```lean
H1PhysicalRHSRouteBefore p u v T V₁ V₂ M L
  → H1SupBoundSqrtRHSIntegrableBefore p u T V₁ V₂ M L
      (H1PhysicalTaxisX p u v)
      (H1PhysicalUvxxX p u v)
      (H1PhysicalReactX p u)
```

It would not prove:

* the physical H¹ identity for these scalars;
* the zero-start component continuities for these scalar functions;
* the taxis/uvxx/reaction pointwise estimates;
* the zero-slab `liftDeriv2` physical RHS representative inputs.

Those remain the real analytic frontiers.

## Danger points to avoid

1. **Do not use** `H1EnergyIdentity_of_classicalSolution_and_uxxL1Cont` to produce route scalars. Its existential witnesses are intentionally degenerate and will not support physical estimates.

2. **Do not add a record with only arbitrary `taxisX uvxx reactX` fields.** That already exists in `H1SqrtTermBoundsBefore`, `H1IdentityRHSComponentsContinuousBefore`, and `H1SupBoundSqrtRHSIntegrableBefore`. A new file is useful only if it fixes the concrete global scalar functions.

3. **Do not make placeholders such as `taxisX := 0` or `reactX := -val + lapL2sq`.** Those satisfy identity shapes but break the physical route.

4. **Do not claim continuity or estimates from zero-slab `liftDeriv2` alone.** The scalar functions also involve `u`, `v`, spatial derivatives, denominator powers, and integral operations. Those estimates should be explicit fields until proved.

5. **Do not conflate zero-slab lap continuity with H¹ RHS scalar continuity.** `IntervalChiNegH1ZeroSlabPhysicalRHS.lean` gives the lap-component representative frontier. It does not give the scalar `taxisX/uvxx/reactX` continuity/estimate package.
