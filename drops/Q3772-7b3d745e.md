theorem chemFluxLifted_bridge_inputs_of_ball
    (p : CM2Params) {M : ℝ} (hM_nonneg : 0 ≤ M)
    {w : intervalDomainPoint → ℝ}
    (hw_cont : Continuous w)
    (hw_nonneg : ∀ x : intervalDomainPoint, 0 ≤ w x)
    (hw_bound : ∀ x : intervalDomainPoint, |w x| ≤ M) :
    AEStronglyMeasurable (chemFluxLifted p w) (intervalMeasure 1) ∧
      ContinuousOn (chemFluxLifted p w) (Set.Icc (0 : ℝ) 1) ∧
      ∀ y : ℝ,

          M * (Real.sqrt (∑' k : ℕ,
            (ShenWork.PDE.intervalNeumannResolverGradWeight p k) ^ 2) *
              (2 * (p.ν * M ^ p.γ))) := by
  have hflux_cont : Continuous (chemFluxLifted p w) :=
    ShenWork.IntervalDuhamelIntegrability.chemFluxLifted_continuous_of_continuous
      p hw_cont hw_nonneg
  have hflux_contOn :
      ContinuousOn (chemFluxLifted p w) (Set.Icc (0 : ℝ) 1) :=
    hflux_cont.continuousOn
  refine ⟨?_, hflux_contOn, ?_⟩
  · exact
      ShenWork.IntervalDuhamelIntegrability.continuousOn_aestronglyMeasurable_intervalMeasure
        hflux_contOn
  · exact ShenWork.Paper2.BFormInitialTrace.chemFluxLifted_bound_of_ball
      p hM_nonneg hw_bound hw_nonneg hw_cont

end ShenWork.Paper2.IntervalGradientSourceBridgeOpen

## What not to try next

Do not try to prove global continuity of `coupledChemDivSourceLift p u s`, nor continuity of a constant-extension representative of the chem-div source. The zero-extension/const-extension chem-div representative is generically discontinuous at `{0,1}`. The Task301 theorem wisely asks only for

```lean
ContinuousOn (coupledChemDivSourceLift p u s) (Set.Icc (0 : ℝ) 1)
```

and the Ktilde theorem similarly avoids false global continuity by using the subtype constant-extension trick.

Do not revive the closed-endpoint ambient derivative assumption

```lean
∀ y ∈ Set.uIcc (0 : ℝ) 1,
  HasDerivAt (chemFluxLifted p (u s)) ... y
```

for the zero-extended flux. That is the old unsatisfiable condition. The correct derivative input is the current open/right-within statement on `Set.Ioo 0 1` with `Set.Ioi y`.

Also, do not extract a fixed-slice `hdiv_cont` from an eventual field

```lean
∀ᶠ s in 𝓝 τ, ContinuousOn (coupledChemDivSourceLift p u s) (Set.Icc (0 : ℝ) 1)
```

unless the selected slice is known to lie in that eventual neighborhood. Existing FAC/physical packages often carry source continuity as such a local/evidential input; they do not by themselves give an arbitrary fixed-slice continuity theorem at every `s`.
