## Why direct kernel coupling is worse

A direct kernel route would define

```lean
Œ∫ t x := Measure.withDensity (intervalMeasure 1)
  (fun y => ENNReal.ofReal (intervalNeumannFullKernel t x y))
```

or an equivalent finite measure, then prove both `Œ∫ t x` and `Œ∫ t y` are probabilities using existing nonnegativity and mass.  But constructing a coupling of `Œ∫ t x` and `Œ∫ t y` supported on the band `|a-b| <= |x-y|` is not supplied by those facts.  It would need quantile coupling or a very strong monotonicity theorem for the Neumann heat kernel.  That is a much larger route than the reflected common-noise construction.
