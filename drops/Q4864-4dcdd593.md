# Problem 2.5: why a fixed rational Delannoy weight cannot be the CMF remainder, and the correct Beukers/AZ target

## Executive conclusion

Let

\[
\mathbf R_N(X)=\mathbf q_N-(1+X)\mathbf p_N
=\mathbf R_0(X)M(0)\cdots M(N-1),
\]

with

\[
\mathbf R_0(X)=
(2829-30921X,\,-3028+32972X,\,760-8240X).
\]

The exact moment identity

\[
GQ_{N,j}-P_{N,j}
=\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}R_{N,j}(t^2)\,dt
\]

is correct, but the proposed representation

\[
R_{N,j}(t^2)=\sum_{k=0}^N F(N,k)A_j(k,t),
\qquad
F(N,k)=2^k\binom{2k}{k}\binom Nk\binom{N+k}{k},
\tag{E1}
\]

with a fixed rational function \(A_j(k,t)\in\mathbf Q(k,t)\), cannot be the required Beukers certificate.

There are two exact obstructions.

1. **For the raw CMF row, (E1) is impossible by scale.** The raw sequence contains the seven-Pochhammer factor
   \[
   H_N=(-16)^N(2)_N^2(3)_N^2
   \left(\frac52\right)_N
   \left(\frac72\right)_N^2,
   \]
   whereas every fixed rationally weighted Delannoy sum has only exponential-times-polynomial growth.

2. **Even after division by \(H_N\), a fixed rational weight cannot project out the dominant Delannoy saddle.** Triangularity forces every such \(A_j(k,t)\) to be linear in \(t^2\). After performing the Catalan moment, the resulting weight is a nonzero rational function of \(k\), hence is eventually of one sign. Since \(F(N,k)>0\) and is concentrated at the dominant saddle \(k/N=2\sqrt2/3\), the sum necessarily retains the \(17+12\sqrt2\) exponential. It cannot produce the neutral root \(1\) with formal index \(-3\).

Thus the answer to the specific question is:

\[
\boxed{
\text{No fixed }A_j(k,t)\in\mathbf Q(k,t)
\text{ can give the required CMF remainder.}}
\tag{E2}
\]

The correct independent kernel must contain at least one of the following:

- explicit \(N\)-dependence in the summand/certificate;
- an oscillatory factor such as \((-1)^k\) or a complex saddle cycle;
- a nontrivial finite-difference term in \(k\);
- a matrix-valued Almkvist--Zeilberger numerator that annihilates the dominant thimble.

There is an exact independent Delannoy--Catalan family,

\[
B_N^*=D_N^2,
\qquad
A_N^*=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)
\sum_{m=0}^k\frac{(-1)^m}{(2m+1)^2},
\]

with a genuine Beukers integral. It proves \(A_N^*/B_N^*\to G\), but its projective base is \(0.882135\ldots\), not the CMF base

\[
\rho=17-12\sqrt2=0.029437\ldots.
\]

The remaining CMF problem is therefore a **dominant-saddle projection problem**, not a missing application of Brafman's identity.

# 1. Raw versus Pochhammer-normalized quantities

Put

\[
\lambda=17+12\sqrt2,
\qquad
\rho=17-12\sqrt2=\lambda^{-1}.
\]

The determinant-adapted Pochhammer factor is

\[
\boxed{
H_N=(-16)^N(2)_N^2(3)_N^2
\left(\frac52\right)_N
\left(\frac72\right)_N^2.}
\tag{1.1}
\]

Write

\[
\widehat{\mathbf p}_N=\frac{\mathbf p_N}{H_N},
\qquad
\widehat{\mathbf q}_N=\frac{\mathbf q_N}{H_N},
\qquad
\widehat{\mathbf R}_N(X)=\frac{\mathbf R_N(X)}{H_N}.
\tag{1.2}
\]

In the intended normalized Birkhoff basis, a generic coordinate has the form

\[
\widehat Q_{N,j}
=A_{+,j}\lambda^N(1+O(N^{-1}))
+A_{0,j}N^{-3}(1+O(N^{-1}))
+A_{-,j}\rho^N(1+O(N^{-1})).
\tag{1.3}
\]

The target Catalan cancellation says

\[
\widehat E_{N,j}:=rac{GQ_{N,j}-P_{N,j}}{H_N}
=K_{0,j}N^{-3}(1+O(N^{-1}))
+K_{-,j}\rho^N(1+O(N^{-1})).
\tag{1.4}
\]

Consequently

\[
\frac{GQ_{N,j}-P_{N,j}}{Q_{N,j}}
=O\!\left(N^{-3}\rho^N\right).
\tag{1.5}
\]

The common factor \(H_N\) cancels in the quotient. A Beukers proof should therefore represent and bound \(\widehat E_{N,j}\), not attempt to make the raw linear form itself tend to zero.

# 2. First obstruction: the proposed formula has the wrong factorial scale

The Delannoy--Clausen kernel is positive and satisfies

\[
\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)=D_N^2.
\tag{2.1}
\]

The standard asymptotic is

\[
D_N^2=\Theta\!\left(\frac{\lambda^N}{N}\right).
\tag{2.2}
\]

Let \(A(k,t)\in\mathbf Q(k,t)\) have no pole for integer \(k\ge0\) and for a fixed admissible \(t\in[0,1]\). Then for some integer \(d\),

\[
|A(k,t)|\le C_t(k+1)^d.
\]

Since \(F(N,k)\ge0\),

\[
\left|
\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)A(k,t)
\right|
\le C_t(N+1)^dD_N^2
=O_t\!\left(N^{d-1}\lambda^N\right).
\tag{2.3}
\]

By contrast,

\[
H_N
=\text{const}\cdot(-16)^N\Gamma(N)^7N^{39/2}
\left(1+O(N^{-1})\right).
\tag{2.4}
\]

Thus a generic raw CMF coordinate has size

\[
H_N\lambda^N\times\operatorname{poly}(N),
\]

which is superfactorially larger than (2.3). Hence

\[
\boxed{
R_{N,j}(t^2)
\ne\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)A_j(k,t)
}
\tag{2.5}
\]

for every fixed rational \(A_j(k,t)\), except for the zero sequence.

The only dimensionally consistent version is

\[
\boxed{
\widehat R_{N,j}(t^2)
=\frac{R_{N,j}(t^2)}{H_N}
=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)A_j(k,t).
}
\tag{2.6}
\]

The rest of the analysis concerns (2.6).

# 3. Triangular inversion: a weight always exists, but it is generally not rational

The matrix \(F(N,k)\), \(0\le k\le N\), is lower triangular, with

\[
F(N,N)=2^N\binom{2N}{N}^2\ne0.
\tag{3.1}
\]

Therefore every scalar sequence \(S_N(t)\) admits a unique transform

\[
S_N(t)=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)A_k(t),
\tag{3.2}
\]

where

\[
\boxed{
A_N(t)=
\frac{S_N(t)-\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}F(N,k)A_k(t)}
{2^N\binom{2N}{N}^2}.}
\tag{3.3}
\]

Thus the existence of *some* coefficient sequence \(A_k(t)\) says nothing. The substantive question is whether

\[
A_k(t)=A(k,t)
\]

is a fixed rational or hypergeometric function.

There is an additional exact simplification here. Each \(\widehat R_{N,j}(t^2)\) is linear in \(t^2\). Formula (3.3), by induction, implies

\[
\boxed{
A_j(k,t)=a_j(k)+t^2b_j(k).}
\tag{3.4}
\]

No higher rational dependence on \(t\) can occur in the unique triangular transform.

The first coefficients are obtained from

\[
F(0,0)=1,
\]

\[
(F(1,0),F(1,1))=(1,8),
\]

\[
(F(2,0),F(2,1),F(2,2))=(1,24,144).
\]

Hence, coefficientwise in \(1,t^2\),

\[
A_0=S_0,
\qquad
A_1=\frac{S_1-S_0}{8},
\qquad
A_2=\frac{S_2-3S_1+2S_0}{144}.
\tag{3.5}
\]

These identities give a very fast exact rationality screen.

# 4. Second obstruction: a fixed rational weight cannot remove the dominant saddle

Assume the normalized representation (2.6), and use (3.4):

\[
A_j(k,t)=a_j(k)+t^2b_j(k),
\qquad a_j,b_j\in\mathbf Q(k).
\tag{4.1}
\]

The two elementary Catalan moments are

\[
\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}\,dt=G,
\tag{4.2}
\]

and

\[
\int_0^1\frac{-t^2\log t}{1+t^2}\,dt=1-G,
\tag{4.3}
\]

because their sum is \(\int_0^1-\log t\,dt=1\).

Termwise integration would therefore give

\[
\widehat E_{N,j}
=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)e_j(k),
\tag{4.4}
\]

with

\[
\boxed{
e_j(k)=G\,a_j(k)+(1-G)b_j(k).}
\tag{4.5}
\]

If \(e_j\) is not identically zero, it is a nonzero rational function over \(\mathbf Q(G)\). Consequently it has a fixed sign for all sufficiently large integer \(k\), and

\[
|e_j(k)|\ge c(k+1)^{-m}
\tag{4.6}
\]

for suitable \(c>0\) and \(m\ge0\).

Now compute the exact adjacent-term ratio

\[
\boxed{
\frac{F(N,k+1)}{F(N,k)}
=\frac{4(2k+1)(N-k)(N+k+1)}{(k+1)^3}.}
\tag{4.7}
\]

For \(k=xN+O(1)\), this tends to

\[
\frac{8(1-x^2)}{x^2}.
\]

The unique positive saddle is therefore

\[
\boxed{
\kappa=\frac{2\sqrt2}{3}.}
\tag{4.8}
\]

Stirling's formula, or the ratio test applied uniformly around (4.8), shows that a fixed positive fraction of the mass of \(F(N,k)\) lies in a window

\[
|k-\kappa N|\le C\sqrt N.
\]

All terms in this window have the same sign after multiplication by \(e_j(k)\). Contributions from the finitely many exceptional small values of \(k\) are only polynomial in \(N\). Hence

\[
\limsup_{N\to\infty}
\left|
\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)e_j(k)
\right|^{1/N}
=\lambda.
\tag{4.9}
\]

In particular, this sum cannot have neutral exponential base \(1\).

If \(e_j\equiv0\), then \(\widehat E_{N,j}=0\) for every \(N\), which is not the CMF sequence. Therefore (4.4) cannot represent the Catalan remainder.

This proves the promised no-go statement:

\[
\boxed{
\begin{array}{c}
\text{a fixed rational Delannoy weight is eventually nonoscillatory,}\\
\text{so it cannot annihilate the }(1,1)\text{ dominant saddle.}
\end{array}}
\tag{4.10}
\]

This is stronger than the earlier rational Ore-Hom obstruction. It applies directly to the proposed single-sum architecture.

# 5. Exact generating-function characterization of a fixed weight

Although fixed rational weights cannot produce the CMF error, their generating functions are useful for identifying the correct elliptic extension.

For fixed \(k\),

\[
\boxed{
\sum_{N\ge k}
\binom Nk\binom{N+k}{k}z^N
=\binom{2k}{k}\frac{z^k}{(1-z)^{2k+1}}.}
\tag{5.1}
\]

Therefore, if

\[
S_N=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)A(k),
\]

then

\[
\boxed{
\sum_{N\ge0}S_Nz^N
=\frac1{1-z}
\sum_{k\ge0}\binom{2k}{k}^2A(k)
\left(\frac{2z}{(1-z)^2}\right)^k.}
\tag{5.2}
\]

Put

\[
w=\frac{2z}{(1-z)^2},
\qquad
x=4\sqrt w=\frac{4\sqrt{2z}}{1-z}.
\tag{5.3}
\]

The base series is

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal K(w):=
\sum_{k\ge0}\binom{2k}{k}^2w^k
=\frac2\pi K(4\sqrt w).}
\tag{5.4}
\]

Thus (5.2) is exactly Brafman's pullback.

Let \(\theta_w=w\,d/dw\). If \(A(k)=P(k)\) is polynomial, then

\[
\sum\binom{2k}{k}^2P(k)w^k
=P(\theta_w)\mathcal K(w).
\tag{5.5}
\]

If \(A(k)\) contains a partial fraction \((k+\alpha)^{-m}\), it corresponds to an Euler integral:

\[
(\theta_w+\alpha)^{-1}f(w)
=w^{-\alpha}\int_0^wu^{\alpha-1}f(u)\,du,
\tag{5.6}
\]

iterated \(m\) times.

Consequently, a rational \(A(k)\) generates only a finite tower of Euler integrals of the elliptic period \(K\). This gives an exact operator test:

> a rational-weight formula exists only if the normalized CMF generating function belongs to a finite Euler-integral extension of the Brafman Gauss module.

Matching Poincaré roots alone does not imply this membership.

# 6. The integrated-\(K\) candidate and the minimal fixed-weight screen

The distinguished integrated elliptic period is

\[
Y(x)=\frac12\int_0^xK(s)\,ds.
\tag{6.1}
\]

For the rational weight

\[
A(k)=\frac1{2k+1},
\]

we have

\[
\sum_{k\ge0}\binom{2k}{k}^2
\frac{w^k}{2k+1}
=\frac{4}{\pi x}Y(x).
\tag{6.2}
\]

Hence

\[
\boxed{
\sum_{N\ge0}
\left[
\sum_{k=0}^N\frac{F(N,k)}{2k+1}
\right]z^N
=
\frac{4Y(x(z))}{\pi(1-z)x(z)}.}
\tag{6.3}
\]

This is the exact coefficient bridge between the Delannoy sum and the integrated-\(K\) ODE.

The smallest fixed-weight ansatz compatible with an order-three integrated elliptic system and with outer formal power zero is

\[
\boxed{
A_j(k,t)=
\frac{P_j(k)+t^2Q_j(k)}{2k+1},
\qquad
\deg P_j,\deg Q_j\le2.}
\tag{6.4}
\]

Indeed, the quadratic numerator makes \(A_j(k,t)\sim\text{const}\cdot k\), changing the Delannoy-square prefactor \(N^{-1}\) into the required outer prefactor \(N^0\).

This ansatz is completely determined by \(N=0,1,2\). For one coefficient sector, let

\[
S_N=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)\frac{P(k)}{2k+1}.
\]

Set

\[
\alpha_0=S_0,
\qquad
\alpha_1=\frac{S_1-S_0}{8},
\qquad
\alpha_2=\frac{S_2-3S_1+2S_0}{144}.
\tag{6.5}
\]

Then

\[
P(0)=\alpha_0,
\qquad
P(1)=3\alpha_1,
\qquad
P(2)=5\alpha_2,
\]

so

\[
\boxed{
P(k)=\alpha_0
+(3\alpha_1-\alpha_0)k
+\frac{5\alpha_2-6\alpha_1+\alpha_0}{2}k(k-1).}
\tag{6.6}
\]

At \(N=3\), where

\[
(F(3,0),F(3,1),F(3,2),F(3,3))
=(1,48,720,3200),
\]

one exact check accepts or rejects the ansatz.

However, Section 4 proves that even if (6.4) represented \(\widehat R_{N,j}(t^2)\), its termwise Catalan moment could not be the neutral CMF remainder. A nontrivial \(N\)-dependent telescoping correction would still be necessary.

# 7. The genuine independent Delannoy--Catalan approximation

There is a complete independent Beukers family based on the same \(F(N,k)\). Define

\[
C_k=\sum_{m=0}^k\frac{(-1)^m}{(2m+1)^2},
\tag{7.1}
\]

\[
B_N^*=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)=D_N^2,
\qquad
A_N^*=\sum_{k=0}^NF(N,k)C_k.
\tag{7.2}
\]

The tail identity is

\[
C_k-G
=(-1)^k\int_0^1
\frac{x^{2k+2}(-\log x)}{1+x^2}\,dx.
\tag{7.3}
\]

Clausen's identity gives

\[
\sum_{k=0}^N(-1)^kF(N,k)x^{2k}
=\mathsf P_N\!\left(\sqrt{1-8x^2}\right)^2.
\tag{7.4}
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
A_N^*-GB_N^*
=\int_0^1
\frac{x^2(-\log x)}{1+x^2}
\mathsf P_N\!\left(\sqrt{1-8x^2}\right)^2\,dx.}
\tag{7.5}
\]

This is a genuine non-circular Beukers representation. It proves

\[
\frac{A_N^*}{B_N^*}\to G.
\]

Its exponential ratio is

\[
\frac{15+4\sqrt{14}}{17+12\sqrt2}
=0.882135\ldots,
\tag{7.6}
\]

not \(\rho=0.029437\ldots\). The oscillatory factor \((-1)^k\) is exactly what evades the eventual-sign obstruction of Section 4, but it selects a different saddle geometry from the printed CMF.

Thus (7.5) is a model for the desired proof, not an identification of the challenge sequence.

# 8. The correct saddle geometry for the CMF remainder

The double constant-term Delannoy carrier is

\[
\phi(u)=3+\sqrt2(u+u^{-1}),
\qquad
\Phi(u,v)=\phi(u)\phi(v),
\tag{8.1}
\]

\[
D_N^2=rac1{(2\pi i)^2}
\oint\!\oint
\Phi(u,v)^N\frac{du}{u}\frac{dv}{v}.
\tag{8.2}
\]

The four critical points are

\[
(u,v)=(1,1),(1,-1),(-1,1),(-1,-1),
\]

with critical values

\[
\lambda,\quad1,\quad1,\quad\rho.
\tag{8.3}
\]

This makes the target architecture transparent.

- The denominator must have a nonzero component on the \((1,1)\) thimble.
- The Catalan remainder must have zero component on that thimble.
- Its leading contribution must come from the two mixed saddles, whose value is \(1\).
- The formal index \(-3\) requires two additional powers of \(N^{-1}\) beyond an ordinary two-dimensional nondegenerate saddle. Equivalently, after choosing local Gaussian coordinates, the effective amplitude must begin at total degree four, or an equivalent twofold integration/shearing must occur.

A positive one-dimensional sum with a fixed eventually-signed rational weight cannot perform this thimble projection. A signed cycle, an oscillatory factor, or an \(N\)-dependent matrix numerator can.

# 9. The matrix-valued AZ certificate that would close the proof

Let

\[
M_H(N)=\frac{M(N)}{H_{N+1}/H_N}
\]

be the normalized step matrix. The correct independent object is a matrix kernel of the form

\[
\mathcal K_N(u,v,t;\varepsilon)
=\Phi(u,v)^N
\frac{t^\varepsilon}{uv(1+t^2)}
\mathcal P_N(u,v,t;\varepsilon),
\tag{9.1}
\]

where \(\mathcal P_N\) is explicitly \(N\)-dependent. One seeks

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
&\mathcal K_{N+1}(u,v,t;\varepsilon)
-\mathcal K_N(u,v,t;\varepsilon)M_H(N)\\
&\qquad=
\partial_u\mathcal A_N
+\partial_v\mathcal B_N
+\partial_t\mathcal C_N.
\end{aligned}}
\tag{9.2}
\]

The \(u,v\) contours are closed and separate the appropriate Laurent poles, so their total derivatives vanish. The Catalan moment is obtained from

\[
-\left.\frac{\partial}{\partial\varepsilon}
\frac{t^\varepsilon}{1+t^2}\right|_{\varepsilon=0}
=\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}.
\tag{9.3}
\]

To close the proof, the reconstructed certificate must satisfy all of the following.

1. **Exact matrix identity:** (9.2) over \(\mathbf Q(N,u,v,t,\varepsilon)\).
2. **Initial-period matching:** the two integrated initial rows equal \(\mathbf p_0\) and \(\mathbf q_0\).
3. **No dominant thimble in the first \(\varepsilon\)-jet:** the \((1,1)\) saddle coefficient vanishes exactly.
4. **Mixed-saddle order:** the first nonzero local amplitude at \((1,-1)\) and \((-1,1)\) has the order required for \(N^{-3}\).
5. **Boundary control in \(t\):** the \(t=0\) flux vanishes after differentiation, and the \(t=1\) flux either vanishes or is explicitly identified with the neutral extension direction.
6. **Denominator lower bound:** for at least one column,
   \[
   |\widehat Q_{N,j}|\ge c\lambda^N
   \]
   for all sufficiently large \(N\).

Then

\[
|\widehat E_{N,j}|\le CN^{-3}
\]

would immediately give

\[
\boxed{
\left|
\frac{P_{N,j}}{Q_{N,j}}-G
\right|
\le\frac CcN^{-3}\rho^N.}
\tag{9.4}
\]

This is the desired Beukers-style conclusion.

# 10. Exact finite screening procedure

The following is the decisive finite workflow.

```text
1. Generate p_N and q_N exactly from the printed 3x3 matrix.
2. Divide by the exact H_N.
3. Form the constant and t^2 coefficients of Rhat_N(t^2).
4. Apply the triangular inversion (3.3).
5. Test whether either coefficient sequence is rational in k.
6. First test the minimal integrated-K class P_2(k)/(2k+1).
7. Verify any guess on many extra exact N-values.
8. Compute its telescoper and compare it with the corrected CMF operator.
9. If the fixed-weight test fails—as the saddle obstruction predicts—
   move to an N-dependent matrix AZ ansatz based on Phi(u,v).
10. Verify the epsilon=0 and first-epsilon-jet identities together,
    then perform the saddle and boundary analysis.
```

A compact Sage-style triangular inversion is:

```sage
# F(N,k) is exact and lower triangular.
def F(N,k):
    if k < 0 or k > N:
        return QQ(0)
    return QQ(2^k * binomial(2*k,k)
                * binomial(N,k) * binomial(N+k,k))

# S[N] may be either the constant or t^2 coefficient of Rhat_N.
def inverse_delannoy_transform(S):
    A = []
    for N in range(len(S)):
        r = S[N] - sum(F(N,k)*A[k] for k in range(N))
        A.append(r / F(N,N))
    return A

# Rationality test: search U,V with V(k)A_k-U(k)=0,
# then verify on terms not used in reconstruction.
```

Because the transform is triangular, a failed rational interpolation is an exact rejection of the proposed fixed-weight degree bound; it is not a numerical heuristic once the reconstructed identity is checked over \(\mathbf Q\).

# 11. Direct answers

## Can \(R_{N,j}(t^2)\) be written as \(\sum_kF(N,k)A_j(k,t)\) with rational \(A_j\)?

No. For the raw row, the Pochhammer scale makes it impossible. After normalization by \(H_N\), a unique coefficient sequence exists, but a fixed rational coefficient would be eventually nonoscillatory and could not remove the dominant Delannoy saddle. It therefore cannot produce the neutral Catalan remainder.

## What does Brafman's identity provide?

It gives the exact homogeneous elliptic carrier and the quadratic pullback

\[
w=\frac{2z}{(1-z)^2},
\qquad
x=\frac{4\sqrt{2z}}{1-z}.
\]

It also identifies the integrated-\(K\) weight \((2k+1)^{-1}\). It does not determine the CMF extension class or its dominant-saddle projection.

## Is there an independent Beukers integral involving \(F(N,k)\)?

Yes: equation (7.5) gives one for the direct Delannoy--Catalan approximants. It is exact and proves convergence, but it is not the printed CMF and has the wrong exponential rate.

## What is still missing for the CMF?

An \(N\)-dependent, matrix-valued AZ certificate on the double Delannoy carrier, together with a first-parameter-jet Catalan extension and a proof that its cycle/amplitude kills the \((1,1)\) saddle while retaining the mixed saddles with formal index \(-3\).

That is the precise finite certificate needed to turn the exact moment identity into a proof.
