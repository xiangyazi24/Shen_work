# Problem 2.5: the epsilon-regularized matrix AZ identity, boundary conditions, and the Catalan extension class

## Executive verdict

The matrix Almkvist–Zeilberger strategy can in principle identify the Catalan extension class, but the currently stated data do **not** yet close the proof.

There are five precise conclusions.

1. **`d = 2` is not yet a proved minimum.** In the fixed normalization, denominator support, geometric monomial support, and symmetry sector of Q4838, it is the first serious search degree. The exact minimum is the first `d` for which the reconstructed rational certificate survives exact verification and all boundary tests. To prove that the minimum is two, one must certify zero nullity for `d=0,1` and exhibit a valid certificate for `d=2`.

2. **An AZ certificate at `epsilon = 0` does not automatically survive the regularization.** If

   $$
   h_{n,\varepsilon}(u,v,t)
   =\frac{u^n v^n t^\varepsilon}
          {Q(u,v)^{n+1}(1+t^2)},
   \qquad Q=1-u^2v^2,
   $$

   then differentiating the twisted divergence introduces the explicit forcing term

   $$
   \boxed{\frac{1}{t}X_t^{(0)}}.
   $$

   With the Q4838 parametrization

   $$
   X_t=t(1+t^2)C,
   $$

   this becomes

   $$
   \boxed{(1+t^2)C^{(0)}.}
   $$

   One must solve the AZ identity to first order in `epsilon`, or retain this term as a nonzero off-diagonal extension cocycle. Equation (22) of Q4838 exposes the obstruction; it does not by itself prove that the regularized identity exists.

3. **For closed `u`- and `v`-cycles, there are no endpoint conditions at saddle points.** The `u,v` total derivatives integrate to zero because the fluxes are single-valued around closed cycles. Critical points control asymptotics, not integration-by-parts boundaries. If open arcs are used, the exact weighted endpoint-flux conditions are given in Section 7.

4. **The kernel written in the question is not the isolated-saddle Delannoy carrier.** Its exponential phase is

   $$
   \Psi(u,v)=\frac{uv}{1-u^2v^2},
   $$

   whose stationary locus is

   $$
   uv=\pm i.
   $$

   This is a pair of one-dimensional critical curves, not four isolated Delannoy saddles. The four isolated saddles belong instead to the constant-term carrier

   $$
   \Phi(u,v)=\phi(u)\phi(v),
   \qquad
   \phi(u)=3+\sqrt2\left(u+u^{-1}\right).
   $$

   Thus a certificate for the `Q=1-u^2v^2` kernel would not yet establish the desired Delannoy/CMF spectral identification unless a separate birational or contour equivalence is proved.

5. **Even a valid first-jet AZ identity closes only the recurrence part.** To prove that the connection coefficient is exactly

   $$
   G=-\int_0^1\frac{\log t}{1+t^2}\,dt,
   $$

   one must also evaluate the initial period matrix exactly and match it to the two printed rows of the challenge matrix `A`. The finite certificate plus boundary analysis plus initial matching would close the proof; the finite certificate alone would not.

# 1. The normalized matrix and the AZ equation

Use the determinant-adapted Pochhammer factor

$$
H_n=(-16)^n(2)_n^2(3)_n^2
\left(\frac52\right)_n
\left(\frac72\right)_n^2,
$$

with step

$$
\delta(n)=\frac{H_{n+1}}{H_n}
=-2(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2.
$$

The locally normalized cocycle is

$$
M_H(n)=\frac{M(n)}{\delta(n)}.
$$

For degree screening one should also apply the balancing shear

$$
D_n=\operatorname{diag}(1,n+1,(n+1)^2),
$$

and work with

$$
M_{\rm norm}(n)=D_n^{-1}M_H(n)D_{n+1}.
$$

This matrix is bounded at infinity and has normalized formal indices

$$
(0,-3,0).
$$

For the kernel used in Q4838, set

$$
Q(u,v)=1-u^2v^2,
$$

$$
h_n(u,v,t)=
\frac{u^n v^n}{Q(u,v)^{n+1}(1+t^2)}.
$$

Then

$$
\frac{h_{n+1}}{h_n}=\frac{uv}{Q}.
$$

The logarithmic derivatives are

$$
\ell_u
=\frac{\partial_u h_n}{h_n}
=\frac{n+(n+2)u^2v^2}{uQ},
$$

$$
\ell_v
=\frac{n+(n+2)u^2v^2}{vQ},
$$

$$
\ell_t=-\frac{2t}{1+t^2}.
$$

For a matrix numerator `P_n` and certificate matrices `X_u,X_v,X_t`, the divided AZ equation is

$$
\boxed{
\frac{uv}{Q}P_{n+1}-P_nM_{\rm norm}(n)
=(\partial_u+\ell_u)X_u
 +(\partial_v+\ell_v)X_v
 +(\partial_t+\ell_t)X_t.
}
\tag{1.1}
$$

Multiplication by `h_n` turns the right side into ordinary total derivatives:

$$
h_n\left(\frac{uv}{Q}P_{n+1}-P_nM_{\rm norm}\right)
=\partial_u(h_nX_u)+\partial_v(h_nX_v)+\partial_t(h_nX_t).
\tag{1.2}
$$

This is the identity that must ultimately be verified over `Q(n,u,v,t)` after denominators are cleared.

# 2. What `d = 2` means, and what would prove minimality

Fix all of the following before asking for a minimum degree:

- the Pochhammer and balancing gauges;
- the denominator support in `n`;
- the denominator support in `u,v,t`;
- the total geometric monomial support;
- the `u <-> v` symmetry sector;
- whether one solves one row, the two challenge rows, or all three rows;
- the normalization used to remove scalar certificate gauge freedom.

Only after these choices does the phrase “minimum degree in `n`” have a definite meaning.

Q4838 uses coefficient functions of the form

$$
\sum_{j=0}^{d} c_j n^j
$$

for each geometric monomial slot. After the `u <-> v` reduction there are 153 slots, hence

$$
153(d+1)
$$

scalar unknowns for the full `3 x 3` system. At `d=2` this is 459 unknowns.

The reason `d=2` is the first serious bound is heuristic but strong:

- `M_norm(n)` has degree zero at infinity;
- `ell_u` and `ell_v` are affine in `n`;
- one shift in `n` preserves polynomial degree;
- the balanced rank-three shear has width two;
- the first epsilon jet adds one further affine forcing term.

None of these facts proves that `d=0` or `d=1` is impossible. The formal index defect `-3` also does not by itself force `d=2`: cancellations among geometric monomials and denominator shifts can alter the apparent polynomial degree.

The exact minimality test is therefore:

1. construct the full cleared linear map for `d=0` and prove its rational kernel is zero;
2. repeat for `d=1`;
3. reconstruct a nonzero rational kernel at `d=2`;
4. verify the identity exactly over `Q`, not only modulo primes;
5. verify all analytic boundary conditions.

Modular screening at several primes and several geometric points is a fast rejection and reconstruction tool. It is not a proof of minimality until the reconstructed systems are checked exactly.

If no valid certificate appears at `d=2`, the proper ladder is

```text
d = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4
```

before changing the kernel or enlarging unrelated denominator factors.

# 3. Epsilon regularization

Define

$$
h_{n,\varepsilon}(u,v,t)
=t^\varepsilon h_n(u,v,t),
\qquad \Re\varepsilon>-1.
$$

The `u` and `v` logarithmic derivatives do not change. The `t` derivative becomes

$$
\boxed{
\ell_t^{(\varepsilon)}
=\frac{\varepsilon}{t}-\frac{2t}{1+t^2}
=\frac{\varepsilon+(\varepsilon-2)t^2}
       {t(1+t^2)}.
}
\tag{3.1}
$$

Suppose the `t` certificate is parametrized as in Q4838:

$$
X_t=t(1+t^2)C.
$$

Then

$$
\boxed{
(\partial_t+\ell_t^{(\varepsilon)})X_t
=(1+t^2)\left(tC_t+(1+\varepsilon)C\right).
}
\tag{3.2}
$$

This formula is exact. Its first epsilon derivative is the source of the extension class.

# 4. The zeroth and first epsilon-jet equations

Allow every numerator and certificate to depend analytically on `epsilon`:

$$
P_n(\varepsilon)=P_n^{(0)}+\varepsilon P_n^{(1)}+O(\varepsilon^2),
$$

and similarly for `X_u,X_v,X_t`.

Write

$$
\mathcal L_n(P)
:=\frac{uv}{Q}P_{n+1}-P_nM_{\rm norm}(n),
$$

$$
\mathcal D_u=\partial_u+\ell_u,
\qquad
\mathcal D_v=\partial_v+\ell_v,
\qquad
\mathcal D_t=\partial_t+\ell_t.
$$

The epsilon-zero equation is

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal L_n(P^{(0)})
=\mathcal D_uX_u^{(0)}
 +\mathcal D_vX_v^{(0)}
 +\mathcal D_tX_t^{(0)}.
}
\tag{4.1}
$$

Differentiating the regularized equation at `epsilon=0` gives

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal L_n(P^{(1)})
=\mathcal D_uX_u^{(1)}
 +\mathcal D_vX_v^{(1)}
 +\mathcal D_tX_t^{(1)}
 +\frac1tX_t^{(0)}.
}
\tag{4.2}
$$

For

$$
X_t^{(j)}=t(1+t^2)C^{(j)},
$$

the final term is

$$
\boxed{
\frac1tX_t^{(0)}=(1+t^2)C^{(0)}.
}
\tag{4.3}
$$

Equations (4.1)–(4.3), not the epsilon-zero equation alone, are the finite algebraic system that must be reconstructed.

The class of

$$
(1+t^2)C^{(0)}
$$

modulo the image of the twisted divergences and admissible boundary coboundaries is the candidate extension class. There are two cases.

- If it can be absorbed by `P^(1),X_u^(1),X_v^(1),X_t^(1)` with homogeneous boundary conditions, the first jet is split in the chosen relative de Rham complex.
- If a nonzero residue remains and maps to the neutral direction of the CMF, that residue is the nontrivial extension cocycle.

Thus one should reconstruct the zeroth and first epsilon jets simultaneously. Solving only (4.1) and then differentiating informally is insufficient.

# 5. Integration and the block-triangular system

Let

$$
\Gamma=\gamma_u\times\gamma_v\times[0,1]
$$

be the chosen product cycle and define the period matrix

$$
\Pi_n(\varepsilon)
=\int_\Gamma h_{n,\varepsilon}P_n(\varepsilon).
\tag{5.1}
$$

Its first two coefficients are

$$
\Pi_n^{(0)}
=\int_\Gamma h_nP_n^{(0)},
\tag{5.2}
$$

$$
\boxed{
\Pi_n^{(1)}
=\int_\Gamma h_n
\left(P_n^{(1)}+P_n^{(0)}\log t\right).
}
\tag{5.3}
$$

Assume differentiation under the integral is legitimate. A sufficient local condition is that for some `eta<1`, uniformly near `epsilon=0`, the integrand is bounded by

$$
C t^{-\eta}(1+|\log t|),
$$

which is integrable on `[0,1]`.

After integrating (1.2), one obtains

$$
\Pi_{n+1}(\varepsilon)
-\Pi_n(\varepsilon)M_{\rm norm}(n)
=\mathcal B_n(\varepsilon),
\tag{5.4}
$$

where `B_n` is the sum of the three boundary fluxes.

If all boundaries vanish for every epsilon near zero, then

$$
\Pi_{n+1}^{(0)}=\Pi_n^{(0)}M_{\rm norm}(n),
$$

$$
\Pi_{n+1}^{(1)}=\Pi_n^{(1)}M_{\rm norm}(n).
$$

This is a direct sum of two copies of the homogeneous system. By itself it is **not** a non-split extension.

A genuinely block-triangular extension arises in either of two ways.

### 5.1 An epsilon-dependent cocycle

If

$$
M_{\rm norm}(n;\varepsilon)
=M_0(n)+\varepsilon N(n)+O(\varepsilon^2),
$$

then

$$
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\Pi_{n+1}^{(0)}&=\Pi_n^{(0)}M_0(n),\\
\Pi_{n+1}^{(1)}&=\Pi_n^{(1)}M_0(n)+\Pi_n^{(0)}N(n).
\end{aligned}}
\tag{5.5}
$$

### 5.2 A nonzero relative boundary/source class

If the first-jet source or `t=1` flux cannot be removed, then

$$
\Pi_{n+1}^{(1)}
=\Pi_n^{(1)}M_0(n)+F_n,
\tag{5.6}
$$

where `F_n` must be identified with the root-one neutral direction. This is the more natural interpretation of the Q4838 forcing term.

Consequently, simply multiplying an already homogeneous kernel by `t^epsilon` does not automatically construct `E_G`. One must exhibit the off-diagonal map `N(n)` or the nonzero relative class `F_n`.

# 6. The Catalan moment

The elementary parameter integral is

$$
J(\varepsilon)
=\int_0^1\frac{t^\varepsilon}{1+t^2}\,dt
=\frac14\left[
\psi\left(\frac{\varepsilon+3}{4}\right)
-\psi\left(\frac{\varepsilon+1}{4}\right)
\right].
\tag{6.1}
$$

It satisfies

$$
J(0)=\frac\pi4,
$$

$$
\boxed{J'(0)=-G.}
\tag{6.2}
$$

Therefore the logarithmic part of (5.3) can carry Catalan's constant exactly.

There is an important split-case warning. If the matrix numerator is independent of `t`, then the integral factors:

$$
\Pi_n(\varepsilon)=J(\varepsilon)U_n.
$$

Hence

$$
-\Pi_n'(0)=G U_n.
$$

This produces `G` as a scalar multiple of an already homogeneous solution, but it does not identify the nontrivial extension class of the printed CMF. To obtain the desired extension, `P_n` or the certificate/boundary data must couple the `t` direction nontrivially to the neutral mode.

The `pi/4` value at epsilon zero must also be cancelled or placed in a separate basis column when matching the rational challenge rows. The cleanest readout is the derivative functional

$$
-\left.\partial_\varepsilon\right|_{\varepsilon=0},
$$

possibly combined with a rational `t`-polynomial whose epsilon-zero moment vanishes.

# 7. Exact boundary conditions

## 7.1 Closed `u`- and `v`-cycles

Suppose `gamma_u` and `gamma_v` are closed contours. Then

$$
\oint_{\gamma_u}\partial_u(hX_u)\,du=0
$$

provided `hX_u` is single-valued after one circuit and meromorphic in a neighborhood of the contour, with no pole on the contour itself. The same holds in `v`.

Poles strictly inside a closed contour do not obstruct the identity: the derivative of a single-valued meromorphic function has zero residue. What must be excluded are poles on the path, branch jumps, or monodromy of the flux itself.

Therefore there are **no conditions such as**

$$
A(u_\pm,v,t)=0
$$

at saddle points when closed constant-term contours are used. Saddles are not boundaries.

## 7.2 Open `u`-arcs

If an open arc `Gamma_u` is used, the exact boundary condition is

$$
\boxed{
\left[h_{n,\varepsilon}(u,v,t)X_u(n,u,v,t;\varepsilon)
\right]_{u=u_{\rm left}}^{u=u_{\rm right}}=0.
}
\tag{7.1}
$$

A sufficient pointwise condition is that the weighted flux vanish at each endpoint:

$$
\lim_{u\to u_e}h_{n,\varepsilon}X_u=0.
\tag{7.2}
$$

If the weight is finite and nonzero at the endpoint and the certificate denominator is also nonzero there, this reduces to

$$
X_u(u_e,v,t;\varepsilon)=0.
$$

If several oriented arcs meet at one endpoint, the necessary and sufficient condition is cancellation of signed fluxes:

$$
\boxed{
\sum_{\alpha\ni u_e}
\operatorname{sgn}(\alpha,u_e)
\bigl(hX_u\bigr)_\alpha(u_e,v,t)=0.
}
\tag{7.3}
$$

The analogous conditions hold for `v`.

## 7.3 If one artificially cuts at the Delannoy saddles

For the symmetric Delannoy coordinate

$$
\phi(u)=3+\sqrt2(u+u^{-1}),
$$

the critical points are

$$
u_+=1,\qquad u_-=-1.
$$

If one chooses separate arcs that actually terminate there and demands zero flux on every arc separately, a sufficient polynomial condition is

$$
\boxed{X_u\text{ is divisible by }u^2-1.}
\tag{7.4}
$$

Similarly,

$$
\boxed{X_v\text{ is divisible by }v^2-1.}
\tag{7.5}
$$

In the alternative coordinate

$$
\phi(u)=3+u+2/u,
$$

the critical points are `u=+-sqrt(2)`, and the corresponding sufficient factors are `u^2-2` and `v^2-2`.

These divisibility conditions are **not necessary** for closed contours or for glued arcs with cancelling fluxes. They are only a strong sufficient condition for independently terminated open arcs.

A genuine Lefschetz thimble normally passes through a saddle; the saddle is not its boundary. Its boundary lies at a decay end or a singular divisor. Thus imposing zero certificate values at saddles can overconstrain the AZ system.

## 7.4 The `t=0` endpoint

With

$$
X_t=t(1+t^2)C,
$$

one has

$$
h_{n,\varepsilon}X_t
=\frac{u^nv^n}{Q^{n+1}}t^{\varepsilon+1}C.
$$

Hence the `t=0` boundary vanishes for `Re epsilon>-1` provided `C` is bounded there. The differentiated boundary also vanishes because

$$
t^{\varepsilon+1}\log t\longrightarrow0
\qquad(t\to0^+).
$$

## 7.5 The `t=1` endpoint

At `t=1`,

$$
\left.h_{n,\varepsilon}X_t\right|_{t=1}
=\frac{u^nv^n}{Q^{n+1}}C(n,u,v,1;\varepsilon).
\tag{7.6}
$$

For a homogeneous recurrence it is sufficient to impose

$$
\boxed{C(n,u,v,1;\varepsilon)=0.}
\tag{7.7}
$$

To first order this means

$$
C^{(0)}(n,u,v,1)=0,
\qquad
C^{(1)}(n,u,v,1)=0.
\tag{7.8}
$$

A weaker sufficient condition is that the `u,v` period of the boundary value vanish.

For the integrated extension, one may deliberately retain (7.6). Then it must be proved that this boundary vector lies in the neutral submodule and gives the desired off-diagonal class. It cannot simply be discarded.

Notice that differentiation of `t^epsilon` at `t=1` contributes no logarithmic endpoint term because `log 1=0`. Catalan's constant comes from the bulk logarithmic moment, while the `t=1` boundary controls how that moment is coupled into the rank-three system.

# 8. Why the Q4838 phase does not have the four Delannoy saddles

For

$$
\Psi(u,v)=\frac{uv}{1-u^2v^2},
$$

one computes

$$
\partial_u\log\Psi
=\frac{1+u^2v^2}{u(1-u^2v^2)},
$$

$$
\partial_v\log\Psi
=\frac{1+u^2v^2}{v(1-u^2v^2)}.
$$

Thus the stationary equations reduce to

$$
u^2v^2=-1,
$$

or

$$
uv=\pm i.
$$

The critical set is non-isolated. It cannot directly generate three distinct saddle products

$$
17+12\sqrt2,\quad1,\quad17-12\sqrt2.
$$

The standard Delannoy constant-term carrier is instead

$$
\phi(u)=3+\sqrt2(u+u^{-1}),
$$

$$
\Phi(u,v)=\phi(u)\phi(v),
$$

$$
\widetilde h_{n,\varepsilon}
=\Phi(u,v)^n\frac{t^\varepsilon}{uv(1+t^2)}.
\tag{8.1}
$$

Its four product critical points are

$$
(u,v)=(1,1),(1,-1),(-1,1),(-1,-1),
$$

and the three distinct critical values are exactly

$$
(3+2\sqrt2)^2=17+12\sqrt2,
$$

$$
(3+2\sqrt2)(3-2\sqrt2)=1,
$$

$$
(3-2\sqrt2)^2=17-12\sqrt2.
$$

Therefore the modular screening should first be rerun with the correct isolated-saddle carrier, unless an exact transformation from the Q4838 kernel to (8.1) is supplied.

# 9. The extension class in twisted de Rham language

Let `D` denote the relative twisted-divergence operator at epsilon zero. The zeroth certificate says

$$
\mathcal L(P^{(0)})\in\operatorname{im}D.
$$

The first jet says

$$
\mathcal L(P^{(1)})
-D(X^{(1)})
=\frac1tX_t^{(0)}.
$$

Thus the extension is represented by the relative cohomology class

$$
\boxed{
\left[\frac1tX_t^{(0)}\right]
=\left[(1+t^2)C^{(0)}\right].
}
\tag{9.1}
$$

To identify this as the Catalan class one must prove three things.

1. It is nonzero modulo admissible twisted divergences and boundary coboundaries.
2. Its image under the matrix recurrence lies in the root-one neutral direction with formal index `-3`.
3. Its period under `-partial_epsilon at epsilon=0` equals `G` with the exact challenge normalization.

This is the precise mathematical content of “the connection matrix contains Catalan's constant.”

# 10. Exact proof pipeline

A complete proof can follow the proposed screening/interpolation/CRT architecture, but it must include both algebraic and analytic verification.

## Stage A: choose the correct carrier

Use either the isolated-saddle Delannoy carrier (8.1), or prove an exact contour transformation from the Q4838 kernel to it.

## Stage B: solve the zeroth and first epsilon jets together

For each `d=0,1,2,...`, build the coupled system (4.1)–(4.3). Do not solve only at epsilon zero.

## Stage C: modular screening

Use several good primes, several geometric points, and enough admissible `n` samples. Zero nullity is a rejection test. Stable positive nullity is only a reconstruction signal.

## Stage D: interpolate and reconstruct

Interpolate in `u,v,t`, then use CRT and rational reconstruction for the coefficients in `n`. Fix a canonical normalization to remove nullspace scaling.

## Stage E: exact algebraic verification

Clear all denominators and verify both jet equations identically over `Q[n,u,v,t]`.

## Stage F: boundary verification

Check:

- closed-cycle single-valuedness and absence of poles on `gamma_u,gamma_v`;
- the `t=0` valuation;
- the `t=1` homogeneous vanishing or the exact neutral boundary vector;
- uniform bounds allowing differentiation at epsilon zero.

## Stage G: evaluate the initial periods

Compute

$$
\Pi_0^{(0)}
$$

and

$$
-\Pi_0^{(1)}.
$$

The `t` moments reduce to `pi/4` and `G`; the `u,v` moments must be evaluated by residues or constant terms. Match the resulting two rows exactly to

$$
(30921,-32972,8240)
$$

and

$$
(33750,-36000,9000),
$$

up to an explicitly stated rational basis change.

Once the one-step matrix identity and the initial match hold, the equality propagates to every `n`.

## Stage H: asymptotics

Use the established Birkhoff basis to show that the `G`-valued linear form has zero dominant coefficient. The projective error is then

$$
\frac{P_{n,j}}{Q_{n,j}}-G
=\kappa_j n^{-3}(17-12\sqrt2)^n
 +\eta_j(17-12\sqrt2)^{2n}+\cdots.
$$

# 11. Correction to the proposed exact exponential formula

A variable-coefficient recurrence does not have exact solutions equal to pure exponentials. The correct exact decomposition is

$$
S_n=c_+f_+(n)+c_0f_0(n)+c_-f_-(n),
$$

where `f_+,f_0,f_-` are the canonical Birkhoff solutions.

Only asymptotically,

$$
f_+(n)\sim(17+12\sqrt2)^n,
$$

$$
f_0(n)\sim n^{-3},
$$

$$
f_-(n)\sim(17-12\sqrt2)^n.
$$

Thus a formula such as

$$
S_n=c_+G+c_0+c_-\left(\frac{c_-}{c_+}\right)^n
$$

cannot be an exact identity for the printed recurrence without additional `n`-dependent factors. The AZ certificate should identify the exact Birkhoff connection coefficient, while the simple root ratios describe only the asymptotic rate.

4. Closed `u,v` contours require no vanishing at saddles.
