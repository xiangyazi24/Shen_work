## Why `IntervalResolverWeakBounds` is not enough for this frontier

`IntervalResolverWeakBounds` is the right non-circular weak-ball foundation for `L∞` value/gradient bounds and input-difference Lipschitz estimates. It deliberately works from source `ℓ²` data and absolute convergence of the resolver value/gradient series. That level of information does not supply the spatial derivative bound needed to run the mean-value argument for Holder continuity. For the Holder component data consumed by `chemFluxLifted_holder_of_component_holder`, the already committed non-circular route is the stronger source-decay route:

```text
SourceCoeffQuadraticDecay p w
  ├─> resolverGradReal_bounded_of_sourceDecay        -- gives G
  ├─> resolverGradReal_holder_Icc_of_sourceDecay     -- gives Hg
  └─> IntervalResolverSpatialC2.resolverR_contDiff_two
        + compact derivative bound + MVT + |x-y|≤1
        └─> missing value Holder theorem              -- gives Hv
```
