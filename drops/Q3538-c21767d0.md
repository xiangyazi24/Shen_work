## Key algebraic equality

For `τ ∈ (0,T)` and spatial interior `x ∈ (0,1)`, the classical PDE gives:

```lean
liftDeriv2 u τ x =
  liftTimeDeriv u τ x
    + p.χ₀ * liftChemotaxisDivPhysicalRep p u v τ x
    - H1PhysicalLogisticReactionPart p u τ x
```

Equivalently,

```lean
liftTimeDeriv u τ x =
  liftDeriv2 u τ x
    - p.χ₀ * liftChemotaxisDivPhysicalRep p u v τ x
    + H1PhysicalLogisticReactionPart p u τ x
```

The chemotaxis representative splits as:

```lean
H1PhysicalChemTaxisPart p u v τ x
  + H1PhysicalChemUvxxPart p u v τ x
= liftChemotaxisDivPhysicalRep p u v τ x
```

Therefore:

```lean
-(∫ u_xx * u_t)
= -∫ u_xx^2
  + p.χ₀ * ∫ u_xx * (taxisPart + uvxxPart)
  - ∫ u_xx * reactPart
```

Using the physical scalar definitions, each product scalar has a leading minus sign:

```lean
H1PhysicalTaxisX p u v τ = -∫ u_xx * taxisPart
H1PhysicalUvxxX  p u v τ = -∫ u_xx * uvxxPart
H1PhysicalReactX p u τ   = -∫ u_xx * reactPart
```

So the chem terms become

```lean
p.χ₀ * ∫ u_xx * taxisPart = (-p.χ₀) * H1PhysicalTaxisX p u v τ
p.χ₀ * ∫ u_xx * uvxxPart  = (-p.χ₀) * H1PhysicalUvxxX p u v τ
```

and the total is exactly

```lean
-(lapL2sq u τ)
  + (-p.χ₀) * H1PhysicalTaxisX p u v τ
  + (-p.χ₀) * H1PhysicalUvxxX p u v τ
  + H1PhysicalReactX p u τ
```

This sign convention is the main thing to protect in review: `χ₀` appears in the PDE representative, while the H¹ route uses `(-χ₀)` times scalars that are themselves negative integrals.

## Proof-plan details for the substitution lemma

At fixed `τ`, use `intervalIntegral.integral_congr_ae` for the PDE/representative equality, not `integral_congr` over the closed interval. The existing equalities are interior-spatial equalities, and endpoint equality is not available or needed.

A robust proof shape is:

1. Establish the pointwise a.e. equality on the interval:

   ```lean
   liftTimeDeriv u τ x =
     liftDeriv2 u τ x
       - p.χ₀ *
           (H1PhysicalChemTaxisPart p u v τ x +
            H1PhysicalChemUvxxPart p u v τ x)
       + H1PhysicalLogisticReactionPart p u τ x
   ```

   For `x ∈ Ioo 0 1`, combine:
   * `liftDeriv2_eq_liftDeriv2PhysicalRHS_interior_of_classicalSolution`,
   * `lift_chemotaxisDiv_eq_liftChemotaxisDivPhysicalRep_interior`,
   * `H1PhysicalChemParts_sum_eq_liftChemotaxisDivPhysicalRep`.

2. Multiply by `liftDeriv2 u τ x`, integrate, and use `intervalIntegral.integral_congr_ae` to ignore endpoints.

3. Split the resulting integral with `intervalIntegral.integral_add`, `integral_sub`, `integral_const_mul`, and `integral_neg`/ring normalization.

4. Discharge fixed-time integrability locally from existing source facts:
   * `liftDeriv2` is interval-integrable from the `ContDiffOn ℝ 2` time slice in `hsol.regularity`.
   * `liftTimeDeriv` is continuous on a strict slab from `liftTimeDeriv_continuousOn_strictSlab_of_classicalSolution`.
   * the three non-lap parts are continuous on a strict slab from the physical classical continuity file.
   * products with `liftDeriv2` follow via `IntervalIntegrable.mul_continuousOn`.

Do not promote these fixed-time integrability facts into time-window RHS integrability assumptions; they are only proof obligations for splitting a fixed spatial integral.

## Pitfalls to reject

1. **Do not use the existential identity producer as if it gives the physical scalars.**

   ```lean
   H1EnergyIdentity_of_classicalSolution_and_H1UxxL1ContBefore
   ```

   returns `∃ taxisX uvxx reactX`; the existing construction can choose nonphysical placeholders. It proves existence of an identity value, not the concrete split needed by route-C.

2. **Do not use RHS integrability or initial majorants to prove the identity.**

   The identity is pointwise in `τ`. Consuming any of the following would be circular or at least downstream:

   ```lean
   H1IdentityRHSIntegrableBefore
   H1IdentityRHSInitialWindowIntegrableBefore
   H1EnergyDerivativeInitialWindowIntegrableBefore
   H1PhysicalRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore
   H1PhysicalRHSStrictInitialRouteBefore
   H1SupBoundSqrtRHSIntegrableBefore
   IsPaper2BoundedBefore
   ```

3. **Do not require endpoint equality for `liftDeriv2` or chemotaxis representatives.**

   The available physical equalities are on `x ∈ Ioo 0 1`. Use a.e. interval-integral congruence. The endpoint mismatch is measure-zero and should not become an analytic assumption.

4. **Do not use the parametric physical identity wrapper directly.**

   ```lean
   H1PhysicalRHSIdentityBefore_of_parametric_and_substitution
   ```

   is for a derivative value of the form `∫ ux * uxt`. The finite-difference route gives `-∫ uxx * u_t`. Unless an additional IBP/substitution equality is supplied, this is the wrong entry point.

5. **Watch orientation of the substitution equality.**

   The clean wrapper wants:

   ```lean
   H1IdentityRHSValue ... τ = -(∫ uxx * u_t)
   ```

   so that the `HasDerivAt` value from `H1energy_hasDerivAt_of_uxxL1Cont` rewrites directly to the concrete physical RHS value.
