# X₁(18), the elliptic quotient over ℚ(ζ₉)⁺, and a no-Chabauty Lean plan

## Executive verdict

The proposed conclusion is correct only after separating three logically different statements.

1. `rank J(ℚ) = 0` implies that `J(ℚ)` is finite only after invoking Mordell–Weil finite generation, or after replacing rank zero by the stronger already-known statement `J(ℚ) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ`. It then implies that `C(ℚ)` is finite through an injective Abel–Jacobi map. It does not, by itself, identify the rational points.
2. Finiteness of `E₀(L)` is a separate assertion. In the classical Mordell–Weil framework it follows from `rank E₀(L) = 0`, but it does not follow from `rank J(ℚ) = 0`. Ranks can grow after base change, and the elliptic quotient exists only over `L`.
3. Once one has the exact group

$$
E₀(L) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ
$$

and a total degree-two morphism `q₊ : C_L → E₀`, the fiber method is a valid finite non-Chabauty proof: at most `21 · 2 = 42` geometric slots must be checked. The phrase “finite explicit set” nevertheless hides the need to enumerate all 21 target points, prove the fiber table complete in every projective chart, and filter the resulting `L`-points for `ℚ`-rationality.

For the assumptions stated in the question, there is an even shorter endpoint. Since `J(ℚ) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ`, reduction at `p = 5` is injective on `C(ℚ)`, while `C(𝔽₅)` consists of exactly the six reductions of the cusps. Thus the elliptic quotient and its 42 fibers are not needed at all to prove that `C(ℚ)` is the set of six cusps. This proof is also entirely non-Chabauty.

The exact arithmetic answer is

$$
E₀(L) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ,  rank E₀(L) = 0.
$$

Here `E₀/ℚ` is LMFDB `162.c3`, old Cremona label `162b1`. Over `ℚ`, one has `E₀(ℚ) ≅ ℤ/3ℤ`. The degree-three isogenous companion over `L` has rank zero and torsion `ℤ/7ℤ`.

Among the two tasks in Question 4, the total projective quotient plus complete fiber table is the larger Lean bottleneck. The local formal-group argument is more delicate mathematically than an individual polynomial identity, but it is a small, localized, reusable adapter. The quotient/fiber block crosses several APIs that Mathlib does not currently provide: a smooth projective genus-two point model, total rational maps across poles, point-level changes of Weierstrass variables, degree-two quotient semantics, and exhaustive number-field fiber certificates.

# 1. Exact logical chain: what rank zero does and does not prove

## 1.1 The direct Jacobian route

Fix a rational cusp `P₀ ∈ C(ℚ)`. There is an Abel–Jacobi map

$$
ι₍P₀₎ : C(ℚ) → J(ℚ),  P ↦ [P - P₀].
$$

For a smooth projective curve of positive genus, this map is injective. Indeed, if

$$
[P - P₀] = [Q - P₀],
$$

then `P - Q` is principal. If `P ≠ Q`, a function with divisor `P - Q` would give a degree-one map `C → ℙ¹`, forcing `C` to have genus zero. Hence `P = Q`.

The classical chain is therefore

```text
rank J(ℚ) = 0
+ Mordell–Weil finite generation for J(ℚ)
        ⇒ J(ℚ) is finite
        ⇒ C(ℚ) injects into a finite set
        ⇒ C(ℚ) is finite.
```

Under the stronger input in the question,

$$
J(ℚ) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ,
$$

one does not need the abstract rank-to-finiteness step at the endpoint: finiteness and an exponent are already explicit.

What does not follow is equality with the six cusps. An injection

$$
C(ℚ) ↪ ℤ/21ℤ
$$

only gives `#C(ℚ) ≤ 21`. One must still determine which of the 21 classes lie in the Abel–Jacobi image. Possible methods include:

* inverting the Abel–Jacobi map class by class;
* a Mordell–Weil sieve;
* one good reduction prime at which the six known points exhaust the residue curve;
* the elliptic quotient and all of its fibers;
* Chabauty–Coleman.

Thus Chabauty is unnecessary, but finiteness alone is not a point classification.

There is also a formalization caveat. If “rank zero” is represented merely as

```text
J(ℚ) ⊗ℤ ℚ = 0
```

for an arbitrary abelian group, it does not imply finiteness: an infinite torsion group also has rank zero. The arithmetic implication uses Mordell–Weil finite generation. The concrete statement `J(ℚ) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ` avoids that issue completely.

## 1.2 The elliptic-quotient route uses a different rank

Let

$$
L = ℚ(ζ₉)⁺ = ℚ(a),  a³ - 3a - 1 = 0,
$$

and let

```text
q₊ : C_L → E₀
```

be the degree-two quotient morphism defined over `L`.

For every rational point `P ∈ C(ℚ)`, base change gives `P_L ∈ C(L)`, and then

```text
q₊(P_L) ∈ E₀(L).
```

If `E₀(L)` is finite, then

$$
C(ℚ) ⊆ C(L) ⊆ ⋃₍T ∈ E₀(L)₎ q₊⁻¹(T).
$$

A nonconstant morphism between smooth projective curves is finite. Since `deg q₊ = 2`, every geometric fiber has length two counted with multiplicity. Consequently

$$
#C(L) ≤ 2 · #E₀(L)
$$

whenever `E₀(L)` is finite. With `E₀(L) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ`, this gives at most 42 geometric slots.

The needed implication is therefore

```text
explicit total degree-two q₊ : C_L → E₀
+ exact finite enumeration E₀(L) = {T₀, …, T₂₀}
+ complete computation of each q₊⁻¹(Tᵢ)
+ test which resulting L-points descend to ℚ
        ⇒ complete list of C(ℚ).
```

The statement `rank J(ℚ) = 0` is not part of this chain. It does not imply `rank E₀(L) = 0`.

The reason is structural. The map `q₊` induces a nonzero homomorphism

```text
(q₊)₍*₎ : J_L → E₀.
```

The relevant Mordell–Weil group is therefore `J(L)`, not `J(ℚ)`. Rank can increase under a number-field extension. Moreover, `ℚ`-simplicity of `J` only rules out an elliptic quotient defined over `ℚ`; it does not prevent `J_L` from splitting, or acquiring an elliptic quotient, after base change. In this construction the extra involutions are defined over `L`, and the two elliptic quotients are designed to give an `L`-isogeny decomposition of `J_L` up to a power of 2. None of that forces the new `L`-rational Mordell–Weil rank to be zero merely because the `ℚ`-rank is zero.

A second formalization caveat is important:

```text
[21] E₀(L) = 0
```

by itself does not imply that `E₀(L)` is finite as an abstract group. A bounded-exponent group can be infinite. One needs either Mordell–Weil finite generation or a separate finite-cardinality argument. The project’s MW-finite-generation-free route supplies the latter by controlling the 3-primary quotient and injecting the 7-primary part into a seven-element reduction group.

## 1.3 The shortest no-Chabauty endpoint: reduction at p = 5

For the model used in the project,

$$
C : y² = f(x),
$$

where

$$
f(x) = x⁶ + 4x⁵ + 10x⁴ + 10x³ + 5x² + 2x + 1.
$$

The six known rational points are

$$
(-1, ±1),  (0, ±1),  ∞₊,  ∞₋.
$$

There are four affine points and two points at infinity.

At `p = 5`, the sextic is squarefree, so the curve has good reduction. As a function on `𝔽₅`, Fermat’s relation `x⁵ = x` gives

$$
f(x) = x² + x + 1.
$$

The values for `x = 0,1,2,3,4` are

```text
1, 3, 2, 3, 1.
```

The only squares among these are the two occurrences of `1`, at `x = 0` and `x = 4 = -1`. Hence

$$
C(𝔽₅) = {(0, ±1), (-1, ±1), ∞₊, ∞₋},
$$

and `#C(𝔽₅) = 6`.

Now suppose `P,Q ∈ C(ℚ)` have the same reduction modulo 5. Then

$$
D = [P - Q] ∈ J(ℚ)
$$

reduces to zero in `J(𝔽₅)`. Since `J(ℚ)` is killed by 21, one has `21D = 0`. For an abelian variety with good reduction in residue characteristic 5, the reduction kernel has no nonzero torsion of order prime to 5. Thus the order of `D` is simultaneously a divisor of 21 and a power of 5, so `D = 0`. Abel–Jacobi injectivity gives `P = Q`.

Therefore

```text
C(ℚ) → C(𝔽₅)
```

is injective. The six rational cusps already map onto all six residue points, so

$$
C(ℚ) = {(-1, ±1), (0, ±1), ∞₊, ∞₋}.
$$

This proof uses neither Chabauty nor the elliptic quotient over `L`. Even the weaker annihilator `[42]J(ℚ) = 0` is enough because `5 ∤ 42`.

For Lean, the load-bearing interface is approximately:

```lean
-- A rational base point is fixed in the definition of abelJacobiQ.
abelJacobiQ_injective : Function.Injective abelJacobiQ

red_compat :
  ∀ P Q : CPointQ,
    redJ (abelJacobiQ P - abelJacobiQ Q) =
      abelJacobiF5 (redC P) - abelJacobiF5 (redC Q)

jQ_annihilated_21 : ∀ D : JQ, 21 • D = 0

redJ_kernel_no_primeToFive_torsion :
  ∀ D : JQ, redJ D = 0 → 21 • D = 0 → D = 0

redC_injective : Function.Injective redC

cF5_eq_six_cusps :
  Finset.univ = {cusp0p, cusp0m, cuspm1p, cuspm1m, infp, infm}

all_rational_points_are_cusps :
  ∀ P : CPointQ, P ∈ rationalCusps
```

If a generic prime-to-`p` torsion-injectivity theorem for the Jacobian is awkward, a concrete alternative is to enumerate the 21 elements of the already-known `J(ℚ)`, reduce them in the project’s Mumford model, and check directly that only zero reduces to zero. That is still much smaller than a 42-fiber table over a cubic number field.

# 2. The exact group E₀(L)

## 2.1 Models and labels

Use

$$
E₀ : y² + xy + y = x³ - x² - 5x + 5.
$$

Its Weierstrass coefficients are

```text
[1, -1, 1, -5, 5].
```

The label convention is easy to mishandle:

```text
LMFDB label:       162.c3
old Cremona label: 162b1
```

Old Cremona `162c3` is not the same label. The project should pin the equation, not rely on string labels in proofs.

Over `ℚ`,

$$
E₀(ℚ) ≅ ℤ/3ℤ,  rank E₀(ℚ) = 0.
$$

A rational point of order 3 is

```text
T = (1,0),  -T = (1,-2).
```

## 2.2 Base change to the real cubic field

Let

$$
L = ℚ(a),  a³ = 3a + 1.
$$

This is field label `3.3.81.1`, the real cubic subfield of `ℚ(ζ₉)`. Over `L`, the exact Mordell–Weil group is

$$
E₀(L) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ,  rank E₀(L) = 0.
$$

Thus the torsion grows from `C₃` to `C₂₁`: a new `L`-rational point of order 7 appears, and together with the rational order-3 point it gives a point of order 21.

Cremona’s cubic torsion-growth data contain the old-label entry

```text
162b1 ... [21][-1,-3,0,1]
```

where `[-1,-3,0,1]` represents `X³ - 3X - 1`, the defining polynomial of `L`.

The raw ECNF Mordell–Weil record for `3.3.81.1-8.1-a1` gives

```text
rank        0
rank bounds [0,0]
torsion     [21]
```

and identifies the curve as the base change of `162.c3`.

For a fully explicit coordinate certificate, ECNF uses the following integral `L`-model:

$$
Y² + (a²+a-1)XY + (a²-2)Y
= X³ + (-a²+a+2)X²
  + (13a²+2a-44)X - 22a²-3a+88.
$$

On that model the raw `mwdata` file records an order-21 torsion generator with affine coordinates

$$
X = 56 + 5a - 19a²,
$$

$$
Y = -377 - 44a + 131a².
$$

This coordinate is useful as a finite certificate, but the Lean development must also retain the explicit change of variables between this integral model and the pinned base change of the rational equation.

The degree-three isogenous companion `3.3.81.1-8.1-a3`, identified with the base change of `162.c4`, has

$$
E₀'(L) ≅ ℤ/7ℤ,  rank E₀'(L) = 0.
$$

Its ECNF pinned model has `(1,0)` as a generator of the order-7 torsion.

## 2.3 Database record versus proof certificate

The ECNF and LMFDB records are strong independent checks, not suitable as axioms in an axiom-free Lean proof. There are two legitimate proof architectures.

### Classical Mordell–Weil architecture

Prove or import:

```text
rank E₀(L) = 0,
E₀(L)ₜₒᵣₛ ≅ ℤ/21ℤ.
```

Mordell–Weil finite generation then gives the full group immediately.

### Project’s finite-generation-free architecture

The project’s existing route is logically sharper for Lean because it proves an exact finite group without requiring a general formalization of Mordell–Weil finite generation over number fields.

1. A 3-isogeny descent proves a finite quotient and the stronger weak-descent statement

   ```text
   E₀(L) = H₃ + 3E₀(L),
   H₃ = ⟨(1,0)⟩,
   3H₃ = 0.
   ```

2. At the prime `π = a - 1` above 3, a good integral model reduces to a seven-element group over `𝔽₃`.
3. A strict formal-group filtration rules out nonzero infinitely 3-divisible elements in the reduction kernel. Combined with weak descent and the exponent 7 of the reduction group, this yields `[21]E₀(L) = 0`.
4. The finite quotient `E₀(L)/3E₀(L)` bounds the 3-primary part by 3.
5. Prime-to-3 reduction injects the 7-primary part into the seven-element special-fiber group, bounding it by 7.
6. Hence `#E₀(L) ≤ 21`.
7. Explicit points of orders 3 and 7 give an element of order 21, so `#E₀(L) ≥ 21`.
8. Therefore `E₀(L) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ`.

This separation is important in Lean:

```text
bounded exponent       ≠ finiteness,
rank zero abstractly   ≠ finiteness,
finite quotient + finite reduction bounds ⇒ finiteness.
```

# 3. Construction of C_L → E₀ and the true content of the fiber calculation

## 3.1 The quotient is by an L-rational bielliptic involution

Write

```text
A₀ = -a - 1,
q₀ = a² - 1.
```

On the affine chart, define

$$
σ(x,y) = ((A₀x-a)/(x-A₀), q₀³y/(x-A₀)³).
$$

The matrix of the Möbius transformation on `x` squares to a scalar, and a denominator-cleared polynomial identity shows that `σ` preserves the sextic equation. Thus `σ² = 1` on the smooth projective curve. It is not the hyperelliptic involution; its quotient has genus one.

Set

```text
r₊ = A₀ + q₀ = a² - a - 2,
r₋ = A₀ - q₀ = -a² - a,
```

and introduce

$$
z = (x-r₊)/(x-r₋),
$$

$$
w = -8q₀³y/(x-r₋)³.
$$

Then

```text
σ(z,w) = (-z,w).
```

A direct identity transforms the genus-two equation into

$$
w² = c₆z⁶ + c₄z⁴ + c₀,
$$

where

```text
c₆ = 1800a² + 3384a + 960,
c₄ =  420a² +  780a + 240,
c₀ =   84a² -  132a -  48.
```

The invariant coordinate is `z²`. The raw quotient is therefore

$$
E₊ raw : Y² = X³ + c₄X² + c₀c₆²,
$$

with affine quotient coordinates

$$
X = c₆(x-r₊)²/(x-r₋)²,
$$

$$
Y = -8c₆q₀³y/(x-r₋)³.
$$

An explicit change of variables identifies this raw quotient with `E₀`. Put

```text
α₊ = (-2a²-a+9)/48,
β₊ = ( 2a²+a-9)/96,
γ₊ = ( 4a²+a-16)/192.
```

Then

```text
x₀ = α₊X + 3/2,
y₀ = β₊X + γ₊Y - 5/4
```

satisfy

$$
y₀² + x₀y₀ + y₀ = x₀³ - x₀² - 5x₀ + 5.
$$

This constructs the desired degree-two map

```text
q₊ : C_L → E₀.
```

The other involution, the hyperelliptic involution composed with `σ`, gives the second elliptic quotient used in the splitting of `J_L`.

## 3.2 Why the map must be packaged projectively

The affine formula has denominators `x-r₋`. A Lean proof must not define the map by an arbitrary default value when a denominator vanishes. The robust certificate is the homogeneous triple.

Let

```text
D = x - r₋,
```

```text
N_X = α₊c₆(x-r₊)² + (3/2)(x-r₋)²,
```

and

```text
N_Y = β₊c₆(x-r₊)²(x-r₋)
      - 8γ₊c₆q₀³y
      - (5/4)(x-r₋)³.
```

Then `q₊` is represented by

```text
[N_X D : N_Y : D³].
```

The Weierstrass equation for this triple is a denominator-free polynomial identity modulo `a³-3a-1` and `y²-f(x)`. It includes the exceptional chart `D = 0` and is the correct starting point for a total projective morphism.

The project still has to prove:

* the triple is never simultaneously zero on `C`;
* it gives a nonsingular projective point of `E₀`;
* it agrees with the affine formulas when `D ≠ 0`;
* its values at `D = 0` and at the two points at infinity are correct;
* it is invariant under `σ`;
* the induced function-field extension has degree two, or equivalently its fibers are the `{P, σP}` orbits with ramification counted twice.

The coefficient identities are routine `field_simp` and `ring_nf` computations after reducing powers of `a`. The structural packaging is not routine in current Mathlib.

## 3.3 Generic affine fibers really are quadratic

For an affine target `T = (u,v) ∈ E₀(L)`, the first quotient coordinate gives

$$
u - 3/2 = α₊c₆ ((x-r₊)/(x-r₋))².
$$

Set

$$
λ = (u-3/2)/(α₊c₆).
$$

Then `x` satisfies

$$
(x-r₊)² - λ(x-r₋)² = 0,
$$

which is a quadratic polynomial over `L`. Once `x` is known, the `y₀`-coordinate equation is linear in `y`, so `y` is determined. This is the elementary algebra behind the degree-two fibers.

Thus, after all 21 points of `E₀(L)` are explicitly listed, the generic computation is finite:

```text
21 target points
× one quadratic over L
× zero, one, or two L-roots
× one linear recovery and check for y.
```

## 3.4 What the phrase “just compute the fibers” hides

The computation contains no Chabauty and no infinite search, but it is not a one-line formal argument. A complete certificate must handle all of the following.

1. **Coordinate enumeration of E₀(L).** An abstract equivalence `ZMod 21 ≃+ E₀(L)` is not enough for substitution unless its 21 values have computable exact coordinates. One may hard-code the multiples of an order-21 generator and verify them.
2. **Non-split fibers.** A quadratic fiber over an `L`-rational target may have no `L`-rational point. Completeness therefore requires a proof that its discriminant is not a square in `L`, not merely failure to find a root.
3. **Ramification.** A double root is one geometric point of multiplicity two and must occupy a controlled slot.
4. **The elliptic origin.** The target point at infinity is not covered by the affine equation for `λ`.
5. **Poles and exceptional source charts.** The fiber containing `x = r₋`, together with source points at infinity, must be handled through homogeneous coordinates.
6. **Vanishing leading coefficients.** Specialization can turn the displayed quadratic into a linear or constant equation; completeness must branch on this explicitly.
7. **Projective equality.** Point equality cannot be reduced blindly to affine coordinate equality in exceptional charts.
8. **Descent from L to ℚ.** The final list is initially a list of `L`-points. Each candidate must be tested for fixedness under `Gal(L/ℚ)`, or directly shown to have rational coordinates in the selected chart.
9. **Cusp identification.** The six rational candidates must be connected to the modular cusp predicate through the Tate-normal-form bridge, not merely recognized by their coordinates.

A good Lean interface is a 42-slot table:

```lean
abbrev FiberSlot := ZMod 21 × Fin 2

structure DegreeTwoFiberTable where
  pointAt : FiberSlot → Option CPointL
  sound : ∀ s P,
    pointAt s = some P → qPlus P = e0Enum s.1
  complete : ∀ P : CPointL,
    ∃ i : Fin 2, pointAt (e0Enum.symm (qPlus P), i) = some P

structure RationalFiberCertificate extends DegreeTwoFiberTable where
  rationalAt : FiberSlot → Option CPointQ
  rational_sound : ...
  rational_complete : ∀ P : CPointQ,
    ∃ s, rationalAt s = some P
```

Once the 42 entries and their proofs are concrete, the last equality with the six-cusp finset can indeed be a finite `decide` or `native_decide` theorem. The hard part is producing the complete table, not checking the final Boolean equality.

# 4. Lean and Mathlib difficulty ranking

## 4.1 A notation warning about the formal group

Here `Ê₀` denotes the formal completion of `E₀` at the identity, while `E₀'` denotes the 3-isogenous companion. The project has sometimes used `Ehat0` for the companion, so these must not be conflated in declarations.

The full reduction kernel at the prime above 3 is not torsion-free. The rational order-3 point reduces to the identity because the special-fiber group has order 7, so nonzero 3-torsion occurs in the first formal neighborhood. A correct local statement is either:

* torsion-freeness of a sufficiently deep subgroup such as `Ê₀(𝔪²)`, with the depth proved explicitly; or
* the strict separatedness statement used in the project, with the branch `[3]P = 0` treated separately.

An unqualified theorem saying that the entire formal kernel is torsion-free would be false here.

## 4.2 Why the local formal-group argument is comparatively contained

Let `K = L_π`, `π = a - 1`, and normalize `v(π) = 1`. From

$$
π³ + 3π² - 3 = 0
$$

one gets `v(3) = 3`. For the formal parameter `t = -x/y`, the universal one-dimensional formal-group expansion has the form

$$
[3](T) = 3T + T²A(T),  A(T) ∈ 𝒪_K[[T]].
$$

For `z ∈ 𝔪_K`, whenever `[3]z ≠ 0`,

$$
v([3]z) ≥ min(3+v(z), 2v(z)) ≥ v(z)+1.
$$

This is the core two-page local argument. Its Lean difficulty is concentrated in one adapter layer:

* construct the Weierstrass formal parameter;
* connect the reduction kernel to formal-group points in `𝔪_K`;
* prove that the linear coefficient of the multiplication series is 3;
* justify evaluation of integral power series;
* translate Mathlib’s multiplicative valuation ordering into a natural-valued filtration.

Mathlib has generic formal groups, power series, number-field completions, valuations, and reduction of Weierstrass equations. It does not presently supply a ready `WeierstrassCurve.formalGroup` or the point-level exact sequence

```text
0 → formal kernel → E(K) → E(k).
```

That missing adapter is real work, but it is localized and reusable. Once it exists, the valuation argument is small and stable.

## 4.3 Why the geometric middle is larger

The quotient/fiber route needs several independent project-level constructions for which Mathlib has little or no high-level support:

* a smooth projective model of an even-degree genus-two hyperelliptic curve with two infinity points;
* extension of a rational involution across its affine pole;
* total morphisms to projective Weierstrass points;
* point-level maps induced by `WeierstrassCurve.VariableChange`;
* proof that the map is the quotient by an involution and has degree two;
* exact arithmetic in the cubic field for 21 target points;
* square and nonsquare certificates for specialized quadratic fibers;
* a complete 42-slot projective table;
* descent of candidates from `L` to `ℚ`.

Each polynomial identity is elementary, but the interfaces cross algebraic geometry, number fields, projective coordinates, finite computation, and descent. The proof is long because completeness requires every exceptional case, not because the underlying mathematics is conceptually deep.

## 4.4 Practical ranking

Assuming the exact group `E₀(L) ≅ C₂₁` has already been proved, the implementation ranking is:

| Rank | Block | Lean risk |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Total projective `C_L → E₀`, degree-two semantics, and complete 42-fiber and rationality table | Highest; many missing structural APIs and exceptional charts |
| 2 | Elliptic formal-group and reduction-kernel adapter at `π ∣ 3` | Medium-high but localized and reusable |
| 3 | Strict valuation or deep-subgroup torsion-free lemma after the adapter | Low-medium; short power-series and valuation argument |
| 4 | Final finite checks after certificates exist | Low; `decide` or `native_decide` |

For the whole project, however, proving `E₀(L) ≅ C₂₁` from first principles may be harder than either comparison item. The 3-isogeny descent requires explicit isogenies, global cube classes, local images, and a proof that the finite Selmer calculation gives the needed weak-descent equality rather than merely an upper bound on a dimension. Database rank data do not discharge that in Lean.

## 4.5 Recommended architecture

### Preferred endpoint, given J(ℚ) ≅ C₂₁

Do not build the 42-fiber table merely to classify `C(ℚ)`. Use the `p = 5` reduction proof.

```text
A. Define the six projective cusps.
B. Prove good reduction of C at 5 by a finite gcd certificate.
C. Enumerate C(𝔽₅) and prove it is exactly the six cusp reductions.
D. Use [21]J(ℚ)=0 and prime-to-5 torsion injectivity in the reduction kernel.
E. Deduce that red_C is injective.
F. Conclude that C(ℚ) is exactly the six cusps.
```

This endpoint needs no Chabauty, no elliptic quotient, no `E₀(L)` enumeration, and no square tests over `L`.

### Quotient endpoint, if an independent L-elliptic proof is desired

```text
B1. Construct L = ℚ[a]/(a³-3a-1), with a robust normalizer.
B2. Construct σ on the smooth projective C and prove σ²=1.
B3. Construct q₊ by the homogeneous triple and prove it is degree two.
B4. Prove E₀(L) ≃+ ZMod 21, not merely [21]E₀(L)=0.
B5. Export exact coordinates for all 21 points.
B6. Specialize the denominator-cleared quadratic fiber equation 21 times.
B7. Prove a complete 42-slot table, including empty and ramified fibers.
B8. Filter by Gal(L/ℚ)-fixedness and check that the rational list is the six cusps.
```

This route is still a valid proof without Chabauty. It is best viewed as a large finite certificate, not as an automatic consequence of rank zero.

# 5. Direct answers to the four questions

## Question 1

**Yes, Chabauty is logically unnecessary under the stated exact arithmetic inputs. No, `rank J(ℚ) = 0` alone is not the same as what the elliptic-fiber route needs.**

* `rank J(ℚ) = 0` plus Mordell–Weil gives finiteness of `J(ℚ)`, hence finiteness of `C(ℚ)` through Abel–Jacobi. It does not identify the points.
* The fiber route separately needs `E₀(L)` finite, equivalently rank zero over `L` in the classical Mordell–Weil framework, and in practice needs the exact group and all of its points.
* Rank over `ℚ` does not control rank after base change to `L`.
* With `E₀(L) = C₂₁` and a total degree-two `q₊`, the finite-fiber exhaustion is valid.
* With the stronger given statement `J(ℚ) = C₂₁`, the one-prime `p = 5` proof is shorter and avoids `q₊` entirely.

## Question 2

$$
E₀(L) ≅ ℤ/21ℤ,  rank E₀(L) = 0.
$$

Over `ℚ`, `E₀(ℚ) ≅ ℤ/3ℤ` and has rank zero. Over `L`, a 7-torsion point appears, so the torsion becomes cyclic of order 21. The 3-isogenous companion over `L` has torsion `C₇` and rank zero.

## Question 3

The map is the quotient by an explicit non-hyperelliptic involution defined over `L`. After a Möbius change, the involution is `z ↦ -z`, so the invariant `z²` gives a genus-one quotient. An explicit Weierstrass change identifies that quotient with `162.c3`.

The generic fiber is a quadratic equation over `L`, so the calculation is genuinely finite elementary algebra. It nevertheless hides substantial completeness work: projective poles, the target origin, ramification, nonsplit quadratics, exact enumeration of all 21 target points, and descent back to `ℚ`. There is no hidden Chabauty, but there is a large exact algebraic certificate.

## Question 4

Between the two proposed blocks, the geometric middle is the Lean bottleneck. The local formal-group proof has one missing elliptic and reduction adapter but then reduces to a short valuation inequality. The quotient/fiber route requires several absent algebraic-geometric interfaces and a complete 42-case number-field table.

If `J(ℚ) = C₂₁` is already available, the best engineering decision is to avoid the geometric middle and finish with reduction at 5. If the purpose is to formalize the independent elliptic-quotient proof itself, budget the quotient/fiber block as the larger project.
