# 1. Jensen strict positivity

## 1.1 Does the mathematics require `u > 0` in order to prove `u > 0`?

**No, provided the proof is genuinely weak and uses the correct low-regularity coefficient package.** Strict positivity need not bootstrap from itself.

On a buffered positive-time interval, write the equation in exact drift-reaction form. With

\[
W=\frac{R_x}{(1+R)^\beta},
\]

the expansion of the chemotaxis divergence gives, up to the repository's diffusion normalization,

\[
u_t=\nu_u u_{xx}+B u_x+C u,
\]

with

\[
B=-\chi_0 W,
\qquad
C=a-bu^\alpha-\chi_0 W_x.
\]

The term `−χ₀ W_x` is load-bearing. Taking only `C = a - b u^α` leaves a sign-indefinite residual and does not represent the PDE. Likewise, the drift cannot be absorbed into a scalar reaction discount.

For the weak comparison with a squared heat barrier

\[
w(t,x)=e^{-M(t-s)}\bigl(S_\nu(t-s)f\bigr)(x)^2,
\]

one needs:

- `u ≥ 0` and a nontrivial nonnegative seed `f` with `f² ≤ u(s)`;
- an energy-class weak equation for `u`;
- `u(t,·) ∈ H¹` or a stronger Lipschitz package on positive-time windows;
- bounded `B` and a lower bound for `C`;
- the Neumann traces needed by the weak comparison;
- the chain rule for `(w-u)₊` and a Gronwall estimate;
- strict positivity of the Neumann heat semigroup applied to nonzero nonnegative data.

None of these requires a positive lower bound for `u`.

## 1.2 The real-power issue at the Jensen stage

For `γ ≥ 1`, boundedness and nonnegativity are enough for the **first-order Sobolev chain rule**. On `[0,M]`,

\[
|r^\gamma-q^\gamma|
  \le \gamma M^{\gamma-1}|r-q|.
\]

Thus `r ↦ r^γ` is Lipschitz on the relevant bounded nonnegative range, including at zero, and

\[
u u\in H^1,\quad 0\le u\le M
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
u u^\gamma\in H^1.
\]

The elliptic resolver then gives enough low-order regularity to bound `W` and `W_x`. In one dimension, the schematic chain is

\[
u u\in H^1
\Longrightarrow u^\gamma\in H^1
\Longrightarrow R\in H^3
\Longrightarrow W\in H^2
\Longrightarrow W,W_x\in L^\infty.
\]

The denominator is harmless because resolver nonnegativity gives `1 + R ≥ 1`; no positive floor for `u` is needed.

A Lean implementation must use a Lipschitz/Sobolev composition theorem for `(positivePart r)^γ`, not blindly invoke a classical `Real.rpow` derivative theorem whose convenient branch hypothesis is `r ≠ 0`. Requiring `u ≠ 0` merely because of the chosen calculus lemma would reintroduce a formal circularity that is not mathematically necessary.

## 1.3 What is actually missing in the current Jensen layer

The present adapters have the following logical shapes:

```lean

    ...
    (hpos : ∀ t x, ... → 0 < u t x)
    : TruncatedJensenStrictPosDataFor ...
```

and

```lean

    ...
    (hlower : ReactionDiscountedMildLowerOn ...)
    : TruncatedJensenStrictPosDataFor ...
```

The first assumes the result. The second assumes the load-bearing lower comparison. Neither is the weak comparison producer requested in the question.

There is also a prior shared-C² route in which Jensen regularity is obtained from

```lean
mildSlice_contDiffOn_two_neumann S hsrcB hB_restart
```

That theorem explicitly depends on the source `DuhamelSourceTimeC1` and the restart spectral representation. If a strict-positivity proof consumes it, the dependency is

```text
strict positivity
  ← C² regularity
  ← hsrcB / HSpectral source ladder
  ← positive floor from strict positivity,
```

which is circular.

### Jensen verdict

- **Mathematically:** the weak route can close from `u ≥ 0`; no self-use of `u > 0` is necessary.
- **Formally at present:** the required weak `ReactionDiscountedMildLowerOn` producer is a genuine missing theorem unless it exists in a separate module not consumed by the current adapters.

# 2. Energy nonnegativity

## 2.1 Can it avoid a classical time derivative and HSpectral?

**Yes mathematically, but not all time regularity disappears.** The correct weak statement is something like

\[
u u\in L^2(a,b;H^1),
\qquad
u u_t\in L^2(a,b;H^{-1}),
\]

with the truncated PDE holding in the variational sense. Testing by `-u₋` gives

\[
\frac12\frac{d}{dt}\|u_-(t)\|_2^2
  +\nu_u\|(u_-)_x(t)\|_2^2
\le \ell\|u_-(t)\|_2^2.
\]

The chemotaxis term vanishes because

\[
u u_+\,(u_-)_x=0\quad\text{a.e.}
\]

and the reaction has the required one-sided bound. A Steklov/Lions-Magenes chain rule then supplies the time derivative of the negative-part energy. This does **not** require pointwise `u_t`, spatial `C²`, or HSpectral.

But it does require:

- the weak semigroup-to-PDE identity;
- admissibility of `u₋` as an `H¹` test;
- the Sobolev chain rule for positive and negative parts;
- a weak time derivative or an equivalent Steklov argument;
- energy continuity/absolute continuity;
- the initial trace `u(t) → u₀` strongly enough to force `‖u₋(t)‖₂ → 0`;
- continuity of positive-time spatial slices to upgrade a.e. nonnegativity to pointwise nonnegativity.

These are analytic components, not mere record wiring.

## 2.2 The current V6 energy file exposes a circular endpoint

The advertised target is

```lean

  ∀ ...,
    ∀ C : UniformConjugateMildExistenceCore p u₀,
```

Two facts follow immediately:

1. The mild fixed-point core `C` and the truncated map certificate `A` are prior dependencies omitted from the four-piece list.
2. The energy producer is expected to construct the full energy core **without** already knowing nonnegativity.

The terminal constructor currently present is instead

```lean

    (DT : TruncatedConjugateMildExistenceData p u₀)
```

Its fields are then zero certificates because `u₋ = 0`. This is legitimate as a compatibility adapter after nonnegativity, but it is not the proof of nonnegativity.

The separate theorem

```lean

```

proves nonnegativity from an independently constructed energy core. Therefore the missing direction is precisely:

```text
mild fixed point + weak form + Sobolev/Steklov energy regularity
    → TruncatedNegativePartEnergyCoreRegularData
```

without an `hnonneg` input.

Until that producer is present, the chain

```text

```

has only its first arrow missing; using the last constructor for the first arrow is circular.

# 3. The spatial ladder and its base

## 3.1 The first pass is not “the free heat part” alone

The landed seed

```lean
windowCoefficientEnvelope_zero_of_bound
```

is honest:

\[
|u_k(t)|\le 2M
\]

on each positive-time window. It is only `WindowCoefficientEnvelope 0`.

The nonlinear source engine is typed only for `m ≥ 1`:

```lean
SourceFromSolutionEnvelopePass S :=
  ∀ {m}, 1 ≤ m →
    WindowCoefficientEnvelope m ... →
    WindowSourceEnvelope (m - 1) ...
```

Thus `m=0` cannot feed it.

A first `0 → 1` step must regularize the **whole mild solution**, not just

\[
S(t)u_0.
\]

The Duhamel part contains the chemotaxis gradient kernel. Controlling its first spatial derivative requires a genuine closure estimate. In the current truncated bootstrap this is the long short-window argument producing

```lean
truncatedPicardLimit_lipschitzOn_positive_time
```

through locally contracting gradient windows. It is not a one-line heat-semigroup observation.

So the base split can be acyclic, but it should be stated as:

```text
WCE 0 from boundedness
  + independent positive-time full-solution Lipschitz theorem
  → WCE 1,
```

not “the free heat term is smooth, therefore the mild solution is WCE 1.”

## 3.2 The decisive flaw in `WCE m → WSE (m−1)`

Suppose the envelope only says

\[
|u_k|\le Ck^{-m}.
\]

Then

\[
\sum_k (1+k^2)^s|u_k|^2
\lesssim
\sum_k k^{2s-2m}
\]

converges only when

\[
s<m-\tfrac12.
\]

The model sequence `u_k=k^{-m}` proves the endpoint failure: it satisfies the pointwise `m`-decay envelope, while its `H^m` sum is comparable to `∑1` and diverges.

Therefore these implications are invalid without extra hypotheses:

```text
WCE m  ⇒  u ∈ H^m
WCE m  ⇒  bounded m-th derivative
WCE m  ⇒  the H^m Moser estimate for u^γ
WCE m  ⇒  source ∈ H^{m-1}
```

The comment in `SourceFromSolutionEnvelopePass` combines an `H^r` argument with a pointwise coefficient envelope as though they were equivalent at the same index. They are not.

If one starts from WCE `m`, chooses any `s < m - 1/2`, and applies the valid Sobolev bookkeeping, the chem-divergence source is at best in `H^{s-1}`. That is below `H^{m-1}` by more than one half derivative. The claimed exact pointwise decay order does not follow.

## 3.3 The direct IBP proposal also needs stronger input

For

\[
s_k=\int_0^1 S(x)\cos(k\pi x)\,dx,
\]

two integrations by parts give

\[
s_k=-\frac1{(k\pi)^2}
  \int_0^1 S''(x)\cos(k\pi x)\,dx
\]

only when

\[
S'(0)=S'(1)=0
\]

and `S''` is integrable or bounded. Thus quadratic decay requires a weak `H²_N` or a classical `C²`-Neumann package for the **source**.

A generic order-`q` IBP argument requires:

- derivatives of `S` through order `q`;
- integrability or uniform bounds for the last derivative;
- all relevant endpoint terms to vanish. For even `q=2j`, this includes
  `S'(0)=S'(1)=0`, `S'''(0)=S'''(1)=0`, ..., through order `2j-1`;
- an even/cosine representative or equivalent Neumann compatibility. The zero extension `intervalDomainLift` is not a globally smooth representative and cannot silently supply these endpoint identities.

None of that is stored in `WindowCoefficientEnvelope m`.

For the actual representation target `WindowSourceEnvelope 2`, a clean sufficient route is

\[
u u\in H^3,\quad u\ge\delta>0
\Longrightarrow S_{\rm chem}\in H^2_N
\Longrightarrow |s_k|\lesssim k^{-2}.
\]

But WCE 3 does **not** imply `u ∈ H³`; it gives only `H^s` for `s<5/2`. WCE 4 does imply `H³` after choosing `3<s<7/2`, assuming the envelope is uniform and the realization/Parseval bridge is available. This illustrates that the previously quoted final input `WCE 3 → WSE 2` is not justified by the current envelope definition.

There are several valid repairs:

1. Replace WCE by a stronger weighted-ℓ¹ derivative envelope, for example
   `Summable (fun k => (1+k)^m * |u_k|)`.
2. Carry a `WindowHSigmaEnvelope r` with enough margin, such as `r ≥ m` or `r > m+1/2` depending on the desired classical derivative.
3. Carry explicit `WindowCkBounds` plus endpoint compatibility and use the direct IBP route.
4. Weaken the output index to the amount actually implied by WCE, accepting a fractional loss and iterating in a consistent Sobolev scale.

Until one of these is done, `SourceFromSolutionEnvelopePass` is not merely unproved; its current signature is analytically under-specified.

## 3.4 The conservative `α < 1` ladder is a different scale

The repository's `MemHSigma` Duhamel engine proves a gain

\[
H^r\to H^{r+\alpha},\qquad 0<\alpha<1.
\]

That finite iteration is sound inside the `H^s` scale. With initial index `s₀`, a sufficient number of passes is any `N` satisfying

\[
s_0+N\alpha>s_*.
\]

However, this cannot be substituted silently into an integer WCE induction. To obtain WCE `m` from `H^s`, one needs a separate coefficient estimate and enough margin; a safe pointwise estimate is

\[
|u_k|\le (1+k^2)^{-s/2}\|u\|_{H^s},
\]

so reaching WCE `m` requires `s ≥ m`, while reaching classical `C^j` by Sobolev embedding requires `s>j+1/2` in one dimension.

Thus “approximately `4/α` passes” is meaningful only after fixing the target in the same `H^s` scale. It does not prove the current `WindowCoefficientEnvelope 4` statement by itself.

## 3.5 HSpectral has more than one leaf

The current reduced spectral input record is

```lean
structure BFormMildSpectralLeaves ... where
  hsrcB             : DuhamelSourceTimeC1 (bFormSourceCoeffs p S.u)
  hB_restart        : RestartRepresentation S
  hlogData          : ... LogisticCosineFourierData ...
  hchemData         : ... ChemDivCosineFourierData ...
  hResolverWitness  : ResolverSourceWitnessFromMild p S
```

Therefore even a correct `SourceFromSolutionEnvelopePass` does not by itself produce HSpectral. One must also discharge:

- the physical restart cosine representation;
- logistic Fourier inversion data;
- chem-divergence Fourier inversion data and the correct closed-interval representative;
- the resolver source witness.

Some of these may become short corollaries once strong source envelopes are available, but they are logically distinct and should appear in the dependency inventory.

### Ladder verdict

- `WCE 0` is a valid seed.
- The first full-solution Lipschitz/C¹ step is a separate substantial theorem.
- The generic source-pass signature is currently under-hypothesized.
- The integer pass count is not valid until that interface is repaired.
- The fractional `H^s` ladder terminates in finitely many passes, but it is not definitionally the WCE ladder.

# 4. Time differentiation is not a small per-mode corollary

The per-mode ODE

\[
c_k'(t)=-\nu\lambda_kc_k(t)+s_k(t)
\]

is necessary but not sufficient to conclude

\[
\partial_tu(t,x)=\sum_kc_k'(t)\cos(k\pi x).
\]

For a fixed compact interior window `[a,b]`, one needs a **single summable modewise majorant**

\[
|c_k'(t)|\le M_k,
\qquad
\sum_k M_k<\infty,
\qquad t\in[a,b].
\]

A typical choice is

\[
M_k=\nu A_k+E_k,
\]

where

\[
\lambda_k|c_k(t)|\le A_k,
\qquad
|s_k(t)|\le E_k
\]

uniformly on the window and both envelopes are summable. Pointwise summability at each time is not enough; neither is a scalar bound

\[
\sup_t\sum_k\lambda_k|c_k(t)|<\infty.
\]

The M-test needs the stronger coordinatewise summable envelope

\[
\sum_k\sup_t\lambda_k|c_k(t)|<\infty
\]

or an explicitly constructed dominating sequence.

This is why the current target

```lean
MildSolutionSliceHasDerivAtTime S
```

asks not merely for per-mode differentiability, but for:

- an actual spatial function `udot`;
- `HasDerivAt (fun r => S.u r x)` at every interior time and point;
- continuity of each spatial `udot t` slice;
- a uniform bound for `udot` on every compact interior time window.

The proof must also handle:

- the zero mode separately;
- local uniform convergence of the derivative series;
- equality of the coefficient synthesis with the physical mild solution;
- continuity of the source coefficients before differentiating the Duhamel integral;
- the subsequent chain rule that turns `u_t` into time derivatives of the logistic and chemotaxis source coefficients.

There is a valid acyclic staging:

```text
source C⁰ in time + spatial summable envelopes
    → coefficient ODE
    → locally uniform derivative series
    → u_t
    → source C¹ in time
    → DuhamelSourceTimeC1.
```

But that is a real theorem, not just `rw [coeff_ode]`.

# 6. Real exponents: exact fault line

For arbitrary real `γ ≥ 1`:

### Before strict positivity

The following are valid on bounded nonnegative ranges:

- continuity of `u^γ`;
- local Lipschitz continuity of `r ↦ r^γ` on `[0,M]`;
- the first-order Sobolev chain rule `H¹ → H¹`;
- time differentiation at the weak/first-order level, with a dedicated zero-compatible theorem.

Thus real `γ` does not force a Jensen cycle.

### After strict positivity

On a compact positive-time window, continuity and strict positivity give

\[
u u(t,x)\ge\delta>0.
\]

Then every real-power map needed in the source is smooth on `[δ,M]`, and all higher-order Moser and classical chain rules become ordinary composition estimates.

### What fails without the floor

For noninteger `γ`, the `j`-th derivative of `r^γ` behaves like

\[
r^{\gamma-j}.
\]

It is not bounded near zero when `j>γ`, and it may fail to extend continuously at zero. Therefore arbitrary high-order spatial source estimates cannot be stated for all real `γ ≥ 1` from nonnegativity alone.

The same warning applies to the logistic real exponent `α` when high spatial derivatives of `u^{α+1}` or `u^α` are used. The post-Jensen floor resolves both.

A separate formal subtlety is `positivePart`: it is Lipschitz but not classically differentiable at zero. Before strict positivity, all identities involving `u₊` must be Sobolev/a.e. chain-rule identities. Replacing them by classical pointwise derivative formulas at the zero set is invalid.

# Final answer to the five questions

## 1. Jensen

**It can close at `u ≥ 0` and need not assume `u > 0`.** The first-order `u^γ ∈ H¹` chain rule is valid for bounded nonnegative `u` and `γ ≥ 1`. But Jensen also needs independent positive-time `H¹`/Lipschitz regularity, the exact coefficient `C = a - b u^α - χ₀ W_x`, an all-test weak comparison theorem for `(w-u)₊`, and a nontrivial barrier seed. The current adapters do not prove the lower comparison. A route through shared C² regularity would be circular.

## 2. Energy

**It need not depend on HSpectral**, but it does need weak time regularity/Steklov chain rules and an independent positive-time Lipschitz theorem. The current direct file has not yet closed the advertised producer: its final energy-core constructor assumes nonnegativity. This is a genuine missing direction, not wiring.

## 3. Ladder base

`WCE 0` is unconditional. The first `0 → 1` step is not supplied by smoothing the homogeneous heat term alone; it needs a full-solution positive-time gradient/Lipschitz theorem. Such a short-window theorem is present and is independent of HSpectral. The later `WCE m → WSE(m−1)` induction, however, is under-hypothesized because WCE `m` does not imply the required `H^m` or derivative bounds. The claimed finite pass count is therefore not yet justified for the current types.

## 4. Other forgotten hypotheses

Yes: the full/truncated map certificates, mild fixed-point construction, initial trace, faithful-truncation/full-solution bridge, restart representation, Fourier inversion data, resolver witness, and final uniform continuation/gluing layer are outside the four named pieces. Some are banked, but at least the map certificate is an explicit argument of the current energy target and must be accounted for.

## 5. Real `γ`

There is no extra obstruction at the first-order Jensen/energy level beyond using the correct Lipschitz Sobolev chain rule. At higher order, noninteger real powers are not smooth at zero; the post-Jensen uniform floor is essential. The same issue applies to real `α` in high-order logistic derivatives. Positive-part differentiation at zero must remain weak/a.e. until strict positivity is known.

# Bottom line

**The four-piece decomposition is not yet a complete Lean reduction.** It is best described as a plausible high-level strategy with the following genuine outstanding seams:

```text
E1  construct the variational energy core without assuming nonnegativity;
J1  prove the weak squared-heat lower comparison without hsrcB/C²;
R1  strengthen or repair SourceFromSolutionEnvelopePass's input type;
T1  prove local-uniform termwise time differentiation and source TimeC1;
F0  explicitly discharge the fixed-point/map-certificate and restart/leaf inputs.
```

After those corrections, the architecture is mathematically acyclic. In its current four-item form, however, it omits real analytic content and—if the existing `of_nonneg` energy constructor or shared-C² Jensen route is used in the wrong direction—contains an actual circular proof path.
