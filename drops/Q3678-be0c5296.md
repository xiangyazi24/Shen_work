The first two helper theorems are pure interval-integral congruence; no summability or source regularity hypotheses are needed.  The main theorem does one `tsum_congr` and uses Task218.  This is exactly the missing algebraic bridge between true-source restart and profile-source `hSpectralAgree`.

## Precise missing hypotheses if the transport theorem alone is not enough

The transport theorem is enough to convert true-source restart to profile-source restart, but it does **not** produce the inputs needed to call the weak restart theorem.  A later constructor still needs:

* `hœá0 : p.œá‚ÇÄ = 0`;
* fixed-point/mild equation on the needed window, derivable from `D.hmild` when `s ‚â§ D.T`;
* `hu‚ÇÄ_cont` and `hu‚ÇÄ_bound` for the initial data coefficients;
* a weak source package
  ```lean
  DuhamelSourceL1ContOn
    (fun s k => cosineCoeffs (logisticLifted p (D.u s)) k) D.T
  ```
  or inputs for `picardLimitRestart_cosineIdentity_of_iterateData`;
* `hLc`, continuity of `logisticLifted p (D.u s)` for positive-time slices;
* `hprofile`, the profile/true-lift agreement on `œÉ ‚àà [0,t/2]`.

Among these, `hprofile` is the true next analytic/geometric premise.  It is not a field extraction from existing `PicardIterateConvergenceData`: it says the chosen globally-C¬≤ profile is actually a representative of the true slice on `[0,1]` over a time interval.  That is where the restart/profile construction must pay its debt.
