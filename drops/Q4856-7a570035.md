# Problem 2.7: the advertised sequence cannot be recovered from the stated data

Let

\[
\widetilde d_n=
\sum_{j=0}^{n}\binom nj^2\binom{n+j}{j}
\sum_{m=0}^{n-j}\binom{n-j}{m}\binom{j+m}{m}.
\tag{0.1}
\]

The exact audit has a clear outcome:

1. Formula (0.1) defines
   \[
   \boxed{\widetilde d_n=1,4,46,676,11506,212584,\ldots,}
   \tag{0.2}
   \]
   not \(1,5,43,491,6693,\ldots\).
2. I found **no certifiable OEIS match** for the exact string
   \(1,5,43,491,6693\), nor for the tail \(5,43,491,6693\), in the
   OEIS-indexed searches, the challenge repository, or the associated
   project notes. The negative search is not a mathematical proof that no
   OEIS entry exists, but it means that no A-number or published closed form
   should be asserted.
3. Five values do not determine a sequence, a minimal P-recursive operator,
   or a modular form. In fact, infinitely many **order-one P-recursive**
   sequences have exactly those five initial values.
4. The claimed weighted sum cannot be checked to 30 digits because the
   intended \(d_n\) has not been defined beyond five terms. For the only
   sequence that *is* defined—the nested sum (0.1)—the weighted series
   provably diverges because its individual terms tend to infinity in
   modulus.
5. The genuine Cooper level-11 sequence starts
   \[
   1,4,28,268,3004,36784,\ldots,
   \]
   so it is not the advertised sequence either. The level-11 connection of
   the printed Problem 2.7 recurrence is a **spectral/operator connection**,
   not an identification with \(1,5,43,491,6693,\ldots\).

Thus there is not yet a “correct formula” to report. The source statement
contains at least two independent omissions or transcription errors: one in
the binomial expression or initial values, and another in the normalization
of the outer series.

---

# 1. OEIS audit

The exact lookup to repeat manually is:

- [OEIS search for `1,5,43,491,6693`](https://oeis.org/search?q=1%2C5%2C43%2C491%2C6693)
- [OEIS search for `5,43,491,6693`](https://oeis.org/search?q=5%2C43%2C491%2C6693)

No indexed result was found in this audit. This is unsurprising as a matter
of identification strength: the OEIS search guidance recommends entering
about six terms **after omitting an ambiguous initial term or two**. Here
only four nontrivial terms follow the initial \(1\).

More importantly, several nearby famous sequences show that the first two
terms \(1,5\) are not enough to identify an Apéry family.

## 1.1 Apéry's \(\zeta(3)\) numbers

The classical Apéry numbers are

\[
A_n=\sum_{r=0}^{n}\binom nr^2\binom{n+r}{r}^2
=1,5,73,1445,33001,\ldots,
\tag{1.1}
\]

and satisfy

\[
\boxed{
(n+1)^3A_{n+1}
=(2n+1)(17n^2+17n+5)A_n-n^3A_{n-1}.}
\tag{1.2}
\]

They agree with the advertised sequence only at \(n=0,1\).

## 1.2 Apéry's \(\zeta(2)\) numbers

The quadratic Apéry sequence is

\[
B_n=\sum_{r=0}^{n}\binom nr^2\binom{n+r}{r}
=1,3,19,147,1251,\ldots,
\tag{1.3}
\]

with recurrence

\[
\boxed{
(n+1)^2B_{n+1}
=(11n^2+11n+3)B_n+n^2B_{n-1}.}
\tag{1.4}
\]

This is the classical level-5 Zagier/Apéry sequence, not a level-11
sequence.

## 1.3 Cooper's level-11 four-term sequence

The self-starting level-11 sequence in Cooper's classification satisfies

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
(n+1)^3T_{n+1}={}&
2(2n+1)(5n^2+5n+2)T_n\\
&-8n(7n^2+1)T_{n-1}
+22n(2n-1)(n-1)T_{n-2},
\end{aligned}}
\tag{1.5}
\]

with \(T_0=1\). At \(n=0\), the recurrence forces \(T_1=4\), and it gives

\[
\boxed{T_n=1,4,28,268,3004,36784,476476,\ldots.}
\tag{1.6}
\]

Therefore \(1,5,43,491,6693\) is neither the classical Apéry sequence nor
Cooper's level-11 sequence.

# 2. Why five terms cannot determine the requested recurrence

The finite differences of

\[
1,5,43,491,6693
\]

are

\[
\begin{array}{c|rrrrr}
&1&5&43&491&6693\\ \hline
\Delta&4&38&448&6202\\
\Delta^2&34&410&5754\\
\Delta^3&376&5344\\
\Delta^4&4968.
\end{array}
\]

Hence, for every integer \(c\), the polynomial

\[
\boxed{
P_c(n)=1+4\binom n1+34\binom n2+376\binom n3
      +4968\binom n4+c\binom n5}
\tag{2.1}
\]

has

\[
P_c(0),\ldots,P_c(4)=1,5,43,491,6693.
\tag{2.2}
\]

But

\[
P_c(5)=28961+c,
\tag{2.3}
\]

so the sixth term is arbitrary. Moreover, the sequence \(u_n=P_c(n)\)
satisfies the order-one polynomial-coefficient recurrence

\[
\boxed{P_c(n)u_{n+1}-P_c(n+1)u_n=0.}
\tag{2.4}
\]

Thus even the extra statement “the sequence is P-recursive of order at most
three” does not make the continuation unique. A recurrence, generating
function, binomial summand, modular parametrization, or substantially more
terms must be supplied.

This is why fitting an operator to only the five advertised numbers would
not be a mathematical identification: it would merely select one of
infinitely many possible continuations.

# 3. Exact audit of the nested binomial formula

For the literal definition (0.1), write

\[
I(a,j)=\sum_{m=0}^{a}\binom am\binom{j+m}{m}.
\tag{3.1}
\]

The first rows decompose as follows:

| \(n\) | contributions indexed by \(j\) | \(\widetilde d_n\) |
|---:|---|---:|
| 0 | \(1\) | \(1\) |
| 1 | \(2+2\) | \(4\) |
| 2 | \(4+36+6\) | \(46\) |
| 3 | \(8+288+360+20\) | \(676\) |
| 4 | \(16+1600+7020+2800+70\) | \(11506\) |
| 5 | \(32+7200+79800+106400+18900+252\) | \(212584\) |

The discrepancy is already visible at \(n=1\):

\[
\widetilde d_1
=\left(\binom10+\binom11\right)
 +\binom11^2\binom21
=2+2=4.
\tag{3.2}
\]

No indexing shift fixes this: the next literal values are \(46,676,11506\),
not \(43,491,6693\).

## 3.1 Rigorous divergence of the literal weighted series

At \(j=n\), the inner sum has only \(m=0\). That single nonnegative summand
is

\[
\binom nn^2\binom{2n}{n}\binom00\binom n0
=\binom{2n}{n}.
\tag{3.3}
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{\widetilde d_n\geq\binom{2n}{n}.}
\tag{3.4}
\]

It follows that

\[
\left|\frac{(-1)^n\widetilde d_n}{(n+1)^2}\right|
\geq \frac{\binom{2n}{n}}{(n+1)^2}
\sim \frac{4^n}{\sqrt\pi\,n^{5/2}}
\longrightarrow\infty.
\tag{3.5}
\]

Consequently the summands do not tend to zero, and

\[
\boxed{
\sum_{n\geq0}\frac{(-1)^n\widetilde d_n}{(n+1)^2}
\text{ diverges.}}
\tag{3.6}
\]

This rules out the displayed nested formula independently of any recurrence
or modular interpretation.

# 4. The advertised five values do not support the claimed numerical limit

Using only the listed values, the partial sums that are actually defined are

\[
\begin{aligned}
S_1&=1,\\
S_2&=1-\frac54=-\frac14,\\
S_3&=-\frac14+\frac{43}{9}=\frac{163}{36},\\
S_4&=\frac{163}{36}-\frac{491}{16}=-\frac{3767}{144},\\
S_5&=-\frac{3767}{144}+\frac{6693}{25}
     =\frac{869617}{3600}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{4.1}
\]

Numerically,

\[
S_1,S_2,S_3,S_4,S_5
=1,-0.25,4.527777\ldots,-26.159722\ldots,241.560277\ldots,
\tag{4.2}
\]

whereas

\[
\zeta(2)+\zeta(3)
=2.84699097000782072187215332815747517998\ldots.
\tag{4.3}
\]

These five partial sums are not a proof of divergence for an **undefined**
continuation, but they show that there is no 30-digit verification to
perform from the data given. The values \(d_n\) for \(n\ge5\) have not been
defined.

There is also a structural warning. Every standard positive Apéry or
level-11 denominator sequence above grows exponentially. Multiplication by
only \((n+1)^{-2}\) cannot make its terms tend to zero. In a genuine Apéry
construction one normally sums a recessive companion, a normalized error
term, or a hypergeometric summand containing an exponential/factorial
denominator—not the exponentially growing denominator sequence itself.

The outer factor

\[
\frac{(-1)^n}{(n+1)^2}
\]

also carries only weight two by itself:

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{(-1)^n}{(n+1)^2}
=\eta(2)=\frac{\pi^2}{12}.
\tag{4.4}
\]

A \(\zeta(3)\) contribution requires an additional logarithmic derivative,
harmonic number, cubic denominator, or equivalent mixed-period mechanism.
It is not generated merely by “implicit factorial structure” in a positive
integer coefficient.

# 5. What the level-11 statement really means

Cooper's level-11 construction is modular. One convenient normalization uses

\[
Z(q)=\left(\sum_{r,s\in\mathbb Z}q^{r^2+rs+3s^2}\right)^2,
\qquad
X(q)=\frac{\eta(\tau)^2\eta(11\tau)^2}{Z(q)},
\tag{5.1}
\]

and expands

\[
Z(q)=\sum_{n\ge0}T_nX(q)^n.
\tag{5.2}
\]

The resulting \(T_n\) is exactly (1.5)--(1.6). The quadratic form
\(r^2+rs+3s^2\) has discriminant \(-11\), explaining the level-11
arithmetic.

For the printed Problem 2.7 CMF recurrence, the normalized Poincaré cubic is

\[
F(\mu)=4\mu^3-220\mu^2+8\mu-1.
\tag{5.3}
\]

If

\[
H_{11}(t)=t^3-20t^2+56t-44
\tag{5.4}
\]

is Cooper's level-11 spectral cubic, then

\[
\boxed{
16F\!\left(\frac{(t-2)^2}{4}\right)
=-H_{11}(t)H_{11}(4-t).}
\tag{5.5}
\]

This is an exact and meaningful level-11 signature. But it is only a
quadratic map of the **asymptotic spectra**. It does not identify arbitrary
integer coefficients with \(T_n\), and it does not supply the missing
normalization of the claimed scalar series.

The challenge repository's current Problem 2.7 proof likewise describes the
large four-term CMF recurrence and this level-11 spectral signature; it does
not contain the nested formula or the sequence \(1,5,43,491,6693\).

Thus the answer to part (d) is:

\[
\boxed{
\text{No coefficient-level }\Gamma_0(11)\text{ or }\Gamma_1(11)
\text{ identification of }1,5,43,491,6693\text{ is presently established.}}
\tag{5.6}
\]

# 6. The most likely classes of transcription error

The available evidence does not select one correction uniquely, but it
narrows the possibilities.

## Possibility A: the intended sequence was Apéry's \(\zeta(3)\) sequence

Then the formula should have been (1.1), and the values should be

\[
1,5,73,1445,33001,\ldots.
\]

This repairs the first two terms but **does not** repair the outer sum: the
Apéry numbers grow exponentially, so
\(A_n/(n+1)^2\not\to0\).

## Possibility B: the intended sequence was Cooper's level-11 sequence

Then the recurrence should have been (1.5), and the values should be

\[
1,4,28,268,3004,\ldots.
\]

Again the proposed outer sum would diverge.

## Possibility C: a normalizing factor was omitted

A convergent identity could involve something of the schematic form

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}
\frac{(-1)^n d_n}{(n+1)^2 R^n},
\qquad
\sum_{n\ge0}
\frac{(-1)^n}{(n+1)^2d_n},
\qquad
\text{or}
\qquad
\sum_{n\ge0} E_n,
\tag{6.1}
\]

where \(R\) exceeds the coefficient growth rate or \(E_n\) is a recessive
linear form. A factorial/Pochhammer factor may also have been dropped.
Nothing in the five listed values determines which normalization was
intended.

## Possibility D: a harmonic or parameter-derivative term was omitted

A mixed \(\zeta(2)+\zeta(3)\) identity naturally contains, for example,

\[
\frac{1-\tfrac12\log(xy)}{1-xy}
\tag{6.2}
\]

or a discrete equivalent involving \(H_n/(n+1)^2\). Such an extension term
would explain the mixed weight; the bare factor \((-1)^n/(n+1)^2\) does not.

# 7. Reproducibility code

The following exact Python code distinguishes the three nearby sequences
and prints all partial sums discussed above.

```python
from fractions import Fraction
from math import comb


def nested_term(n: int) -> int:
    return sum(
        comb(n, j) ** 2
        * comb(n + j, j)
        * sum(
            comb(n - j, m) * comb(j + m, m)
            for m in range(n - j + 1)
        )
        for j in range(n + 1)
    )


def apery_zeta3(n: int) -> int:
    return sum(
        comb(n, r) ** 2 * comb(n + r, r) ** 2
        for r in range(n + 1)
    )


def cooper_level11(N: int) -> list[int]:
    """Return T_0,...,T_N from Cooper's exact recurrence."""
    if N < 0:
        return []
    T = [1]
    if N == 0:
        return T

    # The recurrence is self-starting; use T_{-1}=T_{-2}=0.
    tm2 = 0
    tm1 = 0
    t = 1
    for n in range(N):
        num = (
            2 * (2*n + 1) * (5*n*n + 5*n + 2) * t
            - 8 * n * (7*n*n + 1) * tm1
            + 22 * n * (2*n - 1) * (n - 1) * tm2
        )
        den = (n + 1) ** 3
        assert num % den == 0
        nxt = num // den
        T.append(nxt)
        tm2, tm1, t = tm1, t, nxt
    return T


literal = [nested_term(n) for n in range(6)]
apery = [apery_zeta3(n) for n in range(5)]
level11 = cooper_level11(6)

print('literal nested sum :', literal)
print('Apery zeta(3)      :', apery)
print('Cooper level 11    :', level11)

assert literal == [1, 4, 46, 676, 11506, 212584]
assert apery == [1, 5, 73, 1445, 33001]
assert level11 == [1, 4, 28, 268, 3004, 36784, 476476]

advertised = [1, 5, 43, 491, 6693]
S = Fraction(0)
for k, dk in enumerate(advertised):
    S += Fraction((-1) ** k * dk, (k + 1) ** 2)
    print(f'S_{k+1} = {S} = {float(S):.15g}')
```

Output:

```text
literal nested sum : [1, 4, 46, 676, 11506, 212584]
Apery zeta(3)      : [1, 5, 73, 1445, 33001]
Cooper level 11    : [1, 4, 28, 268, 3004, 36784, 476476]
S_1 = 1 = 1
S_2 = -1/4 = -0.25
S_3 = 163/36 = 4.52777777777778
S_4 = -3767/144 = -26.1597222222222
S_5 = 869617/3600 = 241.560277777778
```

# 8. Final answer to the four requested tasks

**(a) OEIS/formula.** No exact OEIS match was found, and no closed form can
be inferred from five terms. The sequence is not the classical Apéry
\(\zeta(3)\) sequence and not Cooper's level-11 sequence.

**(b) Recurrence.** There is no uniquely determined recurrence. Formula
(2.1) exhibits infinitely many order-one P-recursive continuations with the
same five initial terms. The exact recurrence must come from the corrected
source statement.

**(c) Numerical limit.** A 30-digit verification is not defined because
terms after \(d_4\) are unspecified. The first five exact partial sums are
(4.1) and oscillate from \(-26.16\ldots\) to \(241.56\ldots\). For the
literal nested formula, the series rigorously diverges by (3.5).

**(d) Level 11.** The established level-11 object is Cooper's sequence
(1.5), and the printed Problem 2.7 recurrence has the exact spectral relation
(5.5). Neither fact identifies the advertised five integers. A corrected
recurrence or generating function is required before any
\(\Gamma_0(11)\)/\(\Gamma_1(11)\) coefficient interpretation can be made.

The minimum source correction needed is one of:

1. the exact recurrence plus enough initial conditions;
2. the corrected binomial/hypergeometric summand;
3. at least 10--20 exact terms together with the missing outer
   normalization;
4. the modular parametrization \(Z(q)=\sum d_nX(q)^n\).

Without one of these, claiming a specific formula, OEIS entry, recurrence,
or \(\zeta(2)+\zeta(3)\) evaluation would be fabrication.
