# Paper 3 Theorem 2.1(1), general-`m` positive-logistic branch: Claim 1 audit

Repository audited at `ebee3d3d`.

## Bottom line

The literal inference

```text
late time + small spatial minimum + a uniform spatial Hölder modulus
  ⇒ small whole-slice supremum
```

is false.  The committed `IntervalDomainGlobalTailHolderM.lean` gives exactly the spatial precompactness needed for a contradiction argument, but it does **not** identify a cluster profile that touches zero with the zero profile.  That identification needs one additional parabolic ingredient: either

1. a concrete tail time-translate/closed-semiflow theorem plus a strong minimum principle, or
2. an equivalent concrete tail Harnack estimate.

The mass/logistic identity does not supply that missing implication.  It is useful only after one has proved that the mass tends to zero, or for comparing the mass at two times a bounded distance apart.

Accordingly, the shortest honest next compilable milestone is the purely spatial theorem

```lean
mass of tail slices → 0  ⟹  sup norm of the same tail slices → 0
```

using the already committed uniform Hölder modulus and slice Arzelà–Ascoli theorem.  It is unconditional, concrete to `intervalDomainM`, non-vacuous, and requires no `CompactnessData` field.  The genuinely new PDE frontier is then isolated as

```lean
small minimum along a tail sequence ⟹ mass tends to zero,
```

or, more canonically, “a tail cluster profile which has a zero is identically zero.”

## 1. Why Hölder continuity alone is insufficient

For a fixed interval coordinate `x ∈ [0,1]`, consider

```text
fₙ(x) = 1/(n+1) + x.
```

Every `fₙ` is positive and has the same Lipschitz constant `1`, hence the same Hölder modulus for every exponent at most one.  Nevertheless,

```text
min_[0,1] fₙ = 1/(n+1) → 0,
sup_[0,1] fₙ = 1 + 1/(n+1) → 1,
∫₀¹ fₙ = 1/2 + 1/(n+1) → 1/2.
```

Thus even “uniform Hölder + a positive limiting mass” is compatible with a minimum tending to zero and a nonzero supremum.  Slice precompactness only produces, after a subsequence, the nonzero limit `f(x)=x`, which touches zero at the endpoint.

This is exactly the bad logical step that must not appear in Lean:

```lean
-- FALSE
have hsup0 : Tendsto (fun n => intervalDomainSupNorm (u (t n))) atTop (𝓝 0) :=
  holder_compactness_only hmin0
```

A PDE orbit rules out the profile `x ↦ x` as a tail limit only through a time-dependent strong-positivity argument.  Nothing in a same-time Hölder estimate does so.

## 3. Exact mass/logistic audit

Let

```text
M(t) = intervalDomainM.integral (u t).
```

For an actual classical `intervalDomainM` solution, the Neumann boundary condition cancels both the diffusion flux and the faithful `u^m` chemotactic flux.  The committed mass identity reduces to

```text
M'(t) = a M(t) - b ∫₀¹ u(t,x)^(1+α) dx.
```

The identity is independent of `m`, `χ₀`, and `β`; these parameters occur only in the cancelled spatial flux.  If an eventual bound `0 ≤ u ≤ K` is fixed, it gives the useful window comparisons

```text
(a - b K^α) M(t) ≤ M'(t) ≤ a M(t).
```

Consequently, on every fixed time window of length `τ`, masses at the two endpoints are comparable by constants depending on `a`, `b`, `K`, and `τ`.

What it does **not** give is any estimate of the form

```text
M(t) ≤ Φ(min_x u(t,x)),       Φ(r) → 0 as r → 0,
```

or

```text
sup_x u(t,x) ≤ Φ(min_x u(t,x)).
```

The explicit profiles above already disprove such a purely spatial inference.  Jensen's inequality goes in the other useful direction: it yields an upper logistic inequality for `M`, not a reverse estimate from a minimum.  The elliptic identity for `v` likewise controls an integral or a resolver norm from `u`; it does not turn one low value of `u` into a low whole slice.

Therefore the mass identity cannot be cited as the proof of Claim 1.  It can support a Harnack/translate proof by transporting a mass-small conclusion through a bounded time window.

## 6. How to prove the genuinely missing parabolic step

The shortest sound analytic route is qualitative tail zero-rigidity, not an
attempt to squeeze the spatial profile with Hölder continuity.

Take a bad sequence `tₙ → ∞` with

```text
min u(tₙ,·) → 0,
sup u(tₙ,·) ≥ ε₀.
```

1. Use the committed tail-slice compactness twice and diagonalize, first at
   `tₙ` and then at `tₙ-τ`, for a fixed small `τ>0`.  This gives uniformly
   convergent pairs of slices without any package field.
2. Prove closedness of the concrete `intervalDomainM` time-`τ` solution map
   under those uniform limits, using the existing restart/mild formulation and
   concrete uniqueness.  Equivalently, prove compactness of the translated
   solutions on `[-τ,0]×[0,1]`.
3. The limit is a nonnegative classical solution on a nontrivial backward time
   interval.  Its final slice touches zero.
4. Expand the faithful PDE as a linear equation in the limit `U`:

   ```text
   U_t - U_xx + A(t,x) U_x = B(t,x) U.
   ```

   With `m ≥ 1`, the factor `U^(m-1)` in `A` and `B` is bounded at zero; there
   is no singular coefficient.  Boundedness of the original solution and the
   concrete elliptic regularity for `v` give bounded coefficients on the fixed
   cylinder.
5. Apply a concrete strong minimum principle.  Endpoint zeros must be handled:
   the spatial minimum is over the closed interval, so an interior-only theorem
   is insufficient.  Reflect the Neumann solution across `0` or `1`, or prove
   the Neumann boundary version directly.
6. A nonnegative solution which touches zero at the final positive time is the
   zero solution on the preceding cylinder.  Hence its final slice is zero,
   contradicting the retained supremum lower bound `ε₀`.

This proves `intervalDomainM_tail_cluster_eq_zero_of_exists_eq_zero`.  No mass
identity is required for this direct version.  If the implementation instead
returns “the earlier slice has zero mass,” use the mass window comparison and
the mass-to-sup milestone above to transport uniform smallness to the crossing
time.

A quantitative Harnack theorem is an equivalent replacement:

```lean
∃ τ C T, 0 < τ ∧ 0 < C ∧
  ∀ t ≥ T,
    intervalDomainM.supNorm (u (t - τ)) ≤
      C * intervalDomainSpatialMin u t.
```

Together with a fixed-window upper propagation estimate for the sup norm, this
gives the same-time Claim 1.  Proving such a Harnack estimate from the concrete
Neumann kernel is longer than the qualitative closed-translate/strong-minimum
route unless an Aronson-type lower bound is already present.

## 7. Sequential Claim 1 and conversion to the eventual `ε`–`δ` form

Once zero-rigidity is available, first prove the sequential theorem

```lean

theorem intervalDomainM_tail_supNorm_tendsto_zero_of_spatialMin_tendsto_zero
    {p : CM2Params}
    {u v : ℝ → intervalDomain.Point → ℝ}
    (ha : 0 < p.a) (hb : 0 < p.b) (hm : 1 ≤ p.m)
    (hsol : PositiveGlobalBoundedSolution intervalDomainM p u v)
    {times : ℕ → ℝ}
    (htimes : Tendsto times atTop atTop)
    (hmin : Tendsto
      (fun n => intervalDomainSpatialMin u (times n))
      atTop (𝓝 0)) :
    Tendsto
      (fun n => intervalDomainM.supNorm (u (times n)))
      atTop (𝓝 0)
```

Then derive the eventual modulus by contradiction.  If it failed for some
`ε>0`, for each `n` choose

```text
tₙ ≥ n,
min u(tₙ,·) ≤ 1/(n+1),
sup u(tₙ,·) > ε.
```

The selected times tend to infinity and their minima tend to zero, contradicting
the sequential theorem.  This conversion is pure order/topology and should be
kept in a separate scalar lemma so that the PDE proof remains uncluttered.

The final exact target is

```lean

theorem intervalDomainM_eventually_supNorm_small_of_spatialMin_small
```

## 8. Handoff to the minimum first-crossing argument

Assume the eventual Claim 1 above.  The remainder is scalar and uses the
already committed growth chain.

1. Since `0 < p.a`, use
   `generalMMinSlopeConst_tendsto_zero_nhdsGT` to choose a positive slice
   ceiling `R` for which the chemotactic slope loss is less than, say,
   `p.a/4`.
2. Apply Claim 1 with `ε=R`, obtaining `δ₀>0` and a tail time `T`.
3. Shrink `δ₀` to `δ>0` so that

   ```text
   p.b * δ^p.α < p.a/4.
   ```

4. Suppose the spatial minimum has a first downward crossing of `δ` after `T`.
   At the crossing time `t*`, Claim 1 supplies the whole-slice ceiling needed by
   `IntervalDomainPersistenceGeneralMGrowthNearArgmin.lean`.
5. The producer in `IntervalDomainPersistenceGeneralMGrowthDini.lean` then gives
   a lower-right Dini growth bound of the form

   ```text
   D⁺ z(t*) ≥ z(t*) *
     (p.a - p.b * z(t*)^p.α - generalMMinSlopeConst p R).
   ```

   At `z(t*)=δ`, the right side is at least `(p.a/2)*δ>0`.
6. The elementary first-downward-crossing lemma gives `D⁺z(t*)≤0`, a
   contradiction.

This is exactly where the same-slice ceiling is needed.  A merely local spatial
bound near an argmin, or a small slice at an earlier time without a propagation
lemma, is not enough for the current growth producer.
