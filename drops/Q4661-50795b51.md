theorem intervalDomainM_classicalSolution_overlap_unique
    {p : CM2Params} {T₁ T₂ : ℝ}
    {u₀ : intervalDomainPoint → ℝ}
    {u₁ v₁ u₂ v₂ : ℝ → intervalDomainPoint → ℝ}

This is stronger than the requested overlap statement in three ways:

1. It does **not** assume `1 ≤ p.m`.
2. It does **not** consume an externally packaged per-subhorizon upper bound.
3. It proves both `u` and `v` equality on the whole common positive lifespan.

The positive lower and absolute upper bounds needed by the contraction are constructed internally on each compact positive-time slab. The `m ≥ 1` guard belongs to the global finite-horizon alternative/boundedness branch, not to overlap uniqueness.

Therefore:

> **Do not port the L² uniqueness chain merely to close overlap uniqueness. Use the theorem already on `main`.**

The remaining work for a faithful general-`m` Theorem 1.3 global branch is structural continuation/gluing plus the endpoint-uniform continuation input; it is no longer the overlap estimate itself.

## What `m ≥ 1` and a supplied upper bound do—and do not do

For the current mild/restart uniqueness theorem, neither is needed. On a compact positive-time slab the proof has `c > 0`; therefore every positive power appearing in the M-flux and elliptic source is Lipschitz on `[c,M]`, including `0 < m < 1` and `0 < γ < 1`.

A supplied upper bound can replace the compactly obtained upper number, but it cannot replace the positive floor when a power exponent is below one. In particular:

```text
m ≥ 1  ⇒  u ↦ u^m is Lipschitz on [0,M].
γ < 1  ⇒  u ↦ u^γ is not Lipschitz at 0.
```

So the statement “`m ≥ 1` plus an upper bound makes every nonlinear map locally Lipschitz at zero” is false. The current proof avoids that problem by deriving a floor on each compact positive-time slab.

There is a separate continuation issue near a hypothetical finite maximal horizon: compact positivity on each fixed `[s,t]` does not by itself give one floor uniform as `t ↑ Tmax`. The existing quantitative local factory

```lean
intervalDomainM_thresholdLocalExistence_positiveStrip_allExponents
```

in `ShenWork/Paper2/IntervalDomainMLocalExistenceAllExponents.lean` depends on **both** `M` and `c`. Thus finite-branch upper boundedness alone does not yet produce a uniform restart length unless one also proves either:

- an M-specific minimum-persistence theorem under `1 ≤ p.m`, or
- a genuinely upper-bound-only M-local factory whose proof handles the `γ < 1` resolver nonlinearity without a floor.

This is a continuation frontier, not an overlap-uniqueness frontier.

## Canonical mild-only continuation versus arbitrary classical uniqueness

Before the latest commits, a canonical mild-only continuation was a plausible way to avoid proving arbitrary classical overlap uniqueness. At the frozen head, that tradeoff has changed:

- arbitrary classical overlap uniqueness is already proved;
- its proof is itself based on the canonical mild/restart map;
- the remaining work is time shift, splice regularity, endpoint-uniform restart, and maximal-horizon assembly.

A canonical-only global construction would still have to prove:

1. every restarted classical branch satisfies the same pointwise mild equation;
2. canonical fixed points agree on overlapping windows;
3. the direct-limit/piecewise function is classical across every seam;
4. the restart windows do not collapse before a finite endpoint;
5. every finite restriction has the required initial trace and bounds.

Items 1 and 2 are already stronger in the current arbitrary-classical theorem. Items 3–5 are essentially the same structural continuation work required by the classical gluing route. Therefore a new canonical-only direct-limit construction is unlikely to have less Lean surface now.

The recommended use of “mild-only” is narrower:

```text
use the mild map to supply local factories and uniqueness,
then use the existing classical restart-and-splice architecture for continuation.
```

That avoids the L² port while also avoiding a second, bespoke direct-limit framework.

## False shortcuts to reject

1. **Casting an M-solution to an m=1 solution with `simpa [intervalDomainM, intervalDomain]`.**

   False. The domains share the regularity, trace, elliptic, and Neumann fields, but `chemotaxisDiv` and `crossDiffusionEnergyTerm` differ. The `pde_u` field is not definitionally the same.

2. **Using `chemFluxMLifted_diff_bound_of_pos_slice` directly as the L² flux estimate.**

   False. It is a pointwise/sup-norm Lipschitz bound in a cone distance. The L² chain needs

   ```text
   ∫ |F₁-F₂|² ≤ C * E_u,
   ```

   with the resolver value and gradient differences controlled by `E_u`.

3. **Replacing `M` by `M^m` everywhere in `CfluxQuant`.**

   False. The mass-power difference term carries `p.m * M^(p.m-1)`.

4. **Claiming `m ≥ 1` and an upper bound handle `u^γ` when `γ < 1`.**

   False. The elliptic source remains non-Lipschitz at zero. Use a genuine positive floor or prove a different nonlinear/Hölder estimate.

5. **Taking a two-sided strip as a theorem/package assumption.**

   Unnecessary for overlap and contrary to the requested non-vacuity. `intervalDomainM_u_two_sided_on_compact` constructs it. For endpoint continuation, prove minimum persistence rather than packaging it.

6. **Using the old a.e. restart identity in a pointwise contraction.**

   Use `intervalDomainM_classical_bform_restart_pointwise`.

7. **Applying positive-time restart uniqueness at `t = 0`.**

   The restart theorem requires `0 < a`. The initial seed must come from `intervalDomainM_classical_initial_u_unique_on_short` and its quantitative initial-trace argument.

8. **Assuming endpoint equality is enough to splice classical solutions.**

   It is not. The current piecewise-classical proof uses equality on an open overlap so derivatives/PDE data transfer locally across the seam.

9. **Assuming a contraction factor is `< 1`.**

   Construct the window with `exists_small_contraction_time_target`.

10. **Using an `sSup`/supremum norm without nonemptiness and attainment/boundedness.**

    The current restart proof avoids this hole by taking a maximum of a continuous difference over a compact domain.

11. **Saying `IntervalDomainL2UDifferenceEnergyFrontier` is itself m=1-specific.**

    Its data shape is generic. The solution-facing builders and final certificate wrappers are what are hard-coded.

12. **Concluding global existence from `IsPaper2BoundedBefore` alone.**

    Boundedness is an a-priori branch estimate. One still needs a non-collapsing restart time, overlap/splice, and maximal-continuation assembly.

13. **Adding `1 ≤ p.m` to the current overlap theorem.**

    It would be sound but strictly weaker and would obscure the fact that the restart proof works for every positive `m` on compact positive strips.

14. **Using the weaker `PositiveInitialDatum` in the new M overlap wrapper without proof.**

    The current faithful theorem consumes `PaperPositiveInitialDatum`, matching the source paper’s uniform positive datum. The legacy m=1 `OverlapUniqueForPID` type is not the exact M interface to copy.

## Recommended implementation decision

At commit `00d66515bb4c52bfbea14118edf476ff60df4cd2`:

```text
Overlap uniqueness: DONE.
L² general-m port: not needed for the shortest proof.
Next structural work: M time shift + M piecewise splice + M restart/glue.
Next genuine analytic continuation work: uniform positive-floor persistence
  (or a truly upper-only local factory) under the m ≥ 1 global guard.
Final feed-ins: existing all-exponent M local existence,
  existing M overlap theorem, and existing finite-branch boundedness.
```

The best route is therefore a **mild-derived arbitrary-classical overlap theorem plus a thin M-specific classical continuation layer**. It is shorter and safer than porting the full L² chain, and it remains faithful, zero-sorry at the overlap layer, and non-vacuous.
