## 1. The norm that makes the nonlinear remainder locally Lipschitz

Write perturbations around the constant equilibrium:

```text
ξ = u - uStar,
ζ = v - vStar.
```

The elliptic equation gives `ζ` from `ξ` by an elliptic resolver. At the linear level this is already encoded in the repo’s scalar per-mode growth rate:

```lean
def sigma (p : CM2Params) (uStar vStar lambdaN : ℝ) : ℝ :=
  -lambdaN +
    p.χ₀ * p.ν * p.γ *
      (uStar ^ (p.m + p.γ - 1) * lambdaN) /
        ((1 + vStar) ^ p.β * (p.μ + lambdaN)) -
    p.a * p.α
```

and stability is currently:

```lean
def LinearlyStable
    (S : SpectralData) (p : CM2Params) (uStar vStar : ℝ) : Prop :=
  ∀ n : ℕ, n ≠ 0 → sigma p uStar vStar (S.eigenvalue n) < 0
```

For the nonlinear Duhamel proof, use a coefficient Sobolev norm on the scalar perturbation after elliptic elimination:

```lean
-- schematic, not meant to compile verbatim
noncomputable def coeffHNormSq (S : SpectralData) (s : ℝ) (a : ℕ → ℝ) : ℝ :=
  ∑' k : ℕ, (1 + S.eigenvalue k) ^ s * (a k)^2
```

Use the mean-free version for the minimal/mass-constrained branch.

### Why `s > 1/2` is the key algebra threshold

In one space dimension, `H^s` is an algebra for `s > 1/2`. This is enough for the nonlinear estimate in the derivative-losing form:

```text
‖N(ξ) - N(η)‖_{H^{s-1}}
  ≤ C_R · (‖ξ‖_{H^s} + ‖η‖_{H^s}) · ‖ξ - η‖_{H^s},
```

on a small ball around the equilibrium. At zero:

```text
‖N(ξ)‖_{H^{s-1}} ≤ C_R · ‖ξ‖_{H^s}².
```

This is the right mapping because the chemotaxis term is a divergence:

```text
N_chem(ξ) = ∂x( nonlinear flux remainder ).
```

The flux itself is locally Lipschitz in `H^s`; the outer `∂x` lowers to `H^{s-1}`. The linear semigroup then recovers one derivative with a `(t-s)^(-1/2)` kernel.

The elliptic resolver is favorable:

```text
ξ ∈ H^s
⇒ ζ = R[uStar + ξ] - vStar ∈ H^{s+2}
⇒ ζ_x ∈ H^{s+1},
```

so all factors in

```text
u^m · ζ_x · (1 + vStar + ζ)^(-β)
```

are controlled by one-dimensional product/composition estimates. Around a positive equilibrium, the real powers are smooth because `uStar > 0` and the small ball keeps `uStar + ξ` away from zero. For the minimal/mass-constrained branch with a positive constant `uStar`, same point.

### If the target is `C¹` convergence

The repo’s conclusion is in `N.c1Distance`; the concrete interval versions define `c1Distance` as sup distance plus sup-gradient distance. There are two ways to bridge from the `H^s` proof to this:

* prove the fixed point in `H^s` with `s > 3/2`, so `H^s ↪ C¹` directly; or
* prove the fixed point in `H^s`, `s > 1/2`, and use a final positive-time smoothing upgrade to `C¹`.

For the current Paper 3 local-exponential statement, the first route is cleaner: choose one fixed order, for example

```text
s = 2
```

or in the repo’s fractional notation choose `σ,pNorm` with

```text
1/2 < σ < 1,
1 < pNorm,
2σ - 1/pNorm > 1.
```

That embedding gives `W^{2σ,p} ↪ C¹`. If the existing theorem quantifies over every `1/2 < σ < 1`, keep the raw `X^σ_p` API, but the concrete proof should either require the embedding inequality or split into `X^σ_p` decay plus a separate smoothing-to-`C¹` lemma.

## 2. Avoiding Henry/sectorial theory: yes, but not avoiding smoothing

The direct route is coefficient-level.

Let `a_k(t)` be the cosine coefficients of the perturbation. The stable linearized problem gives modal evolution

```text
a_k(t) = exp(σ_k t) a_k(0),
σ_k = sigma p uStar vStar λ_k.
```

In the fully coupled unreduced 2×2 presentation, replace `exp(σ_k t)` by `exp(A_k t)` and prove a uniform modal matrix bound. Either way, the required stable semigroup estimates are:

```text
‖E(t) f‖_{H^s} ≤ C0 e^{-ω t} ‖f‖_{H^s},

‖E(t) f‖_{H^s} ≤ C1 t^{-1/2} e^{-ω t} ‖f‖_{H^{s-1}}.
```

Here `ω > 0` is below the spectral gap. In a cosine implementation, these are weighted series inequalities, not abstract sectorial facts. For high modes the `λ_k` heat factor gives the `t^{-1/2}` derivative recovery; for finitely many low modes use the stable matrix bound.

Then write the perturbation mild identity:

```text
ξ(t) = E(t) ξ₀ + ∫₀ᵗ E(t-s) N(ξ(s)) ds.
```

Define the weighted bootstrap norm:

```text
Y(t) := sup_{0 ≤ r ≤ t} e^{ω' r} ‖ξ(r)‖_{H^s},
```

where `0 < ω' < ω`. The estimates give

```text
Y(t)
  ≤ C0 ‖ξ₀‖_{H^s}
    + C1 CN Y(t)^2 · sup_t ∫₀ᵗ (t-s)^(-1/2) e^{-(ω-ω')(t-s)} e^{-ω' s} ds.
```

The integral is uniformly finite; a usable bound is

```text
∫₀ᵗ (t-s)^(-1/2) e^{-(ω-ω')(t-s)} e^{-ω' s} ds
  ≤ ∫₀ᵗ (t-s)^(-1/2) e^{-min(ω-ω',ω')·(t-s or s)} ds
  ≤ C(ω,ω').
```

A simpler Lean-friendly bound is to split at `s=t/2`; both pieces reduce to elementary `∫ r^{-1/2} exp(-κ r)` or `∫ r^{-1/2}` with an exponential factor. The final fixed-point/continuity argument is the standard quadratic bootstrap:

```text
Y ≤ C0 ε + K Y².
```

Choose `ε` so `4 K C0 ε ≤ 1`, and conclude `Y ≤ 2 C0 ε`.

### Why a pure physical `H¹` Gronwall is not the recommended path

A physical `H¹` energy proof would need to differentiate the nonlinear chemotaxis divergence and control terms such as

```text
∂x [ u^m · v_x · (1+v)^(-β) ]
```

against one or two derivatives of the perturbation. That introduces the same derivative loss and product/composition bookkeeping, plus non-self-adjoint lower-order terms. It may be mathematically possible under high regularity and smallness, but it is not simpler in Lean.

The coefficient Duhamel proof is more modular:

1. linear mode decay;
2. smoothing kernel estimate;
3. nonlinear quadratic estimate;
4. weighted Gronwall/bootstrap.

No Krein-Rutman, no abstract Henry theorem, and no full sectorial calculus are needed.

## 4. What exactly to prove in the nonlinear estimate

For perturbation `w = u - uStar`, define the full reduced scalar right-hand side `F(w)` after solving the elliptic equation for `v`. Let `L` be its Fréchet derivative at `0`. Then

```text
N(w) = F(w) - Lw.
```

The estimate should be:

```lean
-- schematic
structure UnitIntervalQuadraticRemainderBounds (p : CM2Params) : Prop where
  quadratic :
    ∀ {s : ℝ}, 1 / 2 < s →
    ∀ {uStar vStar : ℝ}, 0 < uStar → 0 ≤ vStar →
      ∃ r > 0, ∃ C > 0,
        ∀ w, coeffHNorm s w ≤ r →
          coeffHNorm (s - 1) (nonlinearRemainder p uStar vStar w)
            ≤ C * (coeffHNorm s w)^2
  lipschitz :
    ∀ {s : ℝ}, 1 / 2 < s →
    ∀ {uStar vStar : ℝ}, 0 < uStar → 0 ≤ vStar →
      ∃ r > 0, ∃ C > 0,
        ∀ w z, coeffHNorm s w ≤ r → coeffHNorm s z ≤ r →
          coeffHNorm (s - 1)
            (nonlinearRemainder p uStar vStar w - nonlinearRemainder p uStar vStar z)
            ≤ C * (coeffHNorm s w + coeffHNorm s z) * coeffHNorm s (w - z)
```

The estimates use:

* `H^s` algebra for `s > 1/2`;
* elliptic resolver gain `+2`;
* smooth composition for powers near `uStar > 0` and denominator near `1+vStar > 0`;
* one outer derivative for the divergence term, hence output `H^{s-1}`.


```text
(1+λ_k)^s e^{2σ_k t}
```


```text

```

plus the corresponding high-mode smoothing. This is slightly more work algebraically, but it is still coefficient-level and avoids Henry.

## 7. Important pitfalls

### Pitfall A: pure `H¹` energy Gronwall

A direct `H¹` Gronwall can look attractive, but it mixes the derivative-loss from divergence with non-self-adjoint chemotaxis terms. It is not the shortest Lean route. The diagonal Duhamel proof isolates these into one smoothing inequality and one nonlinear quadratic estimate.

### Pitfall B: zero mode

For the nonminimal positive equilibrium with `a>0`, the zero mode is stable because the logistic derivative contributes `-aα`:

```lean
sigma_zero_neg_of_a_pos
```

For the minimal/mass-constrained branch (`a=b=0`), the zero mode is neutral and must be removed by the mass constraint. Use the mean-free/P0-complement semigroup, matching the repo’s `unitIntervalNeumannHeatSemigroupP0Compl` bridge.

### Pitfall C: confusing the linear scalar heat semigroup with the coupled/reduced linear semigroup

`IntervalDomainSectorial.lean` routes the raw orbit bound through `unitIntervalNeumannHeatSemigroupP0Compl` for the exposed decay estimate. The nonlinear orbit frontier must justify that the **actual perturbation evolution** is controlled by that stable semigroup gauge. Do not simply replace the linearized chemotaxis operator by the heat operator without proving the mode-bound comparison.

### Pitfall D: initial sup-smallness versus fractional-smallness

The current concrete interval bridge defines `xpSigmaDistance` as sup distance. That is convenient for theorem statements, but the nonlinear Duhamel proof naturally wants a fractional/Sobolev smallness norm. There are two honest options:

1. redefine or instantiate `xpSigmaDistance` with the actual fractional norm in the proof path; or
2. keep sup-smallness and prove a short-time smoothing/initial-continuity bridge that turns sup-small initial data into fractional smallness after a short restart.

The repo already separates initial-continuity frontiers elsewhere, so option 2 is consistent with the current architecture. Do not silently assume `sup small ⇒ H^s small` at time `0`; that is false without extra regularity.
