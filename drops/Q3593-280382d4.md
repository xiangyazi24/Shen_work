Audited `xiangyazi24/Shen_work` main at commit `dc26e5f0`, with focus on source-side zero-start H1 data only.


from the current Picard / heat-kernel / K1 / K2 machinery.

The strongest source-side route is close, but one core analytic theorem is still missing: a **C¹ approximate-identity theorem for the full Neumann semigroup initial leg**, uniform on `x ∈ [0,1]`:


## Why positive-window K1/K2 bounds do not imply the zero approach

`ShenWork/Paper2/IntervalCompactSliceGradientBounds.lean` explicitly localizes gradient/Hessian bounds to compact positive-time windows `[a', b'] ⊂ (0,T)`.  Its header states that a time-uniform gradient bound on all of `(0,T)` is false for merely continuous initial data because parabolic smoothing can blow up like `σ^{-1/2}` as `σ → 0+`.

Therefore the positive-window machinery can prove strict-time regularity and positive-window boundedness, but it does not give a limit at `t = 0`.  Letting `a' → 0` is precisely where the envelopes lose uniform summability.

Similarly, `ShenWork/Paper2/IntervalPicardLimitK1C2Heat.lean` constructs C2 coefficient fields from a **positive shift** `ε > 0`:

```lean
shiftedHeatCoeff_timeC1
shiftedHeatCoeff_sourceC2CoeffFields
```

Those are restart/positive-time smoothing tools.  They do not prove `∂ₓS(t)u₀ → ∂ₓu₀` at zero.

Existing `intervalFullCoupledDuhamel_grad_initial_bound` already performs the IBP and proves the L¹ bound, but it does not prove the approximate-identity limit.
