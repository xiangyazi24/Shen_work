# Q3936 — Lean implementation strategy for positive-time bounded truncated B-form source coefficients

The clean implementation path is to **black-box the positive-time spatial regularity** and keep the downstream source-coefficient theorem elementary.  Do not try to extract `W^{1,∞}` from uniform convergence of the Picard iterates alone, and do not make the final source-coefficient theorem depend on the Volterra proof internals.  Put the Volterra/smoothing analysis behind one or two named theorems with exactly the shape the coefficient argument needs.

The most useful API is:

1. a window version of the lifted-slice Lipschitz theorem;
2. a pointwise corollary at a single positive time;
3. an IBP-ready `W^{1,1}` certificate for the truncated flux;
4. a small theorem converting that flux certificate into a uniform bound for `kπ * intervalSineInner F k`;
5. the final triangle-inequality theorem for `truncatedBFormSourceCoeff`.

The key Lean design choice is that the gradient theorem should **not** expose derivatives of the subtype function.  State it for the lifted representative

```lean
intervalDomainLift (truncatedConjugatePicardLimit p u₀ DT.T t) : ℝ → ℝ
```

on `Set.Icc (0 : ℝ) 1`.

    IntervalIntegrable (fun y : ℝ => |deriv F y|) volume (0 : ℝ) 1

    ∀ᵐ y ∂volume, y ∈ Set.Ioo (0 : ℝ) 1 → HasDerivAt F (deriv F y) y

                (truncatedConjugatePicardLimit p u₀ DT.T) s k| := by

              (truncatedConjugatePicardLimit p u₀ DT.T) s k| :=
              abs_sub_le _ _ _

              (truncatedConjugatePicardLimit p u₀ DT.T) s k|
            + |p.χ₀| * |truncatedChemDivSourceCoeff p
              (truncatedConjugatePicardLimit p u₀ DT.T) s k| := by
              rw [abs_mul]
      _ ≤ Clog + |p.χ₀| * Cchem := by
              gcongr
              · exact hClog k
              · exact hCchem k

## Why the window Lipschitz theorem is the right black box

The pointwise theorem is enough for `truncatedBFormSourceCoeff_bound_positive_time`, but the theorem you actually want to state as an analytic frontier is the window theorem:

```lean
∃ G : ℝ≥0, ∀ t ∈ Set.Icc a T',
  LipschitzOnWith G
    (intervalDomainLift (truncatedConjugatePicardLimit p u₀ DT.T t))
    (Set.Icc (0 : ℝ) 1)
```

This matches the real proof: for any positive window `[a,T']`, the Volterra singularity is integrable and the constants are uniform on that window.  Then the single-time result follows by taking `[t/2,t]`.  This avoids needing a special one-off estimate at the final source-bound theorem.

The small-time contraction condition behind the black box is:

```text
B₁ * Θq(δ) < 1,
```

where `B₁` is the coefficient multiplying the previous iterate's gradient in the source/flux Lipschitz estimate, and

```text
Θq(δ) = ∫₀^δ q(τ) dτ
```

for the gradient-kernel Volterra weight `q`.  In the usual heat-gradient case `q(τ) ≲ Cq * τ^{-1/2}`, so `Θq(δ) ≲ 2*Cq*sqrt δ`; hence any

```text
δ < (1 / (2 * B₁ * Cq))^2
```

is small enough, up to the exact constants in your kernel estimate.  To cover a larger positive-time window `[a,T']`, subdivide it into finitely many intervals of length at most `δ` and iterate the estimate.

## Practical recommendation

Use the `UnitIntervalW11IBPBound` package rather than exposing a bare derivative bound in the final theorem.  It gives you a stable boundary between the hard analysis and the algebraic coefficient proof.  Later, if you replace the `sorry` proof by a more canonical mathlib absolute-continuity theorem, only the constructor of `UnitIntervalW11IBPBound` changes; the chemDiv and B-form source coefficient theorems stay unchanged.
