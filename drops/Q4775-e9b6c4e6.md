# Paper 1 Remark 5.2, high-speed branch: fidelity and lower-barrier audit

## Executive verdict

The high-speed argument printed after Remark 5.2 is **not justified by the displayed hypotheses as they currently stand**, and there are two independent defects.

1. **Missing analytic producer.**  The estimate needs a quantitative lower barrier for the particular traveling-wave profile \(U\).  The hypotheses

   ```text
   IsTravelingWave
   + strict upper tail \(0<U<\min\{M_\chi,e^{-\kappa x}\}\)
   + right-tail asymptotic \(U/e^{-\kappa x}\to1\)
   ```

   do not by themselves supply the parameter-only lower barrier used in Remark 4.1.  They yield only an eventual lower tail and, with explicit regularity, a profile-dependent positive minimum on the remaining compact region.  That is not enough to recover the displayed parameter-only \(M'''_{\chi,m,\alpha,\gamma,\sigma}\).

2. **The scalar lower estimate for \(d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}\) is false as printed/transcribed.**  The first branch of the displayed high-speed \(M'''\) is exactly what one obtains from that false lower estimate.  A rigorous replacement either keeps the exact factor \(d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}^{-1}\), or inserts one additional factor \(\gamma+|\chi|^\sigma\) in the first branch.

There is also a statement mismatch that should be kept explicit:

- the paper text and the current Lean target state the **one-sided** bound
  
  \[
  \frac{U'(x)}{U(x)}\le \frac{M'''}{|\chi|^{2\sigma}};
  \]
- the proposed high-speed proof, if repaired, actually proves the stronger
  
  \[
  \left|\frac{U'(x)}{U(x)}\right|\le \frac{M'''}{|\chi|^{2\sigma}}.
  \]

The present repository proves Remark 5.2 for monotone negative-sensitivity waves by the trivial implication \(U'\le0\).  That branch is valid, but it does **not** verify the paper's arbitrary-wave high-speed mechanism.

# 2. The exact lower barrier behind Remark 4.1

Remark 4.1 specializes the Lemma 4.2 subsolution to

\[
\widetilde\kappa=2\kappa,
\qquad
0<\kappa<\frac12,
\qquad
\gamma\kappa<1.
\]

Put

\[
\Delta:=1-\gamma^2\kappa^2>0.
\]

The source defines the convenient upper choice

\[
\boxed{
D_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}
=
\frac{2\bigl(\Delta+q(3m\kappa+1)\bigr)}{\Delta}.
}
\tag{D}
\]

The raw lower profile is

\[
R_D(x):=e^{-\kappa x}-De^{-2\kappa x}.
\]

Its maximum occurs at

\[
\boxed{
x_0=x_D^+:=\frac{\log(2D)}\kappa,}
\]

because \(e^{-\kappa x_0}=1/(2D)\).  At this point,

\[
R_D(x_0)=\frac1{4D}.
\]

The patched lower barrier is therefore

\[
\underline U_D(x):=
\begin{cases}
\dfrac1{4D},&x\le x_0,\\[2mm]
e^{-\kappa x}-De^{-2\kappa x},&x\ge x_0.
\end{cases}
\tag{LB}
\]

The constant-subsolution threshold of Lemma 4.2 becomes

\[
d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}
=
\min\left\{\frac1{1+q},\frac1{4D_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}}\right\}.
\]

For the \(D\) in (D), the second entry is always the smaller one.  Indeed,

\[
D
=2+\frac{2q(3m\kappa+1)}\Delta
\ge 2+2q,
\]

and hence \(4D>1+q\).  Thus

\[
\boxed{
d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}
=\frac1{4D_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}}
=\frac{\Delta}
{8\bigl(\Delta+q(3m\kappa+1)\bigr)}.
}
\tag{d-exact}
\]

If a wave has the genuine comparison property

\[
\boxed{\underline U_D(x)\le U(x)\quad\forall x\in\mathbf R,}
\tag{COMP}
\]

then the lower bounds actually available are

\[
\boxed{U(x)\ge d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}
\quad(x\le x_0),}
\tag{L}
\]

and

\[
\boxed{U(x)\ge \frac12e^{-\kappa x}
\quad(x\ge x_0).}
\tag{R}
\]

The second follows because, for \(x\ge x_0\),

\[
De^{-\kappa x}\le De^{-\kappa x_0}=\frac12,
\]

so

\[
R_D(x)=e^{-\kappa x}\bigl(1-De^{-\kappa x}\bigr)
\ge\frac12e^{-\kappa x}.
\]

## The split is not really at zero

Since \(D\ge2\), one has \(x_0=\log(2D)/\kappa>0\).  Therefore the honest proof has three geographic pieces:

\[
(-\infty,0],\qquad [0,x_0],\qquad [x_0,\infty).
\]

- On \(x\le0\), use \(U\ge d\) and (5.8).
- On \(0\le x\le x_0\), **still** use \(U\ge d\) and (5.8).
- Only on \(x\ge x_0\) may one use \(U\ge\frac12e^{-\kappa x}\) together with (5.9).

Thus a two-line argument saying “constant lower bound for \(x\le0\), exponential lower bound for \(x\ge0\)” is not faithful.  The exponential lower bound with coefficient \(1/2\) begins at \(x_0\), not at zero.

One also cannot silently translate the wave to force \(x_0=0\): replacing \(U(x)\) by \(U(x+a)\) changes the normalized right-tail coefficient from \(1\) to \(e^{-\kappa a}\).  The theorem's normalization

\[
\frac{U(x)}{e^{-\kappa x}}\longrightarrow1
\]

fixes this translation freedom.

# 5. Two rigorous repairs of the constant

## 5.1 Best repair: retain the exact \(d\)

Define

\[
\boxed{
M'''_{\rm exact}
:=
\max\left\{
\frac{rM'}{d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}},
\;2M''
\right\}.
}
\tag{H-exact}
\]

Then (A) and (B) immediately yield

\[
\left|\frac{U'}U\right|
\le \frac{M'''_{\rm exact}}{r^2}.
\]

This is the cleanest Lean formulation: all constants are explicit rational combinations and real powers, and no fragile simplification of \(d^{-1}\) is needed.

## 5.2 A corrected coarse branch

The same hypotheses do give a slightly weaker lower bound with one extra factor \(\gamma+r\) in the denominator.

From

\[
\kappa<\frac1{\gamma+r},
\]

one obtains

\[
\Delta
=1-\gamma^2\kappa^2
>
1-\frac{\gamma^2}{(\gamma+r)^2}
=
\frac{r(2\gamma+r)}{(\gamma+r)^2}
\ge
\frac{(1+\gamma)r}{(\gamma+r)^2},
\]

because \(\gamma\ge1\).

Also \(c>5/2\) gives \(\kappa<1/2\), hence

\[
3m\kappa+1\le 2m+1,
\]

and therefore

\[
\Delta+q(3m\kappa+1)
\le 1+q+2mq.
\]

Consequently

\[
\boxed{
d
\ge
\frac{(1+\gamma)r}
{8(1+q+2mq)(\gamma+r)^2}.}
\tag{d-safe}
\]

A valid coarse first branch is therefore

\[
\boxed{
H_1^{\rm safe}
=
8(1+q+2mq)
\frac{(\gamma+r)^2}{1+\gamma}M'.
}
\tag{H-safe}
\]

Compared with the paper's branch, this has exactly one additional factor \(\gamma+r\).

Both (H-exact) and (H-safe) remain bounded as \(\chi\to0\) under the same parameter regime, so the asymptotic motivation of Remark 5.2 is not lost.

# 6. Why the theorem hypotheses do not produce the needed lower barrier

Theorem 1.2 quantifies over an arbitrary traveling wave satisfying the strict upper bound and a right-tail asymptotic.  In current Lean, the amended statement also exposes `TravelingWaveRegularity`, which is necessary because bare `IsTravelingWave` stores derivative equations but does not itself imply differentiability or continuity.

These assumptions imply the following qualitative facts.

## 6.1 Eventual right lower bound

From

\[
\frac{U(x)}{e^{-\kappa x}}\longrightarrow1,
\]

one gets a profile-dependent \(R\) such that

\[
U(x)\ge\frac12e^{-\kappa x}
\qquad(x\ge R).
\]

This is enough to use (5.9) eventually, but \(R\) is not the explicit \(x_0=\log(2D)/\kappa\).

## 6.2 Eventual left lower bound

From \(U(x)\to1\) as \(x\to-\infty\), one obtains a profile-dependent \(L\) such that

\[
U(x)\ge\frac12
\qquad(x\le L).
\]

## 6.3 Compact core

With continuity and pointwise positivity, \(U\) has a positive minimum on the compact interval \([L,R]\).  Therefore one can prove the existence of a profile-dependent constant

\[
m_U>0,
\qquad U(x)\ge m_U\quad(x\le R).
\]

Combining this with (5.8) and the eventual right bound proves some finite estimate

\[
\left|\frac{U'}U\right|
\le
\max\left\{
\frac{M'}{rm_U},
\frac{2M''}{r^2}
\right\}.
\]

But \(m_U\) depends on the particular wave.  Nothing in strict upper-tail control or right-tail asymptotics gives

\[
m_U\ge d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}
\]

uniformly over all admitted waves.

Hence the stated hypotheses may support a **profile-dependent** log-derivative estimate, but not the displayed parameter-only \(M'''\).

# 7. Why merely citing Remark 4.1 is insufficient

Remark 4.1 computes constants for the lower subsolutions used in the Section 4 fixed-point construction.  It does not, by itself, prove the comparison (COMP) for every traveling wave admitted by Theorem 1.2.

To transfer the lower barrier to an arbitrary wave, one needs a genuine theorem of the form

\[
\underline U_D\le U.
\]

That theorem requires at least:

1. a proof that \(\underline U_D\) is a subsolution for the coefficients generated by this particular \(U\);
2. a far-right anchoring inequality;
3. a stationary or parabolic comparison argument propagating the ordering;
4. interface handling where the constant plateau is patched to the raw exponential difference.

None of these is contained in the scalar statement of Remark 4.1.

There is an additional exponent mismatch.  The given asymptotic at some \(\kappa_1>\kappa\) naturally anchors a raw barrier

\[
e^{-\kappa x}-De^{-\widetilde\kappa x}
\]

when \(\widetilde\kappa<\kappa_1\), because the normalized error is then little-\(o(e^{-(\widetilde\kappa-\kappa)x})\).  The explicit Remark 4.1 constants require

\[
\widetilde\kappa=2\kappa.
\]

Thus the asymptotic assumed in Theorem 1.2 anchors the exact Remark 4.1 barrier only if one additionally knows

\[
2\kappa<\kappa_1.
\]

The theorem merely assumes the existence of some \(\kappa_1\in(\kappa,1)\).  That does not imply \(2\kappa<\kappa_1\).  In particular, when the available upper tail-rate range ends at \(2\kappa\), the required strict inequality cannot be recovered.

Therefore even a future comparison proof cannot simply cite the present asymptotic hypothesis and specialize to \(\widetilde\kappa=2\kappa\) without an additional rate argument.

Any theorem using the displayed high-speed \(M'''\) as a coefficient budget for arbitrary waves is therefore unsupported until both defects are repaired.

# Final conclusion

The intended high-speed estimate is the elementary two-mechanism inequality

\[
\left|\frac{U'}U\right|
\le
\begin{cases}
M'/(rd),&x\le x_0,\\[1mm]
2M''/r^2,&x\ge x_0,
\end{cases}
\qquad
x_0=\frac{\log(2D)}\kappa.
\]

Its proof requires the explicit lower barrier

\[
U\ge d\quad(x\le x_0),
\qquad
U\ge\frac12e^{-\kappa x}\quad(x\ge x_0).
\]

Those lower bounds are not consequences of the theorem's upper-tail hypotheses alone, and the particular simplification of \(d^{-1}\) used to obtain the printed first branch of \(M'''\) is false.  The faithful repair is:

\[
M'''_{\rm exact}
=
\max\left\{\frac{|\chi|^\sigma M'}{d_{\kappa,\chi,m,\gamma}},2M''\right\},
\]

or, more coarsely, replace the paper's factor \(\gamma+|\chi|^\sigma\) by

\[
(\gamma+|\chi|^\sigma)^2.
\]

A Lean close must additionally construct the lower barrier for the actual wave; citing Remark 4.1's scalar constants is not that construction.
