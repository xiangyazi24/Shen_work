# Acyclicity audit for the χ₀ < 0 global-existence architecture

## Definitive verdict

**There is no irreducible mathematical circular dependency.** The theorem is genuinely achievable by an acyclic route, but the proposed chain needs three corrections before it is a sound formalization plan:

1. Mere continuity and nonnegativity do **not** imply Hölder regularity. A separate positive-time parabolic Hölder-gain theorem is needed before Schauder can be invoked.
2. The chemotaxis term initially appears in divergence form. One cannot apply the ordinary zeroth-order-source Schauder theorem in one step. A short two-stage bootstrap is needed: first gain one spatial derivative from the divergence-form equation, then expand the divergence and apply the ordinary Schauder theorem.
3. For the linear drift–reaction comparison, the reaction coefficient cannot be only `a - b * u^α`. It must absorb the derivative of the chemotactic velocity. With
   
   \[
   W=\frac{R_x}{(1+R)^\beta},
   \]
   
   the exact coefficients are
   
   \[
   B=-\chi_0 W,
   \qquad
   C_{\mathrm{exact}}=a-bu^\alpha-\chi_0 W_x.
   \]

After these corrections, the dependency order is genuinely directed:

```text
truncated bounded mild solution
        ↓
weak negative-part energy
        ↓
pointwise nonnegativity u ≥ 0
        ↓
positive-time Hölder gain
        ↓
elliptic regularity of R and divergence-form parabolic regularity of u
        ↓
C^{2+θ,1+θ/2} regularity of u on buffered windows
        ↓
exact bounded drift–reaction equation + Neumann comparison
        ↓
strict positivity u > 0
        ↓
uniform positive floor on compact positive-time slabs
        ↓
C² real-power composition, HSpectral reconstruction, full classical bootstrap
        ↓
a-priori bounds + continuation/global existence
```

The remaining difficult items are substantial analytic formalization bricks, especially the weak negative-part chain rule and the pre-positivity parabolic regularity theorem. They are **not** a cycle.

# 1. The corrected dependency graph

It is useful to distinguish two different meanings of “classical regularity.”

The positivity comparison only needs a **low classical level**:

\[
u,\ u_x,\ u_{xx},\ u_t
\]

on positive-time windows, together with continuity up to the spatial boundary and the Neumann condition. It does **not** need the source itself to be spatially `C²`, eigenvalue-weighted coefficient summability, or the complete level-5 spectral reconstruction.

The later HSpectral step needs a **higher nonlinear-source level**. For example, when `1 ≤ γ < 2`, the formula

\[
\partial_{xx}(u^\gamma)
=\gamma(\gamma-1)u^{\gamma-2}u_x^2
 +\gamma u^{\gamma-1}u_{xx}
\]

is singular at `u=0`. Strict positivity supplies a positive lower floor on every compact positive-time slab, after which all real-power chain rules are ordinary smooth composition facts.

Thus the two regularity stages are logically distinct:

```text
pre-positivity regularity:
    Hölder source ⇒ u is C^{2+θ,1+θ/2}

post-positivity regularity:
    u ≥ m > 0 ⇒ u^γ and the full nonlinear source have the higher C²/Hspectral regularity
```

Calling both stages “the classical bootstrap” obscures the DAG. The first is a local parabolic regularization theorem; the second is the full source/spectral reconstruction.

# 2. ENERGY: nonnegativity is genuinely pre-positivity

The correct starting object is the **faithfully truncated mild equation**, not a formulation that has already assumed `u ≥ 0` or `u > 0`. Schematically,

\[
u_t=\nu_u u_{xx}
 -\chi_0\partial_x\!\left(u_+\,W[u_+]\right)
 +\text{truncated logistic}(u).
\]

Test the weak equation with `-u_-`, where

\[
u_-:=\max(-u,0).
\]

The load-bearing cancellation is support-theoretic:

\[
u_+\,\partial_x u_-=0\quad\text{a.e.}
\]

Hence the chemotaxis contribution vanishes in the negative-part energy estimate. Diffusion contributes a nonnegative dissipation term, and the truncated logistic term has a one-sided estimate of the form

\[
\frac12 E'(t)+\nu_u\|\partial_xu_-(t)\|_2^2
\le \ell E(t),
\qquad
E(t)=\|u_-(t)\|_2^2.
\]

Positive-time Gronwall plus the initial trace `u(t) → u₀` and `u₀ ≥ 0` gives `E(t)=0`. Spatial continuity then upgrades the a.e. conclusion to

\[
u(t,x)\ge0
\]

at every point.

## What this step actually requires

It requires:

- a weak form of the truncated PDE;
- `u ∈ L²_t H¹_x` or the corresponding project-level weak spatial package;
- a weak time derivative, typically in `L²_t H^{-1}_x` or an equivalent dual space;
- the chain rule
  
  \[
  \frac12\frac d{dt}\|u_-\|_2^2
  =\langle u_t,-u_-\rangle;
  \]
- the positive-time Gronwall argument;
- an initial trace strong enough to make `\|u_-(t)\|₂ → 0`;
- continuity of each positive-time spatial slice for the pointwise upgrade.

None of these hypotheses requires strict positivity. None requires `u^γ` to be `C²`. None requires the level-5 cosine reconstruction.

The genuine formalization difficulty is the Lions–Magenes/Steklov negative-part chain rule and its integration into the weak mild-to-PDE interface. That difficulty is real, but it is independent of positivity.

# 3. SUPERSOLUTION REGULARITY: possible without strict positivity, but not in one step

## 3.1 Correction: continuity does not imply Hölder continuity

The statement

```text
u ≥ 0 and u continuous ⇒ u^γ is Hölder
```

is false as written. A continuous function on a compact set is uniformly continuous, but need not be Hölder of any positive exponent.

What is true is:

1. the bounded parabolic mild equation gives a positive-time Hölder gain for `u`;
2. once `u` is Hölder and bounded, `u^γ` is Hölder without requiring `u>0` when `γ ≥ 1`.

Indeed, for `0 ≤ r,q ≤ M` and `γ ≥ 1`, the mean-value estimate gives

\[
|r^\gamma-q^\gamma|
\le \gamma M^{\gamma-1}|r-q|.
\]

Thus `r ↦ r^γ` is Lipschitz on `[0,M]`, including at zero. This is exactly why strict positivity is unnecessary at this stage.

For the logistic expression, it is often cleaner to keep

\[
u(a-bu^\alpha)=au-bu^{\alpha+1}.
\]

Since `α+1>1`, this is locally Lipschitz as a function of a nonnegative bounded `u`, even when `0<α<1`.

## 3.2 The base Hölder gain is an independent pre-positivity brick

Write the truncated/nonnegative equation on a buffered slab as

\[
u_t-\nu_u u_{xx}
=\partial_x F+G,
\]

where

\[
F=-\chi_0uW,
\qquad
W=\frac{R_x}{(1+R)^\beta},
\qquad
G=au-bu^{\alpha+1}.
\]

At the first stage, boundedness of `u`, elliptic weak bounds for `R`, and `1+R ≥ 1` give bounded `F` and `G`. The Neumann heat-kernel representation then gives a Hölder modulus on every strip

\[
[a,b]\times[0,1]\subset(0,T)\times[0,1].
\]

In one dimension, the relevant kernel singularities are integrable. For the divergence term one controls differences of `∂ₓK_N`; splitting the time integral at the parabolic scale `|x-y|²` yields, for example, every spatial Hölder exponent strictly below one. A project theorem may package this as

```lean
PositiveWindowHolderRegularity
```

or prove the equivalent estimate directly from the explicit Neumann kernel. This theorem needs boundedness and the mild identity, not strict positivity.

## 3.3 Elliptic resolver regularity also needs only nonnegativity

Once

\[
u\in C^{\theta,\theta/2}
\]

on a buffered slab and `γ≥1`, one has

\[
u^\gamma\in C^{\theta,\theta/2}.
\]

For the elliptic Neumann resolver

\[
-\kappa R_{xx}+\mu R=\eta u^\gamma,
\qquad R_x(0)=R_x(1)=0,
\]

standard elliptic Schauder regularity gives

\[
R\in C^{2+\theta}
\]

spatially, with the corresponding time Hölder dependence inherited from the source. Positivity preservation of the elliptic resolver gives `R≥0` from `u≥0`; strict positivity of `u` is not needed. Therefore

\[
1+R\ge1,
\]

and the denominator never approaches zero.

Consequently,

\[
W=R_x(1+R)^{-\beta}\in C^{1+\theta},
\]

and

\[
F=uW\in C^\theta.
\]

Again, no division by `u` occurs and no positive lower bound for `u` is used.

## 3.4 Why ordinary Schauder cannot be applied immediately

The right-hand side is initially `∂ₓF+G`, not merely a Hölder function. Knowing `F∈C^θ` does not yet make `∂ₓF` a classical Hölder source.

The clean acyclic bootstrap is:

### Stage A: divergence-form regularity

From

\[
u_t-\nu_u u_{xx}=\partial_xF+G,
\qquad F,G\in C^{\theta,\theta/2},
\]

use a divergence-form parabolic Schauder estimate, or the corresponding differentiated heat-potential estimate, to obtain

\[
u_x\in C^{\eta,\eta/2}
\]

on a slightly smaller buffered slab, for some `η>0`. Equivalently, obtain `u∈C^{1+η,(1+η)/2}`.

### Stage B: expand the divergence

Now

\[
\partial_x(uW)=Wu_x+uW_x.
\]

Here `u`, `u_x`, `W`, and `W_x` are Hölder. Hence the full right-hand side is an ordinary Hölder function. Apply the usual nondivergence parabolic Schauder theorem to get

\[
\boxed{u\in C^{2+\eta,1+\eta/2}}
\]

on a still smaller buffered slab.

This supplies the derivatives needed by the comparison principle:

\[
u_t,\quad u_x,\quad u_{xx}.
\]

It does **not** require `u^γ∈C²`; only Hölder continuity of `u^γ` was used.

## 3.5 Boundary regularity is required, not merely interior regularity

The strict-positivity conclusion is needed for every `x∈[0,1]`, and the project comparison structure contains endpoint Neumann fields. Therefore an interior-only theorem on `(0,1)` is not enough.

One must add either:

- parabolic Schauder regularity up to the Neumann boundary on buffered time strips; or
- an even-reflection argument across `x=0` and `x=1`, using the Neumann compatibility of `u` and `R`; or
- a direct Neumann heat-kernel proof of the required endpoint derivative identities and continuity.

This boundary step also does not use strict positivity.

### Answer to question 1

**Yes, the required low classical/supersolution regularity can be obtained from nonnegativity without strict positivity and without the full HSpectral bootstrap.** However, the precise statement is not “continuity makes `u^γ` Hölder, then one Schauder call.” The correct route is:

```text
bounded mild solution
  → positive-time Hölder gain
  → u^γ Hölder
  → R ∈ C^{2+θ}, W ∈ C^{1+θ}
  → divergence-form gain of u_x
  → expand ∂ₓ(uW)
  → ordinary Schauder gives u_t,u_x,u_xx
  → Neumann boundary regularity
```

That entire chain is pre-positivity.

# 4. The exact linear drift–reaction equation

After ENERGY has proved `u≥0`, the positive-part truncation is inactive. Define

\[
W(t,x)=\frac{R_x(t,x)}{(1+R(t,x))^\beta}.
\]

The chemotaxis equation is

\[
u_t
=\nu_u u_{xx}-\chi_0\partial_x(uW)
 +u(a-bu^\alpha).
\]

Expanding the divergence gives

\[
u_t
=\nu_u u_{xx}
 +(-\chi_0W)u_x
 +(a-bu^\alpha-\chi_0W_x)u.
\]

Thus the correct linear coefficients, with the nonlinear solution frozen into them, are

\[
\boxed{
B=-\chi_0W,
\qquad
C_{\mathrm{exact}}=a-bu^\alpha-\chi_0W_x.
}
\]

The derivative of the velocity is explicit:

\[
W_x
=\frac{R_{xx}}{(1+R)^\beta}
 -\beta\frac{R_x^2}{(1+R)^{\beta+1}}.
\]

The resolver equation, boundedness of `u`, the Hölder/elliptic regularity above, and `1+R≥1` give finite bounds for `B` and `C_exact` on every finite buffered strip. No positive lower bound for `u` occurs in these estimates.

This correction is load-bearing. Taking only

\[
C=a-bu^\alpha
\]

leaves the term `-χ₀uW_x` in the residual. Its sign is not controlled, so the claimed supersolution relation is generally false.

For unit diffusion, the squared-barrier damping condition has the form

\[
M\ge \frac{A^2}{2}+D,
\qquad |B|\le A,
\qquad -C_{\mathrm{exact}}\le D.
\]

For diffusion coefficient `ν_u>0`, the corresponding completion of the square gives the adjusted constant

\[
M\ge \frac{A^2}{2\nu_u}+D.
\]

The repository's `UNCONDITIONAL_DOCTRINE.md` records exactly this correction for the `hLinearStripCore` route. In the audited concrete file, the older `bformConcreteReact = a - b*u^α` shape is still the wrong coefficient for an exact supersolution proof and should be replaced by `C_exact`.

# 5. STRICT POSITIVITY is not self-referential

Once the previous section has produced:

- the exact linear drift–reaction identity or supersolution inequality;
- bounded drift and reaction coefficients;
- continuity on the closed strip;
- `u_t`, `u_x`, and `u_xx` on positive times;
- homogeneous Neumann boundary conditions;
- nonnegative, nontrivial initial data;

strict positivity follows by comparison. No assumption `u>0` is used in the comparison.

## Why the squared heat barrier is the correct object

For nonzero drift, a scalar-discounted pure heat lower bound

\[
u(t)\ge e^{-Dt}S_{\nu_u}(t)u_0
\]

is not generally valid: the drift term has no sign and cannot be absorbed into a zeroth-order discount.

Instead take

\[
w(t,x)=e^{-Mt}\bigl(S_{\nu_u}(t)f(x)\bigr)^2,
\]

where `f≥0`, `f` is nontrivial, and `f²≤u₀`. If `h=S_{ν_u}(t)f`, then `h_t=ν_u h_{xx}` and the residual contains

\[
-2\nu_u h_x^2-2Bhh_x.
\]

Completing the square bounds this by

\[
\frac{B^2}{2\nu_u}h^2.
\]

Choosing `M` as above makes `w` a subsolution. The Neumann comparison principle yields `w≤u`; strict positivity of the Neumann heat kernel gives `w(t,x)>0`, hence

\[
u(t,x)>0
\]

for every `t>0` and every `x∈[0,1]`.

For continuous nonnegative, nonzero `u₀`, a suitable seed exists: one may take a continuous bump below `u₀`, or `f=\sqrt{u₀}` when convenient. For a paper-positive datum with a uniform floor, a constant seed is simplest.

# 6. POST-POSITIVITY: why the full HSpectral bootstrap now becomes legal

Strict pointwise positivity plus joint continuity gives more than pointwise positivity on a compact buffered slab. For

\[
0<a<b<T,
\]

compactness yields

\[
\exists m_{a,b}>0,
\qquad
u(t,x)\ge m_{a,b}
\quad
\text{on }[a,b]\times[0,1].
\]

On the range `[m_{a,b},M]`, every real power is smooth with bounded derivatives of all required orders. In particular, for arbitrary real `γ` in the paper's admissible range, the problematic factors

\[
u^{\gamma-1},\qquad u^{\gamma-2}
\]

are bounded. The ordinary chain rules for `u^γ`, time derivatives of the source, resolver differentiation, and the source `C²`/weak-`H²` certificates can then be formalized without a zero-set branch.

This is where the higher spectral machinery belongs:

- source coefficient `ℓ¹` or eigenvalue-weighted summability;
- termwise spatial differentiation;
- locally uniform termwise time differentiation;
- pointwise cosine reconstruction;
- full nonlinear PDE identity and classical source regularity.

A useful correction to the terminology is that **Jensen's inequality itself generally needs only `u≥0`, not `u>0`**. Strict positivity is load-bearing here for smooth real-power differentiation, negative exponents, and a uniform positive floor—not for the basic convex Jensen step.

# 7. Code-level hazards that can make an acyclic proof look circular

The mathematics is acyclic, but the current record packaging can accidentally hide downstream conclusions in upstream records.

## 7.1 Do not consume `ConjugateMildSolutionData.hpos` in the pre-positivity path

The current structure

```lean

```

contains both

```lean

```

and `conjugateMildSolutionData_of_data` fills `hpos` from `ConjugateMildExistenceData.hmapsTo_pos`.

If a theorem intended to prove strict positivity uses this packaged record—or a resolver theorem that internally reads its `hpos` field—the proof becomes architecturally misleading.

- `TruncatedConjugateMildSolutionData`; or
- a new record containing only mildness, boundedness, continuity, measurability, initial trace, and eventually proved nonnegativity.
