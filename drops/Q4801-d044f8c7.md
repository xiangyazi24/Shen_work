# Problem 2.5: exact CMF moment formulas, the Delannoy–Brafman period, and the missing decay certificate

## Executive conclusion

There are two different mathematical objects in the request.

1. For the **actual printed CMF product**, there is an exact integral representation of every numerator, denominator, and Catalan linear form. It is elementary and completely explicit; see Section 2.
2. There is an independent, exact **Delannoy–Brafman representation of Catalan’s constant**, including a positive triple-integral remainder; see Sections 4–5.

What is not currently available is a non-circular identity proving that the finite CMF rows are the Brafman truncations, or a fixed-sign Beukers kernel whose integral is the CMF error. The established failure of a rational Ore map to the homogeneous Delannoy symmetric square means that such an identification cannot simply be a rational same-index combination of

\[
D_N^2,\qquad D_NE_N,\qquad E_N^2.
\]

The exact finite CMF formulas are as follows. Put

\[
M_N=M(0)M(1)\cdots M(N-1),
\]

\[
\mathbf p_N=(30921,-32972,8240)M_N,
\qquad
\mathbf q_N=(33750,-36000,9000)M_N.
\]

Define the row-vector polynomial

\[
\boxed{
\mathbf R_0(X)
=\mathbf q_0-(1+X)\mathbf p_0
=(2829-30921X,\,-3028+32972X,\,760-8240X),
}
\tag{0.1}
\]

and

\[
\boxed{
\mathbf R_N(X)=\mathbf R_0(X)M_N
=\mathbf q_N-(1+X)\mathbf p_N.
}
\tag{0.2}
\]

For each column \(j=1,2,3\), one then has

\[
\boxed{
q_{N,j}=\int_0^1(10-18x)R_{N,j}(x)\,dx,
}
\tag{0.3}
\]

\[
\boxed{
p_{N,j}=\int_0^1(6-12x)R_{N,j}(x)\,dx,
}
\tag{0.4}
\]

and, using

\[
G=-\int_0^1\frac{\log t}{1+t^2}\,dt,
\]

\[
\boxed{
Gq_{N,j}-p_{N,j}
=\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}R_{N,j}(t^2)\,dt
=\int_0^1\!\int_0^1
\frac{R_{N,j}((xy)^2)}{1+(xy)^2}\,dx\,dy.
}
\tag{0.5}
\]

Thus, for the sign convention in the question,

\[
\boxed{
r_{N,j}:=p_{N,j}-Gq_{N,j}
=-\int_{[0,1]^2}
\frac{R_{N,j}((xy)^2)}{1+(xy)^2}\,dx\,dy.
}
\tag{0.6}
\]

These identities are exact for the printed matrix. They are, however, a **moment lift of the matrix product**: the polynomial \(R_{N,j}\) itself contains \(M_N\). They do not yet give a pointwise bound or a fixed sign.

There are also two corrections to the premise.

- It is not known that
  \[
  G\notin\mathbf Q(\sqrt2,\log2).
  \]
  In fact, the irrationality of \(G\) itself remains unknown. A symbolic Ore-Hom computation may establish that a particular rational intertwiner space is zero, but the unproved field-membership assertion cannot be used as its proof.
- The raw linear form \(p_{N,j}-Gq_{N,j}\) generally does **not** tend to zero: the terms have factorial growth. What tends to zero is the ratio error
  \[
  \frac{p_{N,j}}{q_{N,j}}-G.
  \]

The exact Brafman period model is

\[
D_n=P_n(3),\qquad
F(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}D_n^2z^n,
\]

\[
a=3-2\sqrt2,\qquad
\rho=a^2=17-12\sqrt2.
\]

Then

\[
\boxed{
F(z)=\frac1{1-z}
{}_2F_1\!\left(\frac12,\frac12;1;
\frac{32z}{(1-z)^2}\right)
=\frac{2}{\pi(1-z)}
K\!\left(\frac{4\sqrt{2z}}{1-z}\right),
}
\tag{0.7}
\]

and

\[
\boxed{
G=\frac{\pi\sqrt2}{2}
\int_0^\rho
\frac{1+z}{\sqrt z(1-z)}F(z)\,dz.
}
\tag{0.8}
\]

This gives a positive series, a real double integral involving the kernel \((1-6w+w^2)^{-1/2}\), and a positive triple-integral remainder. These Brafman truncations are not presently known to equal the rational CMF approximants.

# 1. Notation and the exact CMF rows

Let

\[
\mathbf p_0=(30921,-32972,8240),
\qquad
\mathbf q_0=(33750,-36000,9000).
\]

The row trajectories are

\[
\mathbf p_N=\mathbf p_0M_N,
\qquad
\mathbf q_N=\mathbf q_0M_N.
\]

For a fixed column \(j\), the challenge approximant is

\[
\frac{p_{N,j}}{q_{N,j}}.
\]

The construction below is independent of the large explicit entries of \(M(n)\); those entries enter only through the product \(M_N\).

Define

\[
\mathbf R_0(X)=\mathbf q_0-(1+X)\mathbf p_0.
\]

Direct subtraction gives

\[
\mathbf R_0(X)
=(2829-30921X,\,-3028+32972X,\,760-8240X).
\]

Since \(M_N\) is independent of \(X\),

\[
\mathbf R_N(X):=\mathbf R_0(X)M_N
=\mathbf q_N-(1+X)\mathbf p_N.
\tag{1.1}
\]

Each component is linear in \(X\). If

\[
R_{N,j}(X)=A_{N,j}+B_{N,j}X,
\]

then (1.1) says

\[
A_{N,j}=q_{N,j}-p_{N,j},
\qquad
B_{N,j}=-p_{N,j}.
\tag{1.2}
\]

Consequently

\[
q_{N,j}=A_{N,j}-B_{N,j}=R_{N,j}(-1),
\qquad
p_{N,j}=-B_{N,j}.
\tag{1.3}
\]

This simple observation is the source of the exact moment formulas.

# 2. Exact definite integrals for the actual CMF solutions

## 2.1 Denominator

For a linear polynomial \(P(X)=A+BX\),

\[
\int_0^1(10-18x)P(x)\,dx
=A-B=P(-1).
\]

Indeed,

\[
\int_0^1(10-18x)\,dx=1,
\qquad
\int_0^1x(10-18x)\,dx=-1.
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
q_{N,j}=\int_0^1(10-18x)R_{N,j}(x)\,dx.
}
\tag{2.1}
\]

In vector form,

\[
\boxed{
\mathbf q_N
=\int_0^1(10-18x)\mathbf R_0(x)M_N\,dx.
}
\tag{2.2}
\]

## 2.2 Numerator

Similarly,

\[
\int_0^1(6-12x)P(x)\,dx=-B,
\]

because

\[
\int_0^1(6-12x)\,dx=0,
\qquad
\int_0^1x(6-12x)\,dx=-1.
\]

Thus

\[
\boxed{
p_{N,j}=\int_0^1(6-12x)R_{N,j}(x)\,dx,
}
\tag{2.3}
\]

or, in vector form,

\[
\boxed{
\mathbf p_N
=\int_0^1(6-12x)\mathbf R_0(x)M_N\,dx.
}
\tag{2.4}
\]

These answer parts (a) and (b) exactly, but not yet in a Delannoy basis.

## 2.3 Catalan functional and exact remainder

For any polynomial \(P\), define

\[
\mathcal E(P)=P(-1)
\]

and

\[
\mathcal A(P)=
\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}
\bigl(P(-1)-P(t^2)\bigr)\,dt.
\tag{2.5}
\]

For \(P(X)=A+BX\),

\[
P(-1)-P(t^2)=-B(1+t^2),
\]

so

\[
\mathcal A(P)
=-B\int_0^1(-\log t)\,dt=-B.
\]

Hence

\[
q_{N,j}=\mathcal E(R_{N,j}),
\qquad
p_{N,j}=\mathcal A(R_{N,j}).
\tag{2.6}
\]

Since

\[
G=\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}\,dt,
\]

one obtains

\[
\begin{aligned}
G\mathcal E(P)-\mathcal A(P)
&=\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}P(t^2)\,dt.
\end{aligned}
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
Gq_{N,j}-p_{N,j}
=\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}R_{N,j}(t^2)\,dt.
}
\tag{2.7}
\]

The product-distribution identity

\[
\int_0^1\!\int_0^1 f(xy)\,dx\,dy
=\int_0^1f(t)(-\log t)\,dt
\tag{2.8}
\]

gives

\[
\boxed{
Gq_{N,j}-p_{N,j}
=\int_{[0,1]^2}
\frac{R_{N,j}((xy)^2)}{1+(xy)^2}\,dx\,dy.
}
\tag{2.9}
\]

Equivalently,

\[
\boxed{
p_{N,j}-Gq_{N,j}
=-\int_{[0,1]^2}
\frac{R_{N,j}((xy)^2)}{1+(xy)^2}\,dx\,dy.
}
\tag{2.10}
\]

This is an exact double integral for the actual CMF linear form, with all constants explicit.

## 2.4 Why (2.10) is not yet the desired Beukers proof

The integrand in (2.10) is not known to have one sign, and the factor

\[
R_{N,j}((xy)^2)=igl(\mathbf R_0((xy)^2)M_N\bigr)_j
\]

still contains the matrix product whose asymptotics are to be understood. Thus (2.10) is exact but not a non-circular decay certificate.

A genuine Beukers formula would replace it by a kernel such as

\[
\frac{\Phi_N(x,y)}{1+x^2y^2}
\]

where \(\Phi_N\) is given independently—for example by a Rodrigues formula, a terminating hypergeometric sum, or a product of simple beta factors—and satisfies a bound of the form

\[
|\Phi_N(x,y)|\le C N^A\rho^N
\]

uniformly on the integration domain.

# 3. The correct object that tends to zero

Put

\[
H_N=(-16)^N(2)_N^2(3)_N^2
\left(\frac52\right)_N
\left(\frac72\right)_N^2.
\tag{3.1}
\]

Then

\[
\frac{H_{N+1}}{H_N}
=-16(N+2)^2(N+3)^2
\left(N+\frac52\right)
\left(N+\frac72\right)^2.
\tag{3.2}
\]

After division by \(H_N\), the established formal modes have characteristic roots

\[
\lambda_+=17+12\sqrt2,
\qquad
1,
\qquad
\lambda_-=17-12\sqrt2=\lambda_+^{-1},
\]

with formal powers \(0,-3,0\), respectively.

If the initial rows select the common dominant connection coefficient \(G\), then

\[
\frac{q_{N,j}}{H_N}
=C_{+,j}\lambda_+^N(1+O(N^{-1}))
+\text{smaller modes},
\tag{3.3}
\]

while cancellation of that dominant mode gives

\[
\frac{p_{N,j}-Gq_{N,j}}{H_N}
=C_{0,j}N^{-3}(1+O(N^{-1}))
+C_{-,j}\lambda_-^N(1+O(N^{-1})).
\tag{3.4}
\]

Consequently

\[
\boxed{
\frac{p_{N,j}}{q_{N,j}}-G
=O\!\left(N^{-3}(17-12\sqrt2)^N\right).
}
\tag{3.5}
\]

The raw difference \(p_{N,j}-Gq_{N,j}\) normally grows like \(H_NN^{-3}\); it is the normalized ratio error that tends to zero.

Thus a manifestly decaying integral should represent either

\[
\frac{p_{N,j}-Gq_{N,j}}{H_N}
\]

or

\[
\frac{p_{N,j}}{q_{N,j}}-G,
\]

not the unnormalized linear form.

# 4. The exact Delannoy–Brafman period

## 4.1 Delannoy numbers and the ordinary kernel

The central Delannoy numbers are

\[
D_n=P_n(3)
={}_2F_1\!\left(\begin{matrix}-n,n+1\\1\end{matrix};-1\right)
=\sum_{k=0}^n\binom nk\binom{n+k}{k}.
\tag{4.1}
\]

Their generating function is

\[
\boxed{
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}D_nw^n
=\frac1{\sqrt{1-6w+w^2}}.
}
\tag{4.2}
\]

The derivative is

\[
\boxed{
\frac{d}{dw}(1-6w+w^2)^{-1/2}
=(3-w)(1-6w+w^2)^{-3/2}.
}
\tag{4.3}
\]

Formula (4.3) explains where the requested \((-3/2)\)-power kernel naturally appears. It generates weighted single Delannoy coefficients, not the squares \(D_n^2\).

## 4.2 Brafman’s square-generating identity

Let

\[
F(z)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}D_n^2z^n.
\]

Brafman’s identity gives

\[
\boxed{
F(z)=\frac1{1-z}
{}_2F_1\!\left(
\frac12,\frac12;1;
\frac{32z}{(1-z)^2}
\right).
}
\tag{4.4}
\]

With the complete elliptic integral in the modulus convention

\[
K(k)=\int_0^{\pi/2}\frac{d\theta}{\sqrt{1-k^2\sin^2\theta}}
=\frac\pi2{}_2F_1\!\left(\frac12,\frac12;1;k^2\right),
\]

this is

\[
\boxed{
F(z)=\frac{2}{\pi(1-z)}
K\!\left(\frac{4\sqrt{2z}}{1-z}\right).
}
\tag{4.5}
\]

Set

\[
a=3-2\sqrt2=(\sqrt2-1)^2,
\qquad
\rho=a^2=17-12\sqrt2.
\tag{4.6}
\]

The substitution

\[
k=\frac{4\sqrt{2z}}{1-z}
\tag{4.7}
\]

maps \([0,\rho]\) to \([0,1]\), since \(k(\rho)=1\), and

\[
\frac{dk}{dz}
=\frac{2\sqrt2(1+z)}{\sqrt z(1-z)^2}.
\tag{4.8}
\]

The classical elliptic integral identity

\[
G=\frac12\int_0^1K(k)\,dk
\tag{4.9}
\]

therefore becomes

\[
\boxed{
G=\frac{\pi\sqrt2}{2}
\int_0^\rho
\frac{1+z}{\sqrt z(1-z)}F(z)\,dz.
}
\tag{4.10}
\]

This is the exact Delannoy–Brafman period formula.

## 4.3 Explicit positive series

Termwise integration gives

\[
\boxed{
G=\frac{\pi\sqrt2}{2}
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}D_n^2I_n,
}
\tag{4.11}
\]

where

\[
I_n=\int_0^\rho z^{n-1/2}\frac{1+z}{1-z}\,dz.
\tag{4.12}
\]

Writing \(z=t^2\) and \(a=\sqrt\rho\), one obtains

\[
\boxed{
I_n
=\log2
-4\sum_{m=0}^{n-1}\frac{a^{2m+1}}{2m+1}
-\frac{2a^{2n+1}}{2n+1},
}
\tag{4.13}
\]

where the finite sum is empty when \(n=0\).

Equivalently,

\[
\boxed{
I_n
=\frac{2a^{2n+1}}{2n+1}
+4\sum_{m=n+1}^{\infty}
\frac{a^{2m+1}}{2m+1}>0.
}
\tag{4.14}
\]

The constant in (4.13) follows from

\[
4\operatorname{artanh}(a)=\log2,
\qquad
\frac{1+a}{1-a}=\sqrt2.
\]

The summands satisfy

\[
\frac{\pi\sqrt2}{2}D_n^2I_n
\sim\frac{3\sqrt2}{32n^2},
\tag{4.15}
\]

so the Brafman tail is \(O(N^{-1})\).

# 5. Double and triple integrals involving the Delannoy kernel

## 5.1 Hadamard-square contour formula

The square generating function is the Hadamard square of (4.2):

\[
\boxed{
F(z)=\frac1{2\pi i}
\oint
\frac{dw}{w
\sqrt{1-6w+w^2}
\sqrt{1-6z/w+z^2/w^2}},
}
\tag{5.1}
\]

where the contour is a small positively oriented circle in an annulus on which both series converge.

Taking \(w=\sqrt z\,e^{i\theta}\), one obtains the real formula

\[
\boxed{
F(z)=\frac1{2\pi}
\int_0^{2\pi}
\frac{d\theta}
{\sqrt{
1+34z+z^2
-12\sqrt z(1+z)\cos\theta
+4z\cos^2\theta
}}.
}
\tag{5.2}
\]

Substitution into (4.10) gives the exact real double integral

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
G={}&\frac{\sqrt2}{4}
\int_0^\rho\!\int_0^{2\pi}
\frac{1+z}{\sqrt z(1-z)}\\
&\times
\frac{d\theta\,dz}
{\sqrt{
1+34z+z^2
-12\sqrt z(1+z)\cos\theta
+4z\cos^2\theta
}}.
\end{aligned}
}
\tag{5.3}
\]

This is a direct answer to the request for an integral built from the kernel \(1-6w+w^2\).

## 5.2 A positive triple integral

For \(x>1\), Legendre’s integral gives

\[
P_n(x)=\frac1\pi\int_0^\pi
\bigl(x+\sqrt{x^2-1}\cos\theta\bigr)^n\,d\theta.
\]

At \(x=3\), define

\[
A(\theta)=3+2\sqrt2\cos\theta.
\]

Then

\[
D_n=\frac1\pi\int_0^\pi A(\theta)^n\,d\theta,
\]

and therefore

\[
F(z)=\frac1{\pi^2}
\int_0^\pi\!\int_0^\pi
\frac{d\theta\,d\phi}
{1-zA(\theta)A(\phi)}.
\tag{5.4}
\]

Combining (5.4) with (4.10) gives

\[
\boxed{
G=\frac{\sqrt2}{2\pi}
\int_0^\rho\!\int_0^\pi\!\int_0^\pi
\frac{1+z}
{\sqrt z(1-z)
\bigl(1-zA(\theta)A(\phi)\bigr)}
\,d\theta\,d\phi\,dz.
}
\tag{5.5}
\]

All factors are nonnegative on the domain. Indeed,

\[
0<A(\theta)\le3+2\sqrt2,
\]

and

\[
\rho(3+2\sqrt2)^2=1.
\]

## 5.3 Manifestly decaying Brafman remainder

Define the Brafman truncation

\[
\mathcal G_N
=\frac{\pi\sqrt2}{2}
\sum_{n=0}^{N}D_n^2I_n.
\tag{5.6}
\]

Using the finite geometric sum in (5.4),

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
G-\mathcal G_N
={}&\frac{\sqrt2}{2\pi}
\int_0^\rho\!\int_0^\pi\!\int_0^\pi
\frac{1+z}{\sqrt z(1-z)}\\
&\times
\frac{\bigl(zA(\theta)A(\phi)\bigr)^{N+1}}
{1-zA(\theta)A(\phi)}
\,d\theta\,d\phi\,dz.
\end{aligned}
}
\tag{5.7}
\]

This remainder is positive and tends monotonically to zero. It is a genuine Beukers-style remainder for the Brafman approximants.

It is **not** yet a remainder formula for the rational CMF approximants \(p_{N,j}/q_{N,j}\). The Brafman partial values contain \(\pi\), \(\sqrt2\), and \(\log2\) through \(I_n\), whereas the CMF approximants are rational.

# 6. Why a Delannoy formula for the finite CMF rows is still missing

Suppose one had rational functions \(r_0(N),r_1(N),r_2(N)\) such that, after the known common hypergeometric twist,

\[
q_{N,j}
=H_N\bigl(
 r_0(N)D_N^2
+r_1(N)D_NE_N
+r_2(N)E_N^2
\bigr).
\tag{6.1}
\]

Then (6.1) would define a rational Ore morphism from the homogeneous Delannoy symmetric-square module to the twisted CMF scalar module. A direct symbolic computation of the relevant Ore-Hom space can rule out (6.1).

This does not rule out an **extension** or an **integrated transform** of the form

\[
q_{N,j}
=\sum_{k=0}^{N}
\bigl(
 f_0(N,k)D_k^2
+f_1(N,k)D_kE_k
+f_2(N,k)E_k^2
\bigr),
\tag{6.2}
\]

or an Euler integral with an additional variable. Such a construction would belong to a larger rank-three extension rather than the homogeneous symmetric square.

The appropriate analytic target is the integrated elliptic period

\[
Y(k)=\frac12\int_0^kK(s)\,ds,
\qquad Y(1)=G.
\tag{6.3}
\]

Since \(Y'(k)=K(k)/2\), the Gauss equation for \(K\) yields

\[
\boxed{
k(1-k^2)Y'''(k)
+(1-3k^2)Y''(k)
-kY'(k)=0.
}
\tag{6.4}
\]

Equation (6.4) is a rank-three extension of the rank-two elliptic module by the constant solution. It can carry a connection coefficient equal to \(G\), whereas the homogeneous symmetric square only carries products of elliptic solutions.

Thus the most plausible finite explanation of the CMF is not

\[
L_{25}^{\sharp}\simeq\operatorname{Sym}^2(L_D),
\]

but rather a gauge or pullback to an integrated elliptic module such as (6.4).

# 7. The exact certificate needed to finish the challenge proof

A complete proof should exhibit a vector integrand

\[
\boldsymbol\Phi_N(\mathbf x)
=(\Phi_{N,1}(\mathbf x),
  \Phi_{N,2}(\mathbf x),
  \Phi_{N,3}(\mathbf x))
\]

on a fixed domain \(\Omega\), together with rational boundary certificates, such that

\[
\boxed{
\boldsymbol\Phi_{N+1}(\mathbf x)
=\boldsymbol\Phi_N(\mathbf x)M(N)
+\operatorname{div}_{\mathbf x}
\boldsymbol\Psi_N(\mathbf x).
}
\tag{7.1}
\]

After integration, vanishing boundary terms would give

\[
\int_\Omega\boldsymbol\Phi_{N+1}
=\left(\int_\Omega\boldsymbol\Phi_N\right)M(N).
\tag{7.2}
\]

One then needs three explicit functionals:

\[
\ell_q,\qquad \ell_p,\qquad \ell_G,
\]

with

\[
q_{N,j}=\ell_q(\Phi_{N,j}),
\qquad
p_{N,j}=\ell_p(\Phi_{N,j}),
\]

and

\[
\ell_p-G\ell_q=\ell_{\mathrm{rem}}
\]

represented by a fixed-sign kernel satisfying an exponential estimate.

The most efficient search is therefore:

1. transform the scalar recurrence to a differential operator for its generating function;
2. compare its singularities and local exponents with the pullback of (6.4);
3. solve for an algebraic/rational differential gauge;
4. convert that gauge into an Euler integral and a creative-telescoping identity of the form (7.1);
5. match the initial rows \((30921,-32972,8240)\) and \((33750,-36000,9000)\);
6. prove decay from the resulting positive or saddle-point-controlled remainder.

This is stronger than matching Poincaré polynomials and weaker than demanding a rational Ore map to the homogeneous symmetric square.

# 8. Comparison with the Rivoal–Zudilin approach

## 8.1 Rivoal–Zudilin, 2003

Rivoal and Zudilin’s paper

> T. Rivoal and W. Zudilin, *Diophantine properties of numbers related to Catalan’s constant*, Math. Ann. **326** (2003), 705–721

constructs hypergeometric linear forms in even Dirichlet beta values. Its main framework uses rational functions, very-well-poised hypergeometric series, denominator estimates, and permutation-group symmetries. It is designed primarily for arithmetic statements about several beta values, rather than for identifying this specific rank-three CMF product.

The method is constructive: the summand is explicit before telescoping, and Zeilberger-type recurrences are derived from it. That is exactly the feature missing in the present CMF analysis.

## 8.2 Zudilin’s Apéry-like Catalan recurrence

Zudilin’s paper

> W. Zudilin, *An Apéry-like difference equation for Catalan’s constant*, Electron. J. Combin. **10** (2003), R14; arXiv:math/0201024

starts from a family of very-well-poised hypergeometric series representing rational linear forms in \(G\), and applies Zeilberger’s creative-telescoping algorithm to obtain a second-order recurrence. The direction of proof is

\[
\text{explicit hypergeometric linear form}
\Longrightarrow
\text{recurrence}
\Longrightarrow
\text{continued fraction and approximation}.
\]

For Problem 2.5 we currently have the reverse situation:

\[
\text{matrix recurrence and numerical period}
\Longrightarrow
\text{need to reconstruct an explicit integrand}.
\]

## 8.3 “A second wind?”

Zudilin’s

> *Ramanujan-type formulae for \(1/\pi\): A second wind?*, arXiv:0712.1332

is primarily a survey of modular and hypergeometric proofs of Ramanujan-type \(1/\pi\) series. Its relevance here is conceptual: it emphasizes elliptic parametrization, pullbacks of Gauss equations, and connection formulas. It does not supply the finite Problem 2.5 Catalan kernel.

## 8.4 Structural comparison

The Rivoal–Zudilin and Zudilin constructions use an explicit hypergeometric summand first and derive the recurrence second. The current CMF problem supplies a high-degree recurrence first. The successful proof should reconstruct an integral or summand whose creative telescoper is the CMF operator.

The Brafman identity identifies the correct elliptic period geometry, but not the finite arithmetic normalization. The missing bridge is therefore a **vector creative-telescoping certificate or differential gauge to the integrated elliptic module**, not another Poincaré comparison.

# 9. Final answer to parts (a)–(e)

## (a) Denominators

For every column \(j\), the exact formula is

\[
q_{N,j}=\int_0^1(10-18x)
\bigl(\mathbf R_0(x)M_N\bigr)_j\,dx,
\]

with \(\mathbf R_0\) given by (0.1).

No non-circular formula in terms of a fixed rational combination of \(D_N^2,D_NE_N,E_N^2\) is presently certified; such a same-index formula would amount to the rational Ore map that the symbolic computation is reported to exclude.

## (b) Numerators

For every column \(j\),

\[
p_{N,j}=\int_0^1(6-12x)
\bigl(\mathbf R_0(x)M_N\bigr)_j\,dx.
\]

## (c) Remainder

The exact CMF linear form is

\[
p_{N,j}-Gq_{N,j}
=-\int_{[0,1]^2}
\frac{\bigl(\mathbf R_0((xy)^2)M_N\bigr)_j}
{1+(xy)^2}\,dx\,dy.
\]

It is exact but not manifestly decaying. The object that tends to zero is the ratio error, with expected/proved formal scale

\[
\frac{p_{N,j}}{q_{N,j}}-G
=O\!\left(N^{-3}(17-12\sqrt2)^N\right).
\]

A manifestly positive decaying integral is available for the independent Brafman truncations, namely (5.7), but those truncations are not the rational CMF approximants.

## (d) Delannoy kernel and Catalan period

The exact kernel formulas are (4.2), (4.3), the Hadamard integral (5.1), the real double integral (5.3), and the positive triple integral (5.5). Catalan’s constant appears as

\[
G=\frac12\int_0^1K(k)\,dk
=\frac{\pi\sqrt2}{2}
\int_0^\rho
\frac{1+z}{\sqrt z(1-z)}
\sum_{n\ge0}D_n^2z^n\,dz.
\]

## (e) Comparison with Rivoal–Zudilin and Zudilin

Their constructions begin from explicit very-well-poised hypergeometric linear forms and use creative telescoping to derive low-order recurrences. Problem 2.5 currently lacks precisely that explicit summand/integrand. Brafman supplies the elliptic period, but a vector telescoping certificate is still needed to identify the printed CMF product with it.
