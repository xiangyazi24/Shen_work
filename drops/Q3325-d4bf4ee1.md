## 1. Which approach?

### Do not take Approach A as a core-record edit yet

Adding `dx_u` joint continuity to `classicalRegularity` is mathematically natural, especially for the cosine/spectral heat solutions. If

```text
u(t,x) = Σ a_n(t) cos(nπx),
∂x u(t,x) = -Σ nπ a_n(t) sin(nπx),
```

and the weighted series is dominated uniformly on compact time windows, the Weierstrass M-test gives joint continuity of `∂x u`. For the constant solution constructor it is trivial.

But making it a field of `classicalRegularity` now forces edits across every constructor and consumer, and the existing repo already has a separate frontier layer for exactly this kind of regularity. A core-record edit is a large mechanical migration with little immediate proof gain.

### Do not take Approach B as the mainline

Approach B says: use the energy inequality to bound `G₂` by a continuous function and prove only measurability of `G₂`.

This is theoretically possible, but it is not the quickest Lean route. A bound by a continuous function is not enough for `IntegrableOn`; you also need `AEStronglyMeasurable` / measurability of the gradient-energy profile. Without joint continuity of `∂x u`, the measurable proof becomes a derivative-as-limit argument:

```text
∂x u(t,x) = lim_n n * (u(t,x+1/n) - u(t,x))
```

on a clipped interior, followed by measurability of the square, then measurability of the spatial integral as a function of `t`. That is a real formalization project and has endpoint/clipping complications. It is heavier than adding a local predicate and proving continuity from the spectral producer.

### Take Approach C: local adjunct predicate + p=2 first

The current best route is:

```text
C1. Define/consume a separate strict-window dx-joint-continuity predicate.
C2. Prove strict-window gradient-energy continuity/integrability from it.
C3. Specialize first to p = 2.
C4. Use the 1D H¹/Agmon bypass to get BoundedBefore.
C5. Only then decide whether universal-p Moser regularity is still needed.
```

This is compatible with both future directions:

* later fold `dx_u` joint continuity into `classicalRegularity`, if desired;
* or keep it as a producer-side predicate for spectral/semigroup solutions.

## 3. What about measurability formalization?

If forced to pursue Approach B, the minimal proof would be:

```text
1. Prove dx_u(t,x) is Borel/measurable on strict slabs as a pointwise limit of difference quotients.
2. Prove (t,x) ↦ dx_u(t,x)^2 is measurable.
3. Prove t ↦ ∫ dx_u(t,x)^2 dx is measurable.
4. Combine measurability with the energy/dissipation inequality bound to get integrability.
```

But each step is annoying in Lean without a named derivative profile.

### Why it is heavier than it looks

The derivative is with respect to `x`, while the profile is a function of `(t,x)`. To get measurability from only joint continuity of `u`, one has to express `∂x u` as a limit of measurable functions. On the interval domain this requires endpoint handling:

```text
x + h may leave [0,1]
```

so you need clipping, one-sided differences, or work only on `Ioo 0 1` and then handle endpoints as null sets. This is doable, but it is a lot of measure-theory plumbing.

Also, after obtaining joint measurability of the integrand, one still needs a theorem of the form:

```text
AEStronglyMeasurable (fun t => ∫ x, F t x)
```

or a parametric integral measurability theorem in the exact `intervalIntegral`/`IntegrableOn` shape used by the code. This is not the shortest path.

## 5. Single-p or universal-p?

Use **single-p = 2** first.

Reasons:

1. It is the actual downstream bypass: p=2 gives H¹, and in 1D H¹ gives L∞.
2. It avoids real-power chain rules for `u^(p/2)` in the gradient profile.
3. It avoids universal-p endpoint issues at `t=0`.
4. It avoids the circular Moser boundedness path.
5. Once `BoundedBefore` is known, universal-p estimates become easier and non-circular.

Universal-p Moser should be deferred until after boundedness, or kept as a stronger optional theorem. It should not block the main boundedness proof.

### 1. Which approach?

Use a narrow third approach: a separate dx-joint-continuity / p=2 gradient-energy-continuity predicate feeding the existing `P3MoserGradientIntegrability` layer, then the 1D H¹ bypass. Do not edit `classicalRegularity` first, and do not pursue measurability-only first.

Possible but not first choice. Without dx joint continuity, measurability requires proving `dx_u` is a pointwise limit of measurable difference quotients, handling interval endpoints, then pushing measurability through a spatial integral. That is heavier than a continuity predicate and is less useful for the p=2 H¹ route.

### 4. Third approach?

Yes: p=2 strict-window gradient-energy continuity/integrability plus H¹/Agmon. This is the route I recommend.

### 5. Single-p or universal-p?

Single-p p=2. Universal-p is only needed for the full Moser iteration. The fastest way to break the current loop is p=2 -> H¹ -> L∞ -> `BoundedBefore`; after that universal-p is optional and easier.
