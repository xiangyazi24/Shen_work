# 11. Noncircularity audit

The chain is noncircular for four separate reasons.

1. **Quadratic negativity is parameter-side.**
   `Paper531StabilityBudget.quadratic_neg` uses only the corrected speed threshold and root-window inequalities. `exists_common_bound_gt_MChi_of_weight_window` uses continuity in the scalar common bound. Neither theorem uses the Cauchy orbit or any energy decay.

2. **The eventual ceiling is maximum-principle-side.**
   `wholeLineCauchyGlobal_le_expCeiling_of_chi_nonpos` and
   `wholeLineCauchyGlobal_uniformLimsupLe_one_of_chi_nonpos` are proved from the nonpositive-sensitivity moving supersolution and canonical gluing. They do not use the weighted-energy inequality.

3. **The energy theorem uses only a target-slice bound.**
   The large construction clamp `M₀` supplies existence and regularity. The smaller selected `M` supplies the coefficient. No conclusion of the energy decay argument is used to place the slice in `[0,M]`; that placement comes from the ceiling.

4. **Gronwall starts after all PDE inputs are known.**
   Positive-time differentiability and exact-weight integrability are independently propagated from the initial weighted closeness. The scalar tail theorem then converts the already-established eventual differential inequality into decay.

Hence there is no bootstrap of the form “assume weighted decay to obtain the small uniform bound needed to prove weighted decay.” The uniform tail bound comes first, from the scalar ceiling.
