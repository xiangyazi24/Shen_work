# Parallel-chord variance for the Apéry orbit: pair variance, the generating-pair audit, and the Racah CD obstruction

## Executive verdict

The variance route is mathematically sound, but the **pair-collision variance** is the right primary target. It is strictly simpler than the triple-collision variance and already implies the desired bound in every direction.

Let

\[
Q_n=(a_n,b_n)\in\mathbf F_p^2,
\qquad 0\le n<p,
\]

and, for a projective normal direction \(\xi\in\mathbf P^1(\mathbf F_p)\), put

\[
\nu_\xi(c)=\#\{n:\xi\cdot Q_n=c\}.
\]

Define the projection energy

\[
E_\xi=\sum_{c\in\mathbf F_p}\nu_\xi(c)^2.
\]

The Apéry value energy in the question is \(E_{[0:1]}\). If \(P_\xi\) denotes the number of **nonuniversal unordered pairs** \(\{n,m\}\) with equal \(\xi\)-projection, then

\[
E_\xi=p+2U+2P_\xi,
\]

where \(U\) is the number of unordered state repetitions \(Q_n=Q_m\). Under the established palindromy and absence of additional state returns,

\[
U=\frac{p-1}{2},
\qquad
E_\xi=2p-1+2P_\xi.
\]

Every nonuniversal pair determines exactly one normal direction. Hence

\[
\sum_\xi P_\xi
=\binom p2-U,
\qquad
\mu_2:=\frac1{p+1}\sum_\xi P_\xi
=\frac{(p-1)^2}{2(p+1)}
=\frac{p-3}{2}+\frac2{p+1}.
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
V_2(p):=\sum_{\xi\in\mathbf P^1}
(P_\xi-\mu_2)^2\ll p^2
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\sup_\xi E_\xi\ll p.
}
\]

Indeed, \(P_\xi\le\mu_2+\sqrt{V_2}=O(p)\). This is a four-index parallel-secant statement, whereas the proposed triple variance is a six-index statement.

The two prioritized bounded-complexity ideas have sharply different outcomes.

1. **Generating-pair / \(t\)-Wronskian:** there is a genuine bounded-order differential-module structure, but the ordinary polynomial Wronskian is not the discrete Wronskian. The exact identity lives in the **Hadamard algebra** and is a finite trilogarithm. CFVZ applies directly only to the \(p\)-Lucas Apéry period, not to the companion. A mixed/unipotent extension is a plausible research program, not a currently available proof.
2. **Racah Christoffel--Darboux:** the proposed bounded separation-rank collapse of the diagonal divided difference is impossible. A simple coefficient-matrix argument shows that its tensor rank is exactly its degree, hence \(\asymp p\). Classical CD may give a \(2\times2\) numerator determinant, but the entries still have degree \(\asymp p\); it does not produce bounded genus or bounded conductor.

The most promising concrete program is therefore:

\[
\boxed{
\text{pair-direction variance}
\;\longrightarrow\;
\text{secant map }\Theta_h(x)
\;\longrightarrow\;
\text{the four-term continuant/Green-kernel identity}.
}
\]

# 1. Pair variance is sufficient and is easier than triple variance

## 1.1 Exact pair bookkeeping

Let

\[
\mathcal U=\{\{n,m\}:n<m,\ Q_n=Q_m\},
\qquad U=|\mathcal U|.
\]

For each \(\xi\), let

\[
P_\xi
=\#\{\{n,m\}:n<m,\ Q_n\ne Q_m,
\ \xi\cdot Q_n=\xi\cdot Q_m\}.
\]

Then

\[
\sum_c\binom{\nu_\xi(c)}2=U+P_\xi
\]

and therefore

\[
\boxed{E_\xi=p+2U+2P_\xi.}
\tag{1.1}
\]

Every nonzero difference \(Q_n-Q_m\) has one projective normal direction, so

\[
\boxed{
\sum_\xi P_\xi=\binom p2-U.
}
\tag{1.2}
\]

Put

\[
\mu_2=\frac{\binom p2-U}{p+1}.
\]

Then

\[
P_\xi\le\mu_2+\sqrt{V_2(p)},
\qquad
V_2(p)=\sum_\xi(P_\xi-\mu_2)^2.
\tag{1.3}
\]

Thus, as soon as \(U=O(p)\),

\[
V_2(p)=O(p^2)
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
E_\xi=O(p)
\]

uniformly in \(\xi\).

This remains valid if there are \(O(p)\) extra state repetitions beyond the forced palindromic ones. In the experimentally supported exact-palindrome-only case,

\[
U=\frac{p-1}{2},
\qquad
E_\xi=2p-1+2P_\xi,
\tag{1.4}
\]

and the average projection energy is

\[
\frac1{p+1}\sum_\xi E_\xi
=3p-4+\frac4{p+1}.
\tag{1.5}
\]

This is the deterministic direction-average version of the constant \(3\).

## 1.2 Triple variance is also sufficient, but unnecessarily harder

Define

\[
R_\xi=\sum_c\binom{\nu_\xi(c)}3.
\]

For every integer \(k\ge0\),

\[
\binom k2
\le \frac k2+3\binom k3,
\tag{1.6}
\]

because the difference between the right and left sides is

\[
\frac{k(k-2)^2}{2}\ge0.
\]

Summing (1.6) over fibers gives

\[
E_\xi
=p+2\sum_c\binom{\nu_\xi(c)}2
\le 2p+6R_\xi.
\tag{1.7}
\]

Therefore, if

\[
\sum_\xi R_\xi=O(p^2)
\]

and

\[
V_3(p):=\sum_\xi(R_\xi-\mu_3)^2=O(p^2),
\qquad
\mu_3=\frac1{p+1}\sum_\xi R_\xi=O(p),
\]

then every \(R_\xi=O(p)\), and hence every \(E_\xi=O(p)\).

But \(V_3\) counts pairs of parallel collinear triples and introduces six indices. The pair statistic \(V_2\) counts pairs of parallel secants and uses only four indices. Unless the triple certificate has a special factorization unavailable at pair level, \(V_2\) is the better theorem to attack.

## 1.3 Correct interpretation of the measured pair variance

With only the forced palindromic state repetitions,

\[
\mu_2
=\frac{(p-1)^2}{2(p+1)}
\sim\frac p2.
\]

At \(p=503\), a direction standard deviation near \(30\) gives

\[
V_2\approx504\cdot30^2
=1.79\,p^2.
\]

At \(p=1009\), a standard deviation near \(45\) gives

\[
V_2\approx1010\cdot45^2
=2.01\,p^2.
\]

Thus the data are already almost perfectly consistent with

\[
\boxed{V_2(p)\sim 2p^2.}
\]

The corresponding per-direction variance is about

\[
4\mu_2,
\]

so the standard deviation is approximately

\[
2\sqrt{\mu_2}.
\]

This explains the apparent relative standard deviations:

\[
\frac{2\sqrt{\mu_2}}{\mu_2}
\asymp \frac1{\sqrt p}.
\]

A ratio near \(0.1\) at \(p\approx500\) is therefore not evidence for \(p^3\)-scale total variance. It is the finite-size manifestation of a constant-factor-overdispersed Poisson law with total variance \(O(p^2)\).

For triples, the measured \(V_3\approx6p^2\) is the analogous statement, with a larger overdispersion constant.

# 2. Exact Fourier/Radon formulation of the pair variance

This gives a cleaner analytic target than the six-point count.

Let \(f:\mathbf F_p^2\to\mathbf Z_{\ge0}\) be the state multiplicity function

\[
f(x)=\#\{n:Q_n=x\}.
\]

Define the unnormalized Fourier transform

\[
\widehat f(\omega)
=\sum_{x\in\mathbf F_p^2}f(x)e_p(\omega\cdot x)
=\sum_{n=0}^{p-1}e_p(\omega\cdot Q_n).
\tag{2.1}
\]

For a nonzero normal vector representing \(\xi\), one-dimensional Fourier inversion on the projection gives the exact slice identity

\[
\boxed{
E_\xi
=\frac1p\sum_{t\in\mathbf F_p}
|\widehat f(t\xi)|^2.
}
\tag{2.2}
\]

Let \(\ell_\xi=\{t\xi:t\in\mathbf F_p\}\) be the radial frequency line and put

\[
A_\xi
=\sum_{\omega\in\ell_\xi\setminus\{0\}}
|\widehat f(\omega)|^2.
\]

Since \(\widehat f(0)=p\),

\[
E_\xi=p+\frac{A_\xi}{p}.
\tag{2.3}
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
\sum_\xi(E_\xi-\overline E)^2
=\frac1{p^2}\sum_\xi(A_\xi-\overline A)^2
=4V_2(p).
}
\tag{2.4}
\]

So the desired pair variance is exactly **radial equidistribution of the Fourier energy** of the orbit measure.

If the only state repetitions are the palindromic pairs, then

\[
\sum_x f(x)^2=2p-1.
\]

Two-dimensional Parseval gives

\[
\sum_{\omega\in\mathbf F_p^2}
|\widehat f(\omega)|^2
=p^2(2p-1),
\]

and hence (1.5) follows immediately from (2.2).

This formulation identifies a precise bounded-conductor theorem that would finish the problem:

> Construct a bounded-complexity sheaf or exponential-sum family whose trace at \((c,d)\) is
> \[
> S_p(c,d)=\sum_n e_p(ca_n+db_n),
> \]
> and prove that the radial masses
> \[
> \sum_{u\ne0}|S_p(uc,ud)|^2
> \]
> have total variance \(O(p^4)\).

By (2.4), this is equivalent to \(V_2=O(p^2)\).

A uniform bound

\[
|S_p(c,d)|\ll\sqrt p
\]

would be stronger and would give \(E_\xi=O(p)\) directly. The variance theorem asks for much less.

Finite-field Radon and X-ray transform estimates explain identity (2.2), but they do not give the required radial anti-concentration for an arbitrary point set. A point set on one affine line is an immediate counterexample. See, for example, Doowon Koh, [*Sharp endpoint estimates for the X-ray transform and the Radon transform in finite fields*](https://arxiv.org/abs/1209.1215).

# 3. Point-count interpretation: what bounded complexity would actually give

## 3.1 Why the raw six-variable count is organized incorrectly

A pair of parallel collinear triples is described by six indices and three scalar equations, so the expected dimension is three and the leading count is \(\asymp p^3\).

Even for a smooth, fixed-complexity threefold, a generic Weil--Deligne estimate is

\[
\#Y(\mathbf F_p)
=c p^3+O(p^{5/2}),
\]

not \(cp^3+O(p^2)\). Thus a direct appeal to a threefold point-count theorem is not enough.

The variance subtraction is load-bearing. It removes the constant part of the family. To see the right mechanism, one should organize the triples as a surface fibred over direction:

\[
\Theta:X\longrightarrow\mathbf P^1,
\qquad
R_\xi=\#X_\xi(\mathbf F_p).
\]

If, after removing forced components,

- \(X\) and \(\Theta\) have complexity bounded independently of \(p\);
- every regular fiber is a curve with bounded total Betti number;
- the number and complexity of singular fibers are bounded;
- the number of geometric one-dimensional components is constant on the regular locus;

then fiberwise Weil gives

\[
R_\xi=c p+O(\sqrt p)
\]

for regular \(\xi\), while each of the \(O(1)\) singular directions contributes at most \(O(p^2)\) to the total squared deviation. Consequently

\[
\sum_\xi(R_\xi-\overline R)^2=O(p^2).
\]

This is the precise reason a bounded-conductor realization would solve the problem.

Equivalently, the nonconstant part of

\[
R^1\Theta_!\mathbf Q_\ell
\]

would have bounded rank and conductor. Katz/Deligne trace estimates would then imply the variance bound. The framework of arithmetic Fourier transforms of fixed perverse sheaves is relevant only after such a fixed-complexity object has been constructed; see Forey--Fresán--Kowalski, [*Arithmetic Fourier transforms over finite fields*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.11961).

At present the certificate polynomials have degrees growing with the gaps and ultimately with \(p\). Their Betti numbers are not uniformly bounded. There is no black-box point-count theorem that ignores this growth.

## 3.2 The pair version is the minimal geometric target

For nonuniversal pairs define the secant-direction map

\[
\Theta(n,m)
=\bigl[b_m-b_n:-(a_m-a_n)\bigr]
\in\mathbf P^1.
\tag{3.1}
\]

Then

\[
P_\xi=\#\Theta^{-1}(\xi).
\]

Thus \(V_2=O(p^2)\) would follow from a bounded-complexity model of the two-dimensional secant domain fibred over \(\mathbf P^1\), again with bounded-genus curve fibers. This is simpler than constructing the corresponding triple-incidence surface.

No known Vinogradov-style decomposition proves this for a deterministic nonautonomous orbit without an additional low-complexity or trace-function input. The plausible analytic substitute is an averaged large sieve for the family of gap-direction maps introduced below.

# 4. The generating pair and the correct \(t\)-Wronskian audit

This is the first prioritized question.

Write

\[
\mathscr B(t)=\sum_{n\ge0}b_nt^n,
\qquad
\mathscr A(t)=\sum_{n\ge0}a_nt^n,
\qquad
\theta=t\frac d{dt}.
\]

Let

\[
\mathcal L
=\theta^3
-t(2\theta+1)(17\theta^2+17\theta+5)
+t^2(\theta+1)^3.
\tag{4.1}
\]

Summing the recurrence from \(n\ge1\) gives, for any recurrence solution \(u\) with generating series \(U\),

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal L U=(u_1-5u_0)t.
}
\tag{4.2}
\]

Consequently

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal L\mathscr B=0,
\qquad
\mathcal L\mathscr A=6t.
}
\tag{4.3}
\]

This has two important consequences.

1. The generating Picard--Fuchs operator is order three; it is the symmetric-square-type object behind the rank-two elliptic period.
2. \(\mathscr A\) is not a second homogeneous solution paired with \(\mathscr B\). It is an **inhomogeneous/logarithmic extension**.

Therefore the ordinary first-order Wronskian

\[
\mathscr A\mathscr B'-\mathscr A'\mathscr B
\]

is not governed by the Abel identity for a second-order equation, and there is no reason for it to have bounded degree or a simple determinant.

## 4.1 The exact rank-two matrix exists in the index variable

At the sequence level,

\[
Y_n=
\begin{pmatrix}
 a_{n+1}&b_{n+1}\\
 a_n&b_n
\end{pmatrix}
\]

satisfies

\[
\boxed{
\det Y_n
=a_{n+1}b_n-a_nb_{n+1}
=\frac6{(n+1)^3}.
}
\tag{4.4}
\]

This is the true rank-two structure.

However, if one forms the ordinary matrix generating polynomial

\[
\sum_nY_nt^n,
\]

its determinant uses **Cauchy convolution** of coefficients. Equation (4.4) controls coefficientwise products. In general,

\[
\det\left(\sum_nY_nt^n\right)
\ne
\sum_n\det(Y_n)t^n.
\]

This is the exact obstruction to the proposed small ordinary \(t\)-Wronskian.

## 4.2 The correct generating identity is a Hadamard Wronskian

For polynomials or series \(F=\sum f_nt^n\), \(G=\sum g_nt^n\), define

\[
F\odot G=\sum_nf_ng_nt^n
\]

and the shift

\[
\mathsf S F=\frac{F-f_0}{t}=\sum_{n\ge0}f_{n+1}t^n.
\]

Then (4.4) gives the exact identity, reduced modulo any prime \(p\ge5\),

\[
\boxed{
(\mathsf S\mathscr A_p)\odot\mathscr B_p
-\mathscr A_p\odot(\mathsf S\mathscr B_p)
=6\sum_{n=0}^{p-2}\frac{t^n}{(n+1)^3}.
}
\tag{4.5}
\]

Equivalently, with the finite trilogarithm

\[
\operatorname{li}_{3,p}(t)
=\sum_{j=1}^{p-1}\frac{t^j}{j^3},
\]

one has

\[
\boxed{
(\mathsf S\mathscr A_p)\odot\mathscr B_p
-\mathscr A_p\odot(\mathsf S\mathscr B_p)
=\frac{6}{t}\operatorname{li}_{3,p}(t).
}
\tag{4.6}
\]

The finite polylogarithm has fixed depth:

\[
\theta^3\operatorname{li}_{3,p}(t)
=\sum_{j=1}^{p-1}t^j
=\frac{t^p-t}{t-1}.
\tag{4.7}
\]

This is genuine additional structure. It suggests that the companion belongs to a bounded-length unipotent/polylogarithmic extension of the Apéry period. Finite and \(p\)-adic polylogarithms are studied, for example, by Besser: [*Finite and p-adic polylogarithms*](https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0006051).

But (4.6) lives in the Hadamard algebra. Collision geometry of the coefficients is not converted into an ordinary low-degree algebraic curve by (4.6). In characteristic \(p\), the group algebra of the additive cyclic group of order \(p\) is nonsemisimple, so there is no ordinary \(p\)-point DFT over \(\mathbf F_p\) that diagonalizes this Hadamard structure.

## 4.3 What CFVZ gives, and what it does not give

Caruso--Fürnsinn--Vargas-Montoya--Zudilin prove for the Apéry **period truncation**

\[
\mathscr B_p(t)=\sum_{n=0}^{p-1}b_nt^n
\]

a factorization

\[
\mathscr B_p(t)=D_p(t)R_p(t)^2,
\qquad
D_p(t)\in\{1,t^2-34t+1\},
\]

using the \(p\)-Lucas relation and the modular parametrization

\[
\mathscr B(t(x))=(1+x)h(x)^2,
\qquad
 t=\frac{x(1-8x)}{1+x}.
\]

See [*Galois Groups of Apéry-like Series Modulo Primes*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.23298).

The companion \(\mathscr A\) is not \(p\)-Lucas and satisfies the inhomogeneous equation (4.3). The CFVZ Kummer argument therefore does not directly give an analogous square factorization for

\[
\mathscr A_p(t)=\sum_{n=0}^{p-1}a_nt^n.
\]

A realistic extension program is:

1. find a hypergeometric or modular deformation \(\mathscr B(t;\varepsilon)\) for which the Apéry companion is a fixed logarithmic derivative—possibly of order three—in \(\varepsilon\);
2. differentiate the CFVZ modular parametrization and the Frobenius/Lucas relation;
3. obtain an upper-triangular mixed Frobenius matrix whose diagonal is the known Apéry Kummer character and whose extension class is a finite-polylogarithmic function;
4. prove that this mixed object has conductor bounded independently of \(p\).

The harmonic-weight-three form of \(a_n\) makes such a deformation plausible, but the required identity has not been established in the current project state.

Even success here would not automatically prove the direction variance. One would still need to transport the bounded-complexity object from the geometric variable \(t\) to the **coefficient-character variable** \(n\), and then control a mod-\(p\) additive character of its Mellin transform. That is a genuine local-limit theorem, not a formal consequence of the factorization.

## 4.4 The most useful generating-pair theorem to seek

A theorem strong enough to feed the variance argument would construct a fixed-rank mixed sheaf/crystal \(\mathcal M\) and a two-parameter trace function

\[
(c,d,n)\longmapsto ca_n+db_n
\]

on direction \(\times\) character index, with conductor bounded independently of \(p\). One would then apply Deligne to the fiber product controlling the radial fourth moment in (2.4).

The ordinary polynomial identity

\[
\mathscr A_p\mathscr B_p'
-\mathscr B_p\mathscr A_p'
=\text{small}
\]

is not the right target. The correct first target is the mixed-module/deformation identity behind (4.6).

# 5. The secant Green kernel: a more direct rank-two route

The rank-two recurrence gives all two-state determinants explicitly. Put

\[
K(r,s)=\det(Q_r,Q_s)=a_rb_s-b_ra_s.
\]

The established cross-kernel/continuant formula expresses \(K(r,s)\) through the Wronskian at the earlier index and the gap continuant.

For gap \(h\), define the chord normal

\[
\Theta_h(x)
=\bigl[b_{x+h}-b_x:-(a_{x+h}-a_x)\bigr].
\tag{5.1}
\]

Two chords are parallel exactly when

\[
\Theta_h(x)=\Theta_k(y).
\]

Equivalently,

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\Pi_{h,k}(x,y)
:={}&\det(Q_{x+h}-Q_x,Q_{y+k}-Q_y)\\
={}&K(x+h,y+k)-K(x+h,y)\\
&-K(x,y+k)+K(x,y)=0.
\end{aligned}
}
\tag{5.2}
\]

This four-term mixed difference is the exact parallel-secant certificate. It uses only the known rank-two Green kernel and is closer to \(V_2\) than the triple certificate.

After choosing a unique representation of unordered pairs, the pair variance can be written schematically as

\[
V_2
=\sum_{h,k}
\left(
\#\{(x,y):\Theta_h(x)=\Theta_k(y)\}
-\frac{M_hM_k}{p+1}
\right),
\tag{5.3}
\]

with explicit diagonal and palindromic corrections.

This suggests a Linnik/Vinogradov-style program:

- use the cocycle identity for gap transfer matrices to split long gaps dyadically;
- exploit the continuant expression in each dyadic block;
- prove a large-sieve inequality for the rational maps \(\Theta_h\);
- use the Plücker identity for the kernels \(K(r,s)\) to simplify repeated-gap terms.

No existing theorem carries this out, but it is a more faithful use of the rank-two recurrence than the ordinary generating-polynomial Wronskian.

A particularly sharp geometric sub-conjecture is:

> After removing diagonal, palindromic, and state-return components, the fiber products
> \[
> \Theta_h(x)=\Theta_k(y)
> \]
> have total nonconstant cohomology whose average over \((h,k)\) is \(O(1)\).

This averaged statement is enough; individual fiber-product genera may grow with \(h+k\).

# 6. The Racah Christoffel--Darboux proposal: a rigorous obstruction

This is the second prioritized question.

Let

\[
\sigma(x)=x(x+1)
\]

and

\[
\Phi_k(X)
=\frac{\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}(X-j(j+1))}{(k!)^2}.
\]

Then

\[
b_n=\sum_{k=0}^n\Phi_k(\sigma(n))^2,
\]

and the self-dual hypergeometric kernel may be written

\[
\mathcal R(r,s)
=\sum_k\Phi_k(\sigma(r))\Phi_k(\sigma(s))
={}_4F_3\!\left(
\begin{matrix}-r,r+1,-s,s+1\\1,1,1\end{matrix};1
\right).
\]

## 6.1 Why the standard CD formula does not apply in the hoped-for way

The classical Christoffel--Darboux identity concerns an orthogonal sequence \(P_k\) with fixed truncation index \(N\):

\[
\sum_{k=0}^N\frac{P_k(X)P_k(Y)}{h_k}
=c_N\frac{P_{N+1}(X)P_N(Y)-P_N(X)P_{N+1}(Y)}{X-Y}.
\tag{6.1}
\]

The expression \(\mathcal R(r,s)\) above is a **single Racah polynomial/6j coefficient**, symmetric by self-duality. Its terminating hypergeometric expansion is not automatically the reproducing kernel (6.1):

- the \(\Phi_k\) are Newton factors, not the normalized Racah orthogonal sequence;
- the weights are not the Racah norm weights;
- the truncation endpoint depends on \(r,s\);
- on the diagonal \(r=s=n\), the degree label and the spectral variable are tied together.

Even if one rewrites a related fixed-\(N\) Racah kernel using CD, comparing \(b_x\) and \(b_y\) changes the diagonal/truncation endpoint, creating tail terms rather than one fixed boundary Casoratian.

For standard CD theory see Barry Simon, [*The Christoffel--Darboux Kernel*](https://arxiv.org/abs/0806.1528), and for Racah bispectrality see Geronimo--Iliev, [*Bispectrality of multivariable Racah--Wilson polynomials*](https://arxiv.org/abs/0705.1469).

## 6.2 Full-rank theorem for every diagonal divided difference

There is a decisive algebraic obstruction independent of the precise Racah normalization.

Palindromy implies that the Lagrange interpolation polynomial \(B_p(x)\) belongs to the invariant ring

\[
\mathbf F_p[x]^{x\mapsto-1-x}
=\mathbf F_p[\sigma(x)].
\]

Hence

\[
B_p(x)=q_p(\sigma(x))
\]

for a polynomial \(q_p(X)\) of degree

\[
d=\frac{p-1}{2}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\frac{p-3}{2}.
\]

Consider

\[
H_p(X,Y)
=\frac{q_p(X)-q_p(Y)}{X-Y}.
\tag{6.2}
\]

Write

\[
q_p(X)=\sum_{j=0}^d c_jX^j,
\qquad c_d\ne0.
\]

Then

\[
H_p(X,Y)
=\sum_{j=1}^dc_j\sum_{r=0}^{j-1}X^{j-1-r}Y^r.
\tag{6.3}
\]

In the monomial bases \(1,X,\ldots,X^{d-1}\) and \(1,Y,\ldots,Y^{d-1}\), the coefficient matrix is the truncated Hankel matrix

\[
M_{i,r}=c_{i+r+1}
\quad(i+r\le d-1),
\]

and zero otherwise. Reversing the columns makes this matrix triangular with diagonal entries \(c_d\). Therefore

\[
\boxed{\operatorname{rank}M=d.}
\tag{6.4}
\]

Equivalently, every separated representation

\[
H_p(X,Y)=\sum_{j=1}^R F_j(X)G_j(Y)
\]

requires

\[
\boxed{R\ge d\asymp p.}
\tag{6.5}
\]

Tensor rank is invariant under invertible changes of polynomial basis. Passing from monomials to a Racah basis cannot reduce this to bounded rank.

Finally,

\[
\sigma(x+h)-\sigma(x)
=h(2x+h+1),
\]

so the user's divided-difference polynomial is exactly

\[
D_p(x,h)
=H_p(\sigma(x+h),\sigma(x)).
\tag{6.6}
\]

Thus a bounded-rank sum-of-products CD identity for \(D_p\) is rigorously ruled out.

## 6.3 A \(2\times2\) determinant would not be enough anyway

A classical CD formula may compress a long sum into

\[
\det
\begin{pmatrix}
P_{N+1}(X)&P_N(X)\\
P_{N+1}(Y)&P_N(Y)
\end{pmatrix},
\]

but the entries have degree \(N\). The zero curve can still have genus \(\asymp N^2\). Bounded **matrix size** is not bounded algebraic complexity.

The coefficient matrix in (6.3) has bounded **displacement rank** because it is Hankel. That property is universal for univariate divided differences and is useful computationally, but it does not give bounded genus or a uniform Weil constant.

## 6.4 What Racah bispectrality may still provide

The useful salvage is not bounded rank, but bounded-order **difference equations**. One can seek:

- a low-order partial difference equation for \(H_p(X,Y)\);
- an isomonodromic/Lax description of the secant maps \(\Theta_h\);
- factorization of the four-term kernel \(\Pi_{h,k}\) in (5.2);
- uniform control of singularities or monodromy after quotienting forced factors.

These would exploit Racah structure without contradicting the full-rank theorem.

# 7. What finite-field incidence geometry gives

The answer is substantially weaker than the desired theorem.

For a fixed direction \(\xi\), let

\[
K_\xi=\max_c\nu_\xi(c).
\]

Then

\[
\boxed{
E_\xi=\sum_c\nu_\xi(c)^2
\le K_\xi\sum_c\nu_\xi(c)
=pK_\xi.
}
\tag{7.1}
\]

Thus a new bound \(K_\xi\ll p^\alpha\) would give \(E_\xi\ll p^{1+\alpha}\). But incidence theory does not supply a nontrivial uniform bound on \(K_\xi\).

For the complete family of \(p\) parallel lines of one direction, the total number of incidences with a \(p\)-point set is exactly \(p\), regardless of whether the occupancies are

\[
1,1,\ldots,1
\]

or

\[
p,0,\ldots,0.
\]

Consequently point-line incidence theorems are essentially blind to the quantity \(\sum_c\nu_\xi(c)^2\) inside a fixed parallel class.

Vinh's spectral incidence theorem and the Stevens--de Zeeuw improvements bound total incidences with a collection of lines; see

- Le Anh Vinh, [*Szemerédi--Trotter type theorem and sum-product estimate in finite fields*](https://arxiv.org/abs/0711.4427);
- Stevens--de Zeeuw, [*An Improved Point-Line Incidence Bound Over Arbitrary Fields*](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.06284);
- Rudnev, [*Point-plane incidences and some applications in positive characteristic*](https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.03534).

Their rich-line estimates contain a term of order \(p/k\), which is sharp for \(p/k\) disjoint parallel \(k\)-rich lines. Such a configuration has projection energy \(\asymp pk\), so those estimates cannot force \(k=O(1)\).

Near-injectivity of the states does not fix this. A set of \(p/2\) distinct points, each repeated by palindromy, can lie on one affine line and have \(E_\xi\asymp p^2\). Nor does the fact that the entire orbit lies on no fixed low-degree curve rule out a positive proportion of the points lying on one line while the remaining points are generic.

Therefore, from cardinality, palindromy, and near-injectivity alone, the sharp unconditional bound remains

\[
\boxed{E_\xi\le p^2.}
\tag{7.2}
\]

There is no general incidence-geometric \(O(p^{3/2})\) bound. Such a bound would already require an independent estimate \(K_\xi=O(\sqrt p)\), which is essentially a value-fiber theorem for the orbit.

# 8. Direct answer to the six-variable / square-root-cancellation question

There is no known general method that gives the required \(O(p^2)\) centered error for this deterministic orbit without first producing one of the following:

1. a bounded-complexity algebraic fibration over directions;
2. a bounded-conductor trace-function realization;
3. a bilinear decomposition whose completed sums are fixed-degree algebraic exponential sums;
4. an integrable identity reducing long gaps to bounded-complexity pieces.

The Mellin identity

\[
b_j=-\sum_{t\in\mathbf F_p^\times}\mathscr B_p(t)t^{-j}
\]

and its tautological vector analogue for \((a_j,b_j)\) do not by themselves help, because

\[
e_p\!\left(u\sum_t\mathscr B_p(t)t^{-j}\right)
\]

is a nonlinear additive character of a Mellin sum. Current Mellin equidistribution theorems concern linear \(\ell\)-adic traces of fixed sheaves, not this same-characteristic nonlinear transform.

A Vinogradov decomposition in the gap variables is conceivable. The exact starting point should be (5.3), not the six-index triple count. Short gaps can be treated by the known polynomial certificates; long gaps must be split using the transfer-cocycle identity. The missing theorem is an averaged large sieve for the family \(\{\Theta_h\}\).

# 9. Recommended experimental program

## 9.1 Pair variance before triple variance

For each tested prime compute

\[
V_2(p)=\sum_\xi(P_\xi-\mu_2)^2
\]

and report

\[
\frac{V_2(p)}{p^2},
\qquad
\frac{\operatorname{Var}_\xi(P_\xi)}{\mu_2}.
\]

The current two data points predict limits near

\[
\frac{V_2}{p^2}\approx2,
\qquad
\frac{\operatorname{Var}_\xi(P_\xi)}{\mu_2}\approx4.
\]

Also compute the gap covariance matrix

\[
\mathcal C_{h,k}
=\sum_\xi
\left(P_{\xi,h}-\frac{M_h}{p+1}\right)
\left(P_{\xi,k}-\frac{M_k}{p+1}\right).
\]

Rapid decay away from \(h=k\), or low numerical rank after dyadic aggregation, would identify the correct dispersion decomposition.

## 9.2 Verify the exact Hadamard Wronskian

For each prime, form \(\mathscr A_p,\mathscr B_p\) and verify (4.6) symbolically. Separately record:

- degree and factorization of the **ordinary** Wronskian
  \[
  \mathscr A_p\mathscr B_p'-\mathscr A_p'\mathscr B_p;
  \]
- gcd and joint factorization statistics of \(\mathscr A_p,\mathscr B_p\);
- behavior after the CFVZ substitution \(t=x(1-8x)/(1+x)\).

This will quickly distinguish an ordinary determinant phenomenon from the proven Hadamard/polylogarithmic one.

## 9.3 Search for the companion as a deformation derivative

Start from a hypergeometric deformation of

\[
{}_4F_3(-n,-n,n+1,n+1;1,1,1;1)
\]

and test whether a first, second, or third parameter derivative reproduces \(a_n\), modulo an explicit multiple of \(b_n\). If successful, differentiate the modular parametrization

\[
\mathscr B(t(x))=(1+x)h(x)^2.
\]

The expected output is a bounded-length unipotent extension, not a second ordinary homogeneous solution.

## 9.4 Refute or refine the CD hypothesis computationally

Construct the coefficient matrix of

\[
\frac{q_p(X)-q_p(Y)}{X-Y}.
\]

Its rank should equal \(\deg q_p\) exactly, by (6.4). This is a useful regression test: any claimed bounded separated-rank formula must be using a different object.

Then compute displacement rank and the minimal-order partial difference equation. Those are the plausible Racah invariants that may remain bounded.

## 9.5 Analyze the four-term secant certificate

For small \(h,k\), compute and factor

\[
\Pi_{h,k}(x,y)
=K(x+h,y+k)-K(x+h,y)-K(x,y+k)+K(x,y).
\]

After removing forced factors, record:

- irreducible components;
- normalization genus;
- singularity count;
- monodromy of projection to one variable;
- behavior under \((h,k)\mapsto(p-h,p-k)\) and reflection;
- any Dodgson or Plücker factorization.

This is the most direct bounded-complexity diagnostic for the pair variance.

# 10. Paper-ready sufficient propositions

## Proposition A: pair-direction variance criterion

Let \(Q_0,\ldots,Q_{p-1}\in\mathbf F_p^2\), let \(U\) be the number of unordered equal-state pairs, and let \(P_\xi\) count non-equal-state pairs with equal projection in direction \(\xi\). If

\[
U=O(p)
\]

and

\[
\sum_{\xi\in\mathbf P^1}
\left(P_\xi-rac{\binom p2-U}{p+1}\right)^2
=O(p^2),
\]

then

\[
\sup_\xi\sum_c\nu_\xi(c)^2=O(p).
\]

*Proof.* Use (1.1)--(1.3). \(\square\)

## Proposition B: bounded-fiber-complexity criterion

Suppose the nonuniversal secant-direction map admits, for all sufficiently large \(p\), a model over \(\mathbf F_p\) of uniformly bounded complexity whose regular fibers are curves with uniformly bounded total Betti number, with only \(O(1)\) singular directions and \(O(p)\) points in each singular fiber. Then

\[
V_2(p)=O(p^2)
\]

and hence \(E(p)=O(p)\).

## Proposition C: Racah rank obstruction

If \(q\in\mathbf F_p[X]\) has degree \(d\ge1\), then the separation rank of

\[
\frac{q(X)-q(Y)}{X-Y}
\]

is exactly \(d\). In particular, the palindromic Apéry divided difference has separation rank \((p-1)/2\) or \((p-3)/2\); no bounded-rank CD decomposition exists.

# 11. Priority ranking

1. **Pair-direction variance / four-term secant kernel:** best immediate target; exactly sufficient; four-point rather than six-point.
2. **Companion as a mixed modular/hypergeometric deformation:** best route to a genuinely bounded-complexity object, but it still requires a character-index local-limit theorem.
3. **Racah bispectral equations for the secant kernel:** worthwhile, but the proposed bounded-rank CD identity is ruled out.
4. **Generic finite-field incidence geometry:** cannot control one parallel class and does not improve the unconditional \(p^2\) energy bound.
5. **Direct six-variable point count:** too coarse unless reorganized as a bounded-complexity family over directions.

The most concrete next theorem to pursue is therefore

\[
\boxed{
\sum_{\xi\in\mathbf P^1(\mathbf F_p)}
\left(
\#\{(x,h):\Theta_h(x)=\xi\}-\mu_2
\right)^2
\ll p^2,
}
\]

with the palindromic and unordered-pair corrections made explicit. The exact kernel (5.2) is the load-bearing algebraic object.
