# 11. Hidden circularities and false shortcuts

The following claims are false, incomplete, or circular.

1. **`BUC` implies cap-`L2`.** False. `capWeight eta R` has a positive right plateau.

2. **Use `capWeightedPopulationX_eq_three_gradient_legs`.** Wrong target. It caps an already exponentially weighted field and inserts the exponential twice relative to raw-cap exhaustion.

3. **Integrate the heat Hessian against the value-controlled flux.** The operator has a nonintegrable `tau^(-1)` endpoint singularity.

4. **Spatially integrate by parts to `HeatGrad(flux_x)`.** Circular when the available weighted `flux_x` theorem consumes `hWx2`.

5. **Use `capFluxDerivativeExpanded_data_of_raw_population_H1`.** Circular: its raw population `H1` premise is the desired output.

6. **Apply the one-profile clamped quotient theorem as the matched perturbation theorem.** It does not encode cancellation against the wave.

7. **Claim the new coarse genuine source theorem depends only on `H0` and `X_delta`.** Its current public statement also requires `hUquot`.

8. **Drop the relative wave increment in `1<p.m<2`.** The coefficient derivative is singular as `U -> 0`; the committed relative secant theorem is the necessary repair.

9. **Remove the clamp on arbitrary Picard iterates.** Clamp inactivity is available for the final physical solution and wave comparator, not for all globally clamped iterates.

10. **Choose pointwise `L2` witnesses independently in time.** This does not create a strongly measurable Bochner history.

11. **Use global product-`L1` Fubini.** The natural data are weighted `L2`; use finite-time product-square integrability, Schur estimates, and an `L2` Bochner representative theorem.

12. **Ignore the zero-lag endpoint.** Positive-lag kernel theorems apply only off the terminal history point; remove it as a null set.
