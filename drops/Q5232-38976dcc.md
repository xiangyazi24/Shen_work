# 10. Hidden circularities and false shortcuts

The following claims would be invalid or circular.

1. **Using the committed one-profile source quotient theorem as the perturbation theorem.**
   `capWeighted_coMovingTruncatedChemotaxis_spatialDifferenceQuotient_l2_bounded`
   controls a shifted hard-clamped source by `D_delta u`. It does not encode the matched cancellation against the wave. Applied separately, it introduces nonperturbative wave terms and does not close exact-weight `hWx2`.

2. **Spatial integration by parts to `HeatGrad(flux_x)`.**
   The currently available weighted `flux_x` estimate consumes raw population `H1`; using it here is the target in disguise.

3. **Using `capFluxDerivativeExpanded_data_of_raw_population_H1`.**
   This is a successor theorem. Its raw-derivative premise is precisely what the difference-quotient argument is meant to produce.

4. **Inferring cap `L2` from BUC.**
   `capWeight eta R` has a positive right plateau. A bounded nondecaying function need not be cap-square-integrable.

5. **Choosing pointwise `L2` witnesses independently in history time.**
   Pointwise existential representatives do not provide a strongly measurable Bochner history.

6. **Using unrestricted product-`L1` Fubini.**
   The sources are naturally in weighted `L2`, not global `L1` in space. Use Schur estimates, product-square Tonelli on finite time windows, and the `L2` Bochner representative theorem instead.

7. **Removing the clamp on Picard iterates.**
   Clamp inactivity is available for the final physical solution, not for arbitrary globally clamped iterates.

8. **Dropping the relative wave increment in `1<q<2`.**
   An absolute secant bound alone does not control the singular variation of `q*r^(q-1)` near a wave tail where `U -> 0`. The newly committed relative-segment estimate is essential.
