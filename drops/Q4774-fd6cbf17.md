> Let `w : ℝ → ℝ` be a globally defined differentiable solution of
> \[
> w'+w^2+a(x)w+b(x)=0
> \]
> with `|a(x)| ≤ A`, `|b(x)| ≤ B`, and `A,B ≥ 0`. Then
> \[
> |w(x)|\le \frac{A+\sqrt{A^2+4B}}2
> \quad\text{for every }x\in\mathbb R.
> \]

# 1. The sharp global Riccati lemma

Assume

\[
A\ge0,\qquad B\ge0,
\]

and define

\[
D:=\sqrt{A^2+4B},
\qquad
R:=\frac{A+D}{2},
\qquad
S:=\frac{D-A}{2}.
\]

Then

\[
R\ge0,\qquad S\ge0,
\qquad R-S=A,
\qquad RS=B,
\]

and therefore

\[
z^2-Az-B=(z-R)(z+S).
\]

The companion number `S` is useful because it avoids division by `R`; the degenerate case `A=B=0`, where `R=0`, is handled without a separate branch.

## 1.1 The single forward blow-up lemma

The only genuine analytic lemma needed is this.

> **Forward super-Riccati blow-up.** Let `y : [0,∞) → ℝ` be differentiable and globally finite. Suppose `y(0)>R` and, whenever `y(t)>R`,
> \[
> y'(t)\ge (y(t)-R)(y(t)+S).
> \]
> Then a contradiction follows.

### Step 1: the region `y>R` is forward invariant

Initially `y(0)>R`. While `y>R`, both factors on the right are positive, so `y'>0`.

If `y` ever reached `R`, let `τ` be its first hitting time. On every compact interval `[0,s]` with `s<τ`, the derivative is positive, hence `y` is increasing there. Thus

\[
y(s)\ge y(0)>R\qquad(0\le s<\tau).
\]

Continuity and `s ↑ τ` give `y(τ) ≥ y(0)>R`, contradicting `y(τ)=R`. Therefore

\[
y(t)>R\qquad\text{for every }t\ge0.
\]

This is only a finite-interval first-exit argument. It has nothing to do with translated tails or Arzelà–Ascoli.

### Step 2: reciprocate the distance above the root

Set

\[
z(t):=\frac1{y(t)-R}>0.
\]

Then

\[
\begin{aligned}
z'(t)
&=-\frac{y'(t)}{(y(t)-R)^2}\\
&\le-\frac{(y(t)-R)(y(t)+S)}{(y(t)-R)^2}\\
&=-\frac{y(t)+S}{y(t)-R}\\
&=-1-\frac{R+S}{y(t)-R}\\
&\le-1.
\end{aligned}
\]

Consequently `z(t)+t` is nonincreasing, so

\[
z(T)\le z(0)-T.
\]

Taking

\[
T=z(0)+1
\]

gives `z(T) ≤ -1`, contradicting `z(T)>0`. Thus no globally defined forward solution can start above `R` under this differential inequality.

This reciprocal proof is preferable to solving an explicit comparison Riccati equation: it uses only elementary differentiation and monotonicity on compact intervals.

## 1.2 Excluding the negative side

Suppose for contradiction that

\[
w(x_0)<-R.
\]

For `t≥0`, define

\[
y(t):=-w(x_0+t).
\]

Because `w` is defined on all of `ℝ`, this `y` is defined for every `t≥0`, and `y(0)>R`. From

\[
w'=-w^2-aw-b
\]

we obtain

\[
\begin{aligned}
y'(t)
&=-w'(x_0+t)\\
&=w(x_0+t)^2+a(x_0+t)w(x_0+t)+b(x_0+t)\\
&=y(t)^2-a(x_0+t)y(t)+b(x_0+t)\\
&\ge y(t)^2-Ay(t)-B\\
&=(y(t)-R)(y(t)+S)
\end{aligned}
\]

whenever `y(t)>R`. The forward blow-up lemma gives a contradiction. Hence

\[
w(x)\ge-R\qquad\text{for every }x.
\]

This is the requested rigorous “finite-time Riccati blowdown” argument: if `w` ever lies below `-R`, then `-w` must become infinite after a finite increase in `x`, contradicting globality.

## 1.3 Excluding the positive side by spatial reversal

Suppose instead that

\[
w(x_0)>R.
\]

Define

\[
y(t):=w(x_0-t),\qquad t\ge0.
\]

Again, whole-line globality makes `y` available for every `t≥0`. Now

\[
\begin{aligned}
y'(t)
&=-w'(x_0-t)\\
&=w(x_0-t)^2+a(x_0-t)w(x_0-t)+b(x_0-t)\\
&\ge y(t)^2-Ay(t)-B\\
&=(y(t)-R)(y(t)+S)
\end{aligned}
\]

while `y>R`. The same blow-up lemma contradicts globality. Therefore

\[
w(x)\le R\qquad\text{for every }x.
\]

Combining the two inequalities proves

\[
\boxed{|w(x)|\le R\quad\text{for every }x\in\mathbb R.}
\]

The limit `w(-∞)=0` is not used.

## 1.4 Sharpness of the constant

The constant is the smallest universal symmetric bound available from only `|a|≤A` and `|b|≤B`.

Indeed, with constant coefficients

\[
a=-A,\qquad b=-B,
\]

the constant function `w≡R` solves the Riccati equation. With

\[
a=A,\qquad b=-B,
\]

the constant function `w≡-R` solves it. Thus neither side of `[-R,R]` can be improved uniformly without extra information.

# 2. Specialization to the stationary chemotaxis profile

Let

\[
w=\frac{U'}U.
\]

The stationary profile equation, after division by `U>0`, has the form

\[
\frac{U''}{U}+a(x)\frac{U'}U+b(x)=0.
\]

Since

\[
w'=\frac{U''}{U}-w^2,
\]

we obtain

\[
w'+w^2+a(x)w+b(x)=0.
\]

For the coefficients displayed in the paper,

\[
a(x)=c-\chi mU(x)^{m-1}V'(x),
\]

and

\[
b(x)=-\chi U(x)^{m-1}\bigl(V(x)-U(x)^\gamma\bigr)
      +1-U(x)^\alpha.
\]

Using the Lemma 5.1 signal bounds and `0<U≤M_χ`, the paper uses

\[
A=c+|\chi|mM_\chi^{m+\gamma-1}
\]

and

\[
B=|\chi|M_\chi^{m+\gamma-1}+M_\chi^\alpha.
\]

Here one also uses the paper's `M_χ≥1`, so

\[
|1-U^\alpha|\le M_\chi^\alpha.
\]

Then the generic root is exactly

\[
R=\frac12\left(
 c+|\chi|mM_\chi^{m+\gamma-1}
 +\sqrt{
 (c+|\chi|mM_\chi^{m+\gamma-1})^2
 +4|\chi|M_\chi^{m+\gamma-1}
 +4M_\chi^\alpha}
\right),
\]

which is the paper's displayed constant.

The specialization should therefore be factored into three independent pieces:

1. the quotient identity producing the Riccati equation;
2. the two coefficient-envelope lemmas `|a|≤A` and `|b|≤B`;
3. the generic whole-line Riccati theorem.

This is substantially shorter than reproducing the paper's maximizing-sequence argument.

# 3. Exact regularity that must be present

The proof is elementary, but it is not valid from a bare equation written with Mathlib's total `deriv` function.

A sound theorem needs:

- `U(x)>0` for every `x`, so `U'/U` is defined without zeros;
- genuine second-order regularity of `U`, enough to prove that `w=U'/U` is differentiable and
  \[
  w'=U''/U-w^2;
  \]
- a pointwise stationary equation with genuine derivatives;
- finite constants `A,B≥0` and pointwise coefficient bounds;
- `w` defined on the entire real line for the two-sided argument.

`ContDiff ℝ 2 U` is more than enough. A weaker collection of pointwise `HasDerivAt` hypotheses is also enough.

The repository's bare `IsTravelingWave` structure is not, by itself, a proof of this regularity: it records pointwise equations involving total `deriv`/`iteratedDeriv`, but those functions do not assert differentiability. The corrected Lemma 5.2 route should consume the existing explicit traveling-wave regularity layer, or state the needed `HasDerivAt` facts directly. It should not derive a C¹ logarithmic derivative merely by unfolding `IsTravelingWave`.

There is also an orientation distinction worth recording:

- on a half-line with only **forward** existence, the blowdown argument proves `w≥-R`;
- the upper estimate `w≤R` follows from backward existence, or alternatively from a left-tail value below `R` plus a scalar barrier argument;
- on all of `ℝ`, spatial reversal gives the upper estimate with no tail assumption.

## 5.1 The important range `1<m<2`

The new absolute logarithmic bound is even more useful in the cusp range where `s↦s^{m-1}` is not Lipschitz at zero.

Let

\[
q=m-1\in(0,1),
\]

and let `a_q(s,r)` denote the paper's secant/mean coefficient for `s^q`. For `r>0`,

\[
r\,a_q(s,r)\le r^q.
\]

To see this, write `s=rt`. If `s≠r`, then

\[
r\,a_q(s,r)
=r^q\frac{t^q-1}{t-1}.
\]

For `0≤q≤1` and `t≥0`,

\[
0\le\frac{t^q-1}{t-1}\le1.
\]

At `t=1`, the coefficient is `q≤1`. Thus, if `0<r≤M`,

\[
r\,|a_q(s,r)|\le M^q.
\]

Applying this with `r=U(x)` gives

\[
\begin{aligned}
|U'(x)|\,|a_{m-1}(u(t,x),U(x))|
&=\left|\frac{U'}U\right|
  U(x)|a_{m-1}(u,U)|\\
&\le R M^{m-1}.
\end{aligned}
\]

Consequently, if `|v_x|≤M^γ`,

\[
|b_2(t,x)|\le mR M^{m+\gamma-1}.
\]

This is the natural missing bridge for `1<m<2`. It avoids the false attempt to use an unweighted Lipschitz constant for `s^{m-1}` near zero.

# 6. Does `w→0` at `-∞` follow from the profile limits?

## 6.1 From both `U→1` and `U'→0`: yes

If

\[
U(x)\to1,
\qquad
U'(x)\to0
\qquad(x\to-\infty),
\]

then

\[
\frac{U'(x)}{U(x)}\to0.
\]

The cleanest Lean proof avoids delicate quotient-filter automation. Since `U→1`, eventually

\[
U(x)\ge\frac12.
\]

Using positivity of `U`, eventually

\[
\left|\frac{U'(x)}{U(x)}\right|
\le2|U'(x)|,
\]

and the right side tends to zero.

One can also use the standard `Tendsto.div` theorem because the denominator tends to the nonzero value `1`.

The current repository correctly treats the derivative limit as an additional analytic input: `Paper1Lemma51FrontierData.deriv_tends` supplies `WaveDerivativeTendsZero U`. Combining its left-tail component with the traveling-wave limit `U→1` is enough to prove the logarithmic derivative limit.

## 6.2 From `U→1` alone: no

Convergence of a smooth function does not force convergence of its derivative. A concrete globally positive smooth example is

\[
U(x)=1+e^x\sin(e^{-2x}).
\]

As `x→-∞`, `U(x)→1`, but

\[
U'(x)=e^x\sin(e^{-2x})-2e^{-x}\cos(e^{-2x})
\]

does not tend to zero and is unbounded along suitable sequences. The function is positive for all real `x`: for `x<0`, the perturbation has magnitude below `1`, and for `x≥0` the sine factor is positive.

Therefore the paper's statement `w(-∞)=0` is justified only because Lemma 5.1 separately proves or assumes `U'(-∞)=0`; it is not a consequence of the endpoint value of `U` alone. A Lean theorem must expose that derivative-tail fact.

# 7. Audit of the paper's translated-tail proof

The paper's intended mathematical conclusion is correct, but the printed proof takes a longer route and hides regularity bookkeeping.

It argues:

1. `w(-∞)=0` and `limsup_{x→∞}|w(x)|<∞`, hence `w` is bounded;
2. if `|w|` attains its supremum, differentiate at the maximizer and use the quadratic equation;
3. otherwise choose translations approaching the supremum and extract locally uniform limits of `w` and `w'`;
4. evaluate the limiting equation at the limiting maximizer.

The delicate point is Step 3. Local-uniform compactness of the translated derivatives requires equicontinuity of `w'`, normally obtained from a uniform bound on `w''`. The paper sketches such a bound by differentiating expressions involving `U''/U`; this requires one more layer of profile/coefficient regularity and bounded derivatives than appears in the bare abstract Riccati statement. Passing the coefficients to their tail limits also needs their asymptotics.

Those facts may be available for the paper's smooth stationary profile, but they are hidden dependencies and are expensive to formalize. None is needed for the reciprocal blow-up proof.

The following shortcuts would be false or insufficient:

- **Monotonicity alone.** `U'≤0` proves only `U'/U≤0`; it does not bound the negative magnitude.
- **A bare total-`deriv` equation.** It does not imply that `w` is differentiable.
- **`U→1` alone.** It does not imply `U'→0` or `w→0`.
- **Bounded `w'` alone for translated `w'`.** It gives Lipschitz control of `w`, not equicontinuity of `w'`.
- **An unweighted Lipschitz bound for `s^{m-1}` when `1<m<2`.** It fails at zero; the factor `U` supplied by `|U'|≤RU` is what repairs the Section 5 coefficient.
