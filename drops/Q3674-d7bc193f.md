## Why I do not recommend more C1eta wrapping now

The obvious C1eta-side thin wrappers are already present:

* canonical phase-1 representative C1eta slice;
* true-lift coefficient summability for one positive time;
* all-positive-time true-lift coefficient summability in `ChemMildC1etaPositiveTime.lean`;
* grad2/uniform-source `ChemLegData` routes.

Adding another synonym there would mostly rename the same interface.  The remaining assumptions are not C1eta packaging assumptions anymore.

## Next real analytic frontiers after the wrapper above

After `IntervalMildToLocalInitialHolder.lean`, the remaining hard frontiers toward the general-χ headline are:

1. **Produce `HasRestartCosineRepresentations D.T D.u`**, or equivalently a usable `GradientMildHalfStepRestartData D`.  The current positive-time C1eta theorem consumes `HasRestartCosineRepresentations`; it does not produce it.  This is why the route is not yet unconditional.
2. **Produce `GradientMildClassicalFrontierCoreData p D`**, especially the parabolic PDE/time-regularity/joint-continuity fields not already supplied by restart-cosine spatial regularity.  Existing local-existence wrappers in `IntervalMildToLocalExistence.lean` are ready to consume this record.
3. **For the older `localExistence_of_fp_and_regularity` path only:** prove the componentwise equality between the gradient-form Duhamel map and the old `intervalDuhamelOperator`.  For general χ this is not a thin wrapper because the old operator lacks the chemotaxis term; the direct `localExistence_of_gradientMildSolutionData_*` path avoids this mismatch and should remain preferred.
