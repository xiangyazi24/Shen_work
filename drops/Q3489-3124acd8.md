# Task95 audit: make the spatial Young estimate public first

## Executive recommendation

The cheapest next non-circular Lean task is **not** to produce the full `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareZeroDataBefore` from PDE regularity yet. The cheapest real progress is a small source-side spatial estimate layer:

1. a generic `[0,1]` interval-integral Young lemma for `‖-∫ f*g‖`,
2. a tiny definitional bridge between `lapL2sq` and the `liftDeriv2` square integral if `simp [lapL2sq, liftDeriv2]` is not already enough,
3. three physical wrappers for `H1PhysicalTaxisX`, `H1PhysicalUvxxX`, and `H1PhysicalReactX`.

This directly attacks the hard-looking fields in Task94:

```lean

    + ((1 : ℝ) / 2) * H1PhysicalTaxisPartSq p u v r
```

and analogously for `uvxx` and `react`. It does so without touching time-window RHS integrability, derivative integrability, strict/initial route packages, or bounded-before data.

The real analytic frontier is then exposed cleanly: produce the zero-window time integrability and a.e. spatial integrability of the square profiles from primitive PDE regularity/bounds. Do **not** bury that analytic work inside a theorem named like a majorant producer.

## B. Honest primitive inputs versus circular inputs

### Honest primitive inputs

The following are acceptable source-side inputs for producers of the Task96 shape:

- zero-start primitive measurability/continuity of the raw spatial fields, such as `liftDeriv2`, the three non-lap physical parts, and their products, on `(0, δ] × [0,1]` or a.e. slices;
- local, source-derived lower bound for denominators such as `1 + v`, ideally from nonnegativity/resolver positivity rather than from global bounded-before;
- local `L∞` or polynomial bounds obtained from initial data and short-time classical regularity, provided the theorem does **not** import or consume `IsPaper2BoundedBefore`;
- spatial slice integrability of `(liftDeriv2)^2`, each component square, and each product;
- time-window integrability of `lapL2sq u`, `H1PhysicalTaxisPartSq`, `H1PhysicalUvxxPartSq`, and `H1PhysicalReactPartSq` on one common `(0, δ]`;
- component a.e.-strong measurability, then assembled via the Task94 generic theorem `H1IdentityRHSValue_aestronglyMeasurable_of_components`.

### Suspicious or circular inputs

The following would smuggle the target or a downstream consequence:

- `H1IdentityRHSIntegrableBefore`: this is already the full RHS integrability package the route is trying to reach.
- `H1IdentityRHSInitialWindowIntegrableBefore`: Task87/Task90 can turn integrability into a majorant/derivative input; using it here reverses the route.
- `H1EnergyDerivativeInitialWindowIntegrableBefore`, derivative proxy, or derivative majorant packages: these are downstream of the RHS majorant/integrability path.
- `H1PhysicalRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore`, `H1PhysicalRHSZeroWindowMajorantBefore`, `H1PhysicalRHSYoungScalarZeroMajorantsBefore`, or `H1PhysicalRHSAdditive*`: these are precisely what Task94’s data lowers into.
- `H1PhysicalRHSStrictInitialRouteBefore`: this already contains the initial majorant and route data.
- `H1SupBoundSqrtRHSIntegrableBefore` or any bounded-before wrapper.
- `IsPaper2BoundedBefore`: using the headline bounded-before output to produce the initial H¹ RHS majorant is circular.
- Any theorem that proves product/square integrability by first showing `IntervalIntegrable (H1IdentityRHSValue ...)`.
- Any theorem that proves `lapL2sq` time integrability from H¹ derivative integrability. That would use the derivative route to justify the RHS route.

## D. Product estimates first, then component-square zero-window producers

The safer order is:

1. **Product estimate lemmas first** — pure fixed-time spatial inequalities. They cannot be circular unless someone adds downstream hypotheses unnecessarily.
2. **A.e. zero-window lowering second** — mechanical packaging from explicit source-side spatial/time integrability to `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareZeroDataBefore`.
3. **Hard analytic producer last** — prove the spatial/time square-profile hypotheses from PDE primitive regularity, local denominator bounds, resolver estimates, and zero-start data.

Do **not** first create a broad “component-square zero-window majorant producer” that simply assumes product bounds or `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareZeroDataBefore`. That risks turning the missing analytic estimate into a tautological record field. If a square-profile record is useful, make it visibly source-side, e.g. require `IntervalIntegrable` of the explicit square profiles and a.e. spatial slice integrability, not any route-level majorant.

## E. The hard analytic task to send out

After Task95, send this to a prover/subagent as the genuine analysis problem:

> Produce a common zero window `(0, δ]` on which `lapL2sq u`, `H1PhysicalTaxisPartSq p u v`, `H1PhysicalUvxxPartSq p u v`, and `H1PhysicalReactPartSq p u` are time-integrable, and on which the spatial square/product integrability hypotheses of `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareZeroDataBefore_of_spatialYoungInputs` hold a.e., using only zero-start primitive regularity, local source bounds, denominator lower bounds, and classical PDE/resolver estimates. Do not use any H¹ RHS integrability, derivative integrability, strict/initial route package, or bounded-before theorem.

This is the real PDE content. In particular:

- `H1PhysicalTaxisPartSq` needs control of `u_x * v_x / (1+v)^β` in `L²_x`, integrated in time.
- `H1PhysicalUvxxPartSq` needs control of the `u*(μv-νu^γ)/(1+v)^β` part and the `β*u*(v_x)^2/(1+v)^(β+1)` part in `L²_x`, integrated in time.
- `H1PhysicalReactPartSq` needs control of `u*(a-b*u^α)` in `L²_x`, integrated in time.
- `lapL2sq` needs true `u_xx` square time integrability near zero. This is not harmless; if it is derived from H¹ derivative integrability or full RHS integrability, the proof has become circular.

A safe source theorem should look closer to “zero-start primitive bounds imply square-profile time integrability” than to “route data imply component-square data.”

## F. Hidden tautologies to reject in review

Reject any Task95/Task96 proof that does one of the following:

1. Uses `H1IdentityRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore_of_initialWindowIntegrable` in reverse spirit. That theorem is useful downstream, but it is tautological if used to manufacture the majorant from the integrability goal.
2. Uses `H1IdentityRHSInitialWindowIntegrableBefore` or `H1IdentityRHSIntegrableBefore` to prove the component-square route. That already proves the target integrability layer.
3. Uses `H1EnergyDerivativeInitialWindowIntegrableBefore` to prove RHS integrability. Task87/Task88 go the other direction.
4. Uses `H1PhysicalRHSYoungScalarZeroMajorantsBefore` to prove the Young fields of `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareZeroDataBefore`. That is just a rename of the desired conclusion.
5. Uses `IsPaper2BoundedBefore` for local `L∞` control unless the theorem is explicitly a conditional bounded-before corollary, not part of the unconditional route.
6. Assumes `∀ᵐ r, ‖H1PhysicalTaxisX p u v r‖ ≤ ...` as a hypothesis in a theorem whose only purpose is to fill that same field. If product estimates are hypotheses, label the theorem as wiring only and do not count it as analytic progress.

The bright line: Task95 should prove facts about **spatial integrals at a fixed time** from **spatial integrability of their explicit factors**. Anything involving the assembled H¹ RHS as an integrable time function is downstream and should be kept out.
