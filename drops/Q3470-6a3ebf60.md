# Q3470 audit: next non-circular H¹ initial-window majorant target

## Verdict

The next useful task is **not** to try to prove the whole physical bounded-before wrapper again, and it is **not** to resurrect zero-start `lapL2sq` continuity.  The correct frontier after Task87/Task88 is:

1. keep `H1PhysicalRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore` as the Task88 input;
2. add a **localized zero-window majorant adapter**, so the hard analytic proof only has to control the assembled physical RHS on `(0, δ]`;
3. separately add a physical scalar/additive decomposition theorem reducing that localized majorant to raw `lapL2sq` and the three physical integral scalars.

This is real progress because Task88 already has strict positive-time component continuity.  Strict continuity should pay for all windows `[a,b]` with `0 < a`; the remaining analytic difficulty is only the singular endpoint at `0`.  Asking a prover for a global `H1PhysicalRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore` on every `[0,b]` is therefore a needlessly large target unless it is first split into “near zero” plus “strictly away from zero”.

## A. Is `H1PhysicalRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore` the right next target?

**Yes as a final interface, but no as a monolithic analytic theorem.**

It is the right final input because Task87 turns

```lean
H1IdentityRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore p u T
  (H1PhysicalTaxisX p u v)
  (H1PhysicalUvxxX p u v)
  (H1PhysicalReactX p u)
```

into initial-window RHS integrability, and with the physical identity into zero-start derivative integrability.  Task88 then combines that with the physical identity, sqrt bounds, and strict positive-time component continuity.

But the producer should not be stated as “from classical solution + existing route packages, prove the full majorant”.  That is too broad and invites circular proof search.  The non-circular producer should be sourced from primitive estimates of the physical RHS near zero:

```text
raw L²/L¹-in-time control of liftDeriv2 / lapL2sq
+ L² or L∞ controls of the physical component factors
+ denominator lower bounds for 1 + v
+ measurability / primitive zero-start continuity
→ localized physical RHS majorant on (0, δ]
→ global initial-window majorant using strict-positive continuity away from zero
```

The important distinction is:

```text
GOOD:  prove a near-zero L¹ majorant from primitive parabolic/component estimates.
BAD:   prove the majorant from bounded-before, full RHS integrability, derivative integrability, or the Task88 strict-initial route itself.
```

## B. Minimal non-circular source hypotheses

### What a producer should consume

For a physical producer, the minimal honest analytic inputs are these, preferably on a small zero window `(0, δ]` rather than on every `[0,b]` directly.

1. **Zero-start primitive continuity/measurability.**

   This is safe when it concerns primitive fields and physical representatives already isolated upstream: `liftTimeDeriv`, chemotaxis representative, reaction representative, `u`, `v`, `ux`, `vx`, denominator powers, etc.  It is useful for `AEStronglyMeasurable` and local boundedness of component factors.

   It must **not** be upgraded silently to zero-start `lapL2sq` continuity.  That is the old hard route.

2. **Raw `liftDeriv2` / `lapL2sq` time integrability.**

   The assembled H¹ RHS contains the term

   ```lean
   -(lapL2sq u τ)
   ```

   inside `H1IdentityRHSValue`.  Therefore some independent control of `lapL2sq u` near zero is unavoidable.  The non-circular form is an L¹-in-time estimate such as

   ```lean
   ∀ {b : ℝ}, 0 ≤ b → b ≤ δ →
     IntervalIntegrable (fun τ => lapL2sq u τ) volume (0 : ℝ) b
   ```

   or a raw spacetime estimate for `liftDeriv2` implying it.  This is weaker than continuity of `lapL2sq` at zero and should be the analytic frontier.

3. **Physical component L²/L∞ estimates.**

   For the three physical integral scalars, the natural estimates are Cauchy-Schwarz/Young estimates of the form

   ```text
   |∫ u_xx * ChemTaxisPart| ≤ 1/2 ∫ u_xx^2 + 1/2 ∫ ChemTaxisPart^2
   |∫ u_xx * ChemUvxxPart| ≤ 1/2 ∫ u_xx^2 + 1/2 ∫ ChemUvxxPart^2
   |∫ u_xx * ReactionPart| ≤ 1/2 ∫ u_xx^2 + 1/2 ∫ ReactionPart^2
   ```

   The component-square terms should be bounded using primitive L∞/L² estimates for `u`, `ux`, `v`, `vx` and the physical powers.

4. **L∞ and lower-bound estimates.**

   These are safe if they come from maximum principle, positivity, elliptic resolver bounds, or local-existence regularity independent of the H¹ bounded-before route.  In particular, denominator bounds such as `κ ≤ 1 + v` are legitimate source hypotheses.

5. **A local H¹ seed only if it is genuinely upstream.**

   A finite initial H¹ datum or a local-existence seed on a fixed small slab can be non-circular.  A hypothesis that is effectively

   ```lean
   IsPaper2BoundedBefore intervalDomain T u
   ```

   or a sup-H¹ bound for every `b < T` is circular because it is already the intended conclusion.

### What would be circular

These should not appear in any producer of `H1PhysicalRHSInitialWindowMajorantBefore`:

```lean
H1PhysicalRHSStrictInitialRouteBefore p u v T V₁ V₂ M L
H1IdentityRHSInitialWindowIntegrableBefore p u T taxisX uvxx reactX
H1IdentityRHSIntegrableBefore p u T taxisX uvxx reactX
H1EnergyDerivativeInitialWindowIntegrableBefore u T
H1SupBoundSqrtRHSIntegrableBefore p u T V₁ V₂ M L taxisX uvxx reactX
IsPaper2BoundedBefore intervalDomain T u
```

The first one literally contains the physical majorant input.  The next two are the target or stronger than the target.  The derivative integrability input is downstream of Task87 when the majorant is the route.  The sqrt/RHS package and bounded-before wrapper are downstream of Task88.

## D. Hidden tautologies and traps

1. **Do not use derivative integrability to prove the RHS majorant.**

   Task87 uses the majorant plus the pointwise identity to obtain

   ```lean
   H1EnergyDerivativeInitialWindowIntegrableBefore u T
   ```

   Using that derivative-integrability input to build the majorant reverses the route and makes a loop.

2. **Do not use full RHS integrability to prove the initial-window majorant.**

   This is stronger than the desired initial-window integrability and is downstream in Task88.  It may be logically possible to manufacture a majorant from it, but it is not progress.

3. **Do not use `H1PhysicalRHSStrictInitialRouteBefore` to prove its own majorant field.**

   That package already contains the physical initial-window RHS majorant.  Any theorem projecting it back out is just a projection, not a producer.

4. **Do not use bounded-before or the final H¹ sup bound as a source.**

   Any hypothesis equivalent to a uniform H¹ bound before `T` is the conclusion of the route.  A local existence seed is only safe if it is genuinely upstream and fixed independently of this bounded-before theorem.

5. **Do not sneak in zero-start `lapL2sq` continuity.**

   The route deliberately avoided zero-start lap continuity because it is the hard/circular H² trace route.  L¹-in-time control of `lapL2sq` near zero is the right weaker replacement.

6. **Do not confuse strict positive-time continuity with zero-window integrability.**

   Continuity on every `[a,b]` with `0 < a` does not control `(0,b]`; a function can be continuous on all strict slabs and still behave like `1/t` at zero.  The new zero-window majorant is exactly the missing endpoint input.
