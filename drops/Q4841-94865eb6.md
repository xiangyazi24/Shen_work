# 9. Assessment of the four proposed approaches

## (a) Formal Birkhoff series

**Useful for:** the three modes, all inverse-power corrections, the exact rate, and a terminal approximation for validated backward computation.

**Insufficient for:** the value \(G\). Formal series do not contain global connection constants.

## (b) Katz–Dettweiler rigidity

This is not the short route.

Katz rigidity concerns differential local systems on a punctured projective line. The recurrence in \(n\) is a difference module; one must first identify an exact generating-function differential system. Matching one Poincaré polynomial at \(n=\infty\) is not matching all local monodromy classes.

For a rank-\(r\) local system with \(m\) singular points, the rigidity index is

\[
\operatorname{rig}=(2-m)r^2+\sum_{i=1}^m\dim Z(T_i).
	ag{9.1}
\]

For \(r=3,m=4\), rigidity requires

\[
\sum_i\dim Z(T_i)=20.
\]

Four generic semisimple monodromies contribute only \(4\cdot3=12\). Special Jordan classes could raise the sum, but they must be computed exactly. Moreover, even a rigid homogeneous local system does not determine the chosen initial cycle or the mixed period functional carrying \(G\).

## (c) Initial vectors plus the Birkhoff connection matrix

**This is the most feasible rigorous asymptotic method.** Formula (4.5) is exact, and Sections 5–6 give a validated way to compute it. It proves existence and rate with no matrix AZ certificate.

To identify the result with \(G\), however, one must still prove (7.1), preferably through a one-column adjoint period or a scalar error integral.

## (d) Specialization in \(n\)

No useful decoupling point is currently known. The negative integer and half-integer factors in the coefficients describe singularities and gauge divisors, but they do not by themselves produce a terminating basis or a connection formula. This route becomes viable only if one finds an exact hypergeometric factor, contiguous relation, or removable singularity giving explicit boundary data.

1. Normalize by \(H_n\) and \(D_n\), and use Birkhoff–Trjitzinsky theory to establish the modes (1.4).
2. Construct \(w_+(0)\) by the validated backward adjoint method. This proves \(A_+
e0\), existence of the quotient, and the rate (2.4).
3. Do **not** try to evaluate the full connection matrix.
4. Construct only one scalar certificate:
   
   \[
   (\mathbf p_0-G\mathbf q_0)w_+(0)=0,
   \]
   
   either by an integral representation of \(w_+\), or by an error integral \(I_n=p_n-Gq_n\) satisfying the recurrence and a subdominant bound.
5. Conclude
   
   \[
   rac{p_n}{q_n}	o G,\qquad
   rac{p_n}{q_n}-G=O(n^{-3}ho^n).
   \]

In other words:

	ext{Birkhoff proves convergence and speed; one scalar period certificate proves the value.}

\]
