# 9. Assessment of the four proposed approaches

## (a) Formal Birkhoff series

**Useful for:** the three modes, all inverse-power corrections, the exact rate, and a terminal approximation for validated backward computation.

**Insufficient for:** the value G. Formal series do not contain global connection constants.

## (b) KatzвЂ“Dettweiler rigidity

This is not the short route.

Katz rigidity concerns differential local systems on a punctured projective line. The recurrence in n is a difference module; one must first identify an exact generating-function differential system. Matching one PoincarГ© polynomial at n=в€ћ is not matching all local monodromy classes.

For a rank-r local system with m singular points, the rigidity index is

$$
rig=(2-m)r^2+ОЈ_i dim Z(T_i).
$$

For r=3 and m=4, rigidity requires

$$
ОЈ_i dim Z(T_i)=20.
$$

Four generic semisimple monodromies contribute only 4В·3=12. Special Jordan classes could raise the sum, but they must be computed exactly. Moreover, even a rigid homogeneous local system does not determine the chosen initial cycle or the mixed period functional carrying G.

## (c) Initial vectors plus the Birkhoff connection matrix

**This is the most feasible rigorous asymptotic method.** The pairing formula is exact, and Sections 5вЂ“6 give a validated way to compute it. It proves existence and rate with no matrix AZ certificate.

To identify the result with G, one must still prove the single scalar relation, preferably through a one-column adjoint period or a scalar error integral.

## (d) Specialization in n

No useful decoupling point is currently known. The negative integer and half-integer factors in the coefficients describe singularities and gauge divisors, but they do not by themselves produce a terminating basis or a connection formula. This route becomes viable only if one finds an exact hypergeometric factor, contiguous relation, or removable singularity giving explicit boundary data.
