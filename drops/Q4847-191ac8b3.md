# 1. A marked Wirtinger presentation and longitude

The convention must be fixed before any word map can be meaningful. Changing the planar diagram, base path, mirror, or preferred-longitude orientation changes all printed words by conjugation, inversion, or Nielsen transformations.

Ham--Lee use a two-meridian twist-knot presentation

\[
\pi_1(X_{2n})
=\left\langle s,t\ \middle|\ s\,w\,t^{-1}w^{-1}=1ightangle,
\qquad
w=(t s^{-1}t^{-1}s)^n.
	ag{1.1}
\]

The five-twist knot \(7_2=T_5\) is represented, after the mirror convention used there, by \(T_{-6}\), hence \(n=-3\).

For the peripheral marking it is safer to use Mathews' convention. Put

\[
B=t s^{-1}t^{-1}s,\qquad
C=s t^{-1}s^{-1}t.
	ag{1.2}
\]

Taking Mathews' generators \(a=t,\ b=s\) and exponent \(3\), the group relation and preferred longitude are

\[
tB^3=B^3s,\qquad
\lambda=B^3C^3,\qquad \mu=t.
	ag{1.3}
\]

The relation in (1.3) is equivalent to the Ham--Lee form

\[
sB^{-3}=B^{-3}t.
\]

With the beta filling convention

\[
\mu\lambda^{-1}=1,
	ag{1.4}
\]

the filled group is therefore

\[
oxed{
G_eta=\left\langle s,t\ \middle|
 tB^3s^{-1}B^{-3}=1,\quad
 tC^{-3}B^{-3}=1
ightangle .}
	ag{1.5}
\]

If the chosen A-polynomial longitude is inverted, the second relator in (1.5) must be inverted accordingly. This is not cosmetic: an exact matrix check of the peripheral word must be performed before searching for a Tietze map.

References:

- Ham--Lee, *Explicit formulae for Chern--Simons invariants of the twist knot orbifolds and edge polynomials of twist knots*, Proposition 2.1 and Lemma 3.3, arXiv:1411.2383.
- Mathews, *An explicit formula for the A-polynomial of twist knots*, Section 2; in particular, \(\lambda=w^n\bar w^n\), arXiv:1403.2020.

# 3. Complete Tietze conversion from (2.6) to the Seifert presentation

This part is fully explicit and does not require a computer.

Starting from (2.6), adjoin the generators

\[
H=X^2,\qquad h=H^{-1},\qquad
x=X,\quad y=Y,\quad z=Y^{-1}X.
	ag{3.1}
\]

Then

\[
x^2h=X^2H^{-1}=1,\qquad
y^3h=Y^3H^{-1}=1,
	ag{3.2}
\]

and (2.5) gives

\[
z^{17}h^3=H^3H^{-3}=1.
	ag{3.3}
\]

Finally,

\[
xyz=XY(Y^{-1}X)=X^2=H=h^{-1}.
	ag{3.4}
\]

Thus one obtains exactly

\[
oxed{
\left\langle h,x,y,z\ \middle|
egin{array}{l}
[h,x]=[h,y]=[h,z]=1,\\
x^2h=1,\quad y^3h=1,\quad z^{17}h^3=1,\\
xyz=h^{-1}
\end{array}ightangle .}
	ag{3.5}
\]

Conversely, from (3.5), eliminate

\[
h=x^{-2},\qquad z=y^{-1}x.
	ag{3.6}
\]

The remaining relations are exactly

\[
x^2=y^3,\qquad (y^{-1}x)^{17}=x^6,
\]

which recovers (2.6). Hence Sections 2--3 are a complete Tietze equivalence.

The only missing word-level step is the marked Kirby isomorphism

\[
G_eta(s,t)\cong G_{2,3,17}(X,Y).
	ag{3.7}
\]

A homeomorphism statement such as (2.1) does not specify (3.7). Kirby moves must be based and tracked to obtain the induced words.

# 4. Exact trace evidence for the commutator normalization

Let \(m=s_eta\) be the beta meridian eigenvalue. The primitive beta polynomial is

\[
egin{aligned}
p_eta(m)={}&m^{16}-7m^{15}+22m^{14}-48m^{13}+87m^{12}-133m^{11}
+178m^{10}-211m^9+223m^8\\
&-211m^7+178m^6-133m^5+87m^4-48m^3+22m^2-7m+1.
\end{aligned}
	ag{4.1}
\]

Put

\[
a=m+m^{-1}.
\]

Then \(a\) satisfies

\[
q_eta(a)=a^8-7a^7+14a^6+a^5-25a^4+9a^3+12a^2-3a-1.
	ag{4.2}
\]

Let

\[
u=2\cosrac{2\pi}{17}.
\]

Its minimal polynomial is

\[
p_{17}(u)=u^8+u^7-7u^6-5u^5+15u^4+6u^3-10u^2-u+1.
	ag{4.3}
\]

Direct expansion gives the exact identity

\[
oxed{q_eta(a)=p_{17}(a-1).}
	ag{4.4}
\]

The selected real beta root therefore gives

\[
oxed{m+m^{-1}=1+2\cosrac{2\pi}{17}.}
	ag{4.5}
\]

Now take the central lift of the maximal triangle representation with

\[
\operatorname{tr}X=0,\qquad
\operatorname{tr}Y=1,\qquad
\operatorname{tr}(XY)=-c,\qquad
c=2\cosrac{\pi}{17}.
	ag{4.6}
\]

The Fricke commutator identity is

\[
\operatorname{tr}[A,B]
=(\operatorname{tr}A)^2+(\operatorname{tr}B)^2
+(\operatorname{tr}AB)^2
-\operatorname{tr}A\,\operatorname{tr}B\,\operatorname{tr}AB-2.
	ag{4.7}
\]

Applying (4.7) to \(X,Y\) gives

\[
\operatorname{tr}[X,Y]=c^2-1
=1+2\cosrac{2\pi}{17}
=m+m^{-1}.
	ag{4.8}
\]

Therefore the beta meridian matrix is conjugate in \(\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb R)\) to the commutator matrix \([X,Y]\).

This is a strong normalization for a word search, but it proves only a matrix conjugacy. It does **not** prove that the based surgery homeomorphism sends the group element \(s\) to the word \([x,y]\).

# 5. What constitutes a valid word-map certificate

A valid compact certificate consists of four explicit reduced words

```text
X_ST(s,t)
Y_ST(s,t)
S_XY(X,Y)
T_XY(X,Y)
```

satisfying all of the following exact checks.

## 5.1 Target relations inside the filled knot group

With

\[
x_0=X_{ST}(s,t),\qquad y_0=Y_{ST}(s,t),
\]

one must prove in \(G_eta\) that

\[
x_0^2=y_0^3,\qquad
(y_0^{-1}x_0)^{17}=x_0^6.
	ag{5.1}
\]

## 5.2 Source relations inside the Seifert group

Substitute

\[
s_0=S_{XY}(X,Y),\qquad t_0=T_{XY}(X,Y)
\]

into both relators of (1.5) and reduce them to the identity in (2.6).

## 5.3 The two compositions

One must also prove

\[
X_{ST}(s_0,t_0)=X,\qquad
Y_{ST}(s_0,t_0)=Y,
	ag{5.2}
\]

and

\[
S_{XY}(x_0,y_0)=s,\qquad
T_{XY}(x_0,y_0)=t.
	ag{5.3}
\]

Equations (5.1)--(5.3) are stronger and less ambiguous than a printed list of elementary Tietze moves: they give mutually inverse marked homomorphisms.

A trace-only pair does not suffice. The maximal Fuchsian representation has a nontrivial central kernel when regarded as a representation of the Seifert group, so matrix equality cannot replace equality in the finitely presented group.

# 6. Exact representation-level word search

The following Sage code searches for words with the required traces in the exact beta field. It is a discovery stage, not the final group certificate.

```sage
# SageMath
R.<x> = PolynomialRing(QQ)
pb = (
    x^16 - 7*x^15 + 22*x^14 - 48*x^13 + 87*x^12 - 133*x^11
    + 178*x^10 - 211*x^9 + 223*x^8 - 211*x^7 + 178*x^6
    - 133*x^5 + 87*x^4 - 48*x^3 + 22*x^2 - 7*x + 1
)

# Select the real root near 0.4068.
K.<m> = NumberField(pb, embedding=RDF(0.4068))

Vdist = ((m^2 + 1)*(m^3 - 1))/((m^2 - 1)*(m^3 + 1))
xi = ((m - m^(-1))^2/2) * (Vdist - 1)

Smat = matrix(K, [[m, 1], [0, m^(-1)]])
Tmat = matrix(K, [[m, 0], [xi, m^(-1)]])
I2 = identity_matrix(K, 2)

# The exact beta simplification xi=(m-1)^2/(m^2-m+1).
assert xi == (m - 1)^2/(m^2 - m + 1)

P17 = x^8 + x^7 - 7*x^6 - 5*x^5 + 15*x^4 + 6*x^3 - 10*x^2 - x + 1

MATS = {
    's': Smat,
    'S': Smat^(-1),
    't': Tmat,
    'T': Tmat^(-1),
}
INV = {'s':'S', 'S':'s', 't':'T', 'T':'t'}


def eval_matrix(word):
    A = I2
    for ch in word:
        A *= MATS[ch]
    return A


def reduced_words_exact_length(length):
    if length == 0:
        yield ''
        return

    def rec(prefix):
        if len(prefix) == length:
            yield prefix
            return
        for ch in ('s','S','t','T'):
            if prefix and INV[ch] == prefix[-1]:
                continue
            yield from rec(prefix + ch)

    yield from rec('')


def words_up_to(max_length):
    yield ''
    for ell in range(1, max_length + 1):
        yield from reduced_words_exact_length(ell)


def target17(A):
    tr = A.trace()
    return P17(-tr) == 0 and RDF(K(-tr)) > 0


for max_length in range(1, 19):
    XC = []
    YC = []

    for w in words_up_to(max_length):
        A = eval_matrix(w)
        if A.trace() == 0 and A^2 == -I2:
            XC.append((w, A))
        if A.trace() == 1 and A^3 == -I2:
            YC.append((w, A))

    print('length', max_length, 'X candidates', len(XC), 'Y candidates', len(YC))

    for wx, Xmat in XC:
        for wy, Ymat in YC:
            XY = Xmat*Ymat
            if not target17(XY):
                continue
            if XY^17 != -I2:
                continue

            Zmat = Ymat^(-1)*Xmat
            if Zmat^17 != -I2:
                continue

            print('REPRESENTATION CANDIDATE')
            print('x word =', wx)
            print('y word =', wy)
            print('tr x   =', Xmat.trace())
            print('tr y   =', Ymat.trace())
            print('tr xy  =', XY.trace())
            raise SystemExit
```

This search should be supplemented by meet-in-the-middle hashing if the first words have length much greater than \(18\).

# 9. Direct face-pairing/SnapPy route

SnapPy can compute matrices for its unsimplified face-pairing generators from assigned shapes through `FundamentalPolyhedronEngine.from_manifold_and_shapes`. The source implementation documents that:

- each generator records a paired pair of tetrahedron faces and a `Perm4` map;
- `GeneratorMatrices` stores the corresponding Möbius matrices;
- the unsimplified relators are satisfied up to the central sign in \(\mathrm{SL}_2\).

A direct workflow is:

1. reconstruct the \(T,U,V,W\) triangulation;
2. transport it to SnapPy's `7_2` triangulation using an explicit combinatorial isomorphism;
3. transport the real beta shapes through that isomorphism;
4. build the fundamental polyhedron and generator matrices;
5. add the exact filling relator from `peripheral_curves()`;
6. run a Tietze simplification while recording old-generator images;
7. compare the simplified presentation with (2.6).

The critical point is step 6: `fundamental_group()` by itself prints a simplified presentation but does not constitute a recorded old-to-new word certificate. The old-generator images must be retained.

Relevant SnapPy source:

- `snappy/snap/fundamental_polyhedron.py`, class `FundamentalPolyhedronEngine`.

# Final answer to the four questions

## 1. Wirtinger presentation

A coherent two-meridian presentation is (1.1); in the marked convention used for the longitude, it is equivalently

\[
\langle s,t\mid tB^3=B^3sangle,\qquad
B=t s^{-1}t^{-1}s.
\]

The preferred longitude in that marking is

\[
\lambda=B^3C^3,\qquad C=s t^{-1}s^{-1}t.
\]

## 2. The \((-1)\)-filled presentation

With meridian \(t\) and relation \(t\lambda^{-1}=1\), it is exactly (1.5). Topologically it is, up to orientation, \(1/3\)-surgery on a trefoil and therefore has the compact presentation (2.6).

## 3. Explicit Tietze sequence

The Tietze sequence from the trefoil surgery presentation (2.6) to the corrected Seifert presentation is given completely in Section 3:

\[
h=(X^2)^{-1},\qquad x=X,\qquad y=Y,\qquad z=Y^{-1}X.
\]

The preceding words \(X=X(s,t)\) and \(Y=Y(s,t)\) are **not present in the supplied sources and have not been output by the prior search**. They are the remaining marked Kirby/Tietze computation. Claiming specific words without the exact checks (5.1)--(5.3) would not be valid.

## 4. Trace verification

Once the words are produced, Section 8 verifies the traces exactly in the beta number field. Independently, Section 4 proves the exact character-level identity

\[
\operatorname{tr}ho_eta(\mu)
=\operatorname{tr}[X,Y]
=1+2\cosrac{2\pi}{17}.
\]

This explains why a commutator-normalized search is the natural one, but it is not a substitute for the marked group-word certificate.

In short: the trefoil-to-Seifert map is explicit; the \(7_2\)-Wirtinger-to-trefoil word map remains the finite missing computation. The included exact search and verifier specify precisely what output will complete that step.
