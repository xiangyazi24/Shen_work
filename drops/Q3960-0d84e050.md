The requested theorem `negativePartTest_diff_off_countable_of_gradient_bound` is not provable from the stated black boxes.  `truncatedPicardLimit_lipschitzOn_positive_time` gives Lipschitz control, and `truncatedPicardLimit_gradient_bound_positive_time` gives a bound on Lean's `deriv`; neither gives pointwise `HasDerivAt` off a countable set.  A Lipschitz real function can fail to be differentiable on an uncountable null set, and Lean's `deriv` is still a total function, so bounding `deriv` does not recover `HasDerivAt`.

The right fix is to add a stronger positive-time regularity black box, for example a C1/off-countable theorem for the lifted Picard-limit slice, or weaken the conclusion to a.e. differentiability via Rademacher-style Lipschitz theory.  The second theorem, the derivative bound, should be proved from the Lipschitz bound for `negativePartTest`, not from countability.
