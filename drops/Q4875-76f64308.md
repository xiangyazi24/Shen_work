# Problem 2.5: explicit finite-field linear system for the matrix Almkvist–Zeilberger certificate

## Executive answer

Let

\[
Q(u,v)=1-u^2v^2,
\qquad
h_n(u,v,t)=\frac{u^n v^n}{Q(u,v)^{n+1}(1+t^2)}.
\]

Normalize the CMF step matrix by

\[
\delta(n)=-2(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2,
\qquad
M_H(n)=\frac{M(n)}{\delta(n)}.
\]

If the normalized row trajectory is

\[
\widehat P_{n+1}=\widehat P_nM_H(n),
\qquad
\widehat P_0=
\begin{pmatrix}
30921&-32972&8240\\
33750&-36000&9000
\end{pmatrix},
\]

then the finite AZ search should **not** try to fit the entries of \(\widehat P_n\) by low-degree rational functions of \(n\). Those entries are P-recursive sequences. Instead factor \(\widehat P_n\) out and solve one universal \(3\times3\) identity.

Write the three fluxes as

\[
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal A_n&=h_n\widehat P_n\frac{uQ}{E(n)}A(n,u,v,t),\\
\mathcal B_n&=h_n\widehat P_n\frac{vQ}{E(n)}B(n,u,v,t),\\
\mathcal C_n&=h_n\widehat P_n\frac{1+t^2}{E(n)}C(n,u,v,t),
\end{aligned}
\]

where \(A,B,C\) now denote polynomial numerator matrices and

\[
B(n,u,v,t)=A(n,v,u,t).
\]

The logarithmic derivatives are

\[
\ell_u=\frac{n+(n+2)u^2v^2}{uQ},
\qquad
\ell_v=\frac{n+(n+2)u^2v^2}{vQ},
\qquad
\ell_t=-\frac{2t}{1+t^2},
\]

and

\[
\frac{h_{n+1}}{h_n}=\frac{uv}{Q}.
\]

A direct calculation gives

\[
(\partial_u+\ell_u)(uQA)
=uQ A_u+\bigl(n+1+(n-1)u^2v^2\bigr)A,
\]

with the analogous formula in \(v\), and

\[
(\partial_t+\ell_t)((1+t^2)C)=(1+t^2)C_t.
\]

Therefore it is sufficient to solve

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
E(n)(uv-Q)M_H(n)
={}&Q\Bigl[
 uQ A_u+vQ B_v+(1+t^2)C_t\\
&\qquad+\bigl(n+1+(n-1)u^2v^2\bigr)(A+B)
\Bigr].
\end{aligned}}
\tag{AZ}
\]

Left multiplication by either initial row then gives the desired \(2\times3\) identity. The initial rows frame the resulting period; they do not enter the Gaussian elimination.

This correction is important: the count \(153\) belongs to the universal \(3\times3\) system. A literal unknown \(2\times3\) trajectory numerator is a different, larger shifted-rational problem.

# 1. Denominator support

## 1.1 Denominator of the normalized matrix

Put

\[
\begin{aligned}
p_{11}&=136n^4+1424n^3+5548n^2+9551n+6141,\\
p_{12}&=384n^6+6384n^5+44168n^4+162698n^3+336377n^2+369933n+169011,\\
p_{13}&=480n^4+4980n^3+19210n^2+32690n+20730,\\
p_{21}&=48n^3+386n^2+1017n+879,\\
p_{22}&=272n^5+3848n^4+21732n^3+61184n^2+85761n+47808,\\
p_{23}&=320n^3+2540n^2+6610n+5640,\\
p_{31}&=32n^4+302n^3+1037n^2+1530n+813,\\
p_{32}&=192n^6+2984n^5+19116n^4+64452n^3+120256n^2+117279n+46476,\\
p_{33}&=16n^5+408n^4+2912n^3+8884n^2+12254n+6240.
\end{aligned}
\]

Then

\[
\boxed{
M_H(n)=
\begin{pmatrix}
\dfrac{p_{11}}{2(n+2)^2(2n+7)^2}
&-\dfrac{p_{12}}{2(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2}
&\dfrac{p_{13}}{2(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2}
\\[4mm]
-\dfrac{p_{21}}{(2n+7)^2}
&\dfrac{p_{22}}{2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2}
&-\dfrac{p_{23}}{2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2}
\\[4mm]
\dfrac{p_{31}}{(2n+7)^2}
&-\dfrac{p_{32}}{2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2}
&\dfrac{p_{33}}{2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2}
\end{pmatrix}.}
\tag{1}
\]

Its least common denominator is

\[
\boxed{D_M(n)=2(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2.}
\tag{2}
\]

Moreover,

\[
\det M_H(n)=\frac{(n+1)(2n+3)^2}{(n+3)(2n+7)^2}.
\]

## 1.2 Denominators of the certificates

The substitutions \(X_u=uQA\), \(X_v=vQB\), \(X_t=(1+t^2)C\) have already absorbed every geometric denominator coming from \(\ell_u,\ell_v,\ell_t\). Thus the numerator ansatz should be polynomial in \(u,v,t\). Do **not** add powers of \(Q\), \(u\), \(v\), or \(1+t^2\) to the certificate denominator at the first stage.

Use one common denominator in \(n\):

\[
A_{\rm rat}=\frac{A}{E(n)},
\qquad
B_{\rm rat}=\frac{B}{E(n)},
\qquad
C_{\rm rat}=\frac{C}{E(n)}.
\]

A practical denominator ladder is

\[
\begin{array}{c|l}
\text{mode}&E(n)\\ \hline
\texttt{one}&1\\[1mm]
\texttt{squarefree}&(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)(2n+3)(2n+5)(2n+7)\\[1mm]
\texttt{matrix}&(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2\\[1mm]
\texttt{orbit}&(n+1)(2n+3)(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2.
\end{array}
\tag{3}
\]

The constant factor \(2\) is irrelevant. Start with `one`, then `squarefree`; only then increase the exponents already present in (2). This is the Abramov-style universal-denominator strategy suggested by the local pole orbits.

Small shifts such as \(E(n\pm1)\) are needed only if one also makes the trajectory numerator \(R_n\) unknown, because the term \(R_{n+1}\) introduces \(E_R(n+1)\). In that direct formulation the cleared denominator is at least

\[
\operatorname{lcm}\bigl(E_R(n+1),E_R(n)D_M(n),E_A(n),E_B(n),E_C(n)\bigr).
\]

That is **not** the 153-variable system. In the recommended universal formulation the known normalized trajectory is factored out, so there is no rational denominator ansatz for \(R_n\).

# 2. Numerator degrees and the exact unknown counts

The ten monomials of total degree at most two in \((u,v,t)\) split under \(u\leftrightarrow v\) into

\[
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal S={}&
\{1,u+v,uv,u^2+v^2,t(u+v),t^2,t\},\\
\mathcal A={}&
\{u-v,t(u-v),(u-v)(u+v)\}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{4}
\]

Thus each entry of \(A\) has ten coefficients. Since \(B=A(v,u,t)\), it has no independent coefficients. A symmetric \(C\) has seven coefficients. For a universal \(3\times3\) matrix this gives

\[
\boxed{9(10+7)=153}
\]

coefficient slots when the coefficients are constant in \(n\). If every slot is a polynomial in \(n\) of degree at most \(d_n\), the scalar unknown count is

\[
\boxed{153(d_n+1).}
\tag{5}
\]

Before symmetry the count is

\[
3\text{ directions}\times9\text{ entries}\times10\text{ monomials}=270.
\]

## 2.1 Removing pure \(C\)-gauges

The four symmetric monomials

\[
1,\quad u+v,\quad uv,\quad u^2+v^2
\]

are independent of \(t\), so they disappear from \((1+t^2)C_t\). They are pure bulk gauges and also cancel in the boundary difference \(C(1)-C(0)\). Removing them leaves

\[
C\text{-basis}=\{t(u+v),t^2,t\}.
\]

The effective count becomes

\[
\boxed{9(10+3)(d_n+1)=117(d_n+1).}
\tag{6}
\]

## 2.2 A further parity reduction

The target \((uv-Q)M_H\) is invariant under

\[
(u,v)\mapsto(-u,-v)
\]

and is even in \(t\). The AZ operator respects these parity blocks, with the caveat that \(C_t\) reverses the \(t\)-parity of \(C\). Averaging any solution over these involutions shows that a particular solution may be sought in the smaller space

\[
\begin{aligned}
A\text{-basis}_{\min}
&=\{1,uv,u^2+v^2,u^2-v^2,t^2\},\\
C\text{-basis}_{\min}
&=\{t\}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{7}
\]

Here \(B\) is obtained by changing the sign of \(u^2-v^2\). This gives

\[
\boxed{9(5+1)(d_n+1)=54(d_n+1)}
\tag{8}
\]

unknowns. The discarded parity sectors can contain homogeneous gauge solutions, but they cannot be needed for a particular solution with the given right-hand side.

Each matrix entry is independent. In the minimal mode one therefore solves nine copies of a system with only

\[
\boxed{6(d_n+1)}
\]

unknowns. Equivalently, solve one coefficient matrix with nine right-hand sides.

## 2.3 Why geometric degree two is minimal

After multiplication by \(Q\), the target contains the monomial \(uv\). A flux numerator of total degree zero or one cannot produce exponent pair \((1,1)\): multiplication by the coefficients \(Q\) and \(n+1+(n-1)u^2v^2\) changes the \((u,v)\)-exponents only by \((0,0)\) or \((2,2)\), while the covariant derivatives \(u\partial_u\), \(v\partial_v\) preserve the parity class of the exponent pair. The term \((1+t^2)C_t\) with degree at most one produces only exponent pairs \((0,0)\) and \((2,2)\).

Hence

\[
\boxed{\deg_{u,v,t}=2\text{ is the first degree that can work}.}
\tag{9}
\]

There is no comparable theorem forcing the degree in \(n\). Use

\[
d_n=0,1,2,3,4
\]

as the search ladder. Degree zero is a useful debugging case; degree two is the first serious bound suggested by the normalized regular-singular behavior. Gaussian elimination, not formal asymptotics, decides the actual minimum.

## 2.4 Where “about 450 equations” comes from

For a degree-two geometric ansatz, clearing \(Q\) produces at most about fifty distinct \((u,v,t)\)-monomials per matrix entry. With nine entries this is about

\[
9\times50\approx450
\]

geometric coefficient equations. If the coefficients are expanded as degree-\(d_n\) polynomials in \(n\), the true scalar equation count is larger. In the modular evaluation implementation below, every additional good value of \(n\) supplies another copy of the geometric coefficient rows. The program prints the exact rank and equation count; the number 450 is only the geometric-support estimate.

# 3. Complete Sage program

Save the following as `az_25_matrix.sage` and run

```bash
sage az_25_matrix.sage
```

The program:

1. audits the Pochhammer normalization exactly;
2. builds the degree-two symmetry-adapted ansatz;
3. assembles one sparse coefficient matrix with nine right-hand sides;
4. solves over \(\mathbf F_{2^{61}-1}\);
5. uses enough distinct values of \(n\) to certify the cleared polynomial identity modulo the prime;
6. checks additional holdout values;
7. reconstructs the three numerator matrices;
8. attempts rational reconstruction and exact verification over \(\mathbf Q(n,u,v,t)\).

```python
#!/usr/bin/env sage
from sage.all import *

# ================================================================
# Configuration
# ================================================================

P_MOD = ZZ(2)**61 - 1             # 2305843009213693951, prime
BASIS_MODE = "minimal"           # "full", "effective", or "minimal"
DEGREES_TO_TRY = [0, 1, 2, 3, 4]
DENOMINATOR_MODES = ["one", "squarefree", "matrix", "orbit"]
EXTRA_HOLDOUT_VALUES = 8
STOP_AT_FIRST_CANDIDATE = True

INITIAL_ROWS = matrix(ZZ, [
    [30921, -32972, 8240],
    [33750, -36000, 9000],
])

# ================================================================
# CMF matrix and normalization
# ================================================================

def p_data(z):
    p11 = 136*z**4 + 1424*z**3 + 5548*z**2 + 9551*z + 6141
    p12 = (384*z**6 + 6384*z**5 + 44168*z**4 + 162698*z**3
           + 336377*z**2 + 369933*z + 169011)
    p13 = 480*z**4 + 4980*z**3 + 19210*z**2 + 32690*z + 20730
    p21 = 48*z**3 + 386*z**2 + 1017*z + 879
    p22 = (272*z**5 + 3848*z**4 + 21732*z**3 + 61184*z**2
           + 85761*z + 47808)
    p23 = 320*z**3 + 2540*z**2 + 6610*z + 5640
    p31 = 32*z**4 + 302*z**3 + 1037*z**2 + 1530*z + 813
    p32 = (192*z**6 + 2984*z**5 + 19116*z**4 + 64452*z**3
           + 120256*z**2 + 117279*z + 46476)
    p33 = 16*z**5 + 408*z**4 + 2912*z**3 + 8884*z**2 + 12254*z + 6240
    return p11, p12, p13, p21, p22, p23, p31, p32, p33


def raw_matrix(z, K):
    p11, p12, p13, p21, p22, p23, p31, p32, p33 = p_data(z)
    return matrix(K, [
        [-(2*z+5)*(z+3)**2*p11,
         p12,
         -p13],
        [2*(2*z+5)*(z+2)**2*(z+3)**2*p21,
         -(z+2)**2*p22,
         (z+2)**2*p23],
        [-2*(2*z+5)*(z+2)**2*(z+3)**2*p31,
         (z+2)**2*p32,
         -(z+2)**2*p33],
    ])


def normalized_matrix(z, K):
    p11, p12, p13, p21, p22, p23, p31, p32, p33 = p_data(z)
    return matrix(K, [
        [p11 / (2*(z+2)**2*(2*z+7)**2),
         -p12 / (2*(z+2)**2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2),
         p13 / (2*(z+2)**2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2)],
        [-p21 / (2*z+7)**2,
         p22 / (2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2),
         -p23 / (2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2)],
        [p31 / (2*z+7)**2,
         -p32 / (2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2),
         p33 / (2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2)],
    ])


def D_M(z):
    return 2*(z+2)**2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2


def exact_normalization_audit():
    Rn = PolynomialRing(QQ, 'N')
    N = Rn.gen()
    K = Rn.fraction_field()
    z = K(N)
    delta = -D_M(z)
    M0 = raw_matrix(z, K)
    MH = normalized_matrix(z, K)
    assert all(x == 0 for x in (MH - M0/delta).list())
    expected_det = (z+1)*(2*z+3)**2 / ((z+3)*(2*z+7)**2)
    assert MH.det() == expected_det
    print("exact normalization audit: PASS")

# ================================================================
# Finite field and geometric polynomial ring
# ================================================================

F = GF(P_MOD)
R = PolynomialRing(F, names=('u', 'v', 't'), order='degrevlex')
u, v, t = R.gens()
Q = 1 - u**2*v**2
suv = u**2*v**2

FULL_SYM = [
    R(1), u+v, u*v, u**2+v**2, t*(u+v), t**2, t,
]
FULL_ANTI = [
    u-v, t*(u-v), (u-v)*(u+v),
]


def choose_bases(mode):
    if mode == "full":
        A_basis = FULL_SYM + FULL_ANTI
        A_swap = FULL_SYM + [-m for m in FULL_ANTI]
        C_basis = list(FULL_SYM)
        return A_basis, A_swap, C_basis

    if mode == "effective":
        A_basis = FULL_SYM + FULL_ANTI
        A_swap = FULL_SYM + [-m for m in FULL_ANTI]
        C_basis = [t*(u+v), t**2, t]
        return A_basis, A_swap, C_basis

    if mode == "minimal":
        anti_even = (u-v)*(u+v)       # u^2-v^2
        A_basis = [R(1), u*v, u**2+v**2, anti_even, t**2]
        A_swap = [R(1), u*v, u**2+v**2, -anti_even, t**2]
        C_basis = [t]
        return A_basis, A_swap, C_basis

    raise ValueError(mode)


A_BASIS, A_SWAP_BASIS, C_BASIS = choose_bases(BASIS_MODE)

# ================================================================
# Certificate denominator ladder
# ================================================================

def cert_den_value(nn, mode):
    z = F(nn)
    if mode == "one":
        return F(1)
    if mode == "squarefree":
        return (z+1)*(z+2)*(z+3)*(2*z+3)*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)
    if mode == "matrix":
        return (z+2)**2*(z+3)**2*(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2
    if mode == "orbit":
        return ((z+1)*(2*z+3)*(z+2)**2*(z+3)**2
                *(2*z+5)*(2*z+7)**2)
    raise ValueError(mode)


def cert_den_degree(mode):
    return {
        "one": 0,
        "squarefree": 6,
        "matrix": 7,
        "orbit": 9,
    }[mode]


def cert_den_exact(N, mode):
    if mode == "one":
        return N.parent()(1)
    if mode == "squarefree":
        return (N+1)*(N+2)*(N+3)*(2*N+3)*(2*N+5)*(2*N+7)
    if mode == "matrix":
        return (N+2)**2*(N+3)**2*(2*N+5)*(2*N+7)**2
    if mode == "orbit":
        return ((N+1)*(2*N+3)*(N+2)**2*(N+3)**2
                *(2*N+5)*(2*N+7)**2)
    raise ValueError(mode)

# ================================================================
# Good n-values and normalized matrix over F_p
# ================================================================

def M_H_mod(nn):
    z = F(nn)
    if D_M(z) == 0:
        raise ZeroDivisionError("pole of M_H modulo p")
    return normalized_matrix(z, F)


def good_n(nn, den_mode):
    z = F(nn)
    return D_M(z) != 0 and cert_den_value(nn, den_mode) != 0


def first_good_ns(count, den_mode, start=0):
    ans = []
    k = ZZ(start)
    while len(ans) < count:
        if good_n(k, den_mode):
            ans.append(k)
        k += 1
    return ans

# ================================================================
# Linearized operator for one coefficient slot
# ================================================================

def labels_for_degree(d):
    labels = []
    for r in range(d+1):
        for j in range(len(A_BASIS)):
            labels.append(('A', j, r))
        for j in range(len(C_BASIS)):
            labels.append(('C', j, r))
    return labels


def contribution(label, nn):
    """Q times the RHS contribution of one numerator coefficient."""
    kind, j, r = label
    z = F(nn)
    scalar = z**r
    Kpoly = R(z+1) + R(z-1)*suv

    if kind == 'A':
        m = A_BASIS[j]
        ms = A_SWAP_BASIS[j]
        inner = (
            u*Q*m.derivative(u)
            + v*Q*ms.derivative(v)
            + Kpoly*(m+ms)
        )
        return scalar * Q * inner

    if kind == 'C':
        m = C_BASIS[j]
        inner = (1+t**2)*m.derivative(t)
        return scalar * Q * inner

    raise ValueError(kind)


def targets_at_n(nn, den_mode):
    E = cert_den_value(nn, den_mode)
    MH = M_H_mod(nn)
    return [E*(u*v-Q)*R(MH[i, j]) for i in range(3) for j in range(3)]

# ================================================================
# Build one coefficient matrix with nine right-hand sides
# ================================================================

def build_system(d, train_ns, den_mode):
    labels = labels_for_degree(d)
    rows = []
    rhs_rows = []

    for nn in train_ns:
        cols = [contribution(label, nn) for label in labels]
        targets = targets_at_n(nn, den_mode)

        support = set()
        for f in cols:
            support.update(f.dict().keys())
        for f in targets:
            support.update(f.dict().keys())

        for exponent in sorted(support, key=lambda e: tuple(e)):
            row = [f.dict().get(exponent, F(0)) for f in cols]
            b = [f.dict().get(exponent, F(0)) for f in targets]
            if any(c != 0 for c in row) or any(c != 0 for c in b):
                rows.append(row)
                rhs_rows.append(b)

    L = matrix(F, rows, sparse=True)
    B = matrix(F, rhs_rows, sparse=True)
    return L, B, labels


def degree_bound_after_clearing(d, den_mode):
    # Multiply the identity by D_M.  The target has degree <= deg(E)+7;
    # the certificate side has degree <= d+1+deg(D_M)=d+8.
    return max(cert_den_degree(den_mode)+7, d+8)


def solve_system(d, den_mode):
    proof_count = degree_bound_after_clearing(d, den_mode) + 1
    all_ns = first_good_ns(proof_count + EXTRA_HOLDOUT_VALUES, den_mode)
    train_ns = all_ns[:proof_count]
    holdout_ns = all_ns[proof_count:]

    L, B, labels = build_system(d, train_ns, den_mode)
    rank = L.rank()
    aug_rank = L.augment(B).rank()
    consistent = (rank == aug_rank)

    print("\n" + "="*78)
    print("basis mode       =", BASIS_MODE)
    print("denominator mode =", den_mode)
    print("n-degree         =", d)
    print("train n-values   =", train_ns)
    print("holdout values   =", holdout_ns)
    print("unknowns/entry   =", L.ncols())
    print("global unknowns  =", 9*L.ncols())
    print("equations/RHS    =", L.nrows())
    print("global equations =", 9*L.nrows())
    print("rank              =", rank)
    print("augmented rank    =", aug_rank)
    print("consistent        =", consistent)
    print("affine dim/entry  =", L.ncols()-rank if consistent else None)

    if not consistent:
        return None

    X = L.solve_right(B)   # dimensions: unknowns/entry by 9 RHS
    return {
        'd': d,
        'den_mode': den_mode,
        'labels': labels,
        'X': X,
        'train_ns': train_ns,
        'holdout_ns': holdout_ns,
        'rank': rank,
        'affine_dim': L.ncols()-rank,
    }

# ================================================================
# Holdout verification
# ================================================================

def verify_mod_p(candidate):
    labels = candidate['labels']
    X = candidate['X']
    den_mode = candidate['den_mode']

    for nn in candidate['holdout_ns']:
        cols = [contribution(label, nn) for label in labels]
        targets = targets_at_n(nn, den_mode)
        for e in range(9):
            got = R(0)
            for k, col in enumerate(cols):
                if X[k, e] != 0:
                    got += X[k, e]*col
            if got != targets[e]:
                print("holdout failure: n=", nn, "entry=", divmod(e, 3))
                print("residual=", got-targets[e])
                return False
    print("mod-p holdout verification: PASS")
    return True

# ================================================================
# Reconstruct modular numerator matrices
# ================================================================

def modular_matrices(candidate):
    labels = candidate['labels']
    X = candidate['X']
    SR = PolynomialRing(R, 'Ncoef')
    Ncoef = SR.gen()

    A = matrix(SR, 3, 3, 0)
    B = matrix(SR, 3, 3, 0)
    C = matrix(SR, 3, 3, 0)

    for e in range(9):
        i, j = divmod(e, 3)
        for k, label in enumerate(labels):
            coeff = X[k, e]
            if coeff == 0:
                continue
            kind, idx, r = label
            factor = SR(coeff)*Ncoef**r
            if kind == 'A':
                A[i, j] += factor*SR(A_BASIS[idx])
                B[i, j] += factor*SR(A_SWAP_BASIS[idx])
            else:
                C[i, j] += factor*SR(C_BASIS[idx])

    return A, B, C

# ================================================================
# One-prime rational reconstruction and exact verification
# ================================================================

def lift_residue(c):
    a = ZZ(c)
    try:
        return QQ(a.rational_reconstruction(P_MOD))
    except (ValueError, ArithmeticError, ZeroDivisionError):
        if a > P_MOD//2:
            a -= P_MOD
        return QQ(a)


def exact_bases(U, V, T, mode):
    sym = [1, U+V, U*V, U**2+V**2, T*(U+V), T**2, T]
    anti = [U-V, T*(U-V), (U-V)*(U+V)]

    if mode == "full":
        return sym+anti, sym+[-m for m in anti], sym
    if mode == "effective":
        return sym+anti, sym+[-m for m in anti], [T*(U+V), T**2, T]
    if mode == "minimal":
        ae = (U-V)*(U+V)
        return ([1, U*V, U**2+V**2, ae, T**2],
                [1, U*V, U**2+V**2, -ae, T**2],
                [T])
    raise ValueError(mode)


def attempt_exact_verification(candidate):
    if candidate['affine_dim'] != 0:
        print("exact lift skipped: modular solution is not unique")
        print("fix gauges or choose a canonical solution before CRT")
        return False

    labels = candidate['labels']
    Xmod = candidate['X']
    den_mode = candidate['den_mode']

    S = PolynomialRing(QQ, names=('N', 'U', 'V', 'T'))
    N, U, V, T = S.gens()
    K = S.fraction_field()
    N, U, V, T = map(K, (N, U, V, T))

    Qe = 1-U**2*V**2
    Ke = N+1+(N-1)*U**2*V**2
    Abasis, Aswap, Cbasis = exact_bases(U, V, T, BASIS_MODE)

    A = matrix(K, 3, 3, 0)
    B = matrix(K, 3, 3, 0)
    C = matrix(K, 3, 3, 0)

    for e in range(9):
        i, j = divmod(e, 3)
        for k, label in enumerate(labels):
            cmod = Xmod[k, e]
            if cmod == 0:
                continue
            coeff = K(lift_residue(cmod))
            kind, idx, r = label
            factor = coeff*N**r
            if kind == 'A':
                A[i, j] += factor*Abasis[idx]
                B[i, j] += factor*Aswap[idx]
            else:
                C[i, j] += factor*Cbasis[idx]

    MH = normalized_matrix(N, K)
    E = K(cert_den_exact(N, den_mode))
    Au = A.apply_map(lambda f: f.derivative(U))
    Bv = B.apply_map(lambda f: f.derivative(V))
    Ct = C.apply_map(lambda f: f.derivative(T))

    inner = U*Qe*Au + V*Qe*Bv + (1+T**2)*Ct + Ke*(A+B)
    residual = E*(U*V-Qe)*MH - Qe*inner

    ok = True
    for i in range(3):
        for j in range(3):
            if K(residual[i, j]).numerator() != 0:
                ok = False
                print("nonzero exact residual at", (i, j))

    if ok:
        print("exact verification over Q(N,U,V,T): PASS")
        print("A_num =", A)
        print("B_num =", B)
        print("C_num =", C)
        print("E(N)  =", E)
        boundary = C.apply_map(
            lambda f: f.subs({T: K(1)}) - f.subs({T: K(0)})
        )
        print("C(N,U,V,1)-C(N,U,V,0) =", boundary)
    else:
        print("one-prime reconstruction failed")
        print("repeat over two or three large primes, CRT each unique coordinate,")
        print("then rerun this exact verifier")

    return ok
```

# 4. How to interpret the computation

The system is inhomogeneous. A solution exists modulo \(p\) precisely when

```text
rank(L) = rank([L | B]).
```

A positive homogeneous nullity alone is not enough. Stable consistency, pivot structure and affine dimension should be checked at several primes. If a solution is unique, reconstruct its coordinates by CRT and rational reconstruction, then verify (AZ) symbolically over \(\mathbf Q(n,u,v,t)\).

The degree bound used in the code comes from multiplying (AZ) by \(D_M(n)\). The target then has \(n\)-degree at most

\[
\deg E+7,
\]

while the certificate side has degree at most

\[
d_n+1+\deg D_M=d_n+8.
\]

Thus

\[
\boxed{\deg_n\le\max(\deg E+7,d_n+8)}
\]

for the cleared residual. One more distinct good \(n\)-value certifies the polynomial identity modulo \(p\); the extra values are implementation holdouts.

The code solves only the **bulk** AZ identity. A complete Catalan proof must also evaluate the boundary

\[
\frac{C(n,u,v,1)-C(n,u,v,0)}{E(n)}.
\]

In the minimal basis \(C=tC_0(n,u,v)\), so this boundary is exactly \(C_0/E\). It must be identified with the neutral extension direction carrying Catalan's constant and then matched to the initial rows. This boundary/framing check is separate from Gaussian elimination of the bulk identity.

Finally, the Chyzak–Kauers–Salvy framework explains why a rational-certificate search is finite once the support and degree bounds are fixed. It does **not** guarantee that total degree two or \(d_n\le4\) contains a solution. Failure of every displayed case means only that the ansatz must be enlarged or the integrated-period kernel corrected.
