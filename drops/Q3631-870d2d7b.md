## Why the positive-time theorem cannot feed the zero-time leg

For every fixed `τ > 0`, `mild_orderBox_positiveTime_holder` can give a Holder constant on `[τ, D.T]`.  But the zero-time Duhamel theorem integrates over `(0,t)`, and as `t → 0+` this interval consists entirely of times below any fixed positive `τ`.  Choosing `τ` depending on `t` does not solve this, because `secondDerivDuhamel_tendsto_zero_of_uniform_holder` needs a single uniform `Hq` for all relevant `s`.

The issue is not a Lean packaging detail.  The proof of the positive-time theorem explicitly uses the heat smoothing singularity `t^{-θ/2}` for the initial leg.  Without a separate assumption that the initial leg already has a non-blowing Holder modulus, the result is not uniform at zero.

