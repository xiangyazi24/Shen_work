# Problem 2.5: the correct AZ carrier, the discrete alternative, and what remains unknown

## Executive conclusion

The failed kernel

\[
h_n(u,v,t)=\frac{u^nv^n}{(1-u^2v^2)^{n+1}(1+t^2)}
\]

is not merely missing a few monomials. Its **phase is the wrong phase** for Problem 2.5.

After the \(H_n\)-normalization, the homogeneous silver-ratio carrier is the Hadamard square of the central-Delannoy/Legendre system. A rational constant-term phase for that carrier is

\[
\boxed{
\phi(u)=\frac{(1+u)(u+2)}{u}=3+u+\frac2u,
\qquad
\Phi(u,v)=\phi(u)\phi(v).}
\tag{0.1}
\]

Its four critical points \(u,v=\pm\sqrt2\) have the three distinct critical values

\[
\boxed{
17+12\sqrt2,\qquad 1,\qquad 17-12\sqrt2.}
\tag{0.2}
\]

Those are exactly the normalized Poincaré roots of the CMF.

Catalan's constant is then an **extension of this carrier**, not part of the homogeneous phase. The natural parameter family is

\[
\boxed{
\mathfrak h_n(u,v,t;\varepsilon)
=\Phi(u,v)^n\,
\frac{t^{\varepsilon}}{uv(1+t^2)},
\qquad
-\left.\partial_\varepsilon
\int_0^1\frac{t^\varepsilon}{1+t^2}\,dt
\right|_{\varepsilon=0}=G.}
\tag{0.3}
\]

However, using (0.3) with a \(t\)-independent scalar numerator only gives \(G\) times a homogeneous Delannoy period. The actual CMF extension requires one of the following equivalent extra structures:

1. a matrix- or row-valued numerator depending nontrivially on \(t\), together with a parameter derivative or a \(t=1\) boundary term;
2. a Jacobi parameter-derivative kernel in the variable \(X=t^2\);
3. a mixed \(P\)-\(Q\) Legendre cycle selecting the critical value \(1\), followed by the Catalan normal-function extension.

At present there is **no certified closed formula for the exact CMF kernel** and no certified single proper-hypergeometric summand \(F(n,k)\) for \(\widehat Q_n\). The most concrete independently defined scalar candidate is the Jacobi–Padé kernel \(R_n^J(X)\) below. The exact CMF kernel is likely a short rational contiguous combination of that family and its Christoffel–Darboux companion.

The smallest useful computation is therefore not another search with the phase \(uv/(1-u^2v^2)\). It is one of these two finite searches:

- a row-valued Almkvist–Zeilberger search with phase (0.1) and logarithmic derivatives (3.3) below;
- a discrete search in a small Jacobi/Christoffel–Darboux band, tested exactly against the degree-13 CMF recurrence.

# 1. Why the attempted kernel cannot carry the CMF spectrum

Put

\[
w=uv,
\qquad
\psi(w)=\frac{w}{1-w^2}.
\]

The exponential part of the attempted kernel is \(\psi(uv)^n\). It depends on \(u,v\) only through one product. Consequently:

1. the logarithmic Hessian has a flat direction \(u\mapsto cu,\ v\mapsto c^{-1}v\);
2. the saddle is not isolated in two variables;
3. the phase cannot produce three independent Lefschetz classes of the required rank-three carrier.

More concretely,

\[
\frac{d\log\psi}{d\log w}
=\frac{1+w^2}{1-w^2}.
\]

Hence the only finite critical points satisfy

\[
w^2=-1,
\]

and their critical values are

\[
\psi(i)=\frac{i}{2},
\qquad
\psi(-i)=-\frac{i}{2}.
\]

They are unrelated to

\[
17+12\sqrt2,\quad1,\quad17-12\sqrt2.
\]

Thus the augmented-rank inconsistency in the reported \(20\) searches is the expected outcome. Enlarging only the \(n\)-degree or the denominator mode does not repair a wrong phase.

The same warning applies to a proposed phase \(xy/(1-xy)\): that is a one-product binomial phase, not the Hadamard-square Delannoy phase.

# 2. The exact homogeneous Delannoy carrier

The central Delannoy numbers are

\[
D_n=P_n(3)=1,3,13,63,321,\ldots
\]

and satisfy

\[
\boxed{(n+1)D_{n+1}=3(2n+1)D_n-nD_{n-1}.}
\tag{2.1}
\]

They have the rational constant-term representation

\[
\boxed{
D_n=\operatorname{CT}_{u}
\left(\frac{(1+u)(u+2)}{u}\right)^n.}
\tag{2.2}
\]

Indeed,

\[
\operatorname{CT}_{u}(1+u)^n(1+2/u)^n
=\sum_{k=0}^n\binom nk^2 2^k=D_n.
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
D_n^2
=\frac1{(2\pi i)^2}
\oint\!\oint
\Phi(u,v)^n\frac{du}{u}\frac{dv}{v},
\qquad
\Phi(u,v)=\phi(u)\phi(v).}
\tag{2.3}
\]

The critical equation is

\[
\phi'(u)=1-\frac2{u^2}=0,
\]

so \(u=\pm\sqrt2\), with

\[
\phi(\sqrt2)=3+2\sqrt2,
\qquad
\phi(-\sqrt2)=3-2\sqrt2.
\]

The product critical values are exactly (0.2). This is the correct geometric source of

\[
(\lambda-1)(\lambda^2-34\lambda+1).
\]

## 2.1 A certified single-sum version

Clausen's identity collapses the square to the terminating proper-hypergeometric sum

\[
\boxed{
D_n^2=\sum_{k=0}^n F_D(n,k),
\qquad
F_D(n,k)=
2^k\binom{2k}{k}\binom nk\binom{n+k}{k}.}
\tag{2.4}
\]

The shift ratios are

\[
\frac{F_D(n+1,k)}{F_D(n,k)}
=\frac{n+k+1}{n-k+1},
\]

\[
\frac{F_D(n,k+1)}{F_D(n,k)}
=\frac{4(2k+1)(n-k)(n+k+1)}{(k+1)^3}.
\]

An explicit Zeilberger certificate is

\[
\begin{aligned}
&(n+2)^2(2n+1)F_D(n+2,k)\\
&\quad-(2n+3)(35n^2+70n+26)F_D(n+1,k)\\
&\quad+(2n+1)(35n^2+70n+26)F_D(n,k)\\
&\quad-(2n+3)n^2F_D(n-1,k)\\
&=\mathcal G_D(n,k+1)-\mathcal G_D(n,k),
\end{aligned}
\tag{2.5}
\]

where

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal G_D(n,k)=
-\frac{4(n+1)(2n+1)(2n+3)k^3}
{(n-k+1)(n-k+2)(n+k)}F_D(n,k).}
\tag{2.6}
\]

This is the correct discrete WZ carrier for the **homogeneous** symmetric square. It is not yet a formula for the optimized CMF sequence \(\widehat Q_n\).

# 3. The correct continuous AZ equation

Let

\[
H_n=(-16)^n(2)_n^2(3)_n^2
\left(\frac52\right)_n
\left(\frac72\right)_n^2
\]

and

\[
M_H(n)=\frac{M(n)}{H_{n+1}/H_n},
\qquad
\frac{H_{n+1}}{H_n}
=-2(n+2)^2(n+3)^2(2n+5)(2n+7)^2.
\tag{3.1}
\]

For

\[
\mathfrak h_n(u,v,t;\varepsilon)
=\Phi(u,v)^n\frac{t^\varepsilon}{uv(1+t^2)},
\]

the exact shift and logarithmic derivatives are

\[
\boxed{
\frac{\mathfrak h_{n+1}}{\mathfrak h_n}=\Phi(u,v),}
\tag{3.2}
\]

\[
\boxed{
\frac{\partial_u\mathfrak h_n}{\mathfrak h_n}
=n\frac{u^2-2}{u(u+1)(u+2)}-\frac1u,}
\tag{3.3a}
\]

\[
\boxed{
\frac{\partial_v\mathfrak h_n}{\mathfrak h_n}
=n\frac{v^2-2}{v(v+1)(v+2)}-\frac1v,}
\tag{3.3b}
\]

and

\[
\boxed{
\frac{\partial_t\mathfrak h_n}{\mathfrak h_n}
=\frac{\varepsilon}{t}-\frac{2t}{1+t^2}.}
\tag{3.3c}
\]

For a row-valued numerator \(\mathcal P_n\in\operatorname{Mat}_{1\times3}\), the correct AZ equation is

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\Phi\,\mathcal P_{n+1}-\mathcal P_nM_H(n)
={}&\mathcal D_u\mathcal A_n
+\mathcal D_v\mathcal B_n
+\mathcal D_t\mathcal C_n,
\end{aligned}}
\tag{3.4}
\]

where

\[
\mathcal D_u
=\partial_u+n\frac{u^2-2}{u(u+1)(u+2)}-\frac1u,
\]

\[
\mathcal D_v
=\partial_v+n\frac{v^2-2}{v(v+1)(v+2)}-\frac1v,
\]

\[
\mathcal D_t
=\partial_t+\frac\varepsilon t-\frac{2t}{1+t^2}.
\]

The two rows of the initial \(2\times3\) matrix can be solved independently. This reduces a \(3\times3\) search to two \(1\times3\) blocks.

## 3.1 Necessary geometric denominator support

The natural geometric denominator is built from

\[
\boxed{
u(u+1)(u+2)\,v(v+1)(v+2)\,t(1+t^2),}
\tag{3.5}
\]

not \(1-u^2v^2\). The numerator basis must allow Laurent monomials in \(u\) and \(v\), because the constant-term realization itself is Laurent.

A reasonable first support is

\[
u^av^bt^c,
\qquad -2\le a,b\le2,
\qquad 0\le c\le2,
\tag{3.6}
\]

split into the \(u\leftrightarrow v\) symmetric and antisymmetric sectors. The coefficients should be affine in \(arepsilon\) and rational in \(n\), with the \(n\)-denominator supported on

\[
(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)(2n+3)(2n+5)(2n+7).
\]

The very small basis

\[
1,\ uv,\ u^2+v^2,\ u^2-v^2,\ t^2
\]

cannot resolve the separate poles at \(u=-1,-2\) and \(v=-1,-2\), nor can it implement the required moving conic/non-scalar shear.

## 3.2 The cycle matters

The constant-term cycle giving \(D_n^2\) selects the dominant \((+,+)\) saddle. The CMF error is on the neutral mode. Its integral must instead select a mixed \((+,-)\) or \((-,+)\) cycle, or contain numerator factors that cancel the dominant saddle.

This point is independent of the formal AZ identity: the same telescoper may act on several cycles, but only the mixed cycle has critical value \(1\) and polynomial \(n^{-3}\) size.

# 4. The most concrete scalar Catalan kernel

The natural parameter family for the Catalan measure is a shifted Jacobi family. Put

\[
\boxed{
J_n(X;\varepsilon)
=P_n^{(\varepsilon-1/2,0)}(1-2X).}
\tag{4.1}
\]

It has the Rodrigues formula

\[
\boxed{
J_n(X;\varepsilon)
=\frac{X^{1/2-\varepsilon}}{n!}
\frac{d^n}{dX^n}
\left[X^{n+\varepsilon-1/2}(1-X)^n\right],}
\tag{4.2}
\]

and the terminating sum

\[
\boxed{
J_n(X;\varepsilon)
=\frac{(\varepsilon+\tfrac12)_n}{n!}
\sum_{k=0}^n
(-1)^k\binom nk
\frac{(n+\varepsilon+\tfrac12)_k}
{(\varepsilon+\tfrac12)_k}X^k.}
\tag{4.3}
\]

Define

\[
B_n(\varepsilon)=J_n(-1;\varepsilon),
\qquad
\kappa_n=\frac{4n+1}{2}.
\]

A dot denotes \(\partial_\varepsilon|_{\varepsilon=0}\). Set

\[
C_n(X)=
\frac{B_n\dot J_n(X)-\dot B_nJ_n(X)}{1+X}.
\tag{4.4}
\]

The numerator vanishes at \(X=-1\), so \(C_n(X)\in\mathbf Q[X]\). Then define

\[
\boxed{
R_n^J(X)=\kappa_n\left[
B_nJ_n(X)
-\frac{1+X}{2}
\left(2X\frac d{dX}+1\right)C_n(X)
\right].}
\tag{4.5}
\]

This family is independent of the CMF and satisfies the exact Catalan-moment identity

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^1\frac{-\log t}{1+t^2}R_n^J(t^2)\,dt
=GQ_n^J-P_n^J,}
\tag{4.6}
\]

with

\[
\boxed{Q_n^J=R_n^J(-1)=\kappa_nB_n^2.}
\tag{4.7}
\]

Equivalently,

\[
\boxed{
GQ_n^J-P_n^J
=\int_0^1\!\int_0^1
\frac{R_n^J((xy)^2)}{1+(xy)^2}\,dx\,dy.}
\tag{4.8}
\]

Formula (4.8) is a literal rational double integral of the requested Catalan type.

It is **not yet certified to be the Problem 2.5 CMF error**. The exact recurrence test must be run first. If the raw family fails, no continuous certificate for that raw family exists.

## 4.1 The likely optimized family

Define the Jacobi Christoffel–Darboux polynomial

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal K_n^J(X)
=\sum_{k=0}^n\frac{4k+1}{2}B_kJ_k(X).}
\tag{4.9}
\]

At \(X=-1\),

\[
\mathcal K_n^J(-1)
=\sum_{k=0}^n\frac{4k+1}{2}B_k^2,
\]

which has the expected \(n\times(\text{silver-ratio period})^2\) scale. This matches the observed fact that \(\widehat Q_n/D_n^2\) grows linearly.

The first serious scalar ansatz is therefore

\[
\boxed{
R_n^{\rm cand}(X)
=\sum_{r=0}^{m}a_r(n)R_{n+r}^J(X)
+\sum_{r=0}^{m}b_r(n)\mathcal K_{n+r}^J(X),}
\tag{4.10}
\]

with \(m=2\) or \(3\), and rational \(a_r,b_r\) supported on the CMF singular shift orbits. This is a finite linear problem.

# 5. What is known about a discrete summand \(F(n,k)\)

## 5.1 Certified formula

For the homogeneous carrier, the answer is exactly (2.4):

\[
F_D(n,k)=2^k\binom{2k}{k}\binom nk\binom{n+k}{k}.
\]

## 5.2 Parameterized Jacobi summand

The coefficient summand in (4.3) is

\[
\boxed{
F_J(n,k;\varepsilon)
=(-1)^k\binom nk
\frac{(\varepsilon+\tfrac12)_n}{n!}
\frac{(n+\varepsilon+\tfrac12)_k}
{(\varepsilon+\tfrac12)_k}.}
\tag{5.1}
\]

It is proper hypergeometric in \(n,k\) for fixed \(arepsilon\). Differentiating at \(arepsilon=0\) produces a hypergeometric term times explicit odd harmonic sums. Keeping \(arepsilon\) symbolic and differentiating the telescoper is cleaner than telescoping the harmonic expression directly.

The product \(B_nJ_n(X)\) naturally produces a **double** summand

\[
F_J(n,r;\varepsilon)F_J(n,k;\varepsilon)(-1)^rX^k,
\tag{5.2}
\]

because the evaluation \(B_n=J_n(-1)\) is itself a terminating sum. Thus a double-sum or Hadamard-product certificate is more natural than a single \(F(n,k)\).

## 5.3 What is not known

No exact identity of the form

\[
\widehat Q_n=\sum_kF(n,k)
\]

with a printed proper-hypergeometric \(F(n,k)\) is currently certified for the challenge CMF. The minimal order-five generating equation and the required non-scalar shear are consistent with a Hadamard product, a parameter derivative, or a short contiguous combination rather than one bare hypergeometric sum.

A useful discrete search is

\[
\widehat Q_n
=\sum_k R(n,k)F_D(n,k),
\tag{5.3}
\]

with \(R(n,k)\in\mathbf Q(n,k)\), or the two-level Christoffel sum

\[
\widehat Q_n
=\sum_{j=0}^n\sum_{k=0}^j
R(n,j,k)(2j+1)F_D(j,k).
\tag{5.4}
\]

Equations (5.3) and (5.4) are finite Zeilberger/Chyzak searches once denominator and degree supports are fixed. They are candidates, not established formulas.

# 6. Exact Sage code for the decisive discrete tests

The following script:

1. reconstructs the normalized scalar recurrence directly from \(M_H(n)\);
2. constructs \(R_n^J\) and \(\mathcal K_n^J\) exactly;
3. computes every Catalan moment as a pair \(qG-p\) with \(p,q\in\mathbf Q\);
4. tests the raw Jacobi family against the CMF recurrence;
5. searches a polynomial-coefficient contiguous band and enforces the three CMF initial pairs.

A nonzero exact residual proves that the tested family cannot possess the desired CT certificate.

```python
from sage.all import *

# ============================================================
# 0. Rings
# ============================================================
Rn.<n> = PolynomialRing(QQ)
Kn = Rn.fraction_field()
RX.<X> = PolynomialRing(QQ)


def eval_n(f, m):
    f = Kn(f)
    return QQ(f.numerator()(m)) / QQ(f.denominator()(m))


# ============================================================
# 1. Printed CMF and H-normalization
# ============================================================
def M_of(x):
    m11 = (-2*x-5)*(x+3)^2*(
        136*x^4+1424*x^3+5548*x^2+9551*x+6141)
    m12 = (384*x^6+6384*x^5+44168*x^4+162698*x^3
           +336377*x^2+369933*x+169011)
    m13 = -(480*x^4+4980*x^3+19210*x^2+32690*x+20730)

    m21 = (x+2)^2*(x+3)^2*(4*x+10)*(
        48*x^3+386*x^2+1017*x+879)
    m22 = -(x+2)^2*(
        272*x^5+3848*x^4+21732*x^3+61184*x^2
        +85761*x+47808)
    m23 = (x+2)^2*(320*x^3+2540*x^2+6610*x+5640)

    m31 = -(4*x+10)*(x+2)^2*(x+3)^2*(
        32*x^4+302*x^3+1037*x^2+1530*x+813)
    m32 = (x+2)^2*(
        192*x^6+2984*x^5+19116*x^4+64452*x^3
        +120256*x^2+117279*x+46476)
    m33 = -(x+2)^2*(
        16*x^5+408*x^4+2912*x^3+8884*x^2
        +12254*x+6240)

    return matrix(x.parent(), [
        [m11,m12,m13],
        [m21,m22,m23],
        [m31,m32,m33],
    ])


def delta_H(x):
    return -2*(x+2)^2*(x+3)^2*(2*x+5)*(2*x+7)^2


def MH_of(x):
    return M_of(x) / delta_H(x)


# ============================================================
# 2. Scalar recurrence for a chosen output column
# ============================================================
def scalar_recurrence(column=0):
    """
    Returns polynomial coefficients ell[0..3] such that every normalized
    row trajectory coordinate in the selected column satisfies
        sum_j ell[j](n) u_{n+j} = 0.
    """
    e = vector(Kn, [1 if i == column else 0 for i in range(3)])
    vectors = [e]
    P = identity_matrix(Kn, 3)

    for j in range(1, 4):
        P = P * matrix(Kn, MH_of(n+j-1))
        vectors.append(P*e)

    coeffs = []
    for j in range(4):
        cols = [vectors[r] for r in range(4) if r != j]
        A = matrix(Kn, [list(v) for v in cols]).transpose()
        coeffs.append((-1)^j * A.det())

    # Clear rational denominators.
    common = Rn(1)
    for c in coeffs:
        common = lcm(common, Rn(Kn(c).denominator()))

    polys = [Rn(Kn(common)*c) for c in coeffs]
    g = gcd(polys)
    polys = [p//g for p in polys]

    # Normalize overall sign/content.
    content = gcd([ZZ(abs(a)) for p in polys for a in p if a != 0])
    if content not in (0,1):
        polys = [p/content for p in polys]
    if polys[-1].leading_coefficient() < 0:
        polys = [-p for p in polys]

    print('degrees =', [p.degree() for p in polys])
    return polys


ELL = scalar_recurrence(column=0)


# ============================================================
# 3. Exact CMF normalized initial pairs qhat*G - phat
# ============================================================
def cmf_pairs(Nmax, column=0):
    p = vector(QQ, [30921,-32972,8240])
    q = vector(QQ, [33750,-36000,9000])
    out = []

    for N in range(Nmax+1):
        out.append((q[column], p[column]))
        if N < Nmax:
            MH = matrix(QQ, MH_of(QQ(N)))
            p = p*MH
            q = q*MH
    return out


TARGET = cmf_pairs(80, column=0)


# ============================================================
# 4. Jacobi polynomial and epsilon derivative at epsilon=0
# ============================================================
def odd_harmonic(m):
    return sum(QQ(1, 2*r+1) for r in range(m))


def J_and_dJ(N):
    pref = rising_factorial(QQ(1,2), N) / factorial(N)
    J = RX(0)
    dJ = RX(0)

    for k in range(N+1):
        c = ((-1)^k * binomial(N,k) * pref
             * rising_factorial(QQ(N)+QQ(1,2), k)
             / rising_factorial(QQ(1,2), k))

        dlog = (2*odd_harmonic(N)
                + 2*sum(QQ(1, 2*N+2*r+1) for r in range(k))
                - 2*odd_harmonic(k))

        J += c*X^k
        dJ += c*dlog*X^k

    return J, dJ


def R_jacobi(N):
    J, dJ = J_and_dJ(N)
    B = J(-1)
    dB = dJ(-1)
    numerator = B*dJ - dB*J
    C, rem = numerator.quo_rem(X+1)
    assert rem == 0

    kappa = QQ(4*N+1, 2)
    return RX(kappa*(B*J - (X+1)/2*(2*X*C.derivative()+C)))


def K_jacobi(N):
    ans = RX(0)
    for k in range(N+1):
        J, _ = J_and_dJ(k)
        B = J(-1)
        ans += QQ(4*k+1,2)*B*J
    return ans


def P_jacobi(N):
    J, _ = J_and_dJ(N)
    B = J(-1)
    return RX(QQ(4*N+1,2)*B*J)


# ============================================================
# 5. Exact Catalan moment pair
# Integral = q*G - p
# ============================================================
def catalan_monomial_pair(k):
    q = QQ((-1)^k)
    partial = sum(QQ((-1)^j, (2*j+1)^2) for j in range(k))
    p = q*partial
    return q, p


def moment_pair(P):
    q = QQ(0)
    p = QQ(0)
    for k, a in enumerate(P.list()):
        qk, pk = catalan_monomial_pair(k)
        q += a*qk
        p += a*pk
    return q, p


# ============================================================
# 6. Exact recurrence test for one family
# ============================================================
def test_family(poly_family, Nmax=20):
    pairs = [moment_pair(poly_family(m)) for m in range(Nmax+4)]
    bad = []

    for m in range(Nmax+1):
        rq = sum(QQ(ELL[j](m))*pairs[m+j][0] for j in range(4))
        rp = sum(QQ(ELL[j](m))*pairs[m+j][1] for j in range(4))
        if rq != 0 or rp != 0:
            bad.append((m, rq, rp))

    if bad:
        print('first exact residual =', bad[0])
        return False

    print('all tested exact residuals vanish')
    return True


print('raw R^J test:')
test_family(R_jacobi, 20)


# ============================================================
# 7. Finite contiguous-band search
# ============================================================
FAMILIES = {
    'R': R_jacobi,
    'K': K_jacobi,
    'P': P_jacobi,
}


def family_pair(name, N):
    return moment_pair(FAMILIES[name](N))


def solve_band(width=2, degree=2, Nrec=25,
               names=('R','K','P'), enforce_initial=True):
    """
    Candidate:
        I_m = sum_{name,r,d} c[name,r,d] * m^d * I^{name}_{m+r}.

    The equations impose the CMF recurrence on both the G coefficient
    and the rational coefficient. Optionally they also impose the three
    exact CMF initial pairs.
    """
    labels = [(name,r,d)
              for name in names
              for r in range(width+1)
              for d in range(degree+1)]

    cache = {}
    def bp(name, m):
        key = (name,m)
        if key not in cache:
            cache[key] = family_pair(name,m)
        return cache[key]

    rows = []
    rhs = []

    # Recurrence equations.
    for m in range(Nrec+1):
        for component in (0,1):
            row = []
            for name,r,d in labels:
                value = QQ(0)
                for j in range(4):
                    value += (QQ(ELL[j](m))
                              * QQ((m+j)^d)
                              * bp(name,m+j+r)[component])
                row.append(value)
            rows.append(row)
            rhs.append(QQ(0))

    # Three initial pairs determine the order-three solution.
    if enforce_initial:
        for m in range(3):
            for component in (0,1):
                row = []
                for name,r,d in labels:
                    row.append(QQ(m^d)*bp(name,m+r)[component])
                rows.append(row)
                rhs.append(TARGET[m][component])

    A = matrix(QQ, rows)
    b = vector(QQ, rhs)
    Aug = A.augment(matrix(QQ, len(rhs), 1, list(rhs)))

    print('unknowns =', A.ncols(),
          'equations =', A.nrows(),
          'rank =', A.rank(),
          'augmented rank =', Aug.rank())

    if A.rank() != Aug.rank():
        return None, labels

    sol = A.solve_right(b)
    return sol, labels
```

## 6.1 How to turn a discovered band into a proof

A fitted band is only a discovery step. A complete certificate consists of:

1. the explicit rational functions \(a_r(n),b_r(n)\);
2. the exact polynomial family \(R_n^{\rm cand}(X)\);
3. an identity
   
   \[
   \sum_{j=0}^3\ell_j(n)R_{n+j}^{\rm cand}(X)
   =\frac1{w_\varepsilon(X)}
    \frac d{dX}
    \left(w_\varepsilon(X)C_n(X;\varepsilon)\right)
   \]
   
   to first order in \(arepsilon\), where
   
   \[
   w_\varepsilon(X)=\frac{X^{\varepsilon-1/2}}{1+X};
   \]
4. zero boundary terms;
5. the three exact initial moment pairs.

Because the recurrence is order three, items 3–5 prove equality with the CMF error for every \(n\).

# 8. Final answers

## What is the correct continuous kernel?

The correct **carrier phase** is

\[
\boxed{
\Phi(u,v)^n
=\left(\frac{(1+u)(u+2)}u\right)^n
 \left(\frac{(1+v)(v+2)}v\right)^n,}
\]

with Catalan introduced by \(t^\varepsilon/(1+t^2)\), a parameter derivative, and a mixed cycle or nontrivial \(t\)-dependent numerator. The phase \(uv/(1-u^2v^2)\) is incompatible with the CMF spectrum.

## Is \(x^ny^n/[(1-xy)^{n+1}(1+x^2y^2)]\) the right kernel?

No evidence supports it for this CMF. Its one-product phase has the same structural defect as the failed phase. Problem 2.5 is naturally a Hadamard square of two rank-two factors, followed by a Catalan extension.

## Is there a discrete WZ route?

Yes. The certified homogeneous summand is

\[
F_D(n,k)=2^k\binom{2k}{k}\binom nk\binom{n+k}{k}.
\]

The natural Catalan parameter summand is \(F_J(n,k;\varepsilon)\) in (5.1). The optimized CMF sequence is expected to require a rational contiguous combination, a double sum, or a Christoffel–Darboux sum. No exact one-sum formula for \(\widehat Q_n\) is presently certified.
