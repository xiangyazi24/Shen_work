After Task94/Task95, the smallest honest next producer is **component-square / zero-window majorants**, not the physical identity/substitution and not the global square-root bounds.

Reason: Task95 is about removing the strict-positive-time continuity inputs for the non-lap physical parts. Once that lands, the current strict/initial physical route still carries exactly the hard packages


The identity/substitution route still needs the genuine H¹ identity machinery: a parametric gradient-time derivative or finite-difference/IBP substitute, plus the algebraic substitution into the physical scalar triple. The square-root bounds still need physical estimates for the three scalar terms. Both are real analytic frontiers. By contrast, the component-square zero-window route has a small, clean, non-circular first step: prove the scalar Young/Cauchy estimates from spatial square integrability, and package the resulting `H1PhysicalRHSComponentSquareZeroDataBefore` from source-facing local square-integrability data.

This does **not** prove the near-zero square integrability itself from downstream H¹ data. It only removes the pure algebra/measure boilerplate and leaves the honest zero-window integrability inputs explicit.

## Why not sqrt bounds next?

`H1PhysicalRHSSqrtBoundsBefore` is just the concrete wrapper around `H1SqrtTermBoundsBefore` for the scalar triple. A producer must prove estimates of the form

```lean
(-p.χ₀) * H1PhysicalTaxisX p u v τ ≤
  (-p.χ₀) * (V₁ * (H1lapL2Norm u τ * H1gradL2Norm u τ))

(-p.χ₀) * H1PhysicalUvxxX p u v τ ≤
  (-p.χ₀) * (M * (V₂ * H1lapL2Norm u τ))

H1PhysicalReactX p u τ ≤
  L * (H1gradL2Norm u τ) ^ 2
```

These are likely true from Cauchy-Schwarz plus uniform sup bounds for the non-lap factors, resolver bounds, and reaction derivative/logistic bounds. But they require choosing concrete constants `V₁,V₂,M,L` and proving physical sup estimates. That is more semantic than the component-square zero-window reducer and should come after the zero-window algebra is cleaned up.
